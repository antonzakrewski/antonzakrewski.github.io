sylow group in general not a normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a finite group and \(\vert G \vert = p^r \cdot m\) with \(p \nmid m\). Suppose \(U \subseteq G\) is a p-sylow group, then \(U\) is in general not a normal subgroup

2. Proof

Consider \(\mathbb{F}_q\) as finite field for \(q = p^k, k \geq 1\) and \(\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{F}_q)\) as general linear group. Then by order of the general linear group for a finite field we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \vert \mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{F}_q) \vert =& \prod_{i=0}^{n-1} \left( q^n - q^{i} \right) \\ =& \prod_{i = 0}^{n-1} q^{n-i} \cdot (q^{i} - 1) \end{align*}

Hence it holds for the multiplicity

\begin{align*} \mu_{q}(\prod_{i = 0}^{n-1} q^{n-i} \cdot (q^{i} - 1)) =& \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} (n-i) \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{n} i \\ =& \frac{n \cdot (n+1)}{2} \end{align*}
