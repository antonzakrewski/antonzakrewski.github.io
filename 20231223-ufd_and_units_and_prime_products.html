ufd and units and prime products

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain, \(T = A^{\times} \cup \{p_1 \cdot ... \cdot p_n\}\) the invertible and prime product set.

TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is an UFD
  2. \(A \setminus T = \{0\}\).

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special caes of the definition

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Existence follows from construction.

It remains to show, that a decomposition is unique up to units

Suppose there exist

\begin{align*} p_1 \cdot ... \cdot p_r = p_1' \cdot ... \cdot p_k' \end{align*}

Then it follows that \(p_1 \mid p_j'\) for some \(j \in \{1,...,k\}\) (cf. prime) By integral domain and cancellable ring it follows after renumbering, that

\begin{align*} p_2 \cdot ... p_r =& \alpha \cdot p_2' \cdot ... \cdot p_k' \end{align*}

for some unit \(\alpha \in A^{\times}\)

This can be repeated until

\begin{align*} p_r =& \alpha' \cdot p_r' \cdot ... p_k' \end{align*}

since primes dont divide unit, as otherwise they would be units themselves.

Furthermore, since the LHS is a prime, so is the right hand side, hence \(r = k\) is the only possibility, showing that the decomposition is unique up to units
