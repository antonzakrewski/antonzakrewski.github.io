composite field of a normal field extension induces normal field extension

1. Proposition

Let \(L/K\) be an algebraic field extension and \(E,F \subseteq L\) subfields, such that \(E/K\) is a normal field extensions Then the composite field \(EF\) is a normal field extenison over \(F\), i.e.

\begin{align*} EF/F \end{align*}

2. Proof

By normal field extension and splitting field, there exist polynomials \(\mathfrak{F} \subseteq K[X]\) such that \(E = K[\mathfrak{F}]\) is the splitting field

Then

\begin{align*} EF = F[\mathfrak{F}] \end{align*}

2.1. a)

Suppose \(\alpha \in EF\), then \(\alpha\) is a rational combination, up to multiplicative inverse of element from \(E\) and \(F\). The elements of \(F\) are by construction in \(F[\mathfrak{F}]\) and the elements from \(E\) are generated by roots from \(\mathfrak{F}\). Since \(F[\mathfrak{F}]\) contains all roots of \(\mathfrak{F}\), it follows, that \(\alpha \in F[\mathfrak{F}]\), as \(F[\mathfrak{F}]\) is closed under addition and multiplication.

2.2. b)

Suppose \(\alpha \in F[\mathfrak{F}]\), then \(\alpha\) is generated by roots of \(\mathfrak{F}\) and elements of \(F\). Thus, since \(E\) contains all roots of \(\mathfrak{F}\), \(\alpha \in EF\) again by closure under addition and multiplication
