regular function

1. Definition

1.1. quasi-affine

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space and \(Y \subseteq \mathbb{P}_{K}^n\) a quasi-affine variety. A function \(f: Y \rightarrow K\) is said to be regular, if it is regular at each Point \(p \in Y\)

1.2. quasi-projective

Let \(K\) be a field, \(\mathbb{P}_{K}^n\) the projective space and \(O \subseteq \mathbb{P}_{K}^n\) a quasi-projective variety A function \(f: Y \rightarrow K\) is said to be regular, if it is regular at a each point \(p \in Y\)
