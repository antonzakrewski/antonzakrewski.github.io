Inverse einer \(2 \times 2\) Matrix

1. Satz

Sei \(A =& \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_{11} & \alpha_{12} \\ \alpha_{21} & \alpha_{22} \end{pmatrix} \in K^{2 \times 2}\) Falls \(A\) regul√§r ist, d.h. das inverse existiert, so gilt

\begin{align*} A^{-1} =& \frac{1}{d} \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_{22} & - \alpha_{12} \\ -\alpha_{21} & \alpha_{11} \end{pmatrix} \\ =& \begin{pmatrix} \frac{\alpha_{22}}{d} & - \frac{\alpha_{12}}{d} \\ - \frac{\alpha_{21}}{d} & \frac{\alpha_{11}}{d} \end{pmatrix} \\ \end{align*}

mit \(d = \mathrm{det}(A)\)

2. Beweis

2.1. rechtsinvers

\begin{align*} A \cdot A^{-1} =& \frac{1}{d} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_{11} \alpha_{22} -\alpha_{12} \alpha_{21} & -\alpha_{11} \alpha_{12} + \alpha_{12} \alpha_{11} \\ \alpha_{21}\alpha_{22} - \alpha_{22}\alpha_{21} & -\alpha_{21}\alpha_{12} + \alpha_{22}\alpha_{11} \end{pmatrix} \\ =& \frac{1}{d} \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_{11}\alpha_{22} - \alpha_{12}\alpha_{21} & 0 \\ 0 & \alpha_{22}\alpha_{11} - \alpha_{21} \alpha_{12} \end{pmatrix} \\ =& \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \end{align*}

andere Richtung:

2.2. linksinvers

\begin{align*} \mathrm{det}(A^{-1}) =& \frac{1}{d^2} \cdot \left( \alpha_{22} \alpha_{11} - \alpha_{12} \alpha_{21} \right) \\ =& \frac{1}{d} \end{align*}

Daraus folgt:

\begin{align*} \left(A^{-1} \right)^{-1} =& \frac{1}{\frac{1}{d}} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} \frac{\alpha_{11}}{d} & \frac{\alpha_{12}}{d} \\ \frac{\alpha_{21}}{d} & \frac{\alpha_{22}}{d} \end{pmatrix} \\ =& A \\ \end{align*}

so dass folgt:

\begin{align*} A^{-1} \cdot \left(A^{-1} \right)^{-1} =& A^{-1} \cdot A \\ =& \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \end{align*}
