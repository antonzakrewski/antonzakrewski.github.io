A-zyklischer Unterraum und zyklischer Modul

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M\) an \(R\)-module, \(U \subseteq M\) a submodule and \(A \in \mathrm{End}_{R}(M)\) a module-endomorphism. TFAE:

  1. \(U\) is A-zyklisch
  2. \(U\) as \(R[X]\) module is cyclic

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption there exists an \(m \in U\) such that \(\langle m, Am, A^2m ,...\rangle\) generates \(U\)

Consider the module-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: R[X] \rightarrow& U \\ 1 \mapsto& m \end{align*}

It is surjective, as for \(u \in U\) there exists

\begin{align*} u = \sum_{i=0}^{n} r_i A^i m \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=0}^{n} r_i X^i \mapsto u \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption there exists a module epimorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: R[X] \rightarrow& U \\ 1 \mapsto& m \end{align*}

Then it follows by surjectivity that

\begin{align*} U = \langle m, Am, A^2m, ...\rangle \end{align*}
