covering map from a locally compact hausdorff space and domain as hausdorff space

1. Proposition

Let \(p: E \rightarrow B\) be a surjective covering map where \(E\) is a locally regular compact hausdorff topological space. Then \(B\) is also hausdorff.

2. Proof

Let \(b_1,b_2 \in B\) be points. Choose trivializing opens \(U_1,U_2\). w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(b_1, \not\in U_2, b_2 \not\in U_1\) as otherwise \(U_i\) as subset of \(B\) is hausdorff and hence we may choose disjoint neighbourhoods and lift them to \(E\) (cf. Übertragung der Offenheit von der Unterraumtopologie)d Then using the local trivialization \(U_1 \simeq \coprod U_1^i\) we may take a compact neighbourhood \(C_i \subseteq U_i \subseteq \coprod U_i^j\) of \(b_i\). Then as \(\coprod U_i\) is hausdorff we may conclude that \(C_i\) is closed in \(\corpod U_i\) resp. using the local trivialization that \(C_i\) is closed in \(U_i\) Therefore \(C_i = U_i \cap A_i\) for some closed \(A_i \subseteq B\). Then \(A_1 \cap A_2\) is closed.

Note that as \(b_2 \not\in C_1 \subseteq U_1\) resp. \(b_1 \not\in C_2 \subseteq U_2\) it follows that

\begin{align*} b_1,b_2 \not\in A_1 \cap A_2 \end{align*}

Thus consider

\begin{align*} O_i \mathrm{int}(C_i) \setminus (A_1 \cap A_2) \end{align*}

(cf. Komplement einer abgeschlossenen und offenen Menge as open neighbourhoods

It is open as \(\mathrm{int}(C)_i \subseteq U_i \subseteq E\) is open and \((A_1 \cap A_2)\) is closed. It contains \(b_i\) as \(b_i \not\in (A_1 \cap A_2)\) and \(b_i \in \mathrm{int}(C_i)\)

Furthermore \(O_1, O_2\) are by construction disjoint as \(O_i \subseteq C_i\) and \((C_1 \cap C_2) \subseteq (A_1 \cap A_2)\) with \((A_1 \cap A_2) \cap C_i = \emptyset\)
