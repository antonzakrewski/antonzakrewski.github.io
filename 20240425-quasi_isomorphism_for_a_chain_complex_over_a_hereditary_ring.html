quasi isomorphism for a chain complex over a hereditary ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a hereditary ring, \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod and \(C\) a chain complex in \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod})\). Then there exists a chain complex of projectives \(P\) and a quasi-isomorphism

\begin{align*} P \rightarrow& C \\ P \rightarrow& H(C) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Choose projective resolutions

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 & Q_n & P_n & H_n(C) & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

(cf. hereditary ring and resolution of length 1)

Note that by projectivity

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & Z_n \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{}\\ P_n \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[dashed]{ru}{\exists}& H_n(C) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

there exists a lift

Now consider

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} Q_n \arrow[]{d}{}& \\ P_n \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & C_n \arrow[]{d}{}\\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{n-1} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} Q_n \arrow[bend right]{rrr}{0} \arrow[]{r}{} & P_n \arrow[]{r}{} & Z_n \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(C) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

this lifts to a map

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & & B_n \arrow[]{d}{} & \\ Q_n \arrow[dashed]{rru}{} \arrow[bend right]{rrr}{0} \arrow[]{r}{} & P_n \arrow[]{r}{} & Z_n \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(C) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} Q_n \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{}\\ P_n \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & C_n \arrow[]{d}{}\\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{n-1} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

The above diagram devines a chain map which induces n iso in degree \(n\) and the homology of the domain is trivial in all other degrees

This can be repeated over all degrees and then taken the coproduct, as category RMod as Ab4 category and hence by Fernbahnhof theorem for exact functors the homology commutes with \(\coprod -\).

Hence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} Q_n \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n+1}(C) \\ P_n \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n+1}(C) \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n+1}(C) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
