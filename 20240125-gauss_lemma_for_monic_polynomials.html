gauss lemma for primitive polynomials

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a UFD, \(Q(R)\) the quotient ring, \(h \in R[X]\) and \(f,g \in Q(R)[X]\) each primitive such that

\begin{align*} h = f \cdot g \end{align*}

Then \(f,g \in R[X]\)

2. Proof

Note that, since \(h\) is primitive, we get for \(p \in R\)

\begin{align*} \nu_p(h) = 0 \end{align*}

(see: Vielfachheit von Primelementen für ein Polynom)

Furthermore, by Gauss-Lemma, for arbitrary \(p \in R\)

\begin{align*} 0 =& \nu_p(h) \\ =&\nu_p(f) + \nu_p(g) \\ \end{align*}

Since \(f,g\) are monic as well, \(\nu_p(f), \nu_p(g) \leq 0\) and therefore

\begin{align*} \nu_p(f), \nu_p(g) = 0 \end{align*}

for arbitrary \(p \in R\)

hence \(f,g \in R[X]\)
