finite subgroup of the coproduct of C2 up to conjugation

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \coprod \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} = \langle a,b \vert a^2,b^2\rangle\) be the coproduct in category Group Then finite subgroups are conjugate to one of \(\{1,a\}, \{1,b\}, \{1\}\)

2. Proof

Let \(H\) be a nontrivial, finite subgroup. Then there exists an \(h \in H\). w.l.o.g. after iteratively cancelling possible \(a^2, b^2\) we may assume, that

\begin{align*} h =& \begin{cases} aba \cdot ... \cdot b a \\ aba \cdot ... \cdot b a b \end{cases} \end{align*}

the case of a leading \(b\) follows analogously from reasons of symmetry.

Furthermore, we may assume, that

\begin{align*} h = aba \cdot ... \cdot ba \end{align*}

as \(aba \cdot ... \cdot bab\) has non finite group order.

Suppose there exists another \(h' \in H\), then for \(h' = ba \cdot ... \cdot b\) it follows, that \(h \cdot h'\) has non finite order. So we may assume, that \(h' = aba \cdot ... \cdot ba\) with w.l.o.g. shorter length than \(h\). Multiplying and cancelling results in

\begin{align*} (aba \cdot ... \cdot ba) \cdot (aba \cdot ...' \cdot ba) =& ab \cdot ... \cdot ab \end{align*}

again with non finite order.

Thus it follows, that

\begin{align*} H = \{1, h\} \end{align*}

where \(h^2 = 1\)

Note that \(h\) has odd length and choosing the middle, we get

\begin{align*} a \cdot b \cdot ... a ... \cdot b\cdot a \end{align*}

where \((a \cdot b \cdot ...) = (... \cdot b \cdot a)^{-1}\) and thus

\begin{align*} H \simeq \{1,a\} \end{align*}

for conjugation with \((a \cdot b \cdot ...)\)
