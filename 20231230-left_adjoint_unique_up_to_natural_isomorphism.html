left adjoint unique up to natural isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{L}_1 \vdash \mathcal{R}\) and \(\mathcal{L}_2 \vdaash \mathcal{R}\) adjoints for \(\mathcal{R}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\)

Then \(\mathcal{L}_1 \cong \mathcal{L}_2\) are isomorphic in the functor category, i.e. there exists a canonical natural isomorphism

\begin{align*} \eta: \mathcal{L}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_2 \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. yoneda

By assumption there exist natural isomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}(\mathcal{L}_1(-), -) \cong \mathrm{Hom}(-, \mathcal{R}(-)) \cong \mathrm{Hom}(\mathcal{L}_2(-),-) \end{align*}

which is natural in both arguments.
