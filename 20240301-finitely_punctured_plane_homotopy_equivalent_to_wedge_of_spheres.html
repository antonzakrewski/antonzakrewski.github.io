finitely punctured plane homotopy equivalent to wedge of spheres

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{R}^2\) be the plane, \(S \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{2}\) a finite subset and

\begin{align*} \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus S \end{align*}

the puncture.

Then

\begin{align*} \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus S \cong \bigvee_{s \in S} S^1 \end{align*}

are homotopy equivalent

2. Proof-sketch

after homeomorphic stretching, we may assume w.l.o.g., that all \(s\) are on a circle. Then using

\begin{align*} D^2 \setminus \{0\} \cong S^1 \end{align*}

and applying suitable homotopy, which results in each \(S^1\) connected with a line the origin.

Then using

  1. CW Pair as cofibration
  2. cofibration from a contractible space induces homotopy equivalence to the collapse
