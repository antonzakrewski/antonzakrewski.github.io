K-Endomorphism for an algebraic field extension as automorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) an algebraic field extension and

\begin{align*} \sigma: L \rightarrow L \end{align*}

an k-Endomorphism.

Then \(\sigma\) is an automorphism

2. Proof

2.1. surjectivity

Let \(\alpha \in L\), then by assumption, \(\alpha\) is an algebraic element. Hence there exists a minimal polynomial \(f \in K[X]\).

by degree root theorem, there exist only finitely many roots.

By root under A-Algebra homomorphisms, there exists a welldefined map

\begin{align*} \sigma_{\vert \mathrm{roots}}: \{roots\} \rightarrow \{root\} \end{align*}

By Körperhomomomorphismus als Monomorphismus, \(\sigma\) is injective, hence also the restriction of an injective map as injection. Thus by Äquivalenz von Bijektivität, Surjektivität und Injektivität bei gleich mächtigen, endlichen Mengen

\begin{align*} \sigma_{\vert \mathrm{rotos}} \end{align*}

is an Bijection, as \(\{roots\}\) is a finite set.
