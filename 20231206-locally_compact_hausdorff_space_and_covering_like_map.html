free and proper action on a locally compact hausdorff space as covering like map

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a topological group, \(X\) a topological space and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a topological group action Suppose that \(X\) is locally compact hausdorff, \(\varphi\) is free and proper.

Then \(\varphi\) is covering-like

2. Proof

Let \(x \in X\), then choose a compact neighbourhood \(K\) of \(x\). Then since \(G\) is proper, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \{ g \in G \vert gK \cap K \neq \emptyset\} = \{e, g_1,...,g_n\} \end{align*}

is finite.

Let \(g_1 \neq e\). Since \(G\) is free, we conclude, that \(g_1 \cdot x \neq x\).

Then we can choose disjoint neighbourhoods of \(x,g_1x\), namely \(U_x,U_{g_1x}\).

Since \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) is continuous, it follows, that the restriction

\begin{align*} \varphi_{g_1}: \{g_1\} \times X \rightarrow X \end{align*}

is continuous with \(\{g_1\} \times X \cong X\) (cf. product with the terminal object canonically iso) Hence \(U'_{g_1 x} \coloneqq \varphi_{g_1}^{-1}[U_{g_1x}] \subseteq X\) is open with \(x \in U'_{g_1 x}\).

Thus let \(O \coloneqq \mathrm{int}(K) \cap U_x \cap U'_{g_1 x}\) be an open neighbourhood of \(x\). Suppose \(y \in O \cap g_1 O\), then by construction, \(y \in U_x\). Furthermore, there exists an \(y' \in O\) with \(g_1 \cdot y' = y\). But by construction, \(g_1 \cdot y' \in U'_{g_1 x}\), which is disjoint from \(U_x\) and hence also \(O\). Therefore, \(O \cap g_1 O = \emptyset\)

Here \(O\) is open as finite intersection of open sets and we can repeat this process for finitely many \(g_i\), namely \(g_2,...,g_n\) (assuming \(g_i \neq e\)).

Then we get a neighbourhood \(O_f\) of \(x\), where for all \(g \neq e\)

  1. either \(g \neq g_i\), hence since our group action is proper and \(O_f \subseteq K\), it follows, that \(g O_f \cap O_f = \emptyset\)
  2. or \(g = g_i\), where by construction, \(g_1 O_f\) and \(O_f\) were seperated
