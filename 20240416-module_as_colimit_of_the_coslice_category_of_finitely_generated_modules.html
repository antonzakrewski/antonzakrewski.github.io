module as colimit of the coslice category of finitely generated modules

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M\) an \(R\)-module, \(E(M)\) the coslice category of finitely generated free modules. Then

\begin{align*} M \simeq \mathrm{colim}_{E(M)}(M_i) \end{align*}

is the colimit where \(E(M)\) is the indexing category and \(M_i = R^k\) the objects for \(R^k \rightarrow M\)

2. Proof

using isomorphism between covariant hom-sets and objects it suffices to show that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(M,N) !\overset{}{\simeq} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(\mathrm{colim}_{E(M)}(M_i),N) \end{align*}

for arbitrary \(N \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{RMod})\)

Then as contravariant hom-functor dualizes small colimits to limits we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(\mathrm{colim}_{E(M)}(M_i),N) \simeq \mathrm{lim}_{E(M)} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(R^k,N) \end{align*}

by colimit indexed over coslice category gives natural transformation there exists a natural morphism

\begin{align*} \psi: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(M,N) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(\mathrm{colim}_{E(M)}(M_i),N) \simeq \mathrm{lim}_{E(M)} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(R^k,N) (\varphi: M \rightarrow N) \mapsto& (\varphi_i: e_i \mapsto \varphi(\alpha(e_1)) \end{align*}

where

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R^k \arrow[]{dd}{f} \arrow[]{rd}{\alpha}& \\ & M \\ R^l \arrow[]{ru}{\beta} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is a snapshot of the diagram

2.1. injective

Let \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) be a module-homomorphism. Then for each \(m \in M\) there exists by the adjoint property of a free module a morphism

\begin{align*} \alpha_m: R \rightarrow& M \\ 1 \mapsto& m \end{align*}

Therefore for \(\varphi \neq \psi \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(M,N)\) there exist components

\begin{align*} \varphi(\alpha_{m}) \neq \psi(\alpha_m) \end{align*}

of \(\mathrm{lim} \mathrm{Hom}(M_i,N)\) where \(\varphi(m) \neq \psi(m)\)

Therefore the map is injective

2.2. surjective

Let \((n_{\alpha})_{\alpha} \in \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(R^k,N)\) be arbitrary. Then it remains to show that the map

\begin{align*} \varphi: M \rightarrow& N \\ m \mapsto& n_{\alpha_{m}} \end{align*}

is a module-homomorphism and that an element \((n_{\alpha})_{\alpha}\) is fully determined by the objects of rank 1

\begin{align*} \alpha_m: R^1 \rightarrow M \end{align*}

2.2.1. determined

Let \(\alpha: R^k \rightarrow M\) be a morphism. Then there exist inclusions

\begin{align*} \iota_i: R \hookrightarrow R^k \end{align*}

for \(i \in \{1,...,k\}\) and further a natural diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R \arrow[]{dd}{\iota_i}\arrow[]{rd}{\alpha \circ \iota_i} & \\ & M \\ R^k \arrow[]{ru}{} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(\alpha \circ \iota_i\) corresponds to an element in \(R\) (cf. hom out of a ring)

2.2.2. module homomorphism

2.2.2.1. additive

For \(m,m' \in M\) consider the morphisms

\begin{align*} \alpha_m: R \rightarrow M \\ 1 \mapsto& m \\ \alpha_{m'}: R \rightarrow M \\ 1 \mapsto& m' \end{align*}

Then it follows that

\begin{align*} \alpha_{m + m'} =& (1 \mapsto m + m') =& ((1 \mapsto m) + (1 \mapsto m')) =& \alpha_m + \alpha_{m'} \end{align*}

hence showing

\begin{align*} n_{\alpha_{m + m'}} =& n_{\alpha_m + \alpha_{m'}} \end{align*}

Using the diagonal morphism

\begin{align*} R \arrow[]{r}{\Delta} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{\alpha_m + \alpha_{m'}}& R \oplus R \arrow[]{r}{\alpha_m \oplus \alpha_{m'}}& R \end{align*}

and <> the we get

\begin{align*} n_{\alpha_{m} + \alpha_{m'}} =& n_{\alpha_m \olpus \alpha_{m'} \circ \Delta} \\ =& n_{\alpha_m \oplus \alpha_{m'}} \circ \Delta \end{align*}

Since the map

\begin{align*} R \oplus R \rightarrow R \end{align*}

is fully determined by the restriction along \(\iota_1,\iota_2: R \rightarrow R \oplus R\) wtih

\begin{align*} n_{\alpha_m \oplus \alpha_{m'}} \circ \iota_1 = \alpha_m \end{align*}

it follows that

\begin{align*} n_{\alpha_m \oplus \alpha_{m'}} = (n_{\alpha_m} \oplus n_{\alpha_m'}) \end{align*}

which implies

\begin{align*} n_{\alpha_{m} \olpus \alpha_{m'}} =& n_{\alpha_m} + n_{\alpha_{m'}} \end{align*}
