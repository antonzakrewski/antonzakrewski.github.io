reduced nullstellensatz for a general field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field, \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a polynomial ring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) an ideal Then for the vanishing ideal and the zerolocus

\begin{align*} I(V(\mathfrak{a})) \supseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \supseteq \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

zero locus

2. Proof

2.1. \(\mathfrak{a} = K[X_1,...,X_n]\)

Then it follows that $V(K[X1,…,Xn]) = ∅ $ and therefore

\begin{align*} \mathcal{I}(\emptyset) =& K[X_1,...,X_n] \\ =& \end{align*}

2.2. aussage

Suppose \(f \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\) then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} f^n \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

Given \(P \in \mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a})\) and the evaluation homomorphism we get

\begin{align*} f^k(P) =& f(P)^k \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

Since \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is an Integral domain we conclude that

\begin{align*} f(P) = 0 \end{align*}

2.3. \(\mathfrak{a} \subsetneq K[X_1,...,X_n]\)

by finitely generated k-algebra as jacobson ring we conclude that \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is a Jacobson ring and therefore

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(K[X_1,...,X_n] \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}) \\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathcal{M}_{\mathfrak{m}}} \end{align*}

Suppose \(f \in \mathcal{I}(\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}))\). Let \(\mathfrak{m}\) be maximal with \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\). By bijective map from points of an affine space to the maximal ideals over an algebraically closed field we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m} = \mathfrak{m}_P \end{align*}

for some \(P \in \mathbb{A}_K^n\)

Then by

we conclude that

\begin{align*} P \in \mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}) \end{align*}

since \(f \in \mathcal{I(V(}\mathfrak{a})\) we get that \(f(P) = 0\) and hence \(f \in \mathfrak{m} = \mathfrak{m}_P\)
