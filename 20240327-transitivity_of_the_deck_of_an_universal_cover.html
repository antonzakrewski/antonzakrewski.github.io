path connected universal cover as galois cover

1. Proposition

Let \(p: E \rightarrow B\) be a universal cover at \(b \in B\), where \(E\) is path connected covering and \(B\) is connected.

Then \(p\) is a galois cover

2. Proof

2.1. galois at \(b\)

Let \(e,e' \in p^{-1}[b]\). Then this follows from the universal property of a universal cover, as there exist maps of covering

\begin{align*} f,f': E \rightarrow E \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} f(e) =& e' \\ f'(e') =& e \end{align*}

Furthermore, by the uniqueness of map of covering, which fixes \(e\) resp. \(e'\) (namely the identity morphism) it follows, that

\begin{align*} f \circ f' =& \mathrm{id} \\ f' \circ f =& \mathrm{id} \end{align*}

2.2. galois at arbitrary \(b' \in B\)

By connected codomain and surjective covering map it follows that \(p\) is surjective. Hence for \(b' \in B\) choose a fibre \(e' \in p^{-1}[b']\) (as well as a fibre \(e \in p^{-1}[b]\)

Moreover choose a path \(\gamma: b \leadsto b'\) by path inducing natural isomorphism between fundamental groups, we get group-isomorphism

\begin{align*} \pi_1(B,p(e)) \simeq \pi_1(B,p(e')) \end{align*}

Furthermore, naturality ensures that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \pi_1(E,e) \arrow[]{r}{\gamma} \arrow[]{d}{}& \pi_1(E,e') \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \pi_1(B,p(e)) \arrow[]{r}{p(\gamma)}& \pi_1(B,p(e')) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus by Normalteiler unter Isomorphismen it follows that

\begin{align*} \pi_1(E,e') \trianglelefteq \pi_1(B,p(e')) \end{align*}
