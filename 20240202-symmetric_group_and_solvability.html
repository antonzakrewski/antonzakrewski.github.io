Symmetric group and solvability

1. Proposition

Let \(S_n\) be the symmetric group. TFAE:

  1. \(n \leq 4\)
  2. \(S_n\) is solvable

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.1.1. \(S_1\)

follows from \(S_1 = \{1\}\) and hence abelian

2.1.2. \(S_2\)

follows from

\begin{align*} S_2 \simeq \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

and hence abelian

2.1.3. \(S_3\)

follows from

\begin{align*} 1 \trianglelefteq \langle (1,2,3)\rangle \trianglelefteq S_3 \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \langle (1,2,3)\rangle/1 \simeq \mathbb{Z}/ 3 \mathbb{Z} \\ S_2/ \langle (1,2,3)\rangle \simeq \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2.1.4. \(S_4\)

Consider

\begin{align*} V = \{\mathrm{id}, (12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23) \} \trianglelefteq A_4 \trianglelefteq S_4 \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} S_4 / A_4 \simeq& \mathbb{Z}/ 2 \mathbb{Z} \\ A_4 / V \simeq& \mathbb{Z} / 3 \mathbb{Z} \\ V \simeq& (\mathbb{Z} / 2 \mathbb{Z}) \times (\mathbb{Z} / 2 \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

2.1.5. alternatively

\(S_n \hookrightarrow S_4\) for \(n \leq 4\), hence this follows from subgroup of a solvable group as solvable group

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.1. \(S_5\)

2.2.2. general case

Note that \(S_5 \hookrightarrow S_n\) for \(n \geq 5\) and hence again by subgroup of a solvable group as solvable group and contraposition, we conclude that \(S_n\) is not solvable
