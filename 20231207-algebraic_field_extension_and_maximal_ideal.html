simple algebraic field extension and maximal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, then there exists a 1-to 1 correspondence between

  1. simple field extensions \(L\)
  2. maximal ideals \(K[X]/\mathfrak{m}\) for maximal ideals \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq K[X]\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(L = K(\alpha)\).

Then for the evaluation homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\alpha}: K[X] \rightarrow& L \\ x \mapsto& \alpha \end{align*}

it follows that

\begin{align*} K[X]/\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \cong L \end{align*}

(cf. fundamental theorem on ring homomorphisms)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

since \(K[X]\) is a principal ideal domain (cf. polynomial ring as PID and coefficients as field), we conclude, that there exists a polynomial \(f \in K[X]\) such that \((f) = \mathfrak{m}\). furthermore, \(\lambda \not\in \mathfrak{m}\) for \(\lambda \in K^{\times}\) (since \(\mathfrak{m}\) is a strict ideal) Therefore, \(K[X]/\mathfrak{m} \neq 0\) \(\pi \circ \iota: K \rightarrow K[X]/\mathfrak{m}\) is a welldefined ring homomorphism. By ringhomomorphism from an field as monomorphism, \(\iota\) is a ring-monomorphism and thus naturally a field extension

Then \(K[X]/\mathfrak{m}\) is genereted by \(\pi(x)\) as K-Algebra
