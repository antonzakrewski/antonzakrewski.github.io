idempotent element in an integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain, \(\alpha \in A\) an idempotent element. Then

\begin{align*} \alpha \in \{0,1\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Follows from

\begin{align*} (1 - \alpha) \cdot \alpha =& \alpha - \alpha^2 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

hence by integral domain and cancellable ring we conclude that \((1 - \alpha)\) or \(\alpha\) equals zero. Thus \(\alpha \in \{0,1\}\)
