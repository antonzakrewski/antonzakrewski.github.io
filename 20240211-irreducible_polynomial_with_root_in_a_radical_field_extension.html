irreducible polynomial with root in a radical field extension in char 0

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field of \(\mathrm{char}(K) = 0\) and \(f \in K[X]\) an irreducible polynomial. Suppose there exists a root of \(f\), \(\alpha \in \overline{K}\), such that \(\alpha\) is an element of an radical field extension.

Then there exists a radical field extension \(L/K\) such that \(f\) splits resp. all roots of \(f\) are elements of \(L\)

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • existence of galois extension for a radical extension in characteristic 0

as by assumption, there exists a radical field extension \(L'\) with \(\alpha \in L\). w.l.o.g. \(L'\) is normal, hence \(f\) as minimal polynomial splits.
