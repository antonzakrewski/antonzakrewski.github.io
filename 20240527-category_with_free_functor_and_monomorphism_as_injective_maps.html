category with free functor and monomorphism as injective maps

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a concrete category with a free object functor \(\mathrm{Free}: \mathrm{Set} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\). and \(f: A \rightarrow B\) a morphism

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is a monomorphism
  2. \(f\) is a injective map (i.e. \(\mathrm{Set}(f)\) is a monomorphism)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose the map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Set}(f): \mathrm{Set}(A) \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}(B) \end{align*}

is not injective, then there exist different \(a,a' \in \mathrm{Set}(A)\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Set}(f)(a) = \mathrm{Set}(f')(a') \end{align*}

Consider the morphisms

\begin{align*} a: \{*\} \mapsto& \mathrm{Set}(a) \\ a': \{*\} \mapsto& \mathrm{Set}(a') \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Set}(f) \circ a = \mathrm{Set}(f) \circ a' \end{align*}

and using the adjunction there exists a correspondence

\begin{align*} \mathcal{R}(a) \cong \mathcal{R}(a) \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

faithful functor reflects epimorphisms
