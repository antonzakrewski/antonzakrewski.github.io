homotopy of chain maps and sufficiently large degree

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(f,g: P \rightarrow M\) is a a chain map between a chain complex of projectives \(P\) and another chain complex \(M\), such that

\begin{align*} \sigma_{\leq n}(f) = \sigma_{\leq n}(g) \end{align*}

for some \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) and for \(\forall k \geq n + 1\), we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(P_k, H_k(M)) = 0 \end{align*}

Then \(f \circ g\) are chain homotopic

2. Proof

follows from extension of chain homotopies
