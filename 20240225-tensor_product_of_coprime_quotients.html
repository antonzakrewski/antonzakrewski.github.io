tensor product of coprime quotients

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{b}\) be coprime ideals and \(R/\mathfrak{a}, R/\mathfrak{b}\) the quotient module of the ideal as submodule Then it holds for the tensor product

\begin{align*} R/\mathfrak{a} \otimes_{R} R/\mathfrak{b} = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

by assumption, there exists \(r_1,r_2 \in R\) and \(a \in \mathfrak{a}, b \in \mathfrak{b}\) such that

\begin{align*} r_1 \cdot a + r_2 \cdot b = 1 \end{align*}

Then for an elementary tensor \(m_1 \otimes m_2\) with \(m_1 \in R/\mathfrak{a}, m_2 \in R/\mathfrak{b}\) we get

\begin{align*} m_1 \otimes m_2 =& 1 \cdot (m_1 \otimes m_2) \\ =& (r_1 a + r_2 b) \cdot (m_1 \otimes m_2) \\ =& r_1 a \cdot (m_1 \otimes m_2) + r_2 b \cdot (m_1 \otimes m_2) \\ =& r_1 \cdot (a \cdot m_1 \otimes m_2) + r_2 \cdot (b \cdot m_1 \otimes m_2) \end{align*}

where \(a \cdot m_1 = 0, b \cdot m_2 = 0\) and hence

\begin{align*} r_1 \cdot (a \cdot m_1 \otimes m_2) + r_2 \cdot (b \cdot m_1 \otimes m_2) =& r_1 \cdot 0 + r_2 \cdot 0 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
