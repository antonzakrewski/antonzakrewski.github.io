left derived functor for a hereditary ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a hereditary ring, \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod and \(\mathcal{A}\) an abelian category.

Then for a right exact functor \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{RMod} \rightarrow \mathcal{A}\) it follows for the left derived functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{LF}_n(-) = 0 \end{align*}

for \(n \geq 2\)

2. Proof

Let \(M \in \mathrm{RMod}\).

We may choose a projective object \(P\) with an epimorphism \(P \twoheadrightarrow M\) Then we get by exactification of a morphism the exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(\iota) \arrow[]{r}{} & P \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which extends to

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(\iota) \arrow[]{r}{} & P \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which is of length \(1\)
