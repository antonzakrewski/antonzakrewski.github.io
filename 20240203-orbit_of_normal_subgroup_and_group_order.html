orbit of normal subgroup and group order

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a finite group and \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup. then either \(N \subseteq C(G)\) (see: center) or there exists an \(n \in \{2,..., \vert N \vert\}\) such that

\begin{align*} n \mid \vert G \vert \end{align*}

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_n \subseteq N \end{align*}
