quasi affine variety isomorphic to an affine variety

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a algebraically closed field, \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space and \(X \subseteq \mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) be an affine variety. Suppose \(U \subseteq X\) is a simple quasi-affine variety, then there exists an isomorphism

\begin{align*} U \cong w \end{align*}

for another affine variety \(W\) see:

  • birational map from a quasi-affine variety
  • birational map

2. Proof

Let \(U = X \setminus Z(f)\), \(J \coloneqq I(X)\) be the vanishing ideal. Let \(F \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) with \(F \equiv f \mod{J}\).

then for

\begin{align*} \tilde{J} \coloneqq \langle J, y \cdot F - 1\rangle \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n,Y] \end{align*}

consider

\begin{align*} V(\tilde{J}) \eqqcolon W \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^{n+1} \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} (\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n,b) \mapsto& (\alpha_1,...,\alpha_-) \\ (\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n, F(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n)^{-1}) \mapsfrom (\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n) \end{align*}
