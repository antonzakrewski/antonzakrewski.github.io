internal product of disjoint normal subgroups isomorphic to outer product

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(N,H \trianglelefteq G\) normal subgroups such that \(N \cap H = \{1\}\). Then the map

\begin{align*} \varphi: N \oplus H \rightarrow& NH \\ (n,h) \mapsto& n \cdot h \end{align*}

is a group-isomorphism see:

  • Komplexprodukt von Gruppen

2. Proof

2.1. homomorphism

Let \(n \in N\) and \(h \in H\), then since both \(N\) and \(H\) are normal subgroups, it follows that

\begin{align*} n \cdot h \cdot n^{-1} \cdot h^{-1} \in N \cap H = \{1\} \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} n \cdot h = h \cdot n \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} \varphi(n_1 \cdot n_2, h_1 \cdot h_2) =& n_1 \cdot n_2 \cdot h_1 \cdot h_2 \\ =& (n_1 \cdot h_1) \cdot (n_2 \cdot h_2) \\ =& \varphi(n_1, h_1) \cdot \varphi(n_2,h_2) \end{align*}

2.2. surjective

follows from construction of

\begin{align*} NH = \{n \cdot h\} \end{align*}

2.3. injective

Suppose \((n,h) \in N \oplus H\) such that \(n \cdot h = 1\). Then \(n = h^{-1}\) and since \(N,H\) are (almost) disjoint, it follows, that \(n,h = 1\)
