correspondence between monotone maps and functors out of the category

1. Proposition

Let \(M,N\) be partially ordered set and \(\mathcal{M},\mathcal{N}\) also regarded as category (cf. partially ordered set as category) Then there exists a natural correspondence between

  1. \(f: M \rightarrow N\) a monotone map.
  2. functor \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{M} \rightarrow \mathcal{N}\)

using

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \mathcal{M} \rightarrow \mathcal{N} \\ m \mapsto& f(m) \\ (m \leq n) \mapsto& (f(m) \leq f(n)) \end{align*}

respectively

\begin{align*} f: M \rightarrow N \\ m \mapsto& \mathcal{F}(m) \end{align*}

2. Proof

definitions, as a morphism \(m \rightarrow n\) is precisely a relation \(m \leq n\) and hence \(f(m) \leq f(n)\) resp. there exists an \(f(m) \rightarrow f(n)\) Note that the identity gets mapped to the identity, as \(\mathrm{id}_{}\) is the only morphism \(f(m) \rightarrow f(m)\) Furthermore by commuting in a posetal category this satisfies functorality.
