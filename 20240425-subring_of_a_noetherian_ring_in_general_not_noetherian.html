subring of a noetherian ring in general not noetherian

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring and \(B \subseteq A\) a subring. Then \(B\) is in general not noetherian

2. Proof

Let \(K\) be a field and consider

\begin{align*} K[X_n \vert n \in \mathbb{N}] \hookrightarrow K(X_n) \end{align*}

where \(K(X_n)\) is the quotient field. Then \(K(X_n)\) is noetherian (cf. PID as noetherian ring, Field as noetherian ring)

But \(K[X_n]\) is not, as

\begin{align*} (X_1,X_2,...) \end{align*}

is not finitely generated (resp. $(X1) \subseteq (X1,X2) \subseteq …$ is an ascending chain
