finitely presented module

1. Definition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) a \(R\)-module Then \(M\) is finitely presented, if there exists a finite set \(I\) and a epimorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: R^I \twoheadrightarrow M \end{align*}

such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \end{align*}

is a finitely generated module
