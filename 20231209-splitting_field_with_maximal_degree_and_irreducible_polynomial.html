splitting field with maximal degree and irreducible polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f \in K[X]\) a polynomial of degree \(n\). Suppose the splitting field \(K(f)\) has degree \(n!\), then \(f\) is irreducible.

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(f = g_1 \cdot g_2\) is reducible, then by splitting field of a product of polynomials, we conclude that

\begin{align*} K(f) = K(g_1)(g_2) \end{align*}

with \(\mathrm{deg}(g_1), \mathrm{deg}(g_2) \geq 1\)

Hence by Gradlemma

\begin{align*} [K(f) : K] =& [K(g_1)(g_2) : K] \\ =& [K(g_1)(g_2) : K(g_1)] \cdot [K(g_1) : K] \end{align*}

where \([K(g_1)(g_2) : K(g_1)] \leq (\mathrm{deg}(g_2))!\) and \([K(g_1) : K] \leq \mathrm{deg}(g_1)!\) Hence

\begin{align*} [K(g_1)(g_2) : K(g_1)] \cdot [K(g_1) : K] \leq& (\mathrm{deg}(g_2))! \cdot \mathrm{deg}(g_1)! \\ <& ( \mathrm{deg}(g_1) + \mathrm{deg}(g_2)) \\ =& n! \end{align*}

as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(g_1)! < \frac{n!}{\mathrm{deg}(g_2)!} \end{align*}
