degree of composite field extensions

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/E\) and \(E/K\) field extensions (as subfields of a common field extension). then it holds for the degree of the composite field

\begin{align*} [EL : K] \leq [L : K] \cdot [E : K] \end{align*}

2. Proof

By Gradsatz, it holds, that

\begin{align*} [EL : K] = [EL : E] \cdot [E : K] \end{align*}

where by degree and subfield of \([EL : E] \leq [E : K]\)
