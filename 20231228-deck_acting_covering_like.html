deck acting covering like

1. Proposition

Let \(B\) be a topological space, \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a connected covering. Then the deck group canonically acts covering-like on \(E\)

2. Proof

Let \(e \in E\), then choose an open neighbourhood \(U\) which restricts to a homeomorphism \(p_{\vert U}: U \rightarrow V \subseteq B\) let \(f \in \mathrm{Deck}(p)\), such that \(x \in f[U] \cap U\). Then there exists an \(y \in U\) such that

\begin{align*} f(x) = y \end{align*}

furthermore, by commutativity of

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} E \arrow[swap]{rd}{p} \arrow[]{rr}{f}& & E \arrow[]{ld}{p} \\ & B & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

it holds, that

\begin{align*} p(y) =& p(f(x)) \\ =& p(x) \end{align*}

and since \(p\) is a homeomorphism especially also injective, thus \(y = x\)

Since the deck action from a connected space is a free action, we conclude, that \(f = \mathrm{id}_{E}\), hence acting covering like
