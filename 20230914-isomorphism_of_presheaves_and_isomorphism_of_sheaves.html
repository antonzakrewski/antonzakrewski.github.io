isomorphism of sheaves and isomorphism of stalks

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}\) set valued sheaves. Suppose \(f: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}\) is a morphism of presheafs

TFAE:

  1. for each \(p \in X\) and stalks \(\mathcal{F}_p, \mathcal{G}_p\), \(f\) induces an isomorphism \(f_p: \mathcal{F}_p \rightarrow \mathcal{G}_p\)
  2. \(f\) is an isomorphism of presheaves

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.1.1. injective

corollary of

  • injective morphisms on stalks imply injective morphism of sheaves

2.1.2. surjective

Suppose \(t \in \mathcal{G}(U)\). Then, since \(f_x\) is an isomorphism, for each \(x \in U\) and each germ \(t_x \in \mathcal{G}_x\) there exists a unique \(s_x\) such that

\begin{align*} f_x(s_x) = t_x \end{align*}

by assumption, this \(s_x\) is represented by some section \(s_{\vert V} \in \mathcal{F}(V)\) Then \(f(V)(s_{\vert V})\) and \(t_x\) have by construction the same stalk at \(x\), so by equal germs imply equality on neighbourhoods, there exists an \(W \subseteq V\) such that

\begin{align*} f(W)(s_{\vert W}) = t_{\vert W} \end{align*}

Repeating this for each \(x \in U\) results in an open cover with sections \(s_{i,\vert U_i}\) By injectivity of \(f\), we conclude, that \(s_{i, \vert U_i \cap U_j} = s_{j, \vert U_i \cap U_j}\), hence by glueing of a sheaf it follows, that there exists a unique section \(s \in \mathcal{F}(U)\)

By construction, it follows, that \(f(s)_x = t_x\) for each \(x \in U\), hence by germs uniquely define stalk for a sheaf we conclude, that

\begin{align*} s = t \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from isomorphism of sheaves induces isomorphism of stalks
