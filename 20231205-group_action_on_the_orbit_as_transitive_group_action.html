group action on the orbit as transitive group action

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(X\) a set and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a group action, \(x \in \mathcal{O}_x\) an orbit.

then the group action on the orbit is a transitive group action

2. Proof

Let \(y,z \in \mathcal{O}_x\). Then by assumption, there exists \(g_1,g_2 \in G\) such that

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot x =& y \\ g_2 \cdot x =& z \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} x =& g_2^{-1} z \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} (g_1 \cdot g_2^{-1}) z =& (g_1 \cdot (g_2^{-1} z)) \\ =& g_1 \cdot x \\ =& y \end{align*}
