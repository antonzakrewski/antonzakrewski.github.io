degree of a divisor polynomial for an integral ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(f,g \in R[X]\) polynomials such that \(g \neq 0\) and \(f \mid g\) then it holds for the degree, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(f) \leq \mathrm{deg}(g) \end{align*}

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists a polynomial \(c \in R[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} c \cdot f = g \end{align*}

Note that \(c \neq 0\) and hence by degree of the product of polynomials in an integral domain we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(f) \leq& \mathrm{deg}(c) + \mathrm{deg}(f) \\ =& \mathrm{deg}(c \cdot f) \\ =& \mathrm{deg}(g) \end{align*}
