contravariant representable functor dualizes colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a contravariant functor. Suppose \(\mathcal{F}\) is contravariant representable functor, then \(\mathcal{F}\) dualizes colimits to limits.

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • natural isomorphism and preserves limit
  • contravariant hom-functor dualizes small colimits to limits
\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{colim}(-)) \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathrm{colim}(-), R) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{lim} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,R) \\ \simeq \mathrm{lim} \mathcal{F}(-) \end{align*}
