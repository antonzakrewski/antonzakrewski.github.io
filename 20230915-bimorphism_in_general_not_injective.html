bimorphism in general not injective

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a concrete category and \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) a bimorphism. Then \(f\) is in general not injective

2. Proof

same counterexample as in:

  • monomorphism in general not injective

Because

\begin{align*} \pi: \mathbb{Q} \rightarrow& \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \\ \end{align*}

is surjective and hence surjective map as epimorphism, it follows, that \(\pi\) is a bimorphism
