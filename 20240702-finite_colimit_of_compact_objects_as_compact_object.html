finite colimit of compact objects as compact object

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(C_i\) compact objects and \(C \coloneqq \mathrm{colim} C_i\) a finite colimit Then \(C\) is also a compact object

2. Proof

Let \(\mathrm{colim}_{\mathrm{filt}} D_i\) be a a filtered colimit, then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}(\mathrm{colim}_{\mathrm{fin}}(C_i), \mathrm{colim}_{\mathrm{filt}} D_i ) \cong& \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{fin}} \mathrm{Hom}(C_i, \mathrm{colim}_{\mathrm{filt}} D_i) \end{align*}

holds by contravariant hom-functor dualizes small colimits to limits.

Furthermore by assumption each \(C_i\) is compact, thus

\begin{align*} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{fin}} \mathrm{Hom}(C_i, \mathrm{colim}_{\mathrm{filt}} D_i) \cong& \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{fin}} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{filt}} \mathrm{Hom}(C_i, D_i) \end{align*}

and by filtered colimits commute with finite limits in Set we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{fin}} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{filt}} \mathrm{Hom}(C_i, D_i) \cong& \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{filt}} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{fin}} \mathrm{Hom}(C_i, D_i) \end{align*}

Then going backwards gives

\begin{align*} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{filt}} \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{fin}} \mathrm{Hom}(C_i, D_i) \cong \mathrm{lim}_{\mathrm{filt}} \mathrm{Hom}(\mathrm{colim}_{\mathrm{fin}} C_i, D_i) \end{align*}
