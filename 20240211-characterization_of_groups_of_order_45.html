classification of groups of order 45

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group of group order \(45\). Then

\begin{align*} G \cong \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z}/45 \mathbb{Z} \\ \mathbb{Z}/15 \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/3 \mathbb{Z} \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(G\) abelian

By number of sylow groups, we get

\begin{align*} s_{9} = 1, s_5 = 1 \end{align*}

(todo: amount) hence by sole sylow group as normal subgroup it follows, that \(U_9, U_5\) are normal subgroups. Furthermore by internal product of disjoint normal subgroups isomorphic to outer product, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \vert U_9 \cdot U_5 \vert = 45 \end{align*}

hence \(U_9 \cdot U_5 = G\) by classification of groups of order p2 and classification of groups of order p, \(U_9, U_5\) are abelian and thus

\begin{align*} U_9 \cdot U_5 \cong U_5 \times U_9 \end{align*}

is abelian
