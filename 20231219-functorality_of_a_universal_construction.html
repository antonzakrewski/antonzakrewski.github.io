functorality of a universal construction

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, and \(O^{*}\) be a universal construction for an object \(O \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\), i.e. there exists a morphism

\begin{align*} \iota: O \rightarrow O^* \end{align*}

and for each diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} O \arrow[]{d}{\iota}\arrow[]{r}{h}& U \arrow[]{d}{} \\ O^* \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists! h^*} & U^* \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a unique morphism

Then the construction is functorial, i.e.

\begin{align*} ^*: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow& \mathcal{C} \\ O \mapsto& O^* \\ (h: O \rightarrow U) \mapsto& (h^*: O^* \rightarrow U^*) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. identity

since \(\mathrm{id}_{O^*}\) makes the diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} O \arrow[]{d}{\iota}\arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_O}& O \arrow[]{d}{} \\ O^* \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_{O^*} } & O^* \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute, we conclude, that by uniqueness,

\begin{align*} ^*(\mathrm{id}_{O}) = \mathrm{id}_{O^*} \end{align*}

2.2. composition

Consider

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} O \arrow[]{d}{\iota}\arrow[]{r}{f}& U \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{g} & V \arrow[]{d}{}\\ O^* \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists! f^*} & U^* \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists! g^*}& V^* \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where both squares commute.

Therefore also

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} O \arrow[]{d}{\iota}\arrow[]{r}{g \circ f}& V \arrow[]{d}{}\\ O^* \arrow[]{r}{g^* f^*} & V^* \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

and again by uniqueness, it follows, that

\begin{align*} ^*(g \circ f) =& g^* \circ f^* \\ =& ^*(g) \circ ^*(f) \end{align*}
