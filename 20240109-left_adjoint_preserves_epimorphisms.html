left adjoint preserves epimorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) be a left adjoint.

Then an epimorphism \(f \in \mathcal{Mor}(\mathcal{C})\) is preserved under \(\mathcal{L}\), i.e.

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}(f) \end{align*}

is also an epimorphism

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • left adjoint preserves colimits
  • functor preserving pushouts preserves epimorphisms
  • pushout as colimit
