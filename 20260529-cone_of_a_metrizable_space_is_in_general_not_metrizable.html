cone of a metrizable space is in general not metrizable

Proposition

Let \(X\) be a metrizable space, then the Cone \(CX\) is in general not.

Proof

Consider \(X = \mathbb{Z}\) with the subspace metric (or topologically equivalent the discrete metric).
Then the cone is given by \(\mathbb{Z} \times [0,1]/\sim\) or \(\coprod_{z \in \mathbb{Z}} [0,1]/\sim\).
We claim that the cone is not first-countable at the cone point \(0\) and hence \(CX\) can't be metrizable (cf. metric spaces are first countable):

Recall that a subset of \(\mathbb{Z} \times [0,1]/\sim\) is a neighborhood of the cone point if and only if it contains a subset of the form \(\coprod_{z \in \mathbb{Z}} [0, \varepsilon_z)\) for \(\varepsilon_z > 0\).
Now assume that there exists a countable neighbourhood basis \(\mathcal{N} = \{O_n\}\) where each \(O_n\) is – w.l.o.g. – of the form \(O_n = \coprod_{z \in \mathbb{Z}} [0, \varepsilon_{z,n})\).
Then we can – using a diagonal argument – construct a neighbourhood of the cone point, which is not a superset of any \(O_n\):

Indeed, define

\begin{align*} U := \coprod_{z \in \mathbb{Z}} [0, \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_{z,z}) \end{align*}

where \(U \not\supseteq O_n\) since \([0, \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_{z,z}) \not\supseteq [0, \varepsilon_{z,z})\).
