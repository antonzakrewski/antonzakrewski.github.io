sheaf on sets and equalizer

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{Op}(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}\) be a presheaf for a topological space \(X\).

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{F}\) is a sheaf of sets
  2. \(\mathcal{F}\) is a equalizer sheaf

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose there exists a commuting diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[]{r}{} & \prod \mathcal{F}(U_i) \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{} \arrow[shift right]{r}{}& \prod_{(i,j) \in I^2} \mathcal{F}(U_i \cap U_j) \\ X \arrow[swap]{ru}{f}& & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then since \(f\) forms a fork, it follows, that \(f(x)^{U_i}_{\vert U_i \cap U_j} = f(x)^{U_j}_{\vert U_i \cap U_j}\) on their intersection. Hence by glueing, their exists a section \(s_x \in \mathcal{F}\) with \(\mathrm{res}_{\vert U_i}(s_x) = f(x)^{U_i}\). By locality, we conclude, that \(s\) is unique.

Therefore, the map

\begin{align*} g: X \rightarrow& \mathcal{F}(U) \\ x \mapsto& s_x \end{align*}

is welldefined and furthermore unique with the property, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=1mm]-45:\circlearrowleft}]| \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[]{r}{} & \prod \mathcal{F}(U_i) \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{} \arrow[shift right]{r}{}& \prod_{(i,j) \in I^2} \mathcal{F}(U_i \cap U_j) \\ X \arrow[]{u}{g} \arrow[swap]{ru}{f} & {} & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes, since

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.1. locality of a sheaf

follows froms

  • monomorphism in set as injective map
  • equalizer and monomorphism

for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[]{r}{} & \prod \mathcal{F}(U_i) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2.2. glueing of a sheaf

Suppose we have sections \(s_i\), which are compatible on their intersections. Then for the singleton with the map \(f: * \rightarrow (s_i)\), we get a fork

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[]{r}{} & \prod \mathcal{F}(U_i) \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{} \arrow[shift right]{r}{}& \prod_{(i,j) \in I^2} \mathcal{F}(U_i \cap U_j) \\ * \arrow[swap]{rru}{f}& & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

hence by universal property, we get a mediating morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[]{r}{} & \prod \mathcal{F}(U_i) \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{} \arrow[shift right]{r}{}& \prod_{(i,j) \in I^2} \mathcal{F}(U_i \cap U_j) \\ * \arrow[dashed]{u}{\exists! g} \arrow[swap]{ru}{f}& & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
