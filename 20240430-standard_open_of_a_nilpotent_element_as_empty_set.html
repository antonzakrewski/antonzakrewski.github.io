standard open of a nilpotent element as empty set

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(f \in A\) a nilpotent element. then the standard open \(D(f)\) is the empty set.

2. Proof

By definition we get

\begin{align*} D(f) = \mathrm{Spec}(A) \setminus V(f) \end{align*}

Nevertheless it follows that \(f \in \mathfrak{p}\) for any arbitrary prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\) (cf. nilradical, Nilradical as intersection of prime ideals)

Hence \(V(f) = \mathrm{Spec}(A)\)
