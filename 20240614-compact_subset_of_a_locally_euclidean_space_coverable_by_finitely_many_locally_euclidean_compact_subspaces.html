compact subset of a locally euclidean space coverable by finitely many locally euclidean compact subspaces

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a locally euclidean topological space and \(K \subseteq X\) compact. Then there exists finitely many compact \(K_i\) homeomorphic to compact subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) such that

\begin{align*} \cup K_i = K \end{align*}

2. Proof

For \(k \in K\) choose a neighbourhood \(\varphi_k: U_k \cong \mathbb{R}^n\). Then take for example the closed epsilon-neighbourhood \(B[1]\) around \(\varphi_k(k)\), which is compact as it is bounded and closed (cf. compact sets in euclidean spaces). Let \(A_k \cong B[1], A_k \subseteq U_k\) be the corresponding subset in \(X\) Then the interior of \(B[1]\) is nonempty containing \(k\). Therefore \(\mathrm{int}(A_k)\) is an open cover. Hence finitely many \(A_k\) suffice.
