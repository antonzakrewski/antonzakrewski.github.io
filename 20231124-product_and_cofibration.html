product and cofibration

1. Proposition

Let \(\iota_n: A_n \rightarrow X_n\) be finitely many cofibrations. Then the categorical product

\begin{align*} \prod \iota_n: \prod A_n \rightarrow \prod X_n \end{align*}

is also a cofibration

2. Proof

cofibration and retraction:

Since a cofibration is precisely given by a retraction

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Ret}_i: X_i \times [0,1] \rightarrow X_i \times \{0\} \cup A_i \times [0,1] \end{align*}

we construct a Retraction

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Ret}_n: \prod X_n \times [0,1] \rightarrow& \prod A_n \times [0,1] \cup \prod X_n \times \{0\} \\ ((x_n), t) \mapsto& ((\mathrm{pr}_1(\mathrm{Ret}_n(x_n,t)))_n, \sqrt[n]{\prod \mathrm{pr}_2 (\mathrm{Ret}_n(x_n,t))}) \end{align*}

for a handpicked Retraction \(\mathrm{Ret}: X_j \times [0,1] \rightarrow A_j \times [0,1] \cup X_j \times \{0\}\)

2.1. welldefined codomain

Suppose \(t \neq 0\), then by assumption, all \(\mathrm{pr}_2(\mathrm{Ret}_n(x_n,t)) \neq 0\) and hence \(\mathrm{pr}_1(\mathrm{Ret}_n(x_n,t)) \in A_n\) Hence we map to \(\prod A_i \times [0,1]\)

2.2. continuous

follows from

  1. universal property of a product topology
  2. continuous map and restriction to the image

2.3. retraction

Suppose \((x_n) \in \prod A_n\), then by assumption, we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_2(\mathrm{ret}_n(x_n,t) = t) \end{align*}

Thus \(\sqrt[n]{t \cdot ... \cdot t} = t\)

Suppose \(t = 0\), then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_2(\mathrm{Ret}_n(x_n,0)) = 0 \end{align*}

and thus the second component is \(0\)

Note that our original retractions \(\mathrm{Ret}_n\) leaves each component aside from \(t\) intact

  • for a locally compact space:
    • product of locally compact spaces preserves colimits
