covering map induces group monomorphism for fundamental groups

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be topological spaces and \(p: B \rightarrow E\) be a covering map. Then for \(e \in E\), postcomposition on the paths

\begin{align*} p^{ \leftarrow}: \pi_1(E,e) \rightarrow \pi_1(B,p(e)) \end{align*}

is a welldefined algebraic group-monomorphism on the fundamental group

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

homotopy group functor path lifting preserves homotopy

2.2. injective

minimal kernel and monomorphism

Suppose \(\gamma: [0,1] \rightarrow E\) is a path with \([p \circ \gamma] = 0\). Then \(p \circ \gamma\) is homotopic to the constant path on \(p(e)\). by unique path lifting, the lifted path of \(p \circ \gamma\) is precisely \(\gamma\) resp. the constant path on \(e\). Furthermore since path lifting preserves homotopy, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \gamma \sim e \end{align*}

Hence \([\gamma] = 0\)
