cayley's theorem for monoids

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a monoid, then \(M\) is isomorphic to a submonoid of \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(M,M)\), where \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(M,M)\) is the set of maps from \(M\) to \(M\) with composition as binary operation

2. Proof

Let \(\mathrm{TM} = \{f: M \rightarrow M \vert f(x) \cdot y = f(x \cdot y)\} \subseteq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(M,M)\) be the translation monoid. Then consider the map

\begin{align*} \varphi: M \rightarrow& \mathrm{TM} \\ m \mapsto& (\tau_m: x \mapsto m \cdot x) \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

Follows from

\begin{align*} \tau_m(x) \cdot y =& m \cdot x \cdot y \\ =& m \cdot (x \cdot y) \\ =& \tau_m(x \cdot y) \end{align*}

2.2. injective

Suppose \(x,y \in M\) such that \(\tau_x = \tau_y\). Then

\begin{align*} x =& x \cdot e \\ =& \tau_x(e) \\ =& \tau_y(e) \\ =& y \cdot e \\ =& y \end{align*}

2.3. surjective

Let \(f \in \mathrm{TM}\), then we conclude

\begin{align*} f(x) \cdot y = f(x \cdot y) \end{align*}

Let \(w \coloneqq f(e)\), then

\begin{align*} \tau_w(x) =& f(e) \cdot x \\ =& f(e \cdot x) \\ =& f(x) \end{align*}

for any \(x \in M\)
