Banach Tarski Paradox

1. Proposition

Let \(d \in \mathbb{N}\) and \(A,B \subseteq \mathbb{R}^d\) be subsets with nonempty interior Then there exist countably many \(C_k\) and isometries \(\beta_k: \mathbb{R}^d \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^d\) such that

\begin{align*} A =& \coprod_{k \in \mathbb{N}} C_k \\ B =& \coprod_{k \in \mathbb{N}} \beta_{k}(C_k) \end{align*}
