conservative functor reflects preserved limits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a conservative functor, which preserves limits of a shape \(\mathcal{J}\). Suppose that \(\mathcal{C}\) admits limits of this shape \(\mathcal{J}\).

Then \(\mathcal{F}\) reflects limits of the shape \(\mathcal{J}\)

2. Proof

Suppose \((K,\pi^k_i)\) is a cone over \(\mathcal{J}\) in \(\mathcal{C}\), such that \(\mathcal{F}(K)\) is a limit. Suppose \((L, \pi_i^l)\) is a limit, then by assumption, \(\mathcal{F}(L)\) is also a limit.

By universal property, there exists a morphism

\begin{align*} \mu: K \rightarrow L \end{align*}

Thus \(\mathcal{F}(\mu)\) is an isomorphism, since \(\mathcal{F}\) preserves \(L\) as limit (cf. universal property unique up to unique isomorphism) Since \(\mathcal{F}\) is conservative, \(\mu\) is also an isomorphism, hence \(K\) is a limit.
