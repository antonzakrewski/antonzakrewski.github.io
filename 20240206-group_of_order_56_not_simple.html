group of order 56 not simple

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group of group order \(56\). Then \(G\) is not simple

2. Proof

Since \(56\) factorizes as \(56 = 8 \cdot 7 = 2^3 \cdot 7\), by number of sylow groups we get the possibilities of

\begin{align*} s_7 \in& \{1,8\} \\ s_8 \in& \{1,7\} \end{align*}

Suppose \(s_7,s_8 \neq 1\), because otherwise by sole sylow group as normal subgroup, there exists an normal subgroup

Then by group of order p as cyclic group, \(U_7 \simeq \mathbb{Z}/ 7 \mathbb{Z}\) and different cyclic subgroups as disjoint groups it follows, that there exist \(8 \cdot \varphi(7) = 8 \cdot 6 = 48\) different elements of group order \(7\) (see: euler's phi function). Furthermore, there exists at least \(8\) elements of order \(2^n, n \in \mathbb{N}^+\), as there are more than one different subgroups of order \(8\). Together with the neutral element, there exists at least \(57\) elements, contradiction
