image coimage implies monomorphism as normal monomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a pre-abelian category such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{im}(f) \simeq \mathrm{coim}(f) \end{align*}

(aka. abelian category)

Then each monomorphism is a normal monomorphism

2. Proof

Consider

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{ker}(f) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & \arrow[]{d}{} A \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & \mathrm{coker}(f) \\ & \mathrm{coker}(\iota) & \mathrm{ker}(\pi) \arrow[]{u}{}& \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Using monomorphism with zero as kernel we conclude

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{\simeq}& \mathrm{ker}(f) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & \arrow[]{d}{} A \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & \mathrm{coker}(f) \\ & & \mathrm{coker}(\iota) & \mathrm{ker}(\pi) \arrow[]{u}{}& \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then it follows from cokernel of a zero morphism that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & \mathrm{ker}(f) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & A \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & \mathrm{coker}(f) \\ & & \mathrm{coker}(\iota) & \mathrm{ker}(\pi) & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Hence using the image coimage isomorphism we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & \mathrm{ker}(f) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & A \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & \mathrm{coker}(f) \\ & & \mathrm{coker}(\iota) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq}& \mathrm{ker}(\pi) & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Hence it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\pi) \simeq A \end{align*}

are naturally isomorphic
