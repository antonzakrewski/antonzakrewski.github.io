K-Algebraendomorphism for an algebraic field extension as Isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) an algebraic field extension.

Suppose

\begin{align*} \sigma: L \rightarrow L \end{align*}

is a K-Algebrahomomorphism

Then \(\sigma\) is an K-Algebraautomorphismus

2. Proof

2.1. injective

follows from ringhomomorphism from an field as monomorphism

2.2. surjective

Suppose \(\alpha \in L\). Then by assumption, \(\alpha\) is algebraic, hence there exists a minimal polynomial \(f \in K[X]\). Let \(\alpha, \alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\) be all roots of \(f\).

Then by root under A-Algebra homomorphisms, we get a map

\begin{align*} \sigma_{\vert \alpha, \alpha_1,...,\alpha_n}: \{\alpha, \alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\} \rightarrow \{ \alpha, \alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\} \end{align*}

it is, as a restriction of an injection, injective (cf. restriction of an injective map as injection).

By Äquivalenz von Bijektivität, Surjektivität und Injektivität bei gleich mächtigen, endlichen Mengen, it follows, that \(\sigma_{\vert \alpha,...,\alpha_n}\) is surjective, hence there exists a preimage of \(\alpha\) under \(\sigma\)
