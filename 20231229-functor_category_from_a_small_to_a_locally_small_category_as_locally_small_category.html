functor category from a small to a locally small category as locally small category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, where \(\mathcal{C}\) is a small category and \(\mathcal{D}\) a locally small category. Then the functor category

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

is locally small

2. Proof

A morphism in the functor category is a natural transformation \(\eta\) Furthermore, the data of \(\eta\) is given by a map

\begin{align*} \prod_{A \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})}( \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(A), \mathcal{G}(A))) \end{align*}

with some naturality conditions.

Note that each \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(A), \mathcal{G}(A))\) is a set and category set admits small limits, thus also the categorical product for the \(A \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) (cf. set as bicomplete category)
