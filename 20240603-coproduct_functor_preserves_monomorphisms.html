coproduct functor preserves monomorphisms in Set

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{Set}\) be category set (topos), \(J\) a discrete indexing category Then the coproduct functor

\begin{align*} \coprod_J: \mathrm{Fun}(J,\mathrm{Set}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Set} \end{align*}

preserves monomorphisms

2. Proof

follows from

  • injective map as monomorphism

and the fact that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{im}(f_i) \cap \mathrm{im}(f_j) = \emptyset \end{align*}

for \(i \neq j\)
