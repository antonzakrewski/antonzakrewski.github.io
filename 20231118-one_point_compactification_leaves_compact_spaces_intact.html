one point compactification preserves compact spaces

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(X^+\) the one point compactification and \(A \subseteq X\) be compact. Then \(A \subseteq X^+\) is also compact

Note that one point compactification may refine the topology

2. Proof

Let \(A \subseteq X\) be compact, then by construction \(X^+ \setminus A\) is open. Hence \(A \subseteq X^+\) is closed and as closed subset of a compact space also compact inside \(X^+\)
