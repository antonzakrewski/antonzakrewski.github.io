morphism of delta functors

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories and \((\mathcal{F}_n), (\mathcal{G}_n)\) be delta functors. Then a morphism of delta functors is defined as sequence \((\tau_n: \mathcal{F}_n \rightarrow \mathcal{G}_n)\) of natural transformation such that for each short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

the following diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(C) \arrow[]{r}{\partial_n} \arrow[]{d}{\tau_n} & \mathcal{F}_{n-1}(A) \arrow[]{d}{\tau_{n-1}}\\ \mathcal{G}(C) \arrow[]{r}{\partial_n} & \mathcal{F}_{n-1}(A) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
