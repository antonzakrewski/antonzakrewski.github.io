order of the product of coprime subgroups

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(S_p,S_q\) subgroups of order \(\vert S_p \vert = p, \vert S_q \vert = q\) with \(S_p \trianglelefteq G\) as normal subgroup and \(p,q\) coprime. Then for the group order of the product \(S_p \cdot S_q\), it holds that

\begin{align*} \vert S_p \cdot S_q \vert = p \cdot q \end{align*}

2. Proof

Note that there exists a surjection

\begin{align*} \pi: S_p \times S_q \twoheadrightarrow& S_p S_q \\ (s_1,s_2) \mapsto& s_1 \cdot s_2 \end{align*}

Hence by the product formula for the cardinality, we get

\begin{align*} \vert S_p \cdot S_q \vert \leq \vert S_p \vert \cdot \vert S_q \vert = p \cdot q \end{align*}

Note that \(S_p,S_q \subseteq S_p \cdot S_q\) and hence by Lagrange it follows that

\begin{align*} \vert S_p \vert =& p \mid \vert S_p \cdot S_q \vert \\ \vert S_q \vert =& q \mid \vert S_p \cdot S_q \vert \end{align*}
