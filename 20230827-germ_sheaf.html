germ (sheaf)

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{V}\) a category and \(\mathcal{F}\) a V-valued sheaf. Suppose \(U \subseteq X\) is an open set, \(x \in U\) and \(s \in \mathrm{Set}(\mathcal{F}(U))\) a section.

Furthermore, suppose that the stalk \(\mathcal{F}_x\) exists.

Then the germ of \(s\) is defined as the image of the canonical morphism

\begin{align*} U \rightarrow \mathcal{F}_x = \mathrm{colim} \end{align*}

given by \(\mathcal{F}_x\) as colimit (after applying the underlying-set functor)
