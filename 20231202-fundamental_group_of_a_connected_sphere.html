fundamental group of a connected sphere

1. Proposition

Let \(C = \{(t,0,0) \vert t \in [-1,1]\} \subseteq \mathbb{R}^3\) and \(S^2 \subseteq \mathbb{R}^3\). Then the fundamental group of

\begin{align*} S^2 \cup C \end{align*}

is \(\mathbb{Z}\)

2. Proof

we choose topological equivalent 1-Norm and consider \(\partial [0,1]^3\) as our sphere, which is homeomorphic, as

\begin{align*} S^1 \cong \partial ([0,1] \times [0,1]) \\ \varphi: S^1 \rightarrow& [0,1] \times [0,1] \\ t \mapsto& \begin{cases} (0,4t) & \mbox{if } 0 \leq t \leq \frac{1}{4} \\ (4t - 1,1) & \mbox{if } \frac{1}{4} \leq t \leq \frac{1}{2} \\ (1, 4t - 2) & \mbox{if } \frac{1}{2} \leq t \leq \frac{3}{4} \\ (4t - 3, 0) & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

which is a homeomorphism by injektive stetige Abbildung von einen Kompaktum auf einen Hausdorff-Raum als Einbettung and thus by smash product of spheres also \(S^2\)

Note, that this map leaves \(C\) intact.

Let \(A_1 = (S^2 \cup C) \setminus (0,0,0)\) and \(A_2 = C \cup B_{\varepsilon}(\{(x,y,0) \vert x + y = 1\})\) Then \(A_1\) is homotopic to a 2-sphere with

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times A_1 \rightarrow A_1 \\ (t, (s,0,0)) \mapsto& (s - t \cdot s,0,0) \\ \end{align*}

and the rest as identity

Here we choose \(n \coloneqq (1,0,0)\) Hence \(\pi_1(A_1,n) = 0\)

Furthermore, \(A_2\) is homotopic to a 1-sphere, with

\begin{align*} H': [0,1] \times A_2 \rightarrow& A_2 \\ t, (x,y,\varepsilon) \mapsto& (x - t \frac{\varepsilon}{2}, y - t \frac{\varepsilon}{2}, \varepsilon - t \varepsilon) \end{align*}

and the rest as identity.

Hence \(\pi_1(A_2,n) = 0\)

The intersection is, applying the restriction of \(H'\), homtopoic to a line, (note, that \(H^{-1}[(0,0,0)] = [0,1] \times (0,0,0)\)) Therefore \(\pi_1(A_2 \cap A_1, n) = 0\)

applying Seifert-Van Kampen Theorem results in

\begin{align*} \pi_1(S^2 \cup C, x) =& \mathbb{Z} \times_{0} 0 \\ =& \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}
