characterization of a universal cover

1. Proposition

Let \(B\) be a locally and globally connected topological space, \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map

TFAE:

  1. \(p\) is an universal cover for arbitrary \(b \in B\)
  2. \(p\) is an universal cover for at least one \(b_0 \in B\)
  3. \(E\) is connected and a galois covering and for any nonempty covering map \(p: E' \rightarrow B\) there exists a map of coverings
\begin{align*} f: E \rightarrow E' \end{align*} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} E \arrow[swap]{rd}{p} \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & E' \arrow[]{ld}{p'}\\ & B & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

2.2.3. factorization

Let \(p': E' \rightarrow B\) be a nonempty covering map. then by connected codomain and surjective covering map, there exists an \(e' \in p^{-1}(b)\). by universal property of the universal cover, there exists a map of covering

\begin{align*} f: E \rightarrow E' \end{align*}

with \(f(e) = e'\)

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

Note that by uniqueness of connected factorization through a covering map, it suffices to show the existence of a map

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} E \arrow[]{rd}{p} \arrow[]{rr}{\exists! f} & & E' \arrow[]{ld}{p'}\\ & B & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
