equivalence of subcategories of left and right adjoints

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, $\mathrm{Fun}\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}), \(\mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{R}}(\mathcal{D}, \mathcal{C})\) the full subcategories containing left and right adjoints together with the unit resp. counit Then there exists an equivalence

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{L}}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{R}}(\mathcal{D}, \mathcal{C})^{\mathrm{op}} \\ \mathcal{L} \mapsto& \mathcal{R} \\ (\tau: \mathcal{L}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_2) \mapsto& \overline{\tau} \\ \end{align*}

where

  • \(\eta_i\) is the unit
  • \(\epsilon_i\) is the counit

and the

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{R}_2(-) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_1(-)} & \mathcal{R}_1 \circ \mathcal{L}_1 \circ \mathcal{R}_2(-) \arrow[]{rr}{\mathcal{R}_1( \tau( \mathcal{R}_2(-)))} & & \mathcal{R}_1 \mathcal{L}_2 \mathcal{R}_2(-) \arrow[]{rr}{\mathcal{R}_1(\epsilon_2(-))} & & \mathcal{R}_1(-) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

3. alternative

Let

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{L}, \mathcal{R}}(\mathcal{C},\mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

be the category containing

  1. adjoint functors \(\mathcal{L} \dashv \mathcal{R}\) as objects
  2. natural transformations \(\alpha: \mathcal{L}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_2, \beta: \mathcal{R}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{R}_1\) such that
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_1 \arrow[bend left]{rrr}{\alpha} \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_1 (\eta_2)} & \mathcal{L}_1 \mathcal{R}_2 \mathcal{R}_2 \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_1 \beta \mathcal{R}_2}& \mathcal{L}_1 \mathcal{R}_1 \mathcal{R}_2 \arrow[]{r}{\epsilon_1 \mathcal{R}_2}& \mathcal{L}_2 \mathcal{R}_2 \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{R}_2 \eta_1} \arrow[bend right]{rrr}{\beta}& \mathcal{R}_2 \mathcal{L}_1 \mathcal{R}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{R}_2 \alpha \mathcal{R}_1} & \mathcal{R}_2 \mathcal{L}_2 \mathcal{R}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\epsilon_2 \mathcal{R}_1} & \mathcal{R}_1 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute _ Note that there exists evident forgetful functors

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & \mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{L}, \mathcal{R}}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}) \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{ld}{} & \\ \mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{L}}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}) & & \mathrm{Fun}^{\mathcal{R}}(\mathcal{D},\mathcal{C}) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

These forgetful functors are by definition essentially surjective

We show that they are also fully faithful
