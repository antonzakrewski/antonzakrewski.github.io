homogeneous ideal as additive prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a monoid, \(R = \bigoplus_{m \in m} R_m\) a graded ring and \(\mathfrak{h}\) an ideal

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathfrak{h}\) is a homogeneous ideal
  2. for \(r_{m_i} \in R_{m_i)\), \(m_i \neq m_j \Leftrightarrow i \neq j\), \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) and \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} r_{m_i} \in \mathfrak{h}\) it follows that \(r_{m_i} \in \mathfrak{h}\) for all \(i\)
  3. for \(r_{m_i} \in R_{m_i)\), \(m_i \neq m_j \Leftrightarrow i \neq j\), \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) and \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} r_{m_i} \in \mathfrak{h}\) it follows that \(r_{m_i} \in \mathfrak{h}\) for some \(i\)

i.e. if the sum from different homogeneous components is an element, so is at least one summand

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

special case

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)
