inner hom tensor map

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod. Suppose \(M,N \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod})\) are chain complexes of modules and \(C,D \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{Ab})\) chain complexes of abelian groups.

Then for the hom chain complex and tensor product of chain complexes functor there exists a natural map

\begin{align*} \lambda: \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C,M) \otimes_R \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(D,N) \rightarrow \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} D, M \otimes_R N) \end{align*}

i.e. a natural transformation of functors

\begin{align*} \mathrm{\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{Ab})}^{\mathrm{op}} \times \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{Ab})^{\mathrm{op}} \times \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}) \times \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

2.1.1. domain

Note that \(M,N\) are canonically \(R,\mathbb{Z}\)-bimodules (cf. R-module as R,Z module) Then by hom chain complex into a bimodule both

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C,M), \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(D,N) \end{align*}

admit right \(R\)-module structures.

Since \(R\) is assumed to be commutative (cf. module over a commutative ring as canonical bimodule) those admit $\(R,R\)$-structures.

Therefore the tensor product is welldefined
