multiplication with an algebraic element and minimal polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(L\) be a finite field extension and \(\alpha \in L\). Then for the vectorspace-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \alpha_{\cdot}: L \rightarrow& L \\ x \mapsto& \alpha \cdot x \end{align*}

it follows, that the

  • algebraic minimal polynomial \(f_{\alpha, alg}\)
  • linear minimal polyonmial \(f_{\alpha, lin}\)

are the same

2. Proof

We show that

\begin{align*} f_{\alpha, lin} \mid f_{\alpha, alg} \end{align*}

then since \(f_{\alpha, alg}, f_{\alpha,lin}\) are prime, it follows, that \(f_{\alpha,alg} \sim f_{\alpha,lin}\) and since both are monic, it follows, that

\begin{align*} f_{\alpha, alg} = f_{\alpha, lin} \end{align*}

we note, that

\begin{align*} \alpha_{\cdot}^n(x) =& \alpha_{\cdot} \circ ... \alpha_{\cdot} (x) \\ =& \alpha^{n} \cdot x \end{align*}

Hence for arbitrary \(x\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} f_{\alpha, alg}( \alpha_{\cdot}) =& \sum_{i=0}^{k} \lambda_i \alpha^i (x) \\ =& x \cdot \sum_{i=0}^{k} \lambda_i \alpha^i \\ =& x \cdot 0 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

Hence by minimality of \(f_{\alpha, lin}\) we conclude, that

\begin{align*} f_{\alpha, lin} \mid f_{\alpha, alg} \end{align*}
