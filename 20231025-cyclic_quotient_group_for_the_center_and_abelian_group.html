cyclic quotient group for the center and abelian group

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group.

TFAE:

  1. \(G\) is abelian
  2. the quotient group \(G/Z(G)\) is cyclic, where \(Z(G)\) is the center

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Since \(Z(G) = G\) (see Zentrum und abelsche Gruppe), it follows that

\begin{align*} G/Z(G) = G/G = \{0\} \end{align*}

hence it is the zero group and thus generated by the neutral element \(0\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(g_1,g_2 \in G\) be arbitrary. Then by assumption, there exists an \(h \in G\) such that \(G/Z(G) = \langle h \cdot Z(G)\rangle\) Hence

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot Z(G) =& h^n \cdot Z(G) \\ g_2 \cdot Z(G) =& h^m \cdot Z(G) \end{align*}

Therefore, there exists \(z_1,z_2 \in Z(G)\) such that

\begin{align*} g_1 =& h^n \cdot z_1 \\ g_2 =& h^m \cdot z_2 \end{align*}

and so it holds, that

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot g_2 =& h^n \cdot z_1 \cdot h^m \cdot z_2 \\ =& h^n \cdot h^m \cdot z_1 \cdot z_2 \\ =& h^{n + m} \cdot z_1 \cdot z_2 \\ =& h^{m + n} \cdot z_1 \cdot z_2 \\ =& h^m \cdot h^n \cdot z_2 \cdot z_1 \\ =& h^m \cdot h^n \cdot z_2 \cdot z_1 \\ =& h^m \cdot z_2 \cdot h^n \cdot z_1 \\ =& g_2 \cdot g_1 \end{align*}

Therefore, since \(g_1,g_2\) were arbitrary, it follows that

\begin{align*} g_1, g_2 \in Z(G) \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} G = Z(G) \end{align*}

or equivalently, that \(G\) is abelian
