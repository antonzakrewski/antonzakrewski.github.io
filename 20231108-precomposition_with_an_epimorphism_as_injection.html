precomposition with an epimorphism as injection

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a locally small category, \(X,Y \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) objects and \(f: X \twoheadrightarrow Y\) an epimorphism Then precomposition with \(f^{-1}\) is an injection on hom-sets

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(Y,Z) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,Y) g \mapsto g \circ f \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose there exist \(g,h \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(Y,Z)\) such that \(f^{ \rightarrow}(g) = f^{ \rightarrow}(h)\) Then we conclude

\begin{align*} g \circ f = h \circ f \end{align*}

Since \(f\) is an epimorphism, we conclude that

\begin{align*} g = h \end{align*}

hence \(f^{ \rightarrow}\) is injective.
