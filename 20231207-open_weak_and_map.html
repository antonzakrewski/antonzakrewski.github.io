open-weak set and map

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(U_i\) a cover of \(X\) such that a set \(A \subseteq X\) is open if and only if for each \(i \in I\), the intersection \(A \cap U_i\) is open

Then for a space \(Z\), and a map

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow Z \end{align*}

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is continuous
  2. \(f_i \coloneqq f \circ \iota: U_i \rightarrow Z\) is continuous for each \(i \in I\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from composition of continuous maps and Subspace topology as initial topology for the inclusion

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(O \subseteq Z\) be open, then by assumption, each \(f_i^{-1}[O] \subseteq U_i\) is open. Thus by assumption of \((X,U_i)\) being open-weak, it follows, that

\begin{align*} f^{-1}[O] \end{align*}

is open, since \(f_i^{-1}[O] = O \cap U_i\)
