order of an element and quotient group

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup and \(g \in G\) an element with group order \(\mathrm{ord}(g) = n\). Then for \([g] = \pi_N(g) \in G/N\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ord}( [g]) \mid \mathrm{ord}(g) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(N_{\alpha} = N \cap \langle \alpha\rangle\). Then

\begin{align*} \langle \overline{\alpha} \rangle \simeq \langle \alpha\rangle/N_{\alpha} \end{align*}

Note that

\begin{align*} \langle \overline{\alpha} \rangle = \langle \alpha\rangle/(N') \end{align*}

and by Satz von Lagrange, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \vert \langle \overline{\alpha} \rangle \vert \cdot \vert N_{\alpha} \vert = \vert \langle \alpha \rangle \vert \mathrm{ord}( \overline{\alpha}) \cdot \vert N_{\alpha} \vert = \mathrm{ord}(\alpha) \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ord}( \overline{\alpha}) \mid \mathrm{ord}(\alpha) \end{align*}
