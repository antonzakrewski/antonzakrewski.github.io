opposite category of an ab-enriched category as ab-enriched

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an Ab-enriched category. Then the opposite category \(\mathcal{A}\) is also canonically ab-enriched.

2. Proof

2.1. abelian group

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{op}}}(B,A) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(A,B) \\ f^{\mathrm{op}} \mapsto& f \end{align*}

be the set-theroetic Bijection making \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(A,B)\) an abelian group via

\begin{align*} f^{\mathrm{op}} + g^{\mathrm{op}} = (f + g)^{\mathrm{op}} \end{align*}

2.2. composition

By assumption, there exists a bilinear map

\begin{align*} \circ: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(A,B) \times \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(B,C) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(A,C) \end{align*}

Thus we get

\begin{align*} (f_1^{\mathrm{op}} + f_2^{\mathrm{op}}) \circ (g_1^{\mathrm{op}} + g_2^{\mathrm{op}}) =& (f_1 + f_2)^{\mathrm{op}} \circ (g_1 + g_2)^{\mathrm{op}} \\ =& ((f_1 + f_2) \circ (g_1 + g_2))^{\mathrm{op}} \\ =& (f_1 \circ g_1 + f_2 \circ g_1 + f_1 \circ g_2 + f_2 \circ g_2)^{\mathrm{op}} \\ =& (f_1 \circ g_1)^{\mathrm{op}} + (f_2 \circ g_1)^{\mathrm{op}} + (f_1 \circ g_2)^{\mathrm{op}} + (f_2 \circ g_2)^{\mathrm{op}} \end{align*}

Hence the composition is bilinear
