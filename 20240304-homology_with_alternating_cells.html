homology with alternating cells

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex with

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \coprod_{i \in I} S^{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & \mathrm{Sk}_{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \coprod_{i \in I} D^{n} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Sk}_{n} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Sk}_n = \mathrm{Sk}_{n+1} \end{align*}

Then it holds for the singular homology

\begin{align*} H_n(X;M) = \bigoplus_{I} M \end{align*}

2. Proof

using cellular homology isomorphic to singular homology
