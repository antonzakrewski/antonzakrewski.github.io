group ring as Algebra generated by generators of the group

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(G\) a group, \(S\) a generating set. Then for the polynomial ring \(A[X_s \vert s \in S]\) there exists an algebrabic ring-epimorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: A[X_s \vert s \in S] \rightarrow& A[G] \\ X_s \mapsto& s \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(\sum_{g \in G} \alpha_g g \in A[G]\) be an element. Then it suffices to show that \(\alpha_g \cdot g\) is in the image of \(A[X_s]\). By assumption there exist \(s_i\) such that \(\prod_{i=1}^n s_i = g\) Then by construction

\begin{align*} \alpha_g \cdot \prod_{i=1}^n X_{s_i} \end{align*}

suffices
