sum of eulers phi function over divisors

1. Proposition

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), then

\begin{align*} n = \sum_{d \mid n, d \geq 1} \varphi(d) \end{align*}

2. Proof

eulersche Phi Funktion für Primzahlpotenzen

2.1. 1

Note that

\begin{align*} \sum_{d \mid 1} \varphi(d) =& \varphi(1) \\ =& \varphi(1) \end{align*}

2.2. prime power

Note that for \(p^n\) we get

\begin{align*} \sum_{d \mid p^n} \varphi(d) =& \varphi(1) + \varphi(p) + \varphi(p^2) + ... + \varphi(p^n) \\ =& 1 + (p-1) + p \cdot (p-1) + ... + p^{n-1} (p - 1) \\ =& p + p \cdot (p-1) + ... + p^{n-1} (p - 1) \\ =& p^n \end{align*}

2.3. prime decomposition

Suppose \(k = l \cdot p^n\) for some \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) with \(p \not\mid l\), then

\begin{align*} \sum_{d \mid l \cdot p^n} \varphi(d) =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{d \mid l} \varphi(p^i \cdot d) \\ =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \sum_{d \mid l} (p-1) \cdot p^{i-1} \cdot \varphi(d) \\ =& \sum_{d \mid l} \cdot \varphi(d) \cdot \sum_{i=0}^{n} (p-1) \cdot p^{i-1} \\ =& \sum_{d \mid l} \cdot \varphi(d) \cdot p^n \\ =& p^n \cdot \sum_{d \mid l} \cdot \varphi(d) \\ =& p^n \cdot n \end{align*}

The general statement follows by applying the prime decomposition
