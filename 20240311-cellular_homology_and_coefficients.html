cellular homology and coefficients

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a CW-Complex and \(M\) an abelian group Then we have for the cellular chain complex, that

\begin{align*} C_{*}^{\mathrm{cell}}(X,M) \simeq C_{*}^{\mathrm{cell}}(X, \mathbb{Z}) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} M \end{align*}

2. Proof

Consider the diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_{n}(X_n, X_{n-1}; \mathbb{Z}) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} M \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{\partial_{n}^{\mathbb{Z}} \otimes M} & H_{n-1}(X_{n-1}, X_{n-2}; \mathbb{Z}) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} M \arrow[]{d}{} \\ H_n(X_n, X_{n-1}; M) \arrow[]{r}{\partial_{n}^{M}} & H_{n-1}(X_{n-1}, X_{n-2}; M) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

here the vertical map is defined on elementary tensors by

\begin{align*} (\alpha \otimes m) \mapsto& m_{*}(\alpha) \end{align*}

where \(m \in M\) is seen as map

\begin{align*} m: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow& M \\ 1 \mapsto& m \end{align*}

As such, it induces a map

\begin{align*} H_n(X, \mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow H_n(X, M) \end{align*}
