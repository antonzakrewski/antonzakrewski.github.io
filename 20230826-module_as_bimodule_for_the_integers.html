module as bimodule for the integers

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-left-module Then \(M\) is a \(R-\mathbb{Z}\)-bimodule

Analogically for a right-module

2. Proof

2.2. pseudo-Associativity

Let \(r \in R, n \in \mathbb{Z}\) and \(m \in M\). Then

\begin{align*} (r \cdot m) \cdot n =& \sum_{i=1}^{n} r \cdot m \\ =& r \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{n} m \\ =& r \cdot (m \cdot n) \end{align*}
