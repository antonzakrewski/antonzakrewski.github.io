abelian category implies ab-enrichment

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category without the Ab-enrichment. Then there exists a canoncial ab-enrichment

2. Proof

2.1. CMon

follows from semiadditive category CMon enriched

2.2. inverse element

It suffices to show, that \(- \mathrm{id}_{A}\) exists, since

\begin{align*} -f = f \circ (- \mathrm{id}_{A} ) \end{align*}

as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{\Delta_A} & A \oplus A \arrow[]{rr}{(f \circ \mathrm{id}_{A}) \oplus f \circ (- \mathrm{id}_{A})} & & B \oplus B \arrow[]{r}{\nabla_B} & B \\ A & A \oplus A \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id} \oplus (- \mathrm{id}_{A}) } & A \oplus A \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus f} & B \oplus B & B \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where

\begin{align*} f \oplus f (- \mathrm{id}_{A} ) =& (f \circ \mathrm{id}_{A}) \oplus (f \circ -\mathrm{id}_{A}) \\ =& f \circ (\mathrm{id}_{A} - \mathrm{id}_{A} ) \\ =& f \circ 0 \\ =& \end{align*}
