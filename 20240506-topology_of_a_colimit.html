topology of a colimit

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{Top}\) be the category Top and \(I: \mathcal{I} \rightarrow \mathrm{Top}\) a small diagram. Then the colimit exists and is given by the disjoint union

\begin{align*} \coprod X_i/ \sim \end{align*}

modulo the generated equivalence relation by

\begin{align*} x \sim y \Leftrightarrow \exists X_j, f(x) = g(x) \in X_j \end{align*}

where a subset \(O\) is open if and only if the preimage under the coprojection \(\iota_i^{-1}[O]\) is open for all \(\iota_i: X_i \rightarrow \mathrm{colim}(I)\)

2. Proof

2.1. existence

  • category Top as cocomplete category

2.2. underlying set

corollary of

  • forgetful functor from Top to Set as left adjoint
  • colimit in set

hence the underlying set is given up to bijection by the colimit in set. Therefore it remains to show, that the topology does the job.

2.3. topology

2.3.1. welldefined topology

2.3.1.1. trivial opens

follows from \(\iota_i^{-1}[\emptyset] = \emptyset, \iota_i^{-1}[\mathrm{colim}(I)] = X_i\)

2.3.1.2. arbitrary union

follows as preimages commute with unions and the union of opens in \(X_i\) is again open

2.3.1.3. finite intersection

follows as preimage commute with intersections and the finite intersection of opens in \(X_i\) is open

2.3.2. map welldefined

Suppose there exist maps \(f_i: X_i \rightarrow Z\). Then consider the unique, induced set theoretic map in \(\mathrm{Set}\)

\begin{align*} f: \mathrm{colim}(I) \rightarrow Z \end{align*}

it follows that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X_i \arrow[]{r}{\iota_}} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{f_i}& \mathrm{colim}(I) \arrow[]{r}{f}& Z \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes.

Furthermore for an open \(O \subseteq Z\) it holds that

\begin{align*} f_{i}^{-1}[O] \end{align*}

is open.

Hence \(f^{-1}[[O_i]\) is open, as \(f_i^{-1}[\iota^{-1}[O]]\) is open.
