cohomological functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathcal{D}\) a pre-triangulated category. Then a contravariant functor \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{opp}} \rightarrow \mathcal{A}\) is said to be cohomological if the dualized functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{\mathrm{opp}}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{opp}} \end{align*}

is a homological functor
