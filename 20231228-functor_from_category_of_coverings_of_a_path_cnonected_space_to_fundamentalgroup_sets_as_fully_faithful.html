path connected fundamental theory of covering theory about fully faithfullness

1. Proposition

Let \(B\) be a locally and globally pathconnected topological space. Then the functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fib}_ghtarrow& \pi_1(B,b)-\mathrm{Set} \simeq \mathrm{Fun}(\tau_{\leq 1}(b), \mathrm{Set}) \\ (p: E \rightarrow B) \mapsto& (\mathrm{Fib}^p: \tau_{\leq 1}(B) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set}) \\ (f: (p: E \rightarrow B) \rightarrow (p': E' \rightarrow B) \rightarrow& (f_{b}: \mathrm{Fib}^p \rightarrow \mathrm{Fib}^{p'}) \end{align*}

is fully faithful, where

  1. \(\mathrm{Fib}_b\) is the monodromy functor
  2. and \(f_{b}\) is the induced natural transformation

2. Proof

2.1. faithful

locally path connected codomain and covering map connected codomain and surjective covering map

Suppose

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} E \arrow[swap]{rd}{p} \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{rr}{f} \arrow[shift left=1]{rr}{f'} & & E' \arrow[]{ld}{p'}\\ & B & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fib}_b(f) = \mathrm{Fib}_b(f') \end{align*}

Then it follws, that

\begin{align*} f_{\vert p^{-1}[b]} = f'_{\vert p^{-1}[b]} \end{align*}

Then by covering of a locally connected space as coproduct of subcovers we get an open cover \(E_i\) of \(E\) with

\begin{align*} p_0: E_0 \rightarrow B \end{align*}

Thus it suffices to show, that the restrictions of \(f,f'\) on \(E_i\) agree. We conclude by connected codomain and surjective covering, that \(p_0\) is surjective, as \(E_0 \neq \emptyset\).

Thus

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[b] \cap E_0 \neq \emptyset \end{align*}

Using uniqueness of connected factorization through a covering map we get, that

\begin{align*} f_{\vert E_0} = f_{\vert E_0}' \end{align*}

Therefore it follows, that

\begin{align*} f = f' \end{align*}

2.2. full

Let \(f: p^{-1}[b] \rightarrow p'^{-1}[b]\) be a G-equivariant map for \(G = \pi_1(B,b)\) Then again we can decompose \(E\) as sum of the connected components, where \(p_0: E_0 \twoheadrightarrow B\) is analogously surjective Thus choose basepoints

\begin{align*} e_i \in p_i^{-1}[b] \end{align*}

Then using <> it remains to show, that

\begin{align*} p_*(\pi_1(E,e)) \subseteq p_*^{'}(E',f(e)) \end{align*}

Let \(\gamma \in \pi_1(E,E)\) be loop. Then we can uniquely lift \(p(\gamma)\) to a path \(\gamma' \in E'\) with start point \(f(e)\)

Then it remains to show, that \(\gamma'\) is a loop at \(f(e)\)

By it holds by equivariance of \(f\), that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{endpoint} = p(\gamma) \cdot f(e) \\ =& f(p(\gamma) \cdot e) \\ =& f(e) \end{align*}

where \(p(\gamma) \cdot e\) is given by lifting \(p(\gamma)\), which is by uniqueness \(\gamma\), and taking the endpoint, which is \(e\), as \(\gamma\) is a loop.

Thus we get a map

\begin{align*} f_{E_0}: E_0 \rightarrow E' \end{align*}

and using the universal property also a map

\begin{align*} F: E_0 \rightarrow E' \end{align*}

Suppose \(e \in p^{-1}[b]\) is a point in the fibre, then there exists a path component \(E_i\) with \(e \in E_i\) and a base point \(e_i \in E_i\). Choosing a path \(\gamma: e_i \leadsto e\) results in

\begin{align*} f(e) =& f(\gamma \cdot e_i) \\ =& \gamma \cdot f(e_i) \\ =& \gamma \cdot F(e_i) \\ =& F(\gamma \cdot e_i) \\ =& F(e) \end{align*}

as by covering map induces natural transformation on monodromy functor it holds, that \(F\) is an equivariant map
