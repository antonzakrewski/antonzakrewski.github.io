tensor product of cycles gives cycle of tensor product

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M\) an \(R\)-module and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}), \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{ModR})\) the category of chain complexes. Then for chain complexes \(C \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}), D \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{ModR})\), the graded module of cycles \(Z(-)\) and the general tensor functor of modules resp. the tensor product of chain complexes of modules there exists the canonical map generated by

\begin{align*} Z(C) \otimes_R Z(D) \rightarrow& Z(C \otimes_R D) \\ (x_n \otimes_R y_m) \mapsto& (x_n \otimes_R y_m \in C_n \otimes_R D_m \hookrightarrow (C \otimes_R D)_{n+m}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

applying the differential for an elementary tensor results in

\begin{align*} d^{C \otimes_R D}(x_n \otimes y_m) =& d^C(x) \otimes y + (-1)^{p} x \otimes d^D(y) \end{align*}

where by assumption

\begin{align*} d^C(x), d^D(y) = 0 \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} d^{C \otimes_R D}(x_n \otimes y_m) =& d^C(x) \otimes_R y + (-1)^{p} x \otimes_R d^D(y) \\ =& 0 \otimes_R y + (-1)^{p} x \otimes_{R} 0 \\ =& 0 + 0 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
