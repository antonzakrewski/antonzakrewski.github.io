half exact functor as additive functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be additive categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) a half exact functor. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is an additive functor

2. Proof

Using additive functor and biproduct. Let \(A,B \in \mathcal{A}\), then we get the short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{\iota_A} & A \oplus B \arrow[]{r}{\pi_B} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Which is split exact for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[swap]{r}{\iota_A} & \arrow[swap, bend right]{l}{\pi_A} A \oplus B \arrow[swap]{r}{\pi_B} & \arrow[swap, bend right]{l}{\iota_B} B \arrow[swap]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here \(\iota_A, \pi_B\) are split mono resp. split epi, hence by split mono under funtcors / split epi under functors so are

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(\iota_A), \mathcal{F}(\pi_B) \end{align*}

Furthermore since \(\mathcal{F}\) is half-exact, the resulting sequence is exact at \(\mathcal{F}(A \oplus B)\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[swap]{r}{\mathcal{F}(\iota_A)} & \arrow[swap, bend right]{l}{\mathcal{F}(\pi_A)} \mathcal{F}(A \oplus B) \arrow[swap]{r}{\mathcal{F}(\pi_B)} & \arrow[swap, bend right]{l}{\mathcal{F}(\iota_B)} \mathcal{F}(B) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

By Splitting Lemma, we get a natural isomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(A \oplus B) \simeq \mathcal{F}(A) \oplus \mathcal{F}(B) \end{align*}
