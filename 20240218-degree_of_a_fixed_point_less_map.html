degree of a fixed point less map

1. Proposition

Let \(f: S^n \rightarrow S^n\) be a continuous map on the n-spheres. Suppose \(f\) has no fixed points, then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(f)= (-1)^{n+1} \end{align*}

2. Proof

We claim, that

\begin{align*} f \simeq - \mathrm{id}_{S^n} \end{align*}

are homotopic maps (cf. antipodal map).

for \(x \in S^n\), take the shortest path from \(-x\) to \(f(x)\). note that this is welldefined, as \(f(x) \neq x\).

Then this gives a homotopy

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times S^n \rightarrow S^n \\ (t,x) \mapsto& \gamma_x(t) \end{align*}
