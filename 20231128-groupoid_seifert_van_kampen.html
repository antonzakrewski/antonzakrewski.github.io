Seifert van Kampen about groupoids

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(A, B \subseteq X\) subspaces such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{int}(A) \cup \mathrm{int}(B) = X \end{align*}

Then the induced diagram given by the fundamental groupoid of a space

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \pi_{\leq 1}(A \cap B) & \pi_{leq 1}(A) \\ \pi_{\leq 1}(B) & \pi_{\leq 1}(X) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is a pushout in the category of groupoids

2. Proof

Let \(\mathcal{G}\) be a groupoid and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \pi_{\leq 1}(A \cap B) \arrow[]{d}{}\arrow[]{r}{}& \arrow[]{d}{} \pi_{\leq 1}(A) \\ \pi_{\leq 1}(B) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathcal{G} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be a commutative diagram.

Then we want to show, that htere exists a unique functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \pi_{\leq 1}(X) \rightarrow \mathcal{G} \end{align*}

such that \(\mathcal{F}\) restricts no \(\mathcal{F}_A, \mathcal{F}_B\) on \(\pi_{\leq 1}(A), \pi_{\leq 1}(B)\)

2.1. construction

Let

2.1.1. object

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \pi_{\leq 1}(X) \rightarrow& \mathcal{G} \\ x \mapsto& \begin{cases} \mathcal{F}_A(x) & \mbox{if } x \in A \\ \mathcal{F}_B(x) & \mbox{if } x \in B \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

Suppose \(x \in A, x \in B\), then \(x \in A \cap B\), hence it is welldefined by commutativity of

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \pi_{\leq 1}(A \cap B) \arrow[]{d}{}\arrow[]{r}{}& \arrow[]{d}{} \pi_{\leq 1}(A) \\ \pi_{\leq 1}(B) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathcal{G} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.1.2. morphism

Let \([\gamma]: x \rightarrow x'\) be a morphism in \(\pi_{\leq 1(X)\), i.e. \(\gamma: [0,1] \rightarrow X\) is a path from \(x\) to \(x'\).

Consider the open covering of \([0,1] \gamma^{-1}[ \mathrm{int}(A)] \cup \gamma^{-1}[\mathrm{int}(B)]\) for each \(t \in [0,1]\) there is an \(\varepsilon_t > 0\) such that the closed epsilon ball \([t - \varepsilon_t, t + \varepsilon_t] \cap [0,1]\) is contained in \(\gamma^{-1}[A]\) or \(\gamma^{-1}[B]\)

Since \([0,1]\) is compact, we find finitely many intervals \([t - \varepsilon_t, t + \varepsilon_t]\) (by taking the interior as cover), that cover \([0,1]\).

Here we find \(0 = t_0 \leq t_1 \leq ... t_n = 1\) such that

\begin{align*} \gamma([t_i, t_{i+1}]) \end{align*}

is completely contained in \(A\) or \(B\)

In particular, we finde \(\gamma_1,...,\gamma_n\) such that the concatenation

\begin{align*} \gamma = \gamma_{n} * ... * \gamma_1 \end{align*}

where \(\gamma_1\) is \(\gamma\) restricted to \([t_i,t_{i+1}]\) after reparametrization.

In particular, we get

\begin{align*} [\gamma] = [\gamma_n] * ... * [\gamma_1] \end{align*}

Since \(\mathcal{F}\) is supposed to be a functor, we define

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}([\gamma]) = \mathcal{F}([\gamma_n]) \circ ... \circ \mathcal{F}[(\gamma_1)] \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(\gamma_i]) = \begin{cases} \mathcal{F}_A([\gamma_i]) & \mbox{if } \mathrm{im}(\gamma_i) \subseteq A \\ \mathcal{F}_B([\gamma_i]) & \mbox{if } \mathrm{im}(\gamma_i) \subseteq B \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2.1.3. welldefined

2.1.3.1. a) chopped parts

\(\mathcal{F}(\gamma_i)\) is welldefined, since if \(\mathrm{im}(\gamma_i) \subseteq A \cap B\), then by the commutativity of the diagram \mathcal{F}: π≤ 1(X) →& \mathcal{G}
x \mapsto&

\begin{cases} \mathcal{F}_A(x) & \mbox{if } x \in A \\ \mathcal{F}_B(x) & \mbox{if } x \in B \\ \end{cases}

\end{align*}

2.1.3.2. b) choice of refinements

Let \(\gamma_i\) with \(\mathrm{im}(\gamma_i) \subseteq A\) Then we can refine the path \(\gamma_{i,j}\), where \(\mathrm{im}(\gamma_{i,j}) \subseteq A\) Thus

\begin{align*} \gamma_{i} = \gamma_{i,m} * ... \gamma_{i,1} \end{align*}

by functorality of \(\mathcal{F}_A\), we get

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}_{A}(\gamma_i) = \mathcal{F}_A(\gamma_{i,m}) * ... * \mathcal{F}_{A}(\gamma_{i,1}) \end{align*}

By choosing a common refinement, we get by transitivity

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}_{\gamma_n} \circ ... \circ \mathcal{F}_{\gamma_1} = \mathcal{F}_{\gamma_1'} \circ ... \circ \mathcal{F}_{\gamma_m'} \end{align*}
2.1.3.3. c) choice of representative of

Let \(\gamma\) and \(\gamma'\) be paths from \(x\) to \(x'\), homotopic relative to the endpoints. We pick a homotopy

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times [0,1] \rightarrow X \end{align*}

By lebesgue's number lemma we can decompose \([0,1]^2\) into \(n^2\) tiny squares of side-lengh \(\frac{1}{n}, n \in \mathbb{N}^+\), such that

\begin{align*} H[ [t_i, t_i + \frac{1}{n}] \times [s_i, s_i + \frac{1}{n}]] \subseteq A \end{align*}

resp. \(H[ [t_i, t_i + \frac{1}{n}] \times [s_i, s_i + \frac{1}{n}]] \subseteq A\)

We want to show, that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}([\gamma]) \coloneqq \mathcal{F}([\gamma_N]) \circ ... \circ \mathcal{F}([\gamma_1]) = \mathcal{F}([\gamma_n']) \circ ... \circ \mathcal{F}([\gamma_n]) \eqqcolon \mathcal{F}([\gamma']) \end{align*}
