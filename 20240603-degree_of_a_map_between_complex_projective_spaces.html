degree of a map between complex projective spaces

1. Definition

Let \(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n\) be the complex projective space, \(H^{2n}(-)\) be the singular cohomology functor. Fix an isomorphism \(\varphi: H^{2n}(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n) \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}\).

Then for a continuous map \(f: \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n\), the degree is defined as

\begin{align*} f^* \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(H^{2n}(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n), H^{2n}(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n)) \overset{(\varphi^{-1}, \varphi)}{\cong} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z},\mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}
