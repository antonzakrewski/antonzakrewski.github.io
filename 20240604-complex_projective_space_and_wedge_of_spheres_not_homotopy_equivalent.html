complex projective space and wedge of spheres not homotopy equivalent

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^2\) be the complex projective space and \(S^2 \vee S^4\) the wedge product of spheres Then they are not homotopy equivalent

2. Proof

follows from:

  • singular cohomology ring of the sphere
  • singular cohomology ring of the complex projective space_

where

  1. \(H^*(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^2)\) has a degree \(2\) generator, which squares to a non zero element.
  2. \(H^*(S^2 \vee S^4)\) has a degree \(2\) generator, which squares to \(2\)
