hom into bimodules bifunctor

1. Proposition

Let (R,S) be rings and \(\mathrm{R-S-Mod}\) the category of bimodules. Then there exists a hom functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}(-,-): \mathrm{RMod}^{\mathrm{op}} \times (\mathrm{(R,S)Mod}) \rightarrow \mathrm{SMod} \\ \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{SMod}\) denotes the category of left \(S\)-modules

2. Proof

2.1. object

  • hom-set into a bimodule with right module structure
\begin{align*} f(-) \cdot s \coloneqq (m \mapsto f(m) \cdot s) \end{align*}

2.2. morphism

Note that by similar arguments to set of module homomorphisms as abelian group and Category Rmod as abelian category there exists a functor to Category Ab. It remains to show that the abelian group and group-homomorphisms given by precomposition and postcomposition carry a natural module structure.

Let \(N,N' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{R-S-Mod})\), \(M,M' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{RMod})\), \(s \in S\) and \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}(M,N)\)

2.2.1. \(\mathrm{R-Mod}\)

Let \(\varphi: M' \rightarrow M\) be a module-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi^{ \rightarrow}(f \cdot s) =& \varphi^{ \rightarrow}(m \mapsto f(m) \cdot s) \\ =& (m' \mapsto f(\varphi(m') \cdot s) \\ =& f(\varphi) \cdot s \end{align*}

2.2.2. \(\mathrm{R-S-Mod}^{\mathrm{op}}\)

Let \(\varphi: N \rightarrow N'\) be a bimodule homomorphism.

\begin{align*} \varphi^{ \leftarrow}(f \cdot s) =& \varphi(f \cdot s) \\ =& \varphi(f) \cdot s \end{align*}
