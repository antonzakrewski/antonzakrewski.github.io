sheaf functor from Mod as exact functor

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(X = \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the affine scheme. Then the sheaf functor from Mod is an exact functor

2. Proof

Suppose

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M_1 \arrow[]{r}{f} & M_2 \arrow[]{r}{g} & M_3 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is an exact sequence.

Then since the localization is exact, it follows, that for a prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} (M_1)_{\mathfrak{p}} \arrow[]{r}{f_{\mathfrak{p}}} & (M_2)_{\mathfrak{p}} \arrow[]{r}{g_{\mathfrak{p}}} & (M_3)_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is also exact.

By construction, it holds, that the stalk

\begin{align*} (\tilde{M}_1)_{\mathfrak{p}} = (M_1)_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{align*}

and by exact sequence of sheaves it follows, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} (\tilde{M_1})_{\mathfrak{p}} \arrow[]{r}{\tilde{f_{\mathfrak{p}}}} & (\tilde{M_2})_{\mathfrak{p}} \arrow[]{r}{\tilde{g_{\mathfrak{p}}}} & (\tilde{M_3})_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
