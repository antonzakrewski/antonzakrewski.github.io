integral element in an integral domain and irreducible polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a Integral domain, \(B\) an integral A-Algebra and \(\alpha \in B\) an integral element. Then the monic polynomial may be choosen to be irreducible

2. Proof

By assumption there exists a monic polynomial \(f\). Suppose \(f\) is not irreducible, then there exist polynomials \(g_1,g_2\) such that

\begin{align*} f = g_1 \cdot g_2 \end{align*}

Furthermore the leading coefficients of \(g_1,g_2\) have to be units (cf.degree of the product of polynomials in an integral domain)

Since

\begin{align*} 0 =& f(\alpha) \\ =& g_1(\alpha) \cdot g_2(\alpha) \end{align*}

it follows w.l.o.g. that \(g_1(\alpha) = 0\) with \(\mathrm{deg}(g_1) < \mathrm{deg}(f_1)\)

Hence iterating terminates after finitely many steps, since \(\mathbb{N}\) is well-ordered
