adjunction of a primitive root as normal field extension

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(\zeta\) a primitive root of unity. Then \(K(\zeta)\) is a normal field extension.

2. Proof

Note that \(X^n - 1\) splits in \(K(\zeta)\), since \(\zeta\) generates all roots of \(X^n - 1\) for some algebraic closure. Furthermore, \(\zeta\) is a root, hence the minimal polynomial \(f_{\zeta} \mid X^n - 1\) splits (cf. division of a splitting polynomial)
