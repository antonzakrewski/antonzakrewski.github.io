division of a splitting polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(R[X]\) the polynomial ring. Suppose \(f,g \in R[X]\) are polynomials such that \(f \mid g\) and that \(g\) splits.

Then \(f\) also splits.

2. Proof

by assumption we get

\begin{align*} g = \lambda \cdot \prod (X - \alpha_i) \end{align*}

Also there exists an \(h \in R[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} h \cdot f = g \end{align*}

furthermore, by degree of the product of polynomials in an integral domain, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(g) = \mathrm{deg}(f) + \mathrm{deg}(h) \end{align*}

Here, since \(R\) is an Integral domain, we conclude, that a root of \(g\) is given by a root of \(f\) or \(h\).

by degree root theorem, it follows, that \(h\) has at most \(\mathrm{deg}(h)\) many roots (counting multiplicity). Since \(g\) splits, we conclude, that \(g\) has exactly \(\mathrm{deg}(g)\) roots.

Therefore, \(f\) has \(\mathrm{deg}(g) - \mathrm{deg}(h)\) many roots and thus splits
