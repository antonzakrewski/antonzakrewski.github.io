quotient field as finitely generated module and ring as field

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain and \(K = Q(R)\) the quotient field

TFAE:

  1. \(K\) is a finitely generated \(R\)-module
  2. \(K = R\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(\alpha \in R \setminus \{0\}\) be an element. Then it follows that multiplication with an element is linear and that

\begin{align*} \alpha: K \rightarrow K \end{align*}

is a module-epimorphism (even iso) with

\begin{align*} \beta = \frac{\beta}{\alpha} \cdot \alpha \end{align*}

for arbitrary \(\beta \in K\)

Therefore

\begin{align*} (\alpha) K = K \end{align*}

and hence there exists an element \(r \cdot \alpha\) such that

\begin{align*} (1 - r \cdot \alpha) \cdot K = 0 \end{align*}

This holds especially for \(1 \in K\) showing

\begin{align*} (1 - r \cdot \alpha) \cdot 1 = 1 - r \cdot \alpha \end{align*}

and therefore \(r = \alpha^{-1}\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from the generator \(1\)
