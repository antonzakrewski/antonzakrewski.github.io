homological euler characteristic of a product of homologically finite spaces

1. Proposition

Let \(X,Y\) be homologically finite topological spaces and \(X \times Y\) their product Then the homological euler characteristic is given by

\begin{align*} \chi(X \times Y) =& \sum_{n\geq 0} (-1)^n \sum_{0 \leq k \leq n} \mathrm{rank}(H_n(X)) \cdot \mathrm{rank}(H_{n-k}(Y)) \\ & \chi(X) \cdot \chi(Y) \end{align*}

2. Proof

by kunneth formula for singular homology we know that

\begin{align*} H_n(X \times Y) \simeq (H(X) \otimes_R H(Y))_n \oplus \mathrm{Tor}([H_{n-1}(X), H_{n-1}(Y)]) \end{align*}

here by rank of tor of a finitely generated abelian group it suffices to determine \(\mathrm{rank}(H_n(X) \otimes_R H_n(Y))\) By rank and direct sum

\begin{align*} \mathrm{rank}((H(X) \otimes_R H(Y))_n) = \sum_{0 \leq k \leq n} \mathrm{rank}(H_n(X) \otimes_\mathbb{Z} H_{n-k}(Y)) \\ \end{align*}

By rank and tensor product it further follows that

\begin{align*} \sum_{0 \leq k \leq n} \mathrm{rank}(H_n(X) \otimes_\mathbb{Z} H_{n-k}(Y)) =& \sum_{0 \leq k \leq n} \mathrm{rank}(H_n(X)) \cdot \mathrm{rank}(H_{n-k}(Y)) \end{align*}

then we get

\begin{align*} \chi(X \times Y) =& \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \mathrm{rank}(H_n(X \times Y)) \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (\sum_{p+q = n} (-1)^{p + q} \mathrm{rank}(X_p(X)) \cdot \mathrm{rank}(X_p(X))) \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (\sum_{p+q = n} (-1)^{p} \mathrm{rank}(X_p(X)) \cdot (-1)^{q}\mathrm{rank}(X_p(X))) \\ =& (\sum_{p=0}^{\infty} (-1)^p \mathrm{rank}(H_p(X)) \cdot (\sum_{q=0}^{\infty} (-1)^q \mathrm{rank}(H_q(Y)) \\ =& \chi(X) \cdot \chi(Y) \end{align*}

showing the desired equality
