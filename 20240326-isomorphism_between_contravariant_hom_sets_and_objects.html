isomorphism between contravariant hom-sets and objects

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(X,Y\) a n object.

TFAE:

  1. \(X \simeq Y\) are isomorphic
  2. there exists a morphism \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) such that
\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(Y,X) \simeq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,X) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism.

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of

  • isomorphisms under functors
  • contravariant hom-functor of points

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By surjectivity, there exists a morphism \(g: Y \rightarrow X\) such that

\begin{align*} g \circ f = \mathrm{id}_{X} \end{align*}

Furthermore it remains to show, that

\begin{align*} f \circ g = \mathrm{id}_{Y} \end{align*}
