union of a chain of closed irreducible sets as irreducible set

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space,

\begin{align*} X_0 \subseteq X_1 \subseteq X_2 \subseteq ... \end{align*}

a chain of closed irreducible subspace. Then the union

\begin{align*} S \coloneqq \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} X_0 \end{align*}

is also irreducible

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(S\) is reducible, then there exist nonempty, nontrivial closed subsets \(S_1,S_2\) such that

\begin{align*} S_1 \cup S_2 = S \end{align*}

Then by assumption there exist \(s_1 \in S_1 \setminus S_2, s_2 \in S_2 \in S_1\) since \(S_1,S_2 \neq S\)

Then choose a sufficiently large \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), such that

\begin{align*} s_1,s_2 \in X_n \end{align*}

This can be done, since by definition \(S = \bigcup X_n\)

Then consider \(S_1 \cap X_n, S_2 \cap X_n\) as closed sets, as both \(S_i\) and \(X_n\) are closed. Furthermore it follows by Distributivgesetz für Vereinigung und Schnitt that

\begin{align*} (S_1 \cap X_n) \cup (S_2 \cap X_n) = X_n \end{align*}

Furthermore it follows that \(s_1 \not\in S_2 \cap X_n, s_2 \not\in S_1 \cap X_n\) hence showing

\begin{align*} (S_1 \cap X_n), (S_2 \cap X_n) \subsetneq X_n \end{align*}
