generators of the normal closure

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(H \subseteq G\) a subgroup. Then the normal closure is generated by the set

\begin{align*} \{g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} \vert h \in H, g \in G\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. contained in the normal closure

Let \(N\) be a normal subgroup containing \(H\). Then by assumption, for each \(n \in N\) and hence especially for \(h \in H \subseteq N\) the element

\begin{align*} g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} \in N \end{align*}

2.2. generating

We show, that the generated group is a normal subgroup, hence by construction of the normal closure, we get \(\overline{N} \subseteq N \coloneqq \langle \{g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} \vert h \in H, g \in G\} \rangle\)

Let \(n \in N\), then by assumption, we get

\begin{align*} n = g_1 \cdot h_1 \cdot g_1^{-1} g_2 \cdot h_2 \cdot g_2^{-1} ... g_n \cdot h_n \cdot g_n^{-1} \end{align*}

Let \(k \in G\), then conjugation with \(k\) results in

\begin{align*} k \cdot n \cdot k^{-1} =& k \cdot g_1 \cdot h_1 \cdot g_1^{-1} g_2 \cdot h_2 \cdot g_2^{-1} ... g_n \cdot h_n \cdot g_n^{-1} \cdot k^{-1} \\ =& k \cdot g_1 \cdot h_1 \cdot g_1^{-1} k^{-1} \cdot k g_2 \cdot h_2 \cdot g_2^{-1} \cdot k^{-1} ... k \cdot g_n \cdot h_n \cdot g_n^{-1} \cdot k^{-1} \\ =& (k \cdot g_1) \cdot h_1 \cdot (k \cdot g_1)^{-1} ... \cdot (k \cdot g_n) \cdot h_n \cdot (k \cdot g_n)^{-1} \end{align*}

where \((k \cdot g_1 \cdot h_1 \cdot (k \cdot g_1)^{-1} \in \{g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} \vert h \in H, g \in G\}\), hence \(k \cdot n \cdot k^{-1} \in N\)
