union of a chain of prime ideals as prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, $\mathfrak{p}1 \subseteq … $ a chain of prime ideals. Then the union is also a prime ideal

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

  • union of a chain of ideals

2.2. strict ideal

  • union of a chain of strict ideals as strict ideal

2.3. prime

Let \(a,b \in A\) such that \(a \cdot b \in \bigcup \mathfrak{p}_i\). Then there exists an \(n\) such that \(a \cdot b \in \mathfrak{p}_n\) showing \(a \in \mathfrak{p}_n\) or \(b \in \mathfrak{p}_n\). Hence \(a \in \bigcup \mathfrak{p}_i, b \in \bigcup \mathfrak{p}_i\)
