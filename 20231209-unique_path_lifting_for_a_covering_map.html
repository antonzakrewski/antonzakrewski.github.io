unique path lifting for a covering map

1. Proposition

Let \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map and \(\gamma: [0,1] \rightarrow B\) a path with \(\gamma(0) = b\) and \(e \in \mathrm{Fib}_b\) (see: fibre of a covering map) Then there exists a unique path

\begin{align*} \overline{\gamma}_e: [0,1] \rightarrow E \end{align*}

such that

  1. \(p \circ \overline{\gamma} = \gamma\)
  2. \(\overline{\gamma}(0) = e\)

2. Proof

2.1. uniqueness

follows from connected factorization through a covering map, as \([0,1]\) is connected and by assumption,\(\overline{\gamma(0)}\) is fixed

2.2. existence

Pick an trivializing open cover \(U_i\) of \(B\). Then \(\gamma^{-1}[U_i]\) is an open cover of \(U_i\). by open covering of the interval induces closed inclusion, there exists a decomposition

\begin{align*} [0,1] = [0,a_1] \cup [a_1,a_2] ... [a_{n-1}, 1] \end{align*}

Thus \(\gamma\) can be witten as concatenation of paths \(\gamma_1,...\gamma_n\) with \(\gamma_j \subseteq U_i\) for some \(i \in I\).

Therefore, it suffices to find a lift for \(\gamma_n\) respecting the endpoints of \(\gamma_{k-1}, \gamma_{k+1}\). Here the endpoint shall be inductively chosen in the same fiber as \(\gamma_{k-1}\).

Since \(U_i\) is a trivializing cover, there exists a homeomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: F_i \times U_i \cong p^{-1}[U_i] \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} \varphi_{f_i} \{f_i} \times U_i \rightarrow U_i \end{align*}

is also a homeomorphism

Thus choosing \(\varphi_{f_i}^{-1} \circ \gamma_i\) is a suitable path
