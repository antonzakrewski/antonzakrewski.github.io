bijection between lifting problems for adjoints

1. Proposition

Let $\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}$ be categories and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_1,\mathcal{L}_2: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow& \mathcal{D} \\ \mathcal{R}_1,\mathcal{R}_2: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow& \mathcal{C} \end{align*}

be adjoint functors with $\mathcal{L}_i \dashv \mathcal{R}_i$

Let

\begin{align*} \gamma: S \rightarrow& T \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{C}) \\ \partial: X \rightarrow& Y \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

be morphisms

Then there exists a Bijection between

  1. a natural transformation $\eta_{\mathcal{R}}s \mathcal{R}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{R}_2$ together with a diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\lrcorner}]| S \arrow[]{d}{\gamma} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{R}_1(X) \arrow[]{rd}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}(X)}\arrow[]{d}{}& \\ T \arrow[dashed]{ru}{} \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathrm{Pull} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& \mathcal{R}_2(X) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{R}_2(\partial)}\\ & \mathcal{R}_1(Y) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}}& \mathcal{R}_2(Y) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

with a lift $T → \mathcal{R}1(X)

  1. a natural transformation $\eta_{\mathcal{L}}: \mathcal{L}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_1$ together with a digaram
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \mathcal{L}_2(S) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{L}(\gamma)} & \mathcal{L}_1(S) \arrow[]{d}{}& \\ \mathcal{L}_2(T) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Push} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & X \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ & \mathcal{L}_1(T) \arrow[dashed]{ru}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

with a lift \mathcal{L}1(T) → X$

2. Proof

using bijection between pushout / pullback diagrams for adjoints we get a bijection between diagrams Then a morphism

\begin{align*} T \rightarrow \mathcal{R}_1(X) \end{align*}

corresponds naturally to a morphism

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_1 \rightarrow X \end{align*}

Then the diagrams commute by naturality of an adjunction with respect to natural transformations as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & \mathcal{R}_1(X) \arrow[]{rd}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}(X)} & \\ T \arrow[]{ru}{} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & \mathcal{R}_2(X) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ & \mathcal{R}_1(Y) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathcal{R}_2(Y) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes.
