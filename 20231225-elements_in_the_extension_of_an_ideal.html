elements in the extension of an ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed subset. Suppose \(\mathfrak{a}\) is an ideal and \(S^{-1} \mathfrak{a}\) the extension, \(\frac{\alpha}{s} \in S^{-1}A\) an element.

TFAE:

  1. \(\frac{\alpha}{s} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{a}\)
  2. there exists an \(s' \in S\) and an \(\alpha' \in \mathfrak{a}\) such that
\begin{align*} \alpha' = \alpha \cdot s' \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption, there exist \(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n \in \mathfrak{a}\) and \(\beta_1,...,\beta_n,s_1,...,s_n\) such

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{s} = \sum_{i} \frac{\beta_i}{s_i} \cdot \frac{\alpha_i}{1} \end{align*}

w.l.o.g. we may assume, that \(\beta_i = 1\) as \(\alpha'_i \coloneqq \alpha_i \cdot \beta_i \in \mathfrak{a}\). also we may assume, that \(s = 1\) by multiplication with \(\frac{1}{s}\) resp. \(\frac{s}{1}\) Furthremore

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{s} =& \sum_{i} \frac{1}{s_i} \frac{\alpha_i}{1} \\ =& \sum_{i} \frac{1}{\prod s_i} \frac{\alpha_i \cdot \prod_{j \neq i} s_j}{1} \\ =& \frac{\sum_{i} \alpha_i \cdot \prod_{j \neq i} s_j}{\prod s_i} \end{align*}

again by closure under multiplication and addition, let

\begin{align*} \alpha' \coloneqq \sum_{i} \alpha_i \cdot \prod_{j \neq i} s_j \end{align*}

furthermore, let \(\tilde{s} \coloneqq \prod s_i\), as \(S\) is multiplicatively closed.

Then

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{s} = \frac{\alpha'}{\tilde{s}} \end{align*}

with \(\alpha' \in \mathfrak{a}\)

thus there exists an \(s'' \in S\) with

\begin{align*} s'' \cdot \tilde{s} \alpha = 1 \cdot \alpha' \end{align*}

and with \(s' \coloneqq s'' \cdot \tilde{s} \in S\) we get

\begin{align*} s' \cdot \alpha = \alpha' \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha'}{1} \cdot \frac{1}{s \cdot s'} =& \frac{\alpha \cdot s'}{s \cdot s'} \\ =& \frac{\alpha}{s} \end{align*}
