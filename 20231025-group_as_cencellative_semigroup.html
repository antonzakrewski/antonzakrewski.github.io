group as cancellative semigroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group. Then \(G\) is a cancellative semigroup

2. Proof

2.1. left-cancellative

Let \(a,b,c \in G\) such that

\begin{align*} a \cdot b = a \cdot c \end{align*}

Then multiplication with \(a^{-1} \cdot\) results in

\begin{align*} a \cdot b =& a \cdot c && \vert \cdot a^{-1} \\ a^{-1} \cdot a \cdot b =& a^{-1} \cdot a \cdot c \\ e \cdot b =& e \cdot c \\ b =& c \end{align*}

2.2. right-cancellative

follows analogously. Alternative, consider the opposite group \(G^{\mathrm{op}}\), where right-cancellativity in \(G\) is equivalent to left-cancellativity in \(G^{\mathrm{op}}\). Furthermore, \(G^{\mathrm{op}}\) is itself a group, hence \(G\) is right-cancllative

Or consider \(G\) as Gruppoid with a single object. There every morphism is an isomorphism, thus also monics and epics or left-cancellable and right-cancellable
