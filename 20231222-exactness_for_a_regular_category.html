exactness for a regular category

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a regular category, \(A,B,C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) be objects with morphisms of the form

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{g} \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{r}{h} & B \arrow[]{r}{f} & C \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then the diagram is said to be exact, if it is

  1. a coequalizer
  2. a kernel pair, i.e.
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\lrcorner}]| A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & B \arrow[]{d}{}\\ B \arrow[]{r}{} & C \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
