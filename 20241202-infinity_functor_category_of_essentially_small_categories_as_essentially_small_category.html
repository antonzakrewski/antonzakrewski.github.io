infinity functor category of essentially small categories as essentially small category

Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{d}\) be essentially small categories.
Then the infinity functor category \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\) is also an essentially small infinity category

Proof

Choose small infinity categories \(\mathcal{C}_s \simeq \mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}_s \simeq \mathcal{D}\).
Then the functors \(\mathcal{C}_s \rightarrow \mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}_s\) induce an joyal equivalence

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}_s, \mathcal{D}_s) \end{align*}

(cf. joyal equivalence between infinity categories and postcomposition, joyal equivalence of infinity categories and precomposition)

Thus the statement follows from infinity functor category of small categories as small category
