equivalent characterization of an affine k-algebra as artinian ring

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(A\) an affine k-Algebra

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathrm{dim}(A) = 0\) as ring dimension
  2. \(A\) is algebraic field extension
  3. \(A\) is a finite dimensional k vector space
  4. \(A\) is an artinian ring
  5. \(A\) has only finitely many maximal ideals

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

  • follows from dimension of a finitely generated k-algebra as transcendence degree as no element is transcendental since otherwise the transcendence degree (and thus the ring dimension) would be greater than \(0\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

  • finitely generated algebraic field extension as finite field extension

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 4)

  • finite dimensional vector space and artinian module

2.4. 4) \(\implies\) 5)

  • Artinian ring and finite amount of maximal ideals

2.5. 5) \(\implies\) 1)

using

  • finite type algebra over a jacobson ring as jacobson ring

and the general chinese remainder theorem we get

\begin{align*} A \cong A/(0) \cong A/(\bigcap \mathfrak{m}_i) \cong \prod_{i=1}^n A/\mathfrak{m}_i \end{align*}

(cf. Product of coprime Ideals equals Intersection)
