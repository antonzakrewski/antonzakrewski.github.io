sheaf of sets

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{V}\) a concrete category and \(\mathcal{F}\) a V-valued presheaf. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is said to be a sheaf, if it satisfies following properties:

  • locality of a sheaf
  • glueing of a sheaf

Note, that we assume \(\mathcal{F}(X) \neq \emptyset\)
