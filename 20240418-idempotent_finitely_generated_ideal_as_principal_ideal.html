idempotent, finitely generated ideal as principal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) a finitely generated idempotent ideal Then \(\mathfrak{a}\) is a principal ideal.

2. Proof

Let \((a_1,...,a_k)\) be generators of the ideal. Then it follows by product of ideals and generators that the set \(\{a_ia_j\}\) is a generator of \(\mathfrak{a}^2 = \mathfrak{a}\). Therefore choose \(c_{i,j} \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} \sum_{i,j} c_{i,j}^n \cdot a_{i} a_j = a_n \end{align*}

Then let

\begin{align*} \beta_{n,j} \coloneqq \sum_{i} c_{i,j}^n a_i \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} \sum \beta_{n,j} a_j = a_n \end{align*}

Consider the matrix

\begin{align*} C = (\delta_{i,j} - \beta_{i,j}) \end{align*}

Then it follows, that

\begin{align*} 1 - \det(C) \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

as

\begin{align*} \det(C) =& 1 \cdot \prod (1 - \beta_{i,i) + \textrm{stuff with at least one $\beta_{i,j}$} =& 1 + \textrm{stuff with at least one $\beta_{i,j}$} \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} 1 - \det(C) = \textrm{stuff with at least one $\beta_{i,j}$} \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

Furthermore for the vector \(v = (a_1,...,a_n)\) it follows that

\begin{align*} C \cdot (a_1,...,a_n) =& (\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i) - \sum_{i,j} \beta_{i,j} a_i \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

Then multiplication with the Adjunkte \(\tilde{C}\) gives

\begin{align*} C \cdot v =& 0 && \vert \cdot \tilde{C} \\ \tilde{C} \cdot C \cdot v =& 0 \\ \det(C) \cdot v =& 0 \end{align*}

And furthermore as each element \(v'\) can be generated by multiplication with a matrix \(A\) gives

\begin{align*} A \cdot \det(C) \cdot v =& A \cdot 0 \\ \det(C) \cdot A \cdot v =& 0 \\ \det(C) \cdot v' =& 0 \end{align*}

Hence this shows that \(\det(C) \cdot (a_1,...,0) = (0,...,0)\) and therefore

\begin{align*} \det(C) \cdot \mathfrak{a} =& 0 \end{align*}

Thus for \(a \in \mathfrak{a}\) it follows that

\begin{align*} a = a \cdot 1 + a \cdot 0 \\ =& a \cdot (1 - \det(C)) \end{align*}

and hence \(1 - \det(C)\) generates \(\mathfrak{a}\)
