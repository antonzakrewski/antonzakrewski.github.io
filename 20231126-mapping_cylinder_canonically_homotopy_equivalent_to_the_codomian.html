mapping cylinder canonically homotopy equivalent to the codomain

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces, \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map. Then the mapping cylinder \(\mathrm{Cyl}(f)\) is cannonically Homotopy equivalent to \(Y\)

2. Proof

Consider

\begin{align*} f: (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim \rightarrow& Y \\ (x,t) \mapsto& f(x) \\ y \mapsto y \end{align*}

given by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \times [0,1] \coprod Y \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim \arrow[]{r}{f} & Y \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and

\begin{align*} g: Y \rightarrow& (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim \\ y \mapsto& y \end{align*}

given by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} Y \arrow[bend left]{rr}{g} \arrow[]{r}{} & X \times [0,1] \coprod Y \arrow[]{r}{} & (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then

\begin{align*} f \circ g = \mathrm{id}_{Y} \end{align*}

hence it remains to show, that

\begin{align*} g \circ f \sim \mathrm{id}_{(X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim} \end{align*}

let

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y/\sim) \rightarrow& (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y/\sim) \\ (t,(x,h)) \mapsto& (x, t \cdot h) \\ (t,y) \mapsto& y \end{align*}

Since \([0,1]\) is locally regular compact, product of locally compact spaces preserves colimits, hence also \(\coprod, \sim\) and thus we get a canonical homeomorphism

\begin{align*} [0,1] \times (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y/\sim) \simeq (X \times [0,1]^2 \coprod Y \times [0,1]/\sim) \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \times [0,1]^2 \coprod Y \times [0,1] \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{rd}{H'} \arrow[]{r}{\pi}& X \times [0,1]^2 \coprod Y \times [0,1]/\sim \arrow[]{d}{\tilde{H}} \\ & X \times [0,1] \times Y/\sim \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(H'\) is given as coproduct of

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \times [0,1]^2 \arrow[]{r}{(x, t \cdot h)} & X \times [0,1] \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & X \times [0,1] \coprod Y \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim \\ Y \times [0,1] \arrow[]{r}{\pi_1} & Y \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & X \times [0,1] \coprod Y \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & (X \times [0,1] \coprod Y)/\sim \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
