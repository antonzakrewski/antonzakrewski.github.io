classification of groups of order pq

1. Proposition

Let \(p,q\) be prime numbers with \(p < q\) and \(p \nmid q - 1\). Suppose \(G\) is a group with group order \(p \cdot q\). Then

\begin{align*} G \simeq \mathbb{Z}/pq \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Because

\begin{align*} s_q \equiv 1 \mod{q} \end{align*}

and \(s_q \mid p < q\) it follows, that \(s_q \leq p\) and hence

\begin{align*} s_q = 1 \end{align*}

Since

\begin{align*} s_p \mid q \end{align*}

it follows, that \(s_p = 1\) or \(s_p = q\), as \(q\) is prime. Since by assumption \(p \not\mid q - 1\) or equivalently \(q \not\equiv 1 \mod p\) it follows \(s_p = 1\) Thus by sole group of an order as normal subgroup, \(U_q,U_p \trianglelefteq G\) is a normal subgroup

Therefore, by product of a normal subgroup and subgroup as subgroup \(U_p \cdot U_q\) is a group Note that by order of the product of coprime subgroups it follows, that

\begin{align*} \vert U_p \cdot U_q \vert = p \cdot q \end{align*}

Then by group of order p as cyclic group it follows, that \(U_p \simeq \mathbb{Z}/p \mathbb{Z}\) and \(U_q \simeq \mathbb{Z}/q \mathbb{Z}\). Choose generators \(a \in U_p, b \in U_q\). Then it follows from \(U_p,U_q \trianglelefteq G\), that

\begin{align*} z \coloneqq a b a^{-1} b^{-1} \in U_p \cap U_q \end{align*}

Since order of an element divides group order it follows, from \(\mathrm{ord}(z) \mid p, \mathrm{ord}(z) \mid q\) that \(\mathrm{ord}(z) = 1\) and hence \(z = 1\).

Therefore

\begin{align*} a \cdot b = b \cdot a \end{align*}

and hence \(G = \langle a, b\rangle\) is abelian, as the generators commute

Applying classification of finitely generated abelian groups and resp. general chinese remainder theorem results in

\begin{align*} G \simeq \left(\mathbb{Z}/p \mathbb{Z} \right) \otimes \left(\mathbb{Z}/q \mathbb{Z}) \simeq \mathbb{Z}/pq \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}
