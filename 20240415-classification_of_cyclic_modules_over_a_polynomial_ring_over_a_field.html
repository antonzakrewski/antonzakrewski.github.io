classification of cyclic modules over a polynomial ring over a field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K[X]\) the polynomial ring, \(V\) a vector space over \(K\), \(A \in \mathrm{End}_K(M)\) a field-endomorphism.

TFAE:

  1. \(V\) is a cyclic module as \(K[X]\) module with respect to \(A\).
  2. there exists a monic polynomial \(f \in K[X]\) such that
\begin{align*} \varphi: K[X]/(f) \rightarrow V \end{align*}

is a module-isomorphism, where \(K[X]/(f), V\) are regarded as modules over \(K[X]\) with respect to \(A\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption there exists an \(v \in V\) such that

\begin{align*} \langle v\rangle = V \end{align*}

Then consider the module-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: K[X] \rightarrow& V \\ 1 \mapsto& v \\ k \cdot X \mapsto& k \cdot A(v) \end{align*}

by Homomorphiesatz für Moduln it follows that

\begin{align*} K[X]/\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \simeq \mathrm{im}(\varphi) \end{align*}

since \(V\) is generated by \(v\) it follows that \(\varphi\) is surjective, hence

\begin{align*} \mathrm{im}(\varphi) = V \end{align*}

furthermore the kernel is a submodule over \(K[X]\) and hence by submodule as ideal also an ideal. Since \(K[X]\) is a principal ideal domain (cf. polynomial ring as PID and coefficients as field) it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) = (f) \end{align*}

for some \(f\), which is w.l.o.g. monic.

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let

\begin{align*} v = \varphi([1]) \end{align*}

Then it follows that

\begin{align*} \langle v \rangle = V \end{align*}

as \(K[X]\) module, since \(\varphi\) is surjective
