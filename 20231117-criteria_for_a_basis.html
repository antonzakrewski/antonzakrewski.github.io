criteria for a basis

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{B} \subseteq \mathcal{T}\) be a family of open sets

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{B}\) is a basis
  2. for each open set \(O \subseteq X\) and \(x \in O\), there exists an \(U_x \in \mathcal{B}\) such that
\begin{align*} x \in U_x \subseteq O \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

by assumption,

\begin{align*} O = \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i \end{align*}

for \(U_i \in \mathcal{B}\).

Hence choosing an \(U_i\) with \(x \in U_i\) suffices

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Then each open set \(O \subseteq X\) is a union of sets from the basis as

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{x \in O} U_x \end{align*}
