intersection of an ideal and subring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(U\) a subring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq R\) an ideal. Then \(U \cap \mathfrak{a}\) is an ideal of \(U\).

2. Proof

Note that both \(U, \mathfrak{a}\) are additive subgroups of \(R\), hence by Schnitt von Gruppen als Gruppe.

Suppose \(u \in U\) and \(a \in \mathfrak{a} \cap U\), then it follows, that

\begin{align*} u \cdot a \in U \end{align*}

as \(U\) is multiplicatively closed and

\begin{align*} u \cdot a \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

since \(\mathfrak{a}\) is an ideal.
