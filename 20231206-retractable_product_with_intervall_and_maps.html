retractable product with interval and maps

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(A \subseteq X\) a subspace and Let

\begin{align*} Y \coloneqq A \times [0,1] \cup X \times \{0\} \subseteq X \times [0,1] \end{align*}

be a subspace of the product. Suppose there exists a retraction

\begin{align*} R: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

Then for a map \(f: A \times [0,1] \cup X \times \{0\} \rightarrow Y\) for a topological space \(Y\), TFAE:

  1. \(f_1 \coloneqq f_{\vert A \times [0,1]}\) and \(f_2 \coloneqq f_{\vert X \times \{0\}}\) are continuous
  2. \(f\) is continuous

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of:

  • open-weak set and map
  • retractable product with interval and open sets

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

corollary of restriction of a continuous map as continuous map
