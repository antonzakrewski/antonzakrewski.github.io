maximal non finitely generated ideal as prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq A\) a maximal element of the set of non finitely generated ideals. Then \(\mathfrak{m}\) is prime

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

Suppose there exist \(a,b \in A\) such that \(a \cdot b \in \mathfrak{m}\) but \(a,b \not\in \mathfrak{m}\). Then consider the generated Ideal \((\mathfrak{a},a)\) and \(\mathfrak{m}_{a}\) as the ideal relative to \(a\). It follows that \((\mathfrak{a},a), \mathfrak{m}_a \supsetneq \mathfrak{m}\) (cf. ideal relative to a subset as superset), since \(b \in \mathfrak{m}_a\)

Therefore by maximality of \(\mathfrak{m}\) both \((\mathfrak{a},a), \mathfrak{m}_a\) are finitely generated. Furthermore by ideal relative to a subset and product it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m} = (\mathfrak{a}, a) \cdot \mathfrak{m}_a \end{align*}

where

Hence by finite product of finitely generated ideals finitely generated we conclude that \(\mathfrak{m}\) is finitely generated, contradiction
