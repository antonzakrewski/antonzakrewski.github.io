maximal non finitely generated ideal as prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq A\) a maximal element of the set of non finitely generated ideals. Then \(\mathfrak{m}\) is prime

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

2.1. construction

Suppose there exist \(a,b \in A\) such that \(a \cdot b \in \mathfrak{m}\) but \(a,b \not\in \mathfrak{m}\). Then consider the generated Ideal \((\mathfrak{m},a)\) and \(\mathfrak{m}_{a}\) as the ideal relative to \(a\). It follows that \((\mathfrak{m},a), \mathfrak{m}_a \supsetneq \mathfrak{m}\) (cf. ideal relative to a subset as superset), since \(b \in \mathfrak{m}_a\)

Therefore by maximality of \(\mathfrak{m}\) both \((\mathfrak{m},a), \mathfrak{m}_a\) are finitely generated.

We further may assume that

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{m}, a) = (a_i + r_i \cdot a) \end{align*}

for \(i \in \{1,..,n\}\) and

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m}_a = (s_j) \end{align*}

for \(j \in \{1,...,m\}\)

Then consider

\begin{align*} (a_i, s_j \cdot a) \end{align*}

2.1.1. \((a_i, s_j \cdot a) \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\)

It follows from \((a) \cdot \mathfrak{m}_a \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\) that \((a_i, s_j \cdot a) \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\) (cf. sum of ideals and order)

2.1.2. \((a_i, s_j \cdot a) \supseteq \mathfrak{m}\)

Let \(m \in \mathfrak{m}\). Then \(m \in \mathfrak{m} + (a), m \in \mathfrak{m}_a\) and thus there exist \(\alpha \in A\) such that \(\sum_{i} \alpha_{i} (a_i + r_i \cdot a) = m\)

Then after subtracting \(m - \sum_i \alpha_i a_i\) it follows that \(\sum \alpha_i r_i \cdot a \in \mathfrak{m}\), showing that \((\sum \alpha_i r_i) \in \mathfrak{m}_a\).

Therefore there exists a sum \(\beta_j s_j = (\sum \alpha_i r_i)\) and at last

\begin{align*} m = \sum_i \alpha_i (a_i + r_i \cdot a) + \sum_j \beta_j s_j \cdot a \end{align*}

showing that \(m \in (a_i, s_j \cdot a)\)

2.2. conclusion

Therefore \(\mathfrak{m}\) is finitely generated, contradiction.
