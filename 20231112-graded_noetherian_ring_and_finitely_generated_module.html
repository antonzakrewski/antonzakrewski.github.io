graded noetherian ring and finitely generated module

1. Proposition

Let \(R = \bigoplus_{n \in \mathbb{N}_0} R_n\) be a N-graded ring Then \(R_m\) is a finitely generated \(R_0\)-module

2. Proof

Since \(R_{+}\) is an ideal, it follows, that it is finitely generated by \(a_1,...,a_n\), w.l.o.g. for homogeneous elements \(a_i\)

Proof by weak induction

2.1. \(n = 1\)

is then by assumption finitely generated by suitable \(a_1,...,a_l\), as by reasons of degree, \(R_0\) is the only candidate for scalars.

2.2. \(n + 1\)

by assumption, \(R_1,...,R_n\) are finitely generated as \(R_0\)-module, thus there are only finitely many combinations \(r_1 \cdot r_n, r_2 \cdot r_{n-1}\) plus optionally finitely many \(a_j\).
