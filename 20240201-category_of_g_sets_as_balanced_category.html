category of G-Sets as balanced category

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, then the Category of G-Sets is balanced category

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(\varphi: X \rightarrow Y\) is bijective, as this follows from

  1. forgetful set functor as right adjoint and monomorphism as injection
  2. forgetful set functor as left adjoint and epimorphism as surjection
  3. forgetful functor from g-set as left and right adjoint

Hence suppose \(X,Y\) are G-Sets and

\begin{align*} \varphi: X \rightarrow& Y \\ x \mapsto& \varphi(x) \end{align*}

a G-equivariant map

Then for the set theoretic inverse map

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1}: Y \rightarrow& X \\ y \mapsto& \varphi^{-1}(y) \end{align*}

it remains to show, that it is g-equivariant:

Suppose \(g \in G\) and \(y \in Y\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} \varphi(\varphi^{-1}(g \cdot y)) =& g \cdot y \\ =& g \cdot \varphi(\varphi^{-1}(y)) \\ =& \varphi(g \cdot \varphi^{-1}(y)) \end{align*}

hence by injectivity of \(\varphi\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} g \cdot \varphi^{-1}(y) = \varphi^{-1}(g \cdot y) \end{align*}
