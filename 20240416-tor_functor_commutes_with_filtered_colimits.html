tor functor commutes with filtered colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod. Then for a module \(M\) and a filtered diagram \(N_i\), the Tor functor preserves filtered colimits, i.e.

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{i} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,M) \rightarrow \mathrm{Tor}_n^R (\mathrm{colim}_{i} N_i, M) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. delta functor

We show that \(\mathrm{colim}_i \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i, -)\) suffices as universal delta functor. For each short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

we get an exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,B) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,C) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_{n-1}^R(N_i,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_{n-1}^R(N_i,B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_{n-1}^R(N_i,C) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

As RMod is Ab5, filtered colimits are exact.

Thus we get an exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,C) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_{n-1}^R(N_i,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_{n-1}^R(N_i,B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_{n-1}^R(N_i,C) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. natural

For a morphism of short exact sequences

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A' \arrow[]{r}{} & B' \arrow[]{r}{} & C' \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

We get natural morphisms

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A') \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\iota} & \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A') \arrow[]{d}{\iota}\\ \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A') \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N_i,A') \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and hence by universal property also a unique morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{colim}\mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{colim} \mathrm{Tor}_n^R(N,A') \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
