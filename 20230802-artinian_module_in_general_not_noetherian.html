artinian module in general not noetherian

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an Artinian module. Then \(M\) is in general not a Noetherian module

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}_{p} = \{\frac{a}{p^n} \vert a \in \mathbb{Z}, n \in \mathbb{N} \} \end{align*}

be an \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module Consider

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}_p/\mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2.1. artinian

Let \(M\) be a submodule and

\begin{align*} M_{\mathrm{coeff}} \coloneqq \{k \vert \exists n \in \mathbb{N}, \frac{k}{p^n} \in M\} \subseteq \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

It is an ideal :

  1. it contains \(0\) \(\frac{0}{2^n}\)
  2. it is multiplicatively closed as \(M\) is closed under multiplication
  3. it is additively closed for \(\frac{k}{p^n} + \frac{k'}{p^{n+l}} \leadsto \frac{k}{p^n} + \frac{2^l k'}{p^{n+l}} = \frac{k + k'}{2^n}\)

Then a submodule is given by

\begin{align*} M_0 = \begin{cases} (\frac{k}{2^n}) & \mbox{if } \mathrm{sup}{n \frac{k}{2^n} \in M_0 } \\ (...,\frac{k}{2^n},\frac{k}{2^{n+1}},...) \end{cases} \end{align*}

Let $M1 \supseteq M2 …$ be a chain of submodules. Then as \(M_1 \subsetneq M_2\) we may assume that \(M_2\) is finitely generated.

Then the fact that the chain stabilizes follows from the well order of \(\mathbb{N}\)

2.2. not noetherian

Let \(M_i \coloneqq \{\frac{a}{p^{n}} + \mathbb{Z} \vert n \leq i\}\) and \(S \coloneqq \{M_i \vert i \in \mathbb{N}\}\). Then there doesn't exist a maximal module, hence \(\mathbb{Z}_{p}\) is not noetherian (see: noetherian module and maximal module)
