nerve commutes with join

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, then the nerve commutes with the join of a simplicial set resp. the join of a category

\begin{align*} N(\mathcal{C} \star \mathcal{D}) \simeq N(\mathcal{C}) \star N(\mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

using natural isomorphism and isomorphism on the objects we construct a map

\begin{align*} N(\mathcal{C} \star \mathcal{D}))_m \rightarrow (N(\mathcal{C}) \star N(\mathcal{D}))_m \end{align*}

for each \(m \in \mathbb{N}\)

As an element \(\mathcal{F} \in N(\mathcal{C} \star \mathcal{D})\) is given by a functor \(\mathcal{F}: [m] \rightarrow \mathcal{C} \star \mathcal{D}\) Note that by construction of the join of categories this functor determines a cut of \([m]\) by

\begin{align*} \{1,...,l \vert \mathcal{F}(1),...,\mathcal{F}(l) \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C}) \} \\ \{l+1,...,m \vert \mathcal{F}(1),...,\mathcal{F}(l) \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D}) \} \end{align*}

Then we map

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F} \mapsto (\mathcal{F}_{\vert [1,...,l]} \times \mathcal{F}_{\vert [l+1,...,m]}) \in N(\mathcal{C})_l \times N(\mathcal{D})_{m-l} \subseteq \coprod_{(J_1,J_2) \in \mathrm{Cut}([n])} N(\mathcal{C})_{J_i} \times N(\mathcal{D})_{J_2} \end{align*}

Furthermore we can write an inverse map

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}_{J_1} \times \mathcal{F}_{J_2} \rightarrow \mathcal{F}: \begin{cases} a \mapsto& \mathcal{F}_{J_1}(a) & \mbox{if } a \in J_1 \\ b \mapsto& \mathcal{F}_{J_2}(b) & \mbox{if } b \in J_2 \\ (a \rightarrow b) \mapsto& \mathcal{F}_{J_i}(a \rightarrow b) & \mbox{if } a,b \in J_i \\ (a \rightarrow b) \mapsto& 1 & \mbox{if } a \in J_1, b \in J_2 \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}
