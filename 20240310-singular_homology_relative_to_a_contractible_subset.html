singular homology relative to a contractible subspace

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(A \subseteq X\) a contractible subspace. Then it holds for \(n \in \{2,3,...\}\) and the singular homology, that

\begin{align*} H_n(X) \simeq H_n(X, A) \end{align*}

are canonically isomorphic

2. Proof

consider the map

\begin{align*} \iota: A \rightarrow X \end{align*}

then by exactification of a homomorphism, we get a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(\iota) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_*(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_*(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{coker}(\iota) & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

By monomorphism on singular chains and injective map and relative singular homology this reduces to

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & C_*(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_*(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{*}(X,A) & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

by short exact sequence of chain complexes induces long exact sequence we get an exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(A) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Since \(A\) is contractible and \(n - 1 \geq 1\), we conclude by singular homology as homotopy invariant functor and singular homology of the point, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

thus the map \(H_n(X) \rightarrow H_n(X,A)\) is an isomorphism (cf. sandwhiched by zero object and isomorphism)
