hom out of bimodules bifunctor

1. Proposition

Let (R,S) be rings and \(\mathrm{R-S-Mod}\) the category of bimodules. Then there exists the hom functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}(-,-): (\mathrm{R-S-Mod})^{\mathrm{op}} \times \mathrm{RMod} \rightarrow \mathrm{ModS} \\ \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{ModS}\) is the category of right \(S\)-modules

2. Proof

2.1. object

\begin{align*} s \cdot f = f(- \cdot s) \end{align*}
  • hom-set out of a bimodule with module structure

2.2. morphism

note that by similar arguments to set of module homomorphisms as abelian group and Category Rmod as abelian category there exists a functor to Category Ab. It remains to show that the abelian group and group-homomorphisms given by precomposition and postcomposition carry a natural module structure.

Let \(N,N' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{R-S-Mod})\) and \(M,M' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{RMod})\) and \(s \in S\)

2.2.1. \(\mathrm{R-S-Mod}^{\mathrm{op}}\)

Let \(\varphi: N' \rightarrow N\) be a bimodule homomorphism. Then

\begin{align*} \varphi^{ \rightarrow}(f \cdot s) =& \varphi^{ \rightarrow}(n \mapsto f(n \cdot s)) \\ =& n' \mapsto f(\varphi(n') \cdot s) \\ =& n' \mapsto f(\varphi(n' \cdot s)) \\ =& (f \circ \varphi) \cdot s =& \varphi^{ \rightarrow}(f) \cdot s \end{align*}

2.2.2. \(\mathrm{R-Mod}\)

Let \(\varphi: M \rightarrow M'\) be a module homomorphism. Then

\begin{align*} \varphi^{ \leftarrow}(f \cdot s) =& \varphi^{ \leftarrow}(n \mapsto f(n \cdot s)) \\ =& n \mapsto \varphi(f(n \cdot s)) \\ =& (\varphi \circ f) \cdot s =& \varphi^{ \leftarrow}(f) \cdot s \end{align*}
