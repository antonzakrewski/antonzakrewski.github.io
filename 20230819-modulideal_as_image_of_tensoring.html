modulideal as image of tensoring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M\) an \(R\)-module and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq R\) an ideal. Then the Modulideal \(\mathfrak{a}M\) is isomorphic to the tensor product

\begin{align*} M \otimes_R \mathfrak{a} \cong \mathfrak{a}M \end{align*}

2. Proof

let

\begin{align*} \varphi: M \otimes_R \mathfrak{a} \rightarrow \mathfrak{a}M \\ m \otimes_R a \mapsto& a \cdot m \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

Suppose \(m_1 \otimes_R a_1 = m_2 \otimes a_2\), then we conclude

\begin{align*} 0 =& m_1 \otimes_R a_1 \cdot 1 - m_2 \otimes a_2 \cdot 1 \\ =& a_1m_1 \otimes 1 - a_2 m_2 \otimes 1 \\ =& a_1 m_1 - a_2 m_2 \otimes_R 1 \\ =& 0 \otimes_R 1 \end{align*}

or \(a_1 m_1 - a_2 m_2 = 0\)

2.2. monomorphism

Let \(\sum m_i \otimes_R a_i \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\), then we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \sum \left( m_i \otimes_R a_i \right) =& \sum \left(a_i m_i \otimes_R 1 \right) \\ =& \left(\sum a_i m_i\right) \otimes_R 1 \\ =& \left(0 \otimes_R 1\right) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
