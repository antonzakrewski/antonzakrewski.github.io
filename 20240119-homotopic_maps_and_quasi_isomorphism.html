chain homotopy equivalence and quasi-isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(M_n,M_n' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A}))\) are chain complexes Suppose \(f\) is a chain homotopy equivalence, then \(f\) is also a quasi-isomorphism

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists a homotopy inverse \(g: M_n' \rightarrow M_n\). Furthermore, since the Homology functor is invariant under chain homotopy, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{H_k(M_n)} =& H_k(\mathrm{id}_{M_n} ) \\ =& H_k(g \circ f) \\ =& H_k(g) \circ H_k(f) \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{H_k(M_n')} =& H_k(\mathrm{id}_{M_n'} ) \\ =& H_k(f \circ g) \\ =& H_k(f) \circ H_k(g) \end{align*}
