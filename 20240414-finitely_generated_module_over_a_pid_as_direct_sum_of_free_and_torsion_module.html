finitely generated module over a PID as direct sum of free and torsion module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain and \(M\) a finitely generated module. Then

\begin{align*} M \simeq R^k \oplus T(M) \end{align*}

for the torsion submodule and a free module \(R^k\) for some \(k \in \mathbb{N}\)

2. Proof

consider the short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & T(M) \arrow[]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & M/T(M) \arrow[]{r}{} & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

By module modulo torsion module as torsionfree module and finitely generated, torsion free module over a PID as free module we get

\begin{align*} M/T(M) \simeq R^k \end{align*}

for some \(k \leq n\)

by splitting lemma for modules we conclude, that

\begin{align*} M \simeq T(M) \oplus R^k \end{align*}
