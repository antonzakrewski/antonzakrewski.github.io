left slice functor for a simplicial set

1. Definition

Let \(S\) be a simplicial set, \(\mathrm{sSet}\) the category sSet and \(S / \mathrm{sSet}\) the coslice category. Then the left slice functor is defined as functor

\begin{align*} -_{/-}: S / \mathrm{sSet} \rightarrow& \mathrm{sSet} \\ (p: S \rightarrow X) \mapsto& X_{/p} \\ (f: (p: S \rightarrow X) \mapsto (q: S \rightarrow Y)) \mapsto& f_{/-} \end{align*}

where

  1. \(X_{/p}\) is the right simplicial set of a morphism
  2. \(f_{/-}\) is the morphism of simplicial sets given by postcomposition
\begin{align*} f: X_{/p} \rightarrow& Y_{/p} \\ n \mapsto& (f^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{sSet}}(\Delta^n \star S, X) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{sSet}}(\Delta^n \star S, Y)) \end{align*}

Note that naturality follows from bifunctor and natural transformation
