precomposition commutes with postcomposition

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(A,B,C,D \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) be objects and

\begin{align*} f: A \rightarrow& B \\ h: B \rightarrow& C g: C \rightarrow& D \end{align*}

be morphisms then precomposition \(f^{ \rightarrow}\) commutes with postcomposition \(g^{ \leftarrow}\), i.e.

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow }(g^{ \leftarrow} h) =& g^{ \leftarrow}(f^{ \rightarrow} h) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Applying definitions results in

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow }(g^{ \leftarrow} h) =& f^{ \rightarrow} (g \circ h) \\ =& (g \circ h \circ f) \\ g^{ \leftarrow}(f^{ \rightarrow} h) =& g^{ \leftarrow} (h \circ f) \\ =& (g \circ h \circ f) \end{align*}
