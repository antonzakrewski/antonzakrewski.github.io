restricting subbasis for the compact open topology

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces, where \(X\) is Hausdorff, \(\mathrm{Map}(X,Y)\) the mapping space with the compact open topology and \(\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathcal{T}_Y\) a Subbasis.

Then

\begin{align*} \{\mathcal{O}_{K,U} \vert K \subseteq X \text{ compact}, U \in \mathcal{S} \} \end{align*}

is a subbasis on \(\mathrm{Map}(X,Y)\)

2. Proof

Let \(U \subesetq Y\) open, \(k \subseteq X\) copmact and \(f \in \mathcal{O}_{K,U}\).

Since \(\mathcal{S}\) is a subbasis, there exist \(A_{i,j} \in \mathcal{S}\) such that

\begin{align*} U =& \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i \\ =& \bigcap_{i \in I} \bigcap_{j=1}^m A_{i,j} \end{align*}

Then \(K \subseteq f^{-1}[U]\) or because of unions commute with preimages

\begin{align*} K \subseteq \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i f^{-1}[U_i] \end{align*}

For \(x \in K\), pick \(i(x) \in I\) such that \(x \in f^{-1}[U_{i(x)}]\). Because \(K\) as topological space is hausdorff and compact (cf. inheritance of hausdorffness), it is locally compact Hausdorff (cf. compact hausdorff space as locally compact hausdorff space) Hence for \(x \in K \cap f^{-1}[U_i]\), there exists an open neighbourhood \(V_x \subseteq f^{-1}[U_i]\) with compact closure \(K_x\). Since \(K\) is compact and \(\bigcup_{x \in K} V_x\) is an open cover, there exists a finite subcover \(V_{x_1},...,V_{x_n}\). Then it follows, that

\begin{align*} f \in \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathcal{O}_{K_{x_i}, U_{x_i}} \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{K, U} \end{align*}

where \(U_{x_i} \in (U_i)\).

thus by intersection of compact open subbasis sets for different open sets, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathcal{O}_{K_{x_i}, U_{x_i}} =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \bigcap_{j = 1}^m \mathcal{O}_{K_{x_i},A_{i,j}} \end{align*}

and hence for \(C \subseteq X\) compact and \(S \in \mathcal{S}\), \(\mathcal{O}_{C,S}\) forms a subbasis (cf. criteria for a subbasis)
