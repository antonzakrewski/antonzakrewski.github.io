restricting subbasis for the compact open topology

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces, \(\mathrm{Map}(X,Y)\) the mapping space with the compact open topology and \(\mathcal{S} \subseteq Y\) a Subbasis. Then

\begin{align*} \{\mathcal{O}_{K,U} \vert K \subseteq X \text{ compact}, U \in \mathcal{S} \} \end{align*}

is a subbasis on \(\mathrm{Map}(X,Y)\)

2. Proof

Let \(U \subesetq Y\) open, \(k \subseteq X\) copmact and \(f \in \mathcal{O}_{K,U}\)

Write \(U = \bigcap_{i \in I} U_i\) where \(U_i = \bigcap_{j =1}^n A_{i,j}\) for \(A_{i,j} \in \mathcal{S}\).

Then

\begin{align*} K \subseteq \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i f^{-1}[U_i] \end{align*}

Pick \(i(x) \in I\) such that \(x \in f^{-1}[U_{i(x)}]\) and \(x \in V_x \subseteq K_x \subseteq f^{-1}[U_{i(x)}]\) with \(V_x\) open in \(K \cap f^{-1}[U_{i(x)}]\) and \(K_x \subseteq K \cap f^{-1}[U_{i(x)}]\) compact. This can be done, because \(K\) is compact Hausdorff, since \(X\) is hausdorff and hence locally compact.

Since \(K\) is compact, there exist \(x_1,...,x_n\) such that

\begin{align*} K \subseteq \bigcup_{k=1}^n V_{x_k} \end{align*}

Therefore,

\begin{align*} f \in \mathcal{O}_{K_{x_k}, U_{i(x_k)}} \end{align*}

We recall, that

\begin{align*} U_{i(x_k)} =& \bigcap_{j=1}^{m_k} A_{i,j} \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} f \in \bigcap_{k=1}^n \bigcap{j=1}^{m_k} \mathcal{O}_{K_{x_k}, A_{k_i}} =& \mathcal{O}_{K_{x_k}, U_{i(x_k)}} \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{K,U} \end{align*}
