bijection between pushout / pullback diagrams for adjoints

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_1,\mathcal{L}_2: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow& \mathcal{D} \\ \mathcal{R}_1,\mathcal{R}_2: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow& \mathcal{C} \end{align*}

be adjoint functors with \(\mathcal{L}_i \dashv \mathcal{R}_i\)

Let

\begin{align*} \gamma: S \rightarrow& T \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{C}) \\ \partial: X \rightarrow Y \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

be morphisms

Then there exists a Bijection between

  1. a natural transformation \(\eta_{\mathcal{R}}s \mathcal{R}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{R}_2\) together with a diagram
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\lrcorner}]| S \arrow[]{d}{\gamma} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{R}_1(X) \arrow[]{rd}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}(X)}\arrow[]{d}{}& \\ T \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathrm{Pull} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& \mathcal{R}_2(X) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{R}_2(\partial)}\\ & \mathcal{R}_1(Y) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}}& \mathcal{R}_2(Y) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

in \(\mathcal{C}\)

  1. a natural transformation \(\eta_{\mathcal{L}}: \mathcal{L}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_1\) together with a digaram
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \mathcal{L}_2(S) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{L}(\gamma)} & \mathcal{L}_1(S) \arrow[]{d}{}& \\ \mathcal{L}_2(T) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Push} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & X \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ & \mathcal{L}_1(T) \arrow[]{r}{} & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

in \(\mathcal{D}\)

2. Proof

using equivalence of subcategories of left and right adjoints we get a bijection between \(\eta_{\mathcal{L}}\) and \(\eta_{\mathcal{R}}\)

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(\eta_{\mathcal{L}}\) be the natural transformation. Then we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_2(S) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}(S)} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}(\gamma)} & \mathcal{L}_1(S) \arrow[]{dd}{\mathcal{L}(\gamma)} & \\ \mathcal{L}_2(T) \arrow[]{rd}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}(T)} & & X \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ & \mathcal{L}_1(T) & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

for free

Taking the pushout and applying universal property yields

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \mathcal{L}_2(S) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}(S)} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}(\gamma)} & \mathcal{L}_1(S) \arrow[]{d}{} & \\ \mathcal{L}_2(T) \arrow[]{rd}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}(T)} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Push} \arrow[]{d}{} & X \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ & \mathcal{L}_1(T) & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Using the adjunction for the dashed arrows

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} S \arrow[dashed]{rrd}{} \arrow[dashed]{rrdd}{} \arrow[dashed]{rdd}{} \arrow[]{d}{\gamma} \arrow[dashed]{r}{} & \mathcal{R}_1(X) \arrow[]{rd}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}(X)} & \\ T \arrow[dashed]{rr}{} \arrow[dashed]{rd}{} \arrow[dashed]{rrd}{}& & \mathcal{R}_2(X) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{R}_2(\partial)}\\ & \mathcal{R}_1(Y) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}}& \mathcal{R}_2(Y) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

results in

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \mathcal{L}_2(S) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{L}}} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{L}(\gamma)} & \mathcal{L}_1(S) \arrow[dashed]{ddr}{} \arrow[]{d}{}\arrow[dashed]{rd}{}& \\ \mathcal{L}_2(T) \arrow[bend left, dashed]{rr}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Push} & X \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ & \mathcal{L}_1(T) \arrow[dashed]{r}{} & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

analogously
