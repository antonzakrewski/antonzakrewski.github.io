solvable group and quotient group

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(N\) a normal subgroup. TFAE:

  1. \(G\) is solvable
  2. \(G/N, N\) are solvable

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.1.1. a)

corollary of: subgroup of a solvable group as solvable group

2.1.2. b)

corollary of:

  • image of a solvable group as solvable group

for

\begin{align*} \pi: G \rightarrow G/N \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption, there exist composition series

\begin{align*} 1 =& H_1 \trianglelefteq ... \trianglelefteq H_n = G/N \\ 1 =& K_1 \trianglelefteq ... \trianglelefteq K_m= N \end{align*}

By correspondence theorem for subgroups and quotient rings, each \(H_i\) is of the form \(H_i'/N\) and by the correspondence theorem for normal subgroups \(H_i'\) is normal if and only if \(H_i'/N\) is normal.

Note that by first isomorphism theorem for groups, \(H_i'/H_{i+1}' \cong (H_i' / N)/(H_{i+1}'/N) = H_i/H_{i+1}\), where by assumption \(H_i/H_{i+1}\) is abelian.

Thus we get

\begin{align*} 1 \trianglelefteq K_1 ... \trianglelefteq K_m = N \trianglelefteq H_{1}' ... \trianglelefteq H_{n}' = G \end{align*}
