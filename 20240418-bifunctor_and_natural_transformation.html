bifunctor and natural transformation

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \times \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{E} \end{align*}

a bifunctor. Then for \(c_1,c_2 \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C}_1)\) a morphism \(f: c_1 \rightarrow c_2\) determines a natural transformation

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(c_1,-) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}(c_2,-) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(g: d_1 \rightarrow d_2 \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{D})\). Then consider the following commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} (c_1,d_1) \arrow[]{d}{(f,\mathrm{id}) } \arrow[]{r}{(\mathrm{id}, g)} & (c_1,d_2) \arrow[]{d}{(f,\mathrm{id}) } \\ (c_2,d_1) \arrow[]{r}{(\mathrm{id}, g)} & (c_2,d_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then applying \(\mathcal{F}(-,-)\) results in Then consider the following commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(c_1,d_1) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}(f,\mathrm{id}) } \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}, g)} & \mathcal{F}(c_1,d_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}(f,\mathrm{id}) } \\ \mathcal{F}(c_2,d_1) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}, g)} & \mathcal{F}(c_2,d_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which shows naturality
