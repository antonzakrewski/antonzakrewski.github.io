chopping theorem for limits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(I: \mathcal{I} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be a diagram and \(A \in \mathcal{J}\). Suppose \(J: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) is a full subdiagram containing \(A\) and all objects \(B\) without a morphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrow B \end{align*}

Suppose furthermore, that the subdiagram preserves connected components, i.e. for connected compontent

TFAE:

  1. there exists a limit \(\lim_{I}\)
  2. there exists a colimit \(\lim_J\)
  3. there exist \(\lim_I, \lim_J\) and there exists a unique isomorphism \(\mathrm{colim}_I \cong \mathrm{colim}_J\)

2. Proof

dual to chopping theorem for colimits
