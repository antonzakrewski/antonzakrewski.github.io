radical of a primary ideal as prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p}\) a primary ideal. Then the radical \(\sqrt{\mathfrak{p}}\) is a prime ideal

2. Proof

2.1. a)

follows from:

  • primary ideal and zero divisor as nilpotent element
  • prime ideal and quotient ring as integral domain

2.2. b)

Let \(xy \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{p}}\), then \((xy)^m \in \mathfrak{p}\) for some \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) This shows that one of \((x^m), (y)^{m \cdot n} \in \mathfrak{p}\) as \(\mathfrak{p}\) is primary. Then \(x \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{p}}\) resp. \(y \in \mathfrak{p}\)
