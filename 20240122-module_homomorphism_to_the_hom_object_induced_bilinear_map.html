module homomorphism to the hom object induced bilinear map for a commutative ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(M,N,L\) \(R\)-modules and \(f: M \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(N,L)\) the set of module homomorphisms over a commutative ring as module. Then the map

\begin{align*} g: M \times N \rightarrow& L \\ (m,n) \mapsto& (f(m))(n) \end{align*}

is bilinear

2. Proof

Note that for \(m \in M\), by definition

\begin{align*} g(m)(-) \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(N,L) \end{align*}

is a module homomorphism

Hence it remains to show for \(n \in N\): Let \(m_1,m_2 \in M\) and \(r \in R\)

\begin{align*} g((m_1 + r \cdot m_2),n) =& (f(m_1 + r \cdot m_2))(n) \\ =& (f(m_1) + r \cdot f(m_2))(n) \\ =& f(m_1)(n) + r \cdot f(m_2)(n) \\ =& g(m_1,n) + r \cdot g(m_2,n) \end{align*}
