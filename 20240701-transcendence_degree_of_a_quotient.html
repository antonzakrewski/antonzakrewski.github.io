transcendence degree of a quotient algebra

1. Proposition

Let \(k\) be a field and \(A\) a k-Algebra and \(B \subseteq A\) a K-subalgebra. Then the transcendence degree of the quotient algebra is smaller, i.e.

\begin{align*} \mathrm{tr}(A/B) \leq \mathrm{tr}(A) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Given a subset \(T \subseteq A/B\), one may choose a set of representatives \(T'\) in \(A\). If \(T'\) is algebraically dependent, then it \(T\) is especially algebraically dependent (for the polynomial of \(T'\))
