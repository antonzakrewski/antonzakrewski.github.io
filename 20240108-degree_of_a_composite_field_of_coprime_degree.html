degree of a composite field of coprime degree

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L,E\) finite field extensions (as subfields of a common field extension) with coprime degree. Then for the composite field \(LE\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} [LE : K] = [L : K] \cdot [E : K] \end{align*}

2. Proof

By Gradsatz, it holds, that

\begin{align*} [L E : K ] =& [LE : E] \cdot [E : K] \\ [L E : K ] =& [LE : L] \cdot [L : K] \\ \end{align*}

showing that

\begin{align*} [E : K], [L : K] \mid [LE : K] \end{align*}

furthermore, since \([LE : K] \neq 0\), we conclude, that

\begin{align*} [LE : K] \geq [L : K] \cdot [E : K] \end{align*}

by degree of composite field extensions, we get the other inequality
