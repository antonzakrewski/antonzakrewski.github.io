homomorphism between noetherian modules and localization at the generic point and iso on dense set

Proposition

Let \(A\) be a Integral domain and \((0) \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) be the generic point.
Suppose \(M,N\) are Noetherian modules and \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) a module-homomorphism such that

\begin{align*} \varphi_{(0)}: M_{(0)} \rightarrow N_{(0)} \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

Then there exists an open dense subset \(U \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) with

\begin{align*} M_{\mathfrak{p}} = 0 \end{align*}

for all \(\mathfrak{p} \in U\)

Proof

Consider the kernel \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\) and cokernel \(\mathrm{coker}(\varphi)\).
Both are finitely generated modules (cf. submodule of a noetherian module as noetherian module, quotient of a noetherian module as noetherian module).

Furthermore localization is exact, hence commutes with everything.

Hence by finitely generated module with localization at zero and dense open subset we may find open, dense subsets of the form \(D(\alpha), D(\beta)\) with \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi)_{\alpha} = \mathrm{coker}(\varphi)_{\beta} = 0\) for the localization along an element and \(\alpha, \beta \neq 0\).
Especially \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi)_{\mathfrak{p}} = 0\) for \(\mathfrak{p} \in D(\alpha)\) resp. \(\mathrm{coker}(\varphi)_{\mathfrak{q}} = 0\) for \(\mathfrak{q} \in D(\beta)\).

Then consider \(D(\alpha \cdot \beta) \subseteq D(\alpha)\).
It is open and furthermore \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi)_{\mathfrak{p}} = \mathrm{coker}(\varphi)_{\mathfrak{p}} = 0\) for all \(\mathfrak{p} \in D(\alpha \cdot \beta) \subseteq D(\alpha) \cap D(\beta)\).d

Note that \(\alpha \cdot \beta \neq 0\), hence \((0) \in D(\alpha \cdot \beta)\) as \(\alpha \cdot \beta \not\in (0) = \{0\}\).
Thus we may conclude that \(D(\alpha \cdot \beta)\) is dense, since it contains the generic point (cf. Closure of a Subset )
