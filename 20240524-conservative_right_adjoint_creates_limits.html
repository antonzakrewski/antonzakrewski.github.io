conservative right adjoint creates limits for a sufficiently complete domain

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a conservative left adjoint, where \(\mathcal{C}\) admits (enough) limits of a shape \(J\) Then \(\mathcal{F}\) creates limits of the shape \(J\)

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • conservative functor reflects preserved limits
  • right adjoint preserves limits
