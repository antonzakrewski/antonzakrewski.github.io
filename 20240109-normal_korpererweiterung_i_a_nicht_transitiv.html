normal körpererweiterung i.A. nicht transitiv

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) a normal field extension and \(E\) an subfield. Then \(E/K\) is in general not normal

2. Proof

Let \(L = \overline{K}\) be an algebraic closure. Then \(L/K\) is a normal field extension.

Then choose a field \(E\), which is not a splitting field for \(K\). E.g. \(\mathbb{Q}( \mathrm{e}^{\frac{2}{3}\pi \mathrm{i}} \sqrt[3]{2}\), where the minimal polynomial is

\begin{align*} X^3 - 2 \end{align*}

but \(\mathrm{e}^{ \frac{4}{3} \pi \mathrm{i}} \sqrt[3]{2} \not\in E\)
