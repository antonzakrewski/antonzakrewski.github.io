natural isomorphism between diagrams induce isomorphism between colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(J: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) and \(J': \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be diagrams and

\begin{align*} \eta: J \rightarrow J' \end{align*}

a natural isomorphism.

Then if the colimit \(\mathrm{colim}_{J}\) exists, then also \(\mathrm{colim}_{J'}\) exists and furthermore

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{J} \simeq \mathrm{colim}_{J'} \end{align*}

are uniquely isomorphic

2. Proof

2.1. a) special case

corollary of

  1. isomorphisms under functors
  2. restriction of a functor
  3. derived colim functor , to those diagrams that admit a colimit

2.2. b)

Suppose we have an object \(A \in \mathcal{C}\) such that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_{j_1'} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & A_{j_2'} \arrow[]{ld}{}\\ & A & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

By natural isomorphism and isomorphism on the objects, we get natural isos

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_{j_1} \arrow[swap]{dd}{\simeq} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & A_{j_2} \arrow[]{dd}{\simeq}\\ & & \\ A_{j_1'} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & A_{j_2'} \arrow[]{ld}{}\\ & A & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and hence by universal property a morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_{j_1} \arrow[]{rd}{}\arrow[swap]{dd}{\simeq} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & \arrow[]{ld}{} A_{j_2} \arrow[]{dd}{\simeq}\\ & \mathrm{colim}_J \arrow[dashed]{dd}{\exists! }& \\ A_{j_1'} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & A_{j_2'} \arrow[]{ld}{}\\ & A & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and furthermore morphisms to \(A_{j_1'}\) defined by the factorization through to \(A_{j_1}\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_{j_1} \arrow[]{rd}{}\arrow[swap]{dd}{\simeq} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & \arrow[]{ld}{} A_{j_2} \arrow[]{dd}{\simeq}\\ & \mathrm{colim}_J & \\ A_{j_1'} \arrow[]{ru}{} \arrow[]{rr}{} & & A_{j_2'} \arrow[]{lu}{}\\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.3. uniqueness

Suppose \(h,h'\) are making the diagram commute

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & \mathrm{colim}_J \arrow[shift right =1, swap, every label/.style={xshift=-1ex, yshift=2ex}]{dd}{h} \arrow[shift left=1, every label/.style={xshift=+1ex, yshift=2ex}]{dd}{h'}& \\ \arrow[dashed]{rr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{ru}{} A_{j_1'} & & A_{j_2'} \arrow[]{ld}{} \arrow[]{lu}{} \\ & A & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

then also

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_{j_1} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[swap]{dd}{\simeq} \arrow[]{rr}{}& & A_{j_2} \arrow[]{ld}{} \arrow[]{dd}{\simeq} \\ & \mathrm{colim}_J \arrow[shift right =1, swap, every label/.style={xshift=-1ex, yshift=2ex}]{dd}{h} \arrow[shift left=1, every label/.style={xshift=+1ex, yshift=2ex}]{dd}{h'}& \\ \arrow[dashed]{rr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{ru}{} A_{j_1'} & & A_{j_2'} \arrow[]{ld}{} \arrow[]{lu}{} \\ & A & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

and hence especially also

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \arrow[bend right]{rdd}{}A_{j_1} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{}& & A_{j_2} \arrow[]{ld}{} \arrow[bend left]{ddl}{}\\ & \mathrm{colim}_J \arrow[shift right=1, swap,]{d}{h} \arrow[shift left=1,]{d}{h'}& \\ & A & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Hence by uniqueness of the mediating morphism, it follows, that

\begin{align*} h = h' \end{align*}
