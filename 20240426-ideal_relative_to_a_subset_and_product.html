ideal relative to a subset and product

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) an ideal and \(S \subseteq A\) a subset. Then for the ideal relative to a subset \(\mathfrak{a}_S\) and the generated Ideal \(S\) it follows for the product that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a}_S \cdot (\mathfrak{a},S) \subseteq \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

where \(\mathfrak{a}_S\) is the maximal ideal with this property

2. Proof

2.1. \(\subseteq\)

Let \(\alpha \in \mathfrak{a}_s\) and \(\beta \in (\mathfrak{a}, S)\) then w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(\beta \in S\), as otherwise \(\alpha \cdot \beta \in \mathfrak{a}\) by the multiplicative closure of \(\mathfrak{a}\). But it follows from the definition of \(\mathfrak{a}_S\) that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \beta \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2.2. maximality

follows from the definition
