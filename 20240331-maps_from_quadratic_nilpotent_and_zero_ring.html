maps from quadratic nilpotent and zero ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(D = \{x \vert x^2 = 0\} \subseteq R\) be the subset of quadratic nilpotent elements. Suppose for each set theoretic map

\begin{align*} f: D \rightarrow R \end{align*}

there exists a unique \(b \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} f(d) = f(0) + d \cdot b \end{align*}

Then assuming classical logic, it holds that

\begin{align*} R = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} f: d \mapsto& \begin{cases} 0 & \mbox{if } d = 0 \\ 1 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

be the set-theoretic map.

Then we get for \(d \neq 0\)

\begin{align*} 1 =& 1^2 \\ =& (f(d))^{2} \\ =& (f(0) + d \cdot b)^2 \\ =& (0 + d \cdot b)^2 \\ =& (d^2 \cdot b^2) \end{align*}

where \(d\) is nilpotent, hence contradiction to unit is not a zero divisor, nilpotent element as zero divisor
