Kucera Theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. Then \(\mathcal{C}\) is the quotient category of a concrete category

assumig NBG and axiom of global choice

2. Proof

Choose a dense well-order \(\leq\) on \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) We define \(\mathcal{L}\) as category containing

  1. \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) as objects
  2. for objects \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{L})\) the hom-set \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{L}}(A,B)\) consists of pairs of morphisms \((f_1,f_2)\) where \(f_1: A \rightarrow H, f_2: H \rightarrow B\) for \(H \leq A, H \leq B\).

The composition of \((f_1,f_2): A \rightarrow B, (g_1,g_2): B \rightarrow C\) for \(g_1: B \rightarrow K\) and \(f_1: A \rightarrow H\) is defined as

\begin{align*} (g_1,g_2) \circ (f_1,f_2) =& \begin{cases} (f_1,g_2 \circ g_1 \circ f_2 ) & \mbox{if } H < K\\ (g_1 \circ f_2 \circ f_1, g_2) & \mbox{if } H \geq K \\ \end{cases} \end{align*} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{drr}{} \arrow[]{r}{f_1} & H \arrow[]{r}{f_2} & B \arrow[]{d}{g_1}\\ & & K \arrow[]{d}{g_2}\\ & & C \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{f_1} & H \arrow[]{ddr}{} \arrow[]{r}{f_2} & B \arrow[]{d}{g_1}\\ & & K \arrow[]{d}{g_2}\\ & & C \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then the identity is given by \(\mathrm{id}_{A} = (\mathrm{id}_{A} , \mathrm{id}_{A})\):

It follows, that for \((f_1,f_2): A \rightarrow B\) with \(f_1: A \rightarrow K\), as \(K \leq A\), that

2.2. relation

By the first isomorphism theorem for categories it suffices to give a functor from \(\mathcal{L}\) to \(\mathcal{C}\)

Here consider

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \mathcal{L} \rightarrow& \mathcal{C} \\ A \mapsto& A \\ (f_1,f_2) \mapsto& \mathcal{F}(f_2 \circ f_1) \end{align*}

Then \(\mathcal{F}\) respects the identity and composition.
