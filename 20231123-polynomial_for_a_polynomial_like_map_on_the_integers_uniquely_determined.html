polynomial for a polynomial-like map on the integers uniquely determined

1. Proposition

Let \(f: \mathbb{Q} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}\) be a polynomial-like map on \(\mathbb{N}\). Then the polynomial \(g \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) is uniquely determined.

2. Proof

suppose there exist two polynomials \(g,g' \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) such that \(f(n) = g(n), f(m) = g'(m)\) for almost all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) (resp. \(m \in \mathbb{N}\)). Then for almost all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), it holds, that

\begin{align*} g(n) = g'(n) \end{align*}

Hence \(g - g'\) has infinite roots and thus by degree and maximal roots, \(g - g' = 0\) is the zero polynomial.
