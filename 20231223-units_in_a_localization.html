units in a localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed set. Then for the localization \(S^{-1}A\) and \(\frac{\alpha}{s} \in S\).

TFAE:

  1. \(\frac{\alpha}{s} \in (S^{-1}A)^{\times}\) is a unit
  2. \(\frac{\alpha}{1} \in (S^{-1}A)^{\times}\) is a unit
  3. \(\alpha \mid s\) for some \(s \in S\)
  4. \((\alpha) \cap S \neq \emptyset\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} \cdot \left( \frac{1}{s} \cdot \left(\frac{\alpha}{s}\right)^{-1} \right) = 1 \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

By assumption, there exists an inverse

\begin{align*} \frac{\beta}{s} = \left(\frac{\alpha}{1}\right)^{-1} \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} \cdot \frac{\beta}{s} =& 1 \\ s_1 \cdot \alpha \cdot \beta =& s_2 \cdot s \\ \end{align*}

for some \(s_1,s_2 \in S\)

Therefore

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot (s_1 \cdot \beta) = s_2 \cdot s \in S \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \alpha \mid s_2 \cdot s \end{align*}

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 4)

bv assumption, there exists an \(r \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} r \cdot \alpha = s \end{align*}

Furthermore

\begin{align*} r \cdot \alpha \in (\alpha) \end{align*}

hence the intersection is nonempty

2.4. 4) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption, there exists an \(r \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} r \cdot \alpha = s' \end{align*}

therefore

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{s} \cdot \frac{r \cdot s}{s'} =& \frac{\alpha \cdot r \cdot s}{s \cdot s'} \\ =& \frac{s \cdot s'}{s \cdot s'} \\ =& \frac{1}{1} \end{align*}
