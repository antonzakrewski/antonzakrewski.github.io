connected codomain of a covering map and domain in general not connected

1. Proposition

Let \((B,E)\) be topological spaces and \(p: E \rightarrow B\) be a covering map.

Suppose \(B\) is connected, then \(E\) is in general not connected

2. Proof

Let \(B = \{*\}\) and \(F\) be a set with \(\vert F \vert \geq 2\). Then consider the trivial covering map

\begin{align*} p: F \times B \rightarrow B \end{align*}

Here \(F \times B \cong F^{\delta}\) is not connected
