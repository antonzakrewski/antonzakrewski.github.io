nonzero homological degree and surjectivity on the sphere

1. Proposition

Let \(f: S^n \rightarrow S^n\) be a continuous map on the n-sphere, such that the degree is nonzero

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(f) \neq 0 \end{align*}

Then \(f\) is surjective

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(f\) is not surjective, then there exists an \(s \in S^n\) with \(s \not\in \mathrm{im}(f)\). Therefore \(f\) naturally restricts to

\begin{align*} f: S^n \rightarrow (S^n \setminus s) \simeq \mathbb{R}^{n-1} \end{align*}

see: continuous map and restriction to the image, euclidean space without a point homotopy equivalent to a sphere

Here the euclidean space is contractible space, therefore \(f\) is nullhomotopic. This homotopy can be extendend to \(S^n\) Thus it follows by singular homological degree of a nullhomotopic map on the sphere, that \(\mathrm{deg}(f) = 0\)
