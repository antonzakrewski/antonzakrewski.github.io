projective module in general not free

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(M\) a projective \(A\)-module. Then \(M\) is in general not free

2. Proof

by general chinese remainder theorem, note that

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}/6 \mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}/3 \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

Then \(\mathbb{Z}/3 \mathbb{Z}\) is projective by projective module and sum as free module but not free, as a free \(\mathbb{Z}/6 \mathbb{Z}\) module contains \(0\) or at least \(6\) elements
