flatness as point local property

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module.

Then flatness is a point local property

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from

  • localization and tensor functor commute
  • composition of exact functors as exact functor

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

special case

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

let

\begin{align*} \varphi: N \hookrightarrow P \end{align*}

be module-monomorphism for \(R\)-modules \(N,P\)

Then since localization is exact, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\mathfrak{m}}: N_{\mathfrak{m}} \rightarrow P_{\mathfrak{m}} \end{align*}

is injective

Hence by assumption, we get

\begin{align*} N_{\mathfrak{m}} \otimes_{A_{\mathfrak{m}}} M_{\mathfrak{m}} \rightarrow P_{\mathfrak{m}} \otimes_{A_{\mathfrak{m}}} M_{\mathfrak{m}} \end{align*}

as injection

By localization and tensor functor commute, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} (N \otimes M)_{\mathfrak{m}} \rightarrow (P \otimes M)_{\mathfrak{m}} \end{align*}

as injection
