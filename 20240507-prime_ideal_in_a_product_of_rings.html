prime ideal in a finite product of rings

1. Proposition

Let \(R_i\) be rings for \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\), \(\bigoplus R_i\) the finite direct product of rings and \(I\) an ideal.

TFAE:

  1. \(I\) is a prime ideal
  2. \(I = \bigoplus_{i \neq m} R_i \oplus \mathfrak{p}_m\) for a prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}_m \subseteq R_m\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

using Image of an epimorphism and prime ideal we get that the canonical map

\begin{align*} \pi: \bigoplus_{i=1}^n R_i \rightarrow R_l \oplus R_k \end{align*}

preserves prime ideals.

Thus it suffices to show this for \(n = 2\). Let \((a_1,a_2) \in R_1 \oplus R_2\). Then consider

\begin{align*} (1,a_2) \cdot (a_1,1) = (a_1,a_2) \in I \end{align*}

w.l.o.g. we may assume that \((1,a_2) \in I\) hence also \((1,a_2) \cdot (1,0) = (1,0) \in I\). Furthermore by subtraction with \(a_1 \cdot (1,0)\) we conclude that \((0,a_2) \in I\) This shows that \(I = R \times I_2\) for some subset \(I_2 \subseteq R_2\). Since \(I\) is strict, it follows that that \(I_2 \subsetneq I_2\). By assumption of \(I\) prime we conclude that for \((0,b_2) \cdot (0,b_2') \in I\) that wlog \(b_2' \in I\). Hence \(I\) is a prime ideal

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \((a_i) \cdot (b_i) \in I\), then it follows that \(a_m \cdot b_m \in \mathfrak{p}_m\) and thus w.l.o.g. \(a_m \in \mathfrak{p}_m\). Hence \((a_i) \in \bigoplus_{i \neq m} R_i \oplus \mathfrak{p}_m\)
