sequent Fibonacci Numbers are coprime

1. Therem

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), then the greatest common divisor of sequent Fibonacci numbers is \(\mathrm{gcd}(f_n,f_{n+1}) = 1\)

2. Proof

proof by induction:

2.1. Basis

\begin{align*} \mathrm{gcd}(f_0,f_1) =& \mathrm{gcd}(0,1) \\ =& 1 \\ \mathrm{gcd}(f_1,f_2) =& \mathrm{gcd}(1,1) \\ =& 1 \\ \mathrm{gcd}(f_2,f_3) =& \mathrm{gcd}(1,2) \\ =& 1 \end{align*}

2.2. Step

For \(n \geq 2\) we can assume, that \(F_{n+1} \geq F_{n}\) . Using euclids algorithms we conclude:

\begin{align*} F_{n+1} =& F_n + F_{n-1} \end{align*}
