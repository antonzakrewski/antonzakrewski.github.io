precomposition with a proper surjection as open map

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T}_X)\), \((Y, \mathcal{T}_Y)\) and \((Z, \mathcal{T}_Z)\) be topological spaces, \(\mathrm{Map}(Y,Z), \mathrm{Map}(X,Z)\) the mapping spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a proper surjection.

Then the precomposition \(f^{ \rightarrow}\) is an open map onto it's image

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}: \mathrm{Map}(Y,Z) \rightarrow \mathrm{Map}(X,Z) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose \(O \subseteq \mathrm{Map}(X,Y)\) is open, w.l.o.g. \(O = \mathcal{O}_{K,U}\) for a compact set \(K \subseteq X\) and open \(U \subseteq Y\) (cf. open map and subbasis for an injection / precomposition with a surjection as injection).

Then by assumption, \(f^{-1}[K]\) is compact and thus

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}[\mathcal{O}_{K,U}] =& \{g \circ f \vert g[K] \subseteq U\} \\ =& \{g \circ f \vert (g \circ f) \circ f^{-1}[K] \subseteq U\} && \text{ f surjective, split epi in $\mathrm{Set}$ } \\ =& \mathcal{O}_{f^{-1}[K], U} \cap \mathrm{im}(f^{ \rightarrow}) \end{align*}
