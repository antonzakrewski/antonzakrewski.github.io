5-Lemma for quasi isomorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes,

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_1 \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & A_2 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & A_3 \arrow[]{r}{f} \arrow[]{d}{}& A_4 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& A_5 \arrow[]{d}{} \\ B_1 \arrow[]{r}{} & B_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & B_3 \arrow[]{r}{} & B_4 \arrow[]{r}{} & B_5 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a diagram of chain complexes such that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_*(A_1) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{} & H_*(A_2) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} & H_*(A_3) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& H_*(A_4) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}& H_*(A_5) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \\ H_*(B_1) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_*(B_2) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_*(B_3) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_*(B_4) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_*(B_5) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact

Then \(f\) is a quasi-isomorphism
