coarsest topology and subbasis

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a set and \(\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(X)\) a subset of the Power set. Then the topology \(\mathcal{O}\) generated by the subbasis \(\mathcal{T}\) is the coarsest topology containing \(\mathcal{T}\) as open sets

2. Proof

2.1. smallest topology

Given

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}} = \bigcap \{\mathcal{O} \vert \mathcal{O} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(X), \mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O} \text{ topology on } X\} \end{align*}

It is welldefined, see intersection of topologies as topology and contains \(\mathcal{T}\). Furthermore, for a topology \(\mathcal{T}'\) with \(\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathcal{T}'\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}} \subseteq& \mathcal{T}' \end{align*}

2.2. \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}} = \mathcal{O}\)

2.2.1. \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}} \subseteq& \mathcal{O}\)

follows from \(\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathcal{O}\)

2.2.2. \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}} \supseteq& \mathcal{O}\)

suppose \(O \in \mathcal{O}\). Then there finitely many \(O_1,...,O_n\) such that

  1. \(O = \bigcap_{i = 1}^n O_i\)
  2. there exist \(T_{i,j} \in \mathcal{T}\) for \(j \in J\) with
\begin{align*} O_i = \bigcup_{j \in J} T_{i,j} \end{align*}

By the Openness of a union of open sets, we conclude, that \(O_i\) is open in \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}}\) By the Openness of a finite intersection of open sets, we conclude that \(\bigcap_{i = 1}^n O_i\) is also open in \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{min}}\)
