left cofibration and injective map

1. Proposition

Let \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) be a morphism of simplicial objects. TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is a left cofbiration
  2. \(f\) is injective (?)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(\iota: A \rightarrow B\) be a cofibration. the last corollary shows there is an anodyne

\begin{align*} B \times \{0\} \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} A \times \Delta^1 \rightarrow K \end{align*}

to a kan complex \(K\)

This extends to \(B \times \Delta^1 \rightarrow K\) since it is a cofibration and arrows are injective.

Therfeore

\begin{align*} B \times \{0\} \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} A \times \Delta^1 \rightarrow B \times \Delta^1 \end{align*}

is injective

then we can recover \(i\) from the inclusion

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(i\) be an injection and \(\Sigma\) any saturated class of morphisms. Let

\begin{align*} S \coloneqq \{j \vert j \boxtimes i \in S\} \end{align*}

Then \(S\) is saturated by definitino, so in particular the class of left cofibrations is saturated. Thus

\begin{align*} \partial \Delta^n \hookrightarrow \Delta^n \end{align*}

is a cofibration by definition.

For all \(n\) if \(\partial \Delta^0 = \emptyset\).

Earlier examples and corollary show every injective is a left cofbiration
