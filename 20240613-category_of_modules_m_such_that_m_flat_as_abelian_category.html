category of modules M such that M-flat as ab5 category

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod. Let \(N_i\) be \(R\)-modules, and \(\mathcal{M}\) be the set of modules \(M\) such that each \(N_i\) is M-flat

Then the full subcategory containing \(\mathcal{M}\) is an Ab5 category

2. Proof

2.1. abelian category

2.1.1. zero object

  • module as zero-flat module

2.1.2. biproduct

  • Mi-flatness and flat over the sum

2.1.3. kernel

  • M-flat for a submodule
  • M-flatness and submodule inclusion

2.1.4. cokernel

  • quotient-flatness of an M-flat module

2.2. Ab3

  • follows from small coproducts and coequalizer and cocomplete category

2.3. Ab5

  • subcategory of an ab4 category with inherited limits and colimits as ab4 category
