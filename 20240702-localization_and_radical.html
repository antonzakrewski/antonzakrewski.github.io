localization and radical

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed subset Let \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) be an ideal

Then the localization commutes with the radical, i.e.

\begin{align*} S^{-1}\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \sqrt{S^{-1}\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(\supseteq\)

Let \(\frac{a}{s} \in \sqrt{S^{-1}\mathfrak{a}}\), then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} \frac{a^n}{s^n} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} s^n \cdot \frac{a^n}{s^n} = \frac{a^n}{1} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

showing that there exists an \(s' \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} \frac{a}{1} = \frac{a'}{s'} \end{align*}

with \(a' \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\) or

\begin{align*} \overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot a = \overline{s} a' \end{align*}

resp. that \(\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot a \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\). But then

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{\overline{s} \cdot s'} \frac{\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot a}{1} = \frac{a}{1} \in S^{-1}\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

2.2. \(\subseteq\)

Let \(\frac{a}{s} \in S^{-1}\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\), then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} a^n \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} a \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \frac{a}{1} \in S^{-1}\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

and by multiplication with \(s\) we get

\begin{align*} \frac{a}{s} \in S^{-1}\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

alternatively:

  • one side through idempotent
