categorification of a group

1. Definition

Let \(G\) be a group, then the categorification of \(G\) is defined as category \(BG\) with

  1. a single object \(*\)
  2. morphisms \(\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \cong G\)

where for \(g,g' \in \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*)\) we define

\begin{align*} g \circ g' = g \cdot g' \end{align*}

see:

  • categorification of a monoid
  • delooping of a Monoid

2. Proof

identity follows from the neutral element. associativity follows from the associativity of a group
