functor as monoid homomorphism on the endomorphism monoid

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor. Then the induced map on the endomorphism monoid

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \mathrm{End}_{\mathcal{C}}(A) \rightarrow \mathrm{End}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(A)) \end{align*}

is a monoid-homomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. unit

follows from definition as

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}_{A}) = \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{F}(A)} \end{align*}

2.2. multiplicative

follows from definition as

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(f \circ g) =& \mathcal{F}(f) \circ \mathcal{F}(g) \end{align*}
