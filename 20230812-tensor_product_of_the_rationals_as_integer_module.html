tensor product of the rationals as abelian group

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{Q}\) be a $\mathbb{Z}$-module. Then the tensor product is given by the unit

\begin{align*} \iota: \mathbb{Q} \times \mathbb{Q} \rightarrow& \mathbb{Q} (a,b) \mapsto& a \cdot b \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from the torsion exact sequence and rationals as flat module

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathbb{Q}_{\mathrm{tor}} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathbb{Q} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathbb{Q} \otimes \mathbb{Q} \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathbb{Q} \otimes \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where

  1. \(\mathbb{Q}_{\mathrm{tor}} = 0\)
  2. \(\mathbb{Q} \otimes \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} = 0\) since each element of \(\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}\) is a torsion element, hence
\begin{align*} (1 \otimes [\frac{p}{q}]) =& ( \frac{1}{q} \otimes [p \frac{q}{q}]) \\ =& ( \frac{1}{q} \otimes 0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
