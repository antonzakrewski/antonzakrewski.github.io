element of F5 as square of F25

1. Proposition

Let \(\alpha \in \mathbb{F}_{5}\), then there exists an \(\beta \in \mathbb{F}_{25}\) such that

\begin{align*} \beta^2 = \alpha \end{align*}

2. Proof

Note that

\begin{align*} \mathbb{F}_{25} \cong \mathbb{F}_{5}[X]/(X^2 + 2) \end{align*}
