mapping space and coproduct

1. Proposition

Let \(X_i\) be a familiy of topological spaces and \(Y\) another space. Suppose \(\mathrm{Map}()\) is the mapping space with the compact open topology. Then the for the categorical product and the categorical coproduct, the canonical map

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Map}(\coprod_{i \in I} X_i, Y) \rightarrow \prod_{i \in I} \mathrm{Map}(X_i, Y) \end{align*}

is a homeomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. bijective

follows from

  • contravariant hom-functor preserves small colimits

2.2. continuous

follows from induced morphism between limits and functor where \(\coprod X_i\) is a limit in \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}\)

2.3. open

using open injective map and subbasis:

Suppose \(\mathcal{O}_{K,U}\) is open, then the intersection

\begin{align*} K_i \coloneqq K \cap X_i \neq \emptyset \end{align*}

is nonempty for only finitely many elements, as otherwise \(X_i\) would be an open covering without finite subcover

Then

\begin{align*} \varphi[\mathcal{O}_{K,U}] =& \varphi[ \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathcal{O}_{K_i,U}] \\ =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \varphi[\mathcal{O}_{K_i,U}] \\ \end{align*}

is a finite intersection, where \(\varphi[\mathcal{O}_{K_i, U}\) is of the form

\begin{align*} (\prod \mathrm{Map}(X_i,Y)) \times O \end{align*}

for \(O \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{K_i,U} \subseteq \mathrm{Map}\) open.
