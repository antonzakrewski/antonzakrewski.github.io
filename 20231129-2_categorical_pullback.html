2-categorical pullback of a functor diagram

1. * Definition / Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C} \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}_1}& \mathcal{D} \\ & \mathcal{E} \arrow[]{u}{\mathcal{F}_2}\\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a cospan of functors

Then the 2-categorical pullback of the functor diagram defined as 2-pullback

1.1. explicite

the category \(\mathcal{C} \times_{\mathcal{D}} \mathcal{E}\) where

  1. objects are \((X_1,X_2,\alpha)\) where \(X_1 \in \mathcal{C}, X_2 \in \mathcal{E}\) and \(\alpha: \mathcal{F}_1(X_1) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}_2(X_2)\) is an isomorphism
  2. a morphism between \((X_1,X_2,\alpha)\) and \((Y_1,Y_2,\beta)\) consists of morphisms \(f_1: X_1 \rightarrow Y_1, f_2: X_2 \rightarrow Y_2\) such that
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}_1(X_1) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}_1(f_1)} \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & \mathcal{F}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}_2(f_2)}\\ \mathcal{F}_1(Y_1) \arrow[]{r}{\beta} & \mathcal{F}_2(Y_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

see: comma category, basically the subcategory containing only isomorphisms
