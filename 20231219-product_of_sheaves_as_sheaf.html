product of sheaves as sheaf

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(\mathcal{F}_i\) be sheafs and

\begin{align*} \prod_{i \in I} \mathcal{F}_i \end{align*}

the product functor.

Then \(\prod_{i \in I} \mathcal{F}_i\) is also a sheaf

2. Proof

2.1. locality of a sheaf

Suppose there exists sections \(s = (s_i),t = (t_i)\), such that for an open covering \(U_i\) of \(X\), it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U_i}(s) = \mathrm{res}_{U_i}(t) \end{align*}

then especially it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pi}_i(\mathrm{res}_{U_i}(s)) =& \mathrm{pi}_i(\mathrm{res}_{U_i}(t)) \end{align*}

note that by construction of the product functor,

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \arrow[phantom, ]{dr}{\circlearrowleft} (U_i)_{i \in I} \arrow[dashed]{d}{\mathrm{res}} \arrow[]{r}{\pi_i} & U_i \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{res}_i}\\ (V_i)_{i \in I} \arrow[]{r}{\pi_i} & V_i \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes Hence we get for each component

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U_i}(s) =& (s_i)_{\vert U_i} \\ =& (t_i)_{\vert U_i} \end{align*}
