monomorphism of A-Algebras as injective map

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring and \(B,C\) A-Algebras and \(\varphi: B \rightarrow C\) a morphism of A-Algebras.

TFAE:

  1. \(\varphi\) is a monomorphism in category AlgR
  2. \(\varphi\) is monomorphism of A-Algebras

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

faithful functor reflects monomorphisms monomorphism in set as injective map

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Supopse \(\varphi\) is not injective, i.e. there exists different \(b_1,b_2\) such that

\begin{align*} \varphi(b_1) = \varphi(b_2) \end{align*}

Then consider the free Algebra \(A[X]\) and the morphisms induced by

\begin{align*} f_1: x \mapsto b_1 f_2: x \mapsto b_2 \end{align*}

then it follows that

\begin{align*} \varphi \circ f_1 = \varphi \circ f_2 \end{align*}

but \(f_1 \neq f_2\). Hence \(\varphi\) is not a monomorphis
