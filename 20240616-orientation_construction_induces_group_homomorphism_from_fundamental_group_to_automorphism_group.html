orientation construction induces group homomorphism from fundamental group to unit group

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be an \(n\)-dimensional manifold and \(\nu_X: \pi_{\leq 1}(M) \rightarrow \mathrm{Ab}\) be the orientation functor construction Then \(\nu_x\) induces a group-homomorphism from the fundamental group to the unit group

\begin{align*} \nu_m: \pi_{1}(M,m) \rightarrow A^{\times} \end{align*}

for a ring \(A\)

2. Proof

Note that the automorphism group at \(m\) is precisely the fundamental group \(\pi_{\leq 1}(m) = \pi_1(M,m)\) and the automorphism group of a ring as module is given by the unit group (cf. canonical isomorphism between module automorphisms of a ring and units) Furthermore a functor determines a homomorphism between the automorphism groups, hence we get a group homomorphism

\begin{align*} \nu_m: \pi_1(M,m) \rightarrow A^{\times} \end{align*}
