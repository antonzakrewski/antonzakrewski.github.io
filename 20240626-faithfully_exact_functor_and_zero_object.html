faithfully exact functor and zero object

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) be an exact functor

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{F}\) is faithfully exact
  2. \(\mathcal{F}\) reflects the zero object, i.e. \(\mathcal{F}(A) = 0 \Leftrightarrow A = 0\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

consider the sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{0} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{0} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(0) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(0) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ & 0 \arrow[]{u}{=}& & & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact.

Thus by assumption also

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{0} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{0} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

showing that \(A = 0\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
