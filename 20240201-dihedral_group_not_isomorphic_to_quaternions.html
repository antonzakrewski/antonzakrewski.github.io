dihedral group not isomorphic to quaternions

1. Proposition

the dihedral group is not isomorphic to the quaternion group.

2. Proof

Since

\begin{align*} Q = \{\mathrm{i, j, k, 1, -1, -j, -k,}\} \end{align*}

it follows, that \(-1\) is the only element of order \(2\).

But

\begin{align*} D_4 = \langle \{r,s\} \vert r^4, s^2, rsrs \rangle \end{align*}

where both \(r^2\) and \(s\) have order 2$
