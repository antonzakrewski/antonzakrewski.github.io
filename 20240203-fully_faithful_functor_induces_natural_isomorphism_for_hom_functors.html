fully faithful functor induces natural isomorphism for hom functors

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a fully faithful functor. Then there exists a natural isomorphism for the hom-functor and the postcomposition of a functor for the hom functor

\begin{align*} \eta: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,-) \rightarrow& \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(-,-)) \\ (A,B) \mapsto& \begin{cases} \mathcal{R}(-): \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B) &\rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(A), \mathcal{F}(B)) \\ f &\mapsto \mathcal{R}(f) \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

By definition of fully faithfullness

\begin{align*} \mathcal{R}(-): \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B) &\rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(A), \mathcal{F}(B)) \\ f &\mapsto \mathcal{R}(f) \\ \end{align*}

is a Bijection, hence an isomorphism in category set (cf. isomorphism in Set as bijection)

Thus by natural isomorphism and isomorphism on the objects, \(\eta\) is an isomorphism
