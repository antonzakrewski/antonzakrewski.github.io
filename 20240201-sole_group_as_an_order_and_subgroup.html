sole group of an order as normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(U \subseteq G\) a finite subgroup of group order \(m\), such that \(U\) is the only subgroup of order \(m\). Then \(U \trianglelefteq G\) is a normal subgroup

2. Proof

Let \(g \in G\) be arbitrary. Then by image of a group homomorphism as group it follows, that \(g U g^{-1}\) is also a subgroup. Since conjugation is a group automorphism it follows, that \(\vert g U g^{-1} \vert = m\) hence by assumption \(U = g U g^{-1}\). Therefore by Normalteiler und Konjugieren als Teil/Obermenge \(U \trianglelefteq G\)
