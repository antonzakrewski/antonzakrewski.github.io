p-group as subset of a p-maximal normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a finite group, \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup with \(p \not\mid \vert G : N \vert\). Then it holsd for all p-groups \(U_p \subseteq G\), that

\begin{align*} U_p \subseteq N \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(U_p \subseteq N\) be the p-sylow group of \(N\) as group. (see: existence of a sylow subgroup). Then it follows, that \(U_p \subseteq G\) is also a sylow subgroup.

Let \(U_p'\) be an arbitrary p group. Then by Konjugieren einer Sylowgruppe und einer p-Gruppe there exists an \(g \in G\) such that

\begin{align*} g \cdot U_p' g^{-1} \subseteq U_p \subseteq N \end{align*}

conjugation with \(g^{-1}\) results in

\begin{align*} U_p' = g^{-1} g U_p' g^{-1} g \subseteq g^{-1} N g = N \end{align*}
