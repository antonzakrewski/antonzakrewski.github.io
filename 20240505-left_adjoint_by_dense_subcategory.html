left adjoint by dense subcategory

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{C}_0 \hookrightarrow \mathcal{C}\) a dense subcategory Suppose \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{R}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) are functors, such that \(\mathcal{L}\) preserves colimits formed by \(\mathcal{C}_0\)

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{L} \vdash \mathcal{R}\) are adjoints
  2. for \(C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C}_0)\) there exists a natural isomorphism
\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{L}(C),-) \simeq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(C, \mathcal{R}(-)) \end{align*}

here naturalness is required for both components.

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption each object in \(\mathcal{C}\) is given as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}(C_i) \simeq C \end{align*}

for \(C_i \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C}_0)\)

Thne we get by contravariant hom-functor dualizes small colimits to limits and the assumption that \(\mathcal{L}(-)\) preserves this colimit, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{L}(C)), -) \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{L}(\mathrm{colim}(C_i)), -) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{L}(\mathrm{colim}(C_i)), -) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathrm{colim}(\mathcal{L}(C_i)), -) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{lim}\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}((\mathcal{L}(C_i)), -) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{lim}\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(C_i, \mathcal{R}(-)) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathrm{colim}(C_i), \mathcal{R}(-)) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(C, \mathcal{R}(-)) \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
