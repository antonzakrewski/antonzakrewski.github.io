product functor

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories such that \(\mathcal{D}\) contains (suffiently many) products, \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\) the functor category and \(\mathcal{F}_i \in \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\) functors. Then the product functor

\begin{align*} \prod \mathcal{F}_i: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow& \mathcal{D} \\ A \mapsto& \prod \mathcal{F}_i(A) \\ f \mapsto& \prod \mathcal{F}_i(f) \end{align*}

is the categorical product in \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\)
