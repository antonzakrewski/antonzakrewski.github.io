subgroup of a solvable group as solvable group

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a solvable group and \(U \subseteq G\) a subgroup. Then \(U\) is also solvable.

2. Proof

Suppose \(1 = G_1 \trianglelefteq ... \trianglelefteq G_n = G\) is a series such that \(G_{i+1}/G_{i}\) is abelian. Then consider

\begin{align*} G_i' \coloneqq H \cap G_i \subseteq G_i \end{align*}

Note that by intersection of a normal subgroup with a subgroup as normal subgroup of the subgroup it follows, that

\begin{align*} G_i \cap H = G_i' \trianglelefteq G_{i+1}' = G_{i+1} \cap H \end{align*}

since \(G_i \trianglelefteq G_{i+1}\)
