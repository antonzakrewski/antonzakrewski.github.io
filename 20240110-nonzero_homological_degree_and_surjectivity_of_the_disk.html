nonzero homological degree and surjectivity on the disk

1. Proposition

Let \(g: D^n \rightarrow D^n\) be a continuous map on the disk, such that

\begin{align*} g[S^{n-1}] \subseteq S^{n-1} \end{align*}

and the degree of the restriction is nonzero

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(g_{\vert S^{n-1}}) \neq 0 \end{align*}

Then \(g\) is surjective

2. Proof

Suppose \(g\) is not surjective, then there exists a point \(s \in D^n\) such that \(s \not \in \mathrm{im}(g)\). Then define a map

\begin{align*} h: D^n \rightarrow& S^{n-1} \\ x \mapsto& \mathrm{intersection of the line from $s$ $x$ and the sphere} \end{align*}

it follows, that

\begin{align*} h_{\vert S^{n-1}} = g_{\vert S^{n-1}} \end{align*}

and therefore \(h_{\vert S^{n-1}}\) has nonzeroo degree.

Furthermore, it factorizes as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} S^{n-1} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{h_{\vert S^{n-1}}} \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & D^n \arrow[]{r}{h} & S^{n-1} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

applying the singular homology functor gives

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_{n-1}(S^{n-1}) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(D^n) \arrow[]{r}{}& H_{n-1}(S^{n-1}) \\ \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathbb{Z} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which is a contradiction, as \(\mathrm{deg}(h_{\vert S^{n-1}}) \neq 0\), but it doesnt factor
