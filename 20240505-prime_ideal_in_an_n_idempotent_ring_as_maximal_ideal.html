prime ideal in an n-idempotent ring as maximal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an n-idempotent commutative ring. Then each prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\) is a maximal ideal

2. Proof

Let \(x \in A\), then we show that

\begin{align*} (x, \mathfrak{p}) = \begin{cases} \mathfrak{p} & \mbox{if } x \in \mathfrak{p} \\ A & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

By prime ideal and quotient ring as integral domain it follows that \(A/\mathfrak{p}\) is an Integral domain Thus consider

\begin{align*} 0 =& x^n + \mathfrak{p} - x + \mathfrak{p}\\ =& (1 - x + \mathfrak{p}) (x^{n-1} + \mathfrak{p}) \end{align*}

Since \(A/ \mathfrak{p}\) is an Integral domain it follows that \(x^{n-1} + \mathfrak{p} = 0\) or that \(1 - x + \mathfrak{p} = 0\) In the first case, since prime ideals are radical we conclude that \(x \in \mathfrak{p}\). In the second case it follows that \(1 + \mathfrak{p} = x + \mathfrak{p}\) showing that \((x), \mathfrak{p}\) are coprime.
