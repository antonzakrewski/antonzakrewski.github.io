field extension of elements with degree 2 as normal extension

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) an algebraic field extension and \(A \subseteq L\) a subset, such that for the degree

\begin{align*} \vert K(\alpha) : K \vert = 2 && \forall \alpha \in A \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} K(A)/K \end{align*}

is a normal field extension

2. Proof

using normale Körpererweiterung und Zerfällungskörper

Consider \(\mathcal{F} = \{f_{\alpha} \in K[X]\}\) as the set of minimal polynomials of elements in \(A\). Then each \(f_{\alpha} \in \mathcal{F}\) has degree 2 (cf. degree of a simple field extension and degree of the minimal polynomial) and a root in \(K(A)\), namely \(\alpha\). Hence \(f_{\alpha}\) splits in \(K(A)\). Therefore, by minimality of the splitting field, we conclude, that \(K(\mathcal{F}) \subseteq K(A)\)

Note that the splitting field of \(\mathcal{F}\) contains each element \(\alpha\), hence by minimality of the generated field extension, it follows that

\begin{align*} K(A) \subseteq K(\mathcal{F}) \end{align*}

Thus it follows \(K(A) = K(\mathcal{F})\)
