multiplication as group homomorphism for an abelian group

1. Proposition

Let \((G,+)\) be an abelian group and \(n \in \mathbb{N}\).

Then

\begin{align*} \varphi_n: G \rightarrow& G \\ g \mapsto& n \cdot g \coloneqq \sum_{i=1}^{n} g \end{align*}

is a group-homomorphism

2. Proof

Let \(g,g' \in G\), then since \(G\) is abelian, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \varphi_n(g + g') =& \sum_{i=1}^{n} (g + g') \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{n} g + \sum_{i=1}^{n} g' \\ =& \varphi_n(g) + \varphi_n(g') \end{align*}
