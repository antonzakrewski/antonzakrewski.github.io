Divisor of Index as Divisor of Fibonacci Number

1. Proposition

For \(n,m \in \mathbb{N}^+\) such that \(n \mid m\) following statement holds true:

\begin{align*} F_{n} \mid F_{m} \end{align*}

2. Proof

By Assumption there exists an \(k \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(n \cdot k = m\) Using a Proof by induction we show, that \(F_{n} \mid F_{n \cdot (k+1)}\) if \(F_{n} \mid F_{n \cdot k}\)

2.1. Basis

Holds true for \(k = 1\).

2.2. Step

By Assumption \(F_n \mid F_{n \cdot k}\) and therefore there exists an \(r \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(F_n \cdot r = F_{n \cdot k}\) Therefore we can deduce from the recursion formula:

\begin{align*} F_{n \cdot (k+1)} =& F_{n \cdot k + n} \\ =& F_{n \cdot k} F_{n-1} + F_{n \cdot k + 1} F_{n} \\ =& r \cdot F_{n} \cdot F_{n-1} + F_{n \cdot k + 1} \cdot F_n \\ =& F_{n} \cdot \left(r \cdot F_{n-1} + F_{n \cdot k + 1} \right) \end{align*}
