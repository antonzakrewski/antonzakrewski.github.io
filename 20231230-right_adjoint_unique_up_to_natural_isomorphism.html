right adjoint unique up to natural isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{L} \vdash \mathcal{R}_1\) and \(\mathcal{L} \vdash \mathcal{R}_2\) adjoints.

Then \(\mathcal{R}_1 \cong \mathcal{R}_2\) are isomorphic in the functor category, i.e. there exists a natural isomorphism

2. Proof

follows dually from left adjoint unique up to natural isomorphism
