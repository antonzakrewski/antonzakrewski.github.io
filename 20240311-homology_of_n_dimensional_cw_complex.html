n-th homology of an k-dimensional cw-complex for n > k

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a n-dimensional CW-Complex. Let \(n > k\) be natural numbers, then it holds for the singular homology, that

\begin{align*} H_n(X) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

consider the exact sequence of homology for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \emptyset \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathrm{Sk}_{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{} \\ \emptyset \arrow[]{r}{} & X \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(\mathrm{sk}_{n-1}) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{} & \bigoplus_{i \in \emptyset} \tilde{H}_{n-1}(S^{n-1}) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \\ & & H_n(\mathrm{sk}_k(X) & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus by sandwhiched by zero object and isomorphism, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} H_n(\mathrm{sk}_{n-1}) \simeq H_n(\mathrm{sk}_{n}) \end{align*}

Since \(\mathrm{sk}_{n-1}\) is a \(n-1\)-dimensional cw complex, this reduced by inductive reasoning to

\begin{align*} H_n(\mathrm{sk}_{n}) \simeq& H_n(\mathrm{sk}_{n-1}) \\ ... \\ \simeq& H_n(\mathrm{sk}_{-1}) \\ =& H_n(\emptyset) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

(see: singular homology of the empty set)
