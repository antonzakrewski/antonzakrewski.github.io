tensor product of a quotient and localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed subset, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) an ideal. Suppose \(S \cap \mathfrak{a} \neq \emptyset\) and

\begin{align*} R/\mathfrak{a} \otimes S^{-1}R \end{align*}

the tensor product for the localization and quotient module for the ideal as submodule

Then

\begin{align*} R/\mathfrak{a} \otimes S^{-1}R = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

more general ?

By assumption, there exists an \(a \in S \cap \mathfrak{a}\). Then we get for an elementary tensor

\begin{align*} (m_1 \otimes m_2) =& (m_1 \otimes \frac{a}{a} m_2) \\ =& a \cdot (m_1 \otimes \frac{1}{a} m_2) \\ =& (a \cdot m_1 \otimes \frac{1}{a} m_2) \end{align*}

where \(a \cdot m_1 = 0\) and hence

\begin{align*} (a \cdot m_1 \otimes \frac{1}{a} m_2) =& 0 \end{align*}
