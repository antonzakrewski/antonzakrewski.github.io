addition of a constant point to a map as continuos map

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T}_X)\), \((Y, \mathcal{T}_Y)\) and \((Z, \mathcal{T}_Z)\) be topological spaces. For the mapping space and the compact open topology, the map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{add_point} \mathrm{Map}(X,Y) \times Z \rightarrow& \mathrm{Map}(X, Y \times Z) \\ (f,z) \mapsto& (x \mapsto& (f(x),z)) \end{align*}

is a continuous map

2. Proof

stetige Abbildung und stetigkeit der Einschränkung auf das Bild

Let \(I \coloneqq \mathrm{im}(\mathrm{add_point})\) Suppose \(\mathcal{O}_{K,U} \subseteq I\) is open and \(f' \in I\) Then by assumption, there exists an \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) such that \(f'(x) = (f(x),z)\). By definition of the product topology, there exist open sets \(U_y, U_z\) with \(U_y \times U_z \subseteq U\) such that \(f[K] \subseteq U_y\) and \(z \in U_z\), since \(f'[K] \subseteq U\). Therefore \(f \in \mathcal{O}_{K, U_y \times U_z} \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{K, U}\) because of \(U_z \times U_y \subseteq U\). The preimage equals

\begin{align*} \mathrm{add_point}^{-1}[\mathcal{O}_{K, U_y \times U_z} = \mathcal{O}_{K, U_y} \times U_z \end{align*}

which is open in \(\mathrm{Map}(X,Y)\). Thus by preimages of neighbourhoods, we conclude, that \(\mathrm{add_point}\) is continuous
