power of a prime ideal in general not primary

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p}\) a prime ideal and \(\mathfrak{p}^n\) the product. Then \(\mathfrak{p}^n\) is in general not a primary ideal.

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} A = K[x,y,z]/(xy - z^2) \end{align*}

be the polynomial ring over a field \(K\)

Let \(\overline{x}, \overline{y}, \overline{z}\) be the image under the projection. and

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} = \langle \overline{x}, \overline{z}\rangle \end{align*}

\(\mathfrak{p}\) is a prime ideal since

\begin{align*} A/\mathfrak{p} = K[y] \end{align*}

with \(K[y]\) in Integral domain (cf. prime ideal and quotient ring as integral domain)

Note that

\begin{align*} \overline{x} \cdot \overline{y} = \overline{z}^2 \in \mathfrak{p} = (\overline{x}, \overline{z}) \end{align*}

But \(\overline{x} \not\in \mathfrak{p}^2 = (\overline{x}^2, \overline{z}^2, \overline{x}\overline{y})\) and \(\overline{y} \not\in \mathfrak{p} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{p}^2}\) hence \(\overline{y} \not\in \mathfrak{p}^2\) for \(n \geq 1\).
