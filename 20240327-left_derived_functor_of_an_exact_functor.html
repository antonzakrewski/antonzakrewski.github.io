left derived functor of an exact functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) an exact functor. Then the left derived functor is given by \(\mathcal{LF}_n = 0\) as zero functor for \(n \geq 1\)

2. Proof

By assumption for a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

the resulting sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} & \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} & \mathcal{F}(B) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} & \mathcal{F}(C)\arrow[]{d}{\simeq} & 0 \\ \mathcal{LF}_0(F,0) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,C) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_(F,0) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact.

Hence the zero functor suffices as delta functor extending the sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,0) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_0(F,C) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{LF}_(F,0) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
