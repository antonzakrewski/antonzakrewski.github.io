one point compactification of the euclidean space as n-sphere

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{R}^n\) the euclidean space, then the

is homeomorphic to the n-sphere

\begin{align*} (\mathbb{R}^n)^+ \cong S^n \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \((1,0,...,0)\) be a designated point and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{stereo}^{-1}: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow S^n \setminus (1,0,...,0) \end{align*}

the inverse map of the stereographic projection, which is a homeomorphism.

We can extend \(\mathrm{stereo}^{-1}\) by

\begin{align*} \mathrm{stereo}^{-1}': \infty \mapsto& (1,0,...,0) \end{align*}

Suppose \(O \subseteq S^n\) is an open neighbourhood of \((1,...,0)\). Then the complement \(A \coloneqq S^n \setminus O\) is closed and the preimage under \(\mathrm{stereo}^{-1}\) is closed in \(\mathbb{R}^n\). Furthermore, \(\mathrm{stereo}: S^n \setminus (1,0,...,) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n\) is continuous, and therefore \(\mathrm{stereo}[A]\) is compact (see: image of compact space as compact space and closed subset of a compact space as compact set) Hence \(\mathrm{stereo}[O] = (\mathbb{R}^n \sqcup \{\infty\}) \setminus \mathrm{stereo}[A]\) is open by construction

As continuous bijection from a compact space to a hausdorff space as homeomorphism, we are finished
