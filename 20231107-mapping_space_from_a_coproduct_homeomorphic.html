mapping space from a coproduct homeomorphic

1. Proposition

Let \(X_i\) be a family of topological spaces and \(Y\) also a topological space. Then for the compact open topology of the mapping space, it holds for the topological sum and product topology

\begin{align*} \mathrm{unglue}: \mathrm{Map}(\coprod_{i \in I} X_i, Y) \cong \prod_{i \in I} \mathrm{Map}(X_i, Y) \end{align*}

are canonically homeomorphic

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Map}(\coprod_{i \in I} X_i, Y) \rightarrow& \prod_{i \in I} \mathrm{Map}(X_i, Y) (f: a_i \mapsto f_i(a_i)) \mapsto& (f_i) \end{align*}

2.1. Bijection

follows from glueing lemma for an open covering, as a continuous map is precisely given by a covering (here \(X_i\)) with compatible covering (here \(\emptyset\), hence trivially satisfied)

alternatively it follows from: contravariant hom-functor dualizes small colimits to limits

2.2. continuous

??? Suppose \(\mathcal{O}_{K,U} \subseteq \prod_{i \in I} \mathrm{Map}(X_i, Y)\) is open, then by w.l.o.g.

  1. continuous map and subbasis
  2. subbasis and product of subbasis

it suffices, to show it for

\begin{align*} O = \mathcal{O}_{K_i,O_i} \times \prod_{j \neq i} \mathrm{Map}(X_i,Y) \end{align*}

Then we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{unglue}^{-1}[O] =& \{ f \vert f_{\vert X_i} \in \mathcal{O}_{K_i,O_i} \subseteq \mathrm{Map}(X_i,Y)\} \\ =& \{f \vert f[K_i] \subseteq O_i\} \\ =& \mathcal{O}_{K_i,O_i} \subseteq \mathrm{Map}(\coprod X_i, Y) \end{align*}

2.3. open map

Suppose \(O \subseteq \mathrm{Map}(\coprod X_i, Y)\) is open. Then by open injective map and subbasis, we may assume w.l.o.g. that we have an subbasis open set

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_{K,U} \end{align*}

Note that by compact set and finite intersection with disjoint open sets, we have finitely many

\begin{align*} K_i \coloneqq X_i \cap K \neq \emptyset \end{align*}

Thus we get

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_{K,U} =& \{f \vert f_{K} \subseteq U\} \\ =& \{f \vert f_{K_i} \subseteq U \forall i \in \{1,...,n\}\} \\ =& \bigcap_{i=1}^n \{f \vert f_{K_i} \subseteq U\} \\ =& \bigcap_{i \in I} \mathcal{O}_{K_i,U} \end{align*}

and applying our map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{unglue}[\mathcal{O}_{K_i,U}] =& \mathcal{O}_{K_i,U} \times \prod_{j \neq i} \mathrm{Map}(X_j,Y) \end{align*}

hence an open set.

by injective image and intersection, we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{unglue}[\bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathcal{O}_{K_i,U}] =& \bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathcal{O}_{K_i,U} \times \prod_{j \neq i} \mathrm{Map}(X_j,Y) \end{align*}

where the latter is the finite intersection of open sets, hence also open
