transitive subgroup with transposition and n-1 cycle as symmetric group

1. Proposition

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) and \(S_n\) the symmetric group and \(H \subseteq S_n\) a transitive subgroup, such that there exists a Transposition \(\tau\) and an \(n-1\) cycle \(p\) in \(H\). Then \(H = S_n\)

2. Proof

Suppose that \(p = (2,...,n)\) Then there exists two cases

2.1. \(\tau = (1,j)\) with \(j \neq i\)

conjugation results in

\begin{align*} p \tau p^{-1} =& (2,...,n) (1,j) (n,...,2) \\ =& (1,j+1) \\ p^{-1} \tau p =& (n,...,2) (1,j) (2,...,n) \\ =& (1,j-1) \end{align*}

hence by Darstellung einer Permutation als Produkt von Transpositionen we conclude, that

\begin{align*} H = S_n \end{align*}

2.2. \(\tau = (i,j)\) with \(i,j \neq 1\)

Then by generator of a finite symmetric group it follows, that

\begin{align*} S_{n-1} \subseteq S_n \end{align*}

Hence for a permutation \(\sigma\) with \(1 \mapsto i, j \mapsto i\) we may choose \(\sigma'\) with \(1 \leftrightarrow i\) followed by \(j \leftrightarrow i\) and then fixing the \(n-2\) positions
