contractible space as pathconnected space

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a contractible topological space. Then \(X\) is pathconnected

2. Proof

by contractible space and identity map, there exists a homotopy from a point \(c\) to \(\mathrm{id}_{X}\)

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times X \rightarrow X \end{align*}

restriction to \(H_{\vert}: [0,1] \times \{x\} \rightarrow X\) gives a path

\begin{align*} \gamma: [0,1] \rightarrow& X \\ t \mapsto& H(t,x) \end{align*}

with \(\gamma(0) = x, \gamma(1) = c\). Since \(x\) was arbitrary, we get a path from \(x_1\) to \(c\) and then from \(c\) to \(x_2\), hence by concatenation of paths there exists a path from arbitrary \(x_1\) to \(x_2\)
