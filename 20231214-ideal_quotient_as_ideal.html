ideal quotient as ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{b}\) be ideals.

Then the ideal quotient \(\frac{\mathfrak{a}}{\mathfrak{b}}\) is an ideal

2. Proof

Suppose \(x,y \in \frac{\mathfrak{a}}{\mathfrak{b}}\), then for arbitrary \(b \in \mathfrak{b}\), it follows, that

2.1. additive closure

\begin{align*} b \cdot x, b \cdot y \in& \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

therefore, by additive closure of \(\mathfrak{a}\), we conclude, that

\begin{align*} b (x + y) \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2.2. multilplicative closed

Then for \(\alpha \in A\), it follows by closure of \(\mathfrak{a}\), that

\begin{align*} b \cdot (\alpha \cdot x) =& \alpha \cdot (b \cdot x) \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2.3. nonempty

follows from \(0 \cdot b = 0 \in \mathfrak{a}\) for arbitrary \(b \in \mathfrak{b}\)
