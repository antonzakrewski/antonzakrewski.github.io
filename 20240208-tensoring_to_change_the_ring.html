tensoring to change the ring

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(R,S\) be commutative (rings), \(\varphi: R \rightarrow S\) a ring-homomorphism and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. Then \(M\) is canonically an \(S\)-module via

\begin{align*} S \otimes_{R} M \end{align*}

where \(S\) is regarded as \(R\)-module through restriction of scalars for \(S\) as \(S\)-module (cf. ring as module) see:

  • tensor product

2. Proof

2.1. \(1\)

Let \(1 \in R\), then we conclude for \(s \in S\)

\begin{align*} 1_R \cdot_R s =& \varphi(1) \cdot_S s \\ =& s \\ \end{align*}

2.2. associative

Let \(r_1,r_2 \in R\), then it holds for \(s \in S\)

\begin{align*} r_1 \cdot_R (r_2 \cdot_R s) =& r_1 \cdot_R (\varphi(r_2) \cdot s) \\ =& (\varphi(r_1) \cdot_R (\varphi(r_2) \cdot_S s)) \\ =& (\varphi(r_1) \cdot_S \varphi(r_2) \cdot_S s) \\ =& (\varphi(r_1 \cdot r_2) \cdot s) \\ =& (r_1 \cdot r_2) \cdot_R s \end{align*}

2.3. distributive

\begin{align*} (r_1 + r_2) \cdot_R (s_1 + s_2) =& \varphi(r_1 + r_2) \cdot_S (s_1 + s_2) \\ =& \varphi(r_1) \cdot_S s_1 + \varphi(r_2) \cdot_S s_1 + \varphi(r_1) \cdot_S s_2 + \varphi(r_2) \cdot_S s_2 \\ =& r_1 \cdot_R s_1 + r_2 \cdot_R s_1 + r_1 \cdot_R s_2 + r_2 \cdot_R s_2 \end{align*}
