bijection between submodules for restriction of scalars for a surjective ring homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be rings, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) a surjective ring-homomorphism and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Res}_{\varphi}: \mathrm{B-Mod} \rightarrow \mathrm{A-Mod} \end{align*}

the restriction of scalars functor

Then there exists a natural Bijection

\begin{align*} \{B-\mathrm{submodules}\} \hookrightarrow \{A-\mathrm{submodules}\} \end{align*}

given by the identity on the underlying subsets

2. Proof

2.1. a)

restriction of scalars induces injection on submodules

2.2. b)

Let \(N \subseteq M\) be an \(A\)-submodule. Then \(N\) is also a \(B\)-submodule:

Associativity, distributivity, etc. are inherited. Furthermore it is closed under multiplication: For \(m \in M\) and \(\alpha \in A\), there exists an \(\beta \in B\) with \(\varphi(\beta) = \alpha\) showing

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot_A m =& \varphi(\beta) \cdot_A m \\ =& \beta \cdot_B m \in N \end{align*}
