coproduct in set

1. Proposition

Given category set and a collection of sets \((X_i)_{i \in I}\), then the categorical coproduct is given as disjoint union

\begin{align*} \coprod_{i \in I} X_i \coloneqq \bigcup_{i \in I} X_} \times \{i\} \end{align*}

with the canonical inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota: X_i \rightarrow& \bigsqcup_{i \in I} X_} \times \{i\} x \mapsto& (x,i) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. construction of a map

Let \(A\) be a set and

\begin{align*} f_i: X_i \rightarrow A \end{align*}

be a collection of maps

Then we define

\begin{align*} f: \coprod X_i \rightarrow A \\ (x,i) \mapsto& f_i(x) \end{align*}

Then by construction

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X_i \arrow[]{r}{\iota} \arrow[]{rd}{f_i} & \coprod X_i \arrow[]{d}{f}\\ & A \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

2.2. uniqueness

Suppose there exists a map \(g\) making

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X_i \arrow[]{r}{\iota} \arrow[swap]{rd}{f_i} & \coprod X_i \arrow[]{d}{f}\\ & A \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute Then for \((x,i) \in \coprod X_i\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} g((x,i)) = f_i(x) \end{align*}

therefore it follows, that

\begin{align*} g((x,i)) = f((x,i)) \end{align*}

and since \((x,i)\) was arbitrary,

\begin{align*} g = f \end{align*}
