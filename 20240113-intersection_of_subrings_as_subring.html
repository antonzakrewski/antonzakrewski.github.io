intersection of subrings as subring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(S_i \subseteq R\) subrings. Then the intersection

\begin{align*} S \coloneqq \bigcap S_i \end{align*}

is also a subring

2. Proof

2.1. \(0,1 \in S\)

follows from \(0,1 \in S_i\) for arbitrary \(i \in I\)

2.2. closed

Let \(\alpha, \beta \in S\), then for arbitrary \(i \in I\) we get

\begin{align*} \alpha, \beta \in S_i \end{align*}

and hence since \(S_i\) is closed also

\begin{align*} \alpha + \beta, \alpha \cdot \beta \in S_i \end{align*}

and therefore \(\alpha + \beta, \alpha \cdot \beta \in S\)

2.3. additive inverse

Let \(\alpha \in S\), then for arbitrary \(i \in I\), we get that

\begin{align*} - \alpha \in S_i \end{align*}

hence \(\alpha \in S\)

2.4. distributivity, associativity

is inherited
