degree of a polynomial and the degree of the difference polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(f: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}\) be map, polynomial-like on \(\mathbb{Z}\). Then for the successor map

\begin{align*} \Delta f = f(x+1) - f(x) \end{align*}

it holds for the polynomials

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(P_f) = \mathrm{deg}(P_{\Delta(P_f)}) + 1 \end{align*}

see: polynomial for a polynomial-like map on the integers uniquely determined

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} P_f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i \end{align*}

with \(\alpha_n \neq 0\). Then (cf. polynomial-like map and successor difference polynomial-like)

\begin{align*} \Delta_{P_f} =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i (X+1)^i - \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i \\ =& \alpha_n X^n - \alpha_n X^n + \alpha_n \binom{n}{1} X^{n-1} + \alpha_{n-1} X^{n-1} - \alpha_{n-1} X^{n-1} + r \\ =& \alpha_n \binom{n}{1} X^{n-1} + r(x) \end{align*}h where $\mathrm{deg}(r(x)) < n-1$. Hence by [[id:f31d2706-62f7-4d1b-9490-24e8a8f4f892][degree of homogeneous polynomials under addition]], it follows, that \begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(\Delta_{P_f}) =& n - 1 \\ =& \mathrm{deg}(P_f) - 1 \end{align*}
