orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a topological group, \(X\) a topological space and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a free topological group action

TFAE:

  1. the projection to the orbits \(p: X \rightarrow X/G\) is a covering map (cf. orbital collapse)
  2. \(\varphi\) is a covering-like group action

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(U\) be an open neighbourhood of \(x\). Then \(p(U)\) is open (cf. projection to the orbits as open map) and by definition,

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[ p[U] ] \simeq U \times G \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Choose an open cover \(U_i\) of \(X/G\) on which \(p\) is trivialized. Then

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U_i] \simeq U_i \times G \end{align*}

For \(x \in X\), choose \(U_i \times \{g\}\) for suitable \(g \in G\).

Then, since \(p\) is a G-equivariant map (see: group action as g-equivariant map)
