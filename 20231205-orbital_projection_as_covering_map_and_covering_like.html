orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a topological group, \(X\) a topological space and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a topological group action

TFAE:

  1. the projection to the orbits \(p: X \rightarrow X/G\) is a covering map (cf. orbital collapse) and \(G\) is a free group action
  2. \(\varphi\) is a covering-like group action

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(x \in X\). Then choose an open neighbourhood \(U \subseteq X/G\) of \([x]\) such that

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U] \cong \coprod_{F} U \end{align*}

for some \(F\).

Then by compatibility with \(p\) choose \(f \in F\) such that \(\theta(x) \in U \times \{f\}\). Note, that \(U \times \{f\} \cong U\).

Furthermore, assume that there exists an \(g \in G\) and \(y,z \in U \times \{f\}\) such that

\begin{align*} g \cdot y = z \end{align*}

i.e. the intersection \(g(U \times \{f\}) \cap U \times \{f\}\) is nonempty.

Then it follows, that

\begin{align*} p(x) =& p(g \cdot y) \end{align*}

and furthermore, by definition of the orbital collapse, it holds, that

\begin{align*} p(g \cdot y) = [g \cdot y] = [y] \end{align*}

Since \(p: U \times \{f\} \cong U\) is injective, it follows, that \(x = y\) and thus also \(y = g \cdot y\)

Furthermore, because the group action is assumed to be free, we conclude from \(y = g \cdot y\), that

\begin{align*} g = e \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Choose an open cover \(U_i\) of \(X/G\) on which \(p\) is trivialized. Then

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U_i] \cong U_i \times F \end{align*}

for some \(F\), compatible with \(p\).

For \(U_i \times \{f\}\), assume there exists an \(g\) such that

\begin{align*} g[U_i \times \{f\}] \cap U_i \times \{f\} \neq \emptyset \end{align*}

hence there exist \(x,y \in U_i \times \{f\}\) such that

\begin{align*} x = g \cdot y \end{align*}

Thus we get

\begin{align*} [x] =& p(x) \\ =& p(g \cdot y) \\ =& [g \cdot y] \\ =& [y] \\ =& p(y) \end{align*}

and since \(p_{\vert U_i \times \{f\}}\) is a homeomorphism, hence also injective, it follows, that

\begin{align*} y = x \end{align*}

Thus since \(G\) is free (cf. covering like group action as free group action), we also get \(g = e\), showing that

\begin{align*} g[U_i \times \{f\}] \cap U_i \times \{f\} \neq \emptyset \Leftrightarrow g = e \end{align*}
