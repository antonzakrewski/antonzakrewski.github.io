group acts by conjugation on set of subgroups

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(S \coloneqq \{H \vert H \subseteq G\}\) the set of subgroups. Then conjugation acts on \(S\) by

\begin{align*} g \cdot H \rightarrow gHg^{-1} \end{align*}

see:

  • conjugation as group automorphism
  • image of a group homomorphism as group

2. Proof

Note that

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot (g_2 \cdot H) =& g_1 \cdot (g_2 H g_{2}^{-1}) \\ =& g_1 (g_2 H g_2^{-1}) g_2^{-1} \\ =& (g_1 g_2) H (g_1 \cdot g_2)^{-1} \\ =& (g_1 g_2) \cdot H \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} g_1 (g_2 H g_2^{-1}) g_2^{-1} =& \{g_1 h' g_1^{-1} \vert h' \in g_2 H g_2^{-1}\} \\ =& \{g_1 g_2 h g_2^{-1} g_1^{-1} \vert h \in H\} \\ =& \{g_1 g_2 h (g_2 g_1)^{-1} \vert h \in H\} \\ =& (g_1 g_2) H (g_2 g_1)^{-1} \end{align*}
