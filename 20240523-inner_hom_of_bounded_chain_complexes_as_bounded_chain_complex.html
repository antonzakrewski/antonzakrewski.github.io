inner hom of bounded below and bounded above chain complexes as bounded above chain complex

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes

Suppose \(M_*,N_*\) are chain complexes where

  1. \(M_*\) is bounded below
  2. \(N_*\) is bounded above

Then the hom chain complex

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{R}(M_*,N_*) \end{align*}

is bounded above

2. Proof

follows from the definition as for sufficiently large \(n\) it follows that

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(M_*,N_*) = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}^{\mathrm{Ab}}(M_p, N_{p+n}) \end{align*}

where

  1. for sufficiently small \(p\) \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}^{\mathrm{Ab}}(M_p,N_{p+n}) = \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathcal{A}}(0,N_{p+n}) = 0\)
  2. and otherwise, if \(n\) is choosen large enough, \(N_{p+n} = 0\)
