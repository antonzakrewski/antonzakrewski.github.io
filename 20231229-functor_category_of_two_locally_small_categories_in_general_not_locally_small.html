functor category of two locally small categories in general not locally small

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be locally small category categories and \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\) the functor category

Then \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\) is in general not a locally small category

2. Proof

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a indiscrete category with \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) as proper class. Let \(\mathcal{D}\) contain \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) with a designated object \(T\), such that for \(A \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D})\) with \(A \neq T\), there exist two morphisms

\begin{align*} 0,1: A \rightarrow T \end{align*}

Then for the inclusion functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrowtail \mathcal{D} A \mapsto& A \\ \mathrm{id}_{A} \mapsto& \mathrm{id}_{A} \\ \end{align*}

and the constant functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{G}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow& \mathcal{D} \\ A \mapsto& T \\ f \mapsto& \mathrm{id}_{T} \end{align*}
