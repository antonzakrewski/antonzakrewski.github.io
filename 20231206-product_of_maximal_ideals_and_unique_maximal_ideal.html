power of maximal ideals and unique maximal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{m}\) a maximal ideal and \(\mathfrak{m}^n\) the \(n\)-th power. Then \(\mathfrak{m}\) is the only maximal ideal containing \(\mathfrak{m}^n\)

2. Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{m}'\) be a maximal ideal with \(\mathfrak{m}^n \subseteq \mathfrak{m}'\). Suppose \(m \in \mathfrak{m}\), then \(m^n \in \mathfrak{m}^n\) and hence \(m^n \in \mathfrak{m}'\). Since \(\mathfrak{m}'\) is prime (cf. maximal ideal as prime ideal), we conclude, that \(m \in \mathfrak{m}'\) (see: prime ideal as radical ideal)

Thus since \(m\) was arbitrary, we conclude, that \(\mathfrak{m} \subset \mathfrak{m}'\) and therefore by maximality, we get equality
