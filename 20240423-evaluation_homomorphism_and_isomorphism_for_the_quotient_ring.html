evaluation homomorphism and isomorphism for the quotient ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(f \in A[X_1,...,X_n]\) the polynomial ring as A-Algebra, \(a_1,...,a_n \in A\) elements. Then there exists a canonical isomorphism

\begin{align*} A[X_1,...,X_n]/(f,X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n) \simeq A/(f(a_1,...,a_n)) \end{align*}

2. Proof

using Yoneda Lemma and Hom functor as conservative functor it suffices to show, that for an arbitrary A-Algebra \(B\)

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{A-Alg}}(A[X_1,...,X_n]/(f,X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n),B) \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{A-Alg}}(A/(f(a_1,...,a_n),B)) \end{align*}

By universal property of a polynomial ring and universal property of a quotient map a homomorphism \(\varphi \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{A-Alg}}(A[X_1,...,X_n]/(f,X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n),B)\) is uniquely determined by the following data

  1. a map \(X_i \mapsto \varphi(X_i)\).

Since \(X_i - a_i \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\) it follows that \(\varphi(X_i) = \varphi(a_i)\), where \(\varphi(a_i)\) is already uniquely determined by the algebra structure of \(B\).
