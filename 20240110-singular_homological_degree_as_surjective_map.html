singular homological degree as group epimorphism

1. Proposition

Given the n-sphere, the singular homological degree is a algebraic group-epimorphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}: [S^n, S^n] \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(S^1\)

2.1.1. \(\mathbb{N} \subseteq \mathrm{im}(\mathrm{deg})\)

by singular homological degree of the identity on the sphere we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(\mathrm{id}) = 1 \end{align*}

consider

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} S^1 \arrow[]{r}{p} & S^1 \vee S^1 \arrow[]{r}{g \vee f}& S^1 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

for \(p: S^1 \rightarrow S^1 \vee S^1\) as pinch map of circles Since reduced singular homology and wedge product, we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{r}{p_*} & \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{deg}(g) \otimes \mathrm{deg}(f)}& \mathbb{Z} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here \(p_*\) is the diagonal map (?), thus the composition is given by

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(p(f \vee g))= \mathrm{deg}(f) + \mathrm{deg}(g) \end{align*}

Taking \(f,g = \mathrm{id}_{S^1}\) this results in

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(p(\mathrm{id} \vee \mathrm{id})) = 2 \end{align*}

resp. \(n\) by iteration

2.1.2. \(\mathbb{Z} \subseteq \mathrm{im}(\mathrm{deg})\)

follows from:

  • singular homological degree of a reflection
  • multiplicativity of the singular homological degree on the sphere
  • singular homological degree of a nullhomotopic map on the sphere

2.2. \(S^n\)

The general case follows from the suspension isomorphism of singular homology
