degenerate simplex as degeneration of a unique non degenerate simplex

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a simplicial set and \(x \in X_k\) be a degenerate k-simplex. Then there exists an \(n < k\) and a unique non degenerate \(n\)-simplex \(x'\) such that

\begin{align*} \partial_{i_1} \circ ... \circ \partial_{i_{k-n}}(x') = x \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. existence

follows from the well order of \(\mathbb{N}\), as for each degenerate simplex, there exists a preimage. Since \(\mathbb{N}\) is well-ordered and by definition each simplex in \((X)_0\) is non degenerate, choosing a preimage terminates in finitely many steps.

2.2. uniqueness

2.2.1. same morphisms

follows from

  • degeneracy map of an object as split mono
  • split mono as monomorphism
  • monomorphism in set as injective map

2.2.2. morphisms same

w.l.o.g. it suffices to show, for a degenerate simplex with a preimage one level below,

Suppose there exists \(y,y' \in X_{k-1}\) with

\begin{align*} \sigma_i(y) = \sigma_j(y) \end{align*}

wlog for \(i \leq j\)

Proof by induction

2.3. \(i = j\)

2.4. \(i = j + n\) to \(i = j + n-1\)

Using the simplicial identities of degeneracy after face map we get

\begin{align*} y =& \sigma_j \partial_j(y) \\ =& \sigma_j \partial_{j+n}(y') \\ =& \partial_{j + n - 1} \sigma_j(y') \\ =& \partial_{j + n - 1} \partial_{j+n} \sigma_{j+n} \sigma_j(y') \end{align*}

using simplicial identity of commuting face maps gives and the identity equation

\begin{align*} \partial_{j + n - 1} \partial_{j+n} \sigma_{j+n} \sigma_j(y') =& \partial_{j + n - 1} \partial_{j + n - 1} \sigma_{j+ n} \sigma_j(y') \\ =& \partial_{j+ n -1} \mathrm{id} \sigma_j(y') \\ =& \partial_{j+n - 1} \sigma_{j}(y') \end{align*}

Hence after \(n\) steps this reduces to

\begin{align*} y =& \partial_{j} \sigma_j(y') \\ =& y' \end{align*}
