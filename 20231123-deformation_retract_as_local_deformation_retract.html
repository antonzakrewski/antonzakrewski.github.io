deformation retract as locally nullhomotopic

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(A \subseteq X\) a deformation retract. Then for each neighbourhood \(V\) of \(A\), there exists a smaller neighbourhood \(U \subseteq V\) of \(A\), such that the inclusion \(U \iota V\) is nullhomotopic

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists a homotopy relative to \(A\), \(H: [0,1] \times X \rightarrow X\) Furthermore, for each \(a \in A\), it holds, that \(V\) is a neighbourhood of \(a\). Since \(H\) is relative to \(A\), we conclude, that \(H[(t,a)] = a\). Hence \(H^{-1}[V]\) is a neighbourhood of \((t,a)\) and since \([0,1]\) is compact, we conclude by tube lemma, that there exist an open \(U_a\) such that

\begin{align*} U_a \times [0,1] \subseteq V \end{align*}

By the self-indexing trick, we get a neighbourhood \(U \subseteq V\) with

\begin{align*} U \coloneqq \bigcup_{a \in A} U_a \end{align*}

Then the map

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times U \rightarrow X \end{align*}

is continuous as restriction (cf. restriction of a continuous map as continuous map) and furthermore by construction, it holds, that \(V \supseteq \mathrm{im}(H)\), hence restrincting the codomain to \(V\) is also continuous (cf. continuous map and restriction to the image).

Therefore, by construction, it is the desired homotopy
