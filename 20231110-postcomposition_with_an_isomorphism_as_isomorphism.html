postcomposition with an isomorphism as isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) an isomorphism.

  1. \(f\) is an isomorphism
  2. postcomposition with \(f\) is an isomorphism on hom-sets for all \(C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of:

  • covariant hom functor of points
  • isomorphisms under functors

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(C,A) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(C,B) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism. Therfore for \(C \coloneqq B\) we get

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,B) \end{align*}

By surjectivity, there exists an \(g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A)\) such that

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}(g) = f \circ g = \mathrm{id}_{Y} \end{align*}

Furthermore, it holds

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow} ( g \circ f) =& f \circ g \circ f \\ =& f^{ \leftarrow} ( g \circ f) \\ =& f^{ \leftarrow}( \mathrm{id}_X) \\ \end{align*}

hence by injectivity, we conclude that \(g \circ f = \mathrm{id}_{X}\)
