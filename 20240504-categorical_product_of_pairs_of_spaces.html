categorical product of pairs of spaces

1. Proposition

Let \((X_i,A_i)\) be pairs of spaces. Then their product is given by

\begin{align*} \prod (X_i,A_i) = (\prod X_i, \prod A_i) \end{align*}

with the product topology

2. Proof

Suppose three exists an pair of spaces \((Z,C)\) and maps

\begin{align*} f_i: (Z,C) \rightarrow (X_i,A_i) \end{align*}

Then consider

\begin{align*} \prod f_i: Z \rightarrow& (\prod X_i, \prod A_i) \\ z \mapsto& (f_i(z)) \end{align*}

it is continuous by definition of the product topology

furthermore for \(c \in C\) it follows that \(f_i(c) \in A_i\), hence

\begin{align*} \prod f_i(c) \in \prod A_i \end{align*}
