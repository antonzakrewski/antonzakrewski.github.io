inner hom of bounded above and bounded below chain complexes as bounded below chain complex

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(M_*,N_*\) are chain complexes where

  1. \(M_*\) is bounded above
  2. \(N_*\) is bounded below

Then the hom chain complex

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{R}(M_*,N_*) \end{align*}

is bounded below

2. Proof

follows from the definition as for sufficiently small \(n\) it follows that

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(M_*,N_*) = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}^{\mathrm{Ab}}(M_p, N_{p+n}) \end{align*}

where

  1. for sufficiently large \(p\) \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}^{\mathrm{Ab}}(M_p,N_{p+n}) = \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathcal{A}}(0,N_{p+n}) = 0\)
  2. and otherwise, if \(n\) is choosen small enough, \(N_{p+n} = 0\)
