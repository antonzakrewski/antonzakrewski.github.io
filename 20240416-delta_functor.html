delta functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories. A \(\mathcal{B}\)-valued \(\delta\) functor on \(\mathcal{A}\) is a sequence of additive functors

\begin{align*} (\mathcal{F}_n: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B})_{n \geq -1} \end{align*}

and morphisms

\begin{align*} \partial_n^{A,B,C}: \mathcal{F}_{n}(C) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}_{n-1}(A) \end{align*}

for each short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

such that

  1. following sequence is exact
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... & \mathcal{F}_{n+1}(B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}_{n+1}(C) \arrow[]{r}{\partial_n^{A,B,C}} & \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(B) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}_n(C) \arrow[]{r}{} & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
  1. for each morphism of exact sequences
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{d}{f} \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{d}{h} \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A' \arrow[]{r}{} & B' \arrow[]{r}{}& C' \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

the following diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}_n(C) \arrow[]{r}{\partial_n} \arrow[]{d}{h} & \mathcal{F}_{n-1}(A) \arrow[]{d}{f} \\ \mathcal{F}_n(C') \arrow[]{r}{\partial_n} & \mathcal{F}_{n-1}(A') \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
  1. \(F_{-1}\) is defined as constant functor to the zero object
