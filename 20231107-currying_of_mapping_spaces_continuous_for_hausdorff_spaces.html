currying of mapping spaces continuous for hausdorff spaces

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T}_X)\), \((Y, \mathcal{T}_Y)\) and \((Z, \mathcal{T}_Z)\) be topological spaces, such that \(X\) is Hausdorff Then for the mapping spaces equipped with the compact open topology, Currying

\begin{align*} \mathrm{curry}: \mathrm{Map}(X \times Y, Z) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Map}(X, \mathrm{Map}(Y,Z)) \\ f \mapsto& (f_x: y \mapsto f(x,y)) \end{align*}

is continuous

2. Proof

Since \(X\) is Hausdorff, we may choose a Subbasis \(\mathcal{S}\) of \(\mathrm{Map}(Y,Z)\) and then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_{K, \mathcal{S}} \end{align*}

for \(K \subseteq X\) compact is a subbasis of \(\mathrm{Map}(X, \mathrm{Map}(Y,Z))\) (cf. restricting subbasis for the compact open topology)

Therefore, let \(\mathcal{S} = \{\mathcal{O}_{L,U} \vert U \subseteq Z \text{ open}, L \subseteq Y \text{ compact} \}\) be the canonical subbasis

Thus

\begin{align*} \mathrm{curry}^{-1}[ \mathcal{O}_{K, \mathcal{O}_{K,U}}] = \mathcal{O}_{K \times L, U} \end{align*}

where by Tychonoff's Theorem \(K \times L\) is compact.

Note that \(f \in \mathrm{curry}^{-1}[ \mathcal{O}_{K, \mathcal{O}_{L,U}}]\) if and only if

\begin{align*} f[ \{k\} \times L] \subseteq U \forall k \in K \end{align*}

hence the equality
