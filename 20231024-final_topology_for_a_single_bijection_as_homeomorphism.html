final topology for a single bijection as homeomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(Y\) a set. Let

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

be a set-theoretic Bijection and \(\mathcal{T}_Y\) the final topology on \(Y\). Then \(f: (X, \mathcal{T}_X) \rightarrow (Y, \mathcal{T}_Y)\) is a homeomorphism
