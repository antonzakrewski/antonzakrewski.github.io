conjugation as group action

1. Proposition

let \(G\) be a group \(X\) the underlying set, then conjugation

\begin{align*} \triangle: G \times X \rightarrow X (g,x) \mapsto& g \cdot x \cdot g^{-1} \end{align*}

is a group action

2. Proof

2.1. neutral element

It holds that

\begin{align*} g \triangle e =& e \cdot g \cdot e^{-1} \\ =& e \cdot g \cdot e \\ =& g \end{align*}

2.2. composition

It holds, that

\begin{align*} ((g \cdot h) \triangle x) =& (g \cdot h) \cdot x \cdot (g \cdot h)^{-1} \\ =& g \cdot h \cdot x \cdot h^{-1} \cdot g^{-1} \\ =& g \triangle (h \cdot x \cdot h^{-1}) \\ =& g \triangle (h \triangle x) \end{align*}
