group ring of F2, F2 as local ring

1. Proposition

Let \(G,K = \mathbb{F}_2\) be a finite field and

\begin{align*} KG \end{align*}

the group ring

Then \(KG\) is a local ring

2. Proof

The elements are

\begin{align*} KG = \{0 ,1,g, 1 + g\} \end{align*}

and furthermore the units are given by \(1,g\) as \(g^2 = 1\) and

\begin{align*} (1 + g) \cdot g =& g + g^2 \\ =& 1 + g \\ (1 + g)^2 =& 1 + 2g + g^2 \\ =& 1 + g^2 \\ =& 1 + g \end{align*}

by idempotent and trivial unit it follows that \(1 + g\) is not a unit

Therefore the local ring and non-units as maximal ideal \(\{0,1+g\}\) form an ideal
