restriction of scalars for a g-map

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups and \(\varphi: G \rightarrow H\) a group-homomorphism Suppose \(X,Y\) are H-Sets and \(\psi: G \rightarrow H\) a G-equivariant map then there exists a canonical H-equivariant-map

\begin{align*} \psi^*: \varphi^{*}(X) \rightarrow& \varphi^{*}(Y) \\ x \mapsto& \psi(y) \end{align*}

where \(\varphi^{*}(X), \varphi^{*}(Y)\) are the restriction of scalars for a g-set

2. Proof

element chasing

Let \(g \in G\) and \(x \in X\), then it follows, that

\begin{align*} \psi(g \cdot_G x) =& \psi( \varphi(h) \cdot_H x) \\ =& \varphi(h) \cdot_H \psi(x) \\ =& g \cdot_G \psi(x) \end{align*}
