composite field of a normal field extensions as normal field extension

1. Proposition

Let \(L/K\) be an algebraic field extension and \(E,F \subseteq L\) subfields, such that \(E/K, F/K\) are normal field extensions Then the composite field \(EF\) is a normal field extenison over \(K\)

2. Proof

By normal field extension and splitting field, there exist polynomials \(\mathcal{F}_1,\mathcal{F}_2 \subesteq K[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} E =& K[\mathfrak{F}_1] \\ F =& K[\mathfrak{F}_2] \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} EF = K[\mathfrak{F}_1 \cup \mathfrak{F}_2] \end{align*}

2.1. a)

Suppose \(\alpha \in EF\), then it is generated by elements of \(E\) and \(F\). Those are generated by roots of \(\mathfrak{F}_1,\mathfrak{F}_2\), hence \(\alpha \in F[\mathfrak{F}_1 \cup \mathfrak{F}_2]\)

2.2. b)

Suppose \(\alpha \in K[\mathfrak{F}_1 \cup \mathfrak{F}_2]\), then it is generated by roots of \(\mathfrak{F}_1,\mathfrak{F}_2\) Those are by construction elements of \(E,F\), hence especially also in \(EF\)

Thus \(EF/K\) is a normal field extension, as it is a splitting field
