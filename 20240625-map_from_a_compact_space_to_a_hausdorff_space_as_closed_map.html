map from a compact space to a hausdorff space as closed map

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map. Suppose \(X\) is compact and \(Y\) closed.

Then \(f\) is a closed map

2. Proof

Let \(X \rightarrow Y\) be a continuous map from a compact hausdorff space and \(A \subseteq X\) a closed subset. Then \(A\) is compact (cf. closed subset of a compact space as compact set) and by image of compact space as compact space we conclude that \(f[A]\) is compact. Then by closedness of compact sets in Hausdorff spaces it follows that \(A\) is closed
