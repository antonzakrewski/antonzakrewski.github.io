monomorphism on singular chains and injective map

1. Proposition

Let \(X,Y\) be topological spaces, \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map.

TFAE:

  1. \(f_*: C_{n}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \rightarrow C_{n}^{\mathrm{sing}}(Y)\) is a algebraic group-monomorphism for \(n \in \mathbb{N}\)
  2. there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), such that \(f_*: C_{n}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \rightarrow C_{n}^{\mathrm{sing}}(Y)\) is a algebraic group-monomorphism.
  3. \(f\) is injective

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

Suppose \(x,x' \in X\) are different points. Then it follows, that the constant topological simplex

\begin{align*} f_*(\mathrm{const}_x) \neq f_{*}(\mathrm{const}_{x'}) \end{align*}

Hence it follows, that there exists an \(p \in \Delta^n\) with

\begin{align*} f_*(\mathrm{const}_x(p)) \neq f_*(\mathrm{const}_{x'}(p)) f(x) \neq f(x') \end{align*}

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(g,g'\) are different simplices. Then there exists a point \(p \in \Delta^n\), such that

\begin{align*} g(p) \neq g(p') \end{align*}

hence it follows, that

\begin{align*} f(g(p)) \neq f(g(p')) \end{align*}
