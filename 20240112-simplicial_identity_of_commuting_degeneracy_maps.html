simplicial identity of commuting degeneracy maps

1. Proposition

Given the simplex category, for \(i \leq j\), it holds for the degeneracy map

\begin{align*} \sigma_j \circ \sigma_i = \sigma_i \circ \sigma_{j+1} \end{align*}

2. Proof

\(\sigma_j \circ \sigma_i\) first unites the spots \(i,i+1\) and then the resulting \(j,j+1\), where both get shifted by \(1\), since \(i+1\) went missing. Hence they were originally \(j+1,j+2\). \(\sigma_i \circ \sigma_{j+1}\) first unites the spots \(j+1,j+2\) and then \(i,i+1\), which weren't shifted.
