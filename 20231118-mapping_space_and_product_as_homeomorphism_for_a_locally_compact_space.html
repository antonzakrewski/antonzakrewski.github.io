mapping space and product as homeomorphism for a locally compact space

1. Proposition

Let \(X_i\) be a familiy of topological spaces and \(Y\) locally regular compact. Suppose \(\mathrm{Map}()\) is the mapping space with the compact open topology. Then the for the categorical product, the canonical map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Map}(Y, \prod_{i \in I} X_i) \rightarrow \prod_{i \in I} \mathrm{Map}(Y, X_i) \end{align*}

is a homeomorphism

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • right adjoint preserves limits
  • covariant compact open functor as right adjoint for a locally compact space
