higher homotopy group of the d-torus

1. Proposition

Let \(d \in \mathbb{N}\) and \(n \geq 2\). Then the homotopy group of the d-torus

\begin{align*} \pi_n(T^d,1) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

note, that the d-torus is a quotient of a covering-like group action (cf. torus as Rn/Zn, missing: proof covering like)

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}^2 \times \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow& \mathbb{R}^2 \\ \end{align*}

hence by orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like, we get a covering map

\begin{align*} \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2/(\mathbb{Z}^2) \cong K \end{align*}

Thus since covering maps on higher homotopy groups induces isomorphisms, we get

\begin{align*} \pi_n(\mathbb{R}^2,1) \cong \pi_n(K,1) \end{align*}

furthermore, the homotopy group functor is homotopy invariant, hence since the euclidean space is contractible space and contractible spaces have trivial homotopy group we get

\begin{align*} 0 \cong \pi_n(*,*) \cong \pi_n(\mathbb{R}^2, 1) \cong \pi_n(K,1) \end{align*}
