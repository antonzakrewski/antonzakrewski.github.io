set theoretic complete intersection

1. Definition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space.

An affine variety \(Y \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\) is said to be a set-theoretic complete intersection, if there exists \(j \coloneqq n - \mathrm{dim}(Y)\) polynomials such that the radical of the generated Ideal is the vanishing ideal

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\langle f_1,...,f_j \rangle} = I(Y) \end{align*}
