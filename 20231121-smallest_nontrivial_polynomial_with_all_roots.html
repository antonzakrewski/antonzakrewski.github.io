smallest nontrivial polynomial for a prime field with all roots

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{F}_q\) a finite field for \(q = p^n\) and a prime number \(p\)

Then

\begin{align*} X^q - X = \prod_{\alpha \in \mathbb{F}_q} (X - \alpha) \end{align*}

2. Proof

By Little Fermat, we conclude, that for the multiplicative group of group order \(q - 1\), that

\begin{align*} (\alpha)^q = \alpha && \forall \alpha \in \mathbb{F}_q \setminus \{0\} \end{align*}

Furthermore

\begin{align*} (0)^q - 0 = 0 \end{align*}

Hence we have \(q\) roots and by polynomial division, the polynomial splits into

\begin{align*} \prod_{\alpha \in \mathbb{F}_q}(X - \alpha) \end{align*}
