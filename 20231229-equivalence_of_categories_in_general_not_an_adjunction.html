equivalence of categories in general not an adjunction

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{G}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be an equivalence with \(\eta: \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{C}} \rightarrow \mathcal{GF}, \epsilon: \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{D}} \rightarrow \mathcal{FG}\) Then \(\eta, \epsilon\) are in general not adjunctions

2. Proof

Let \(G \coloneqq \mathbb{Z} / 2 \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\) be the klein four-group. It is abelian (cf. externe direkte Produkt von abelschen Gruppen als abelsche Gruppe)

Let the categorification \(BG\) be \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\).

Then a natural isomorphism is precisely given by an element \(\eta(*), \epsilon(*)\) such that

(cf. natural transformation between categorified groups and factorization through inner automorphism )
