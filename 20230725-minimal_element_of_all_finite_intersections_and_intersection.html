minimal element of all finite intersections and intersection

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a set, \(S_i \subseteq X\) a family of subsets, \(\mathcal{S}\) the set of finite intersections and \(S \in \mathcal{S}\) a minimal element. Then

\begin{align*} S = \bigcap_{i \in I} S_i \end{align*}

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition Suppose \(S \supseteq \bigcap_{i \in I} S_i\), then there exists an element \(m \in S \setminus \bigcap_{i \in I} S_i\). By Assumption, there also exists an \(S_m\) such that \(m \not\in S_m\) and therefore

\begin{align*} S \cap S_m \end{align*}

is a finite intersection with

\begin{align*} (S \cap S_m) \subsetneq S \end{align*}

Therefore, \(S\) can't be a minimal element
