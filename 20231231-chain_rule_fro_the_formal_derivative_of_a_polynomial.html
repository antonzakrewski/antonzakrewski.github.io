chain rule for the formal derivative of a polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(f,g \in R[X]\) polynomials and \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\) the formal derivative of a polynomial. Then it holds, that

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}f(g) =& f'(g) \cdot g' \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. monomial

we show, that

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} g(x)^{n} =& n \cdot g(x)^{n-1} g'(x) \\ =& (x^n)'(g(x)) \cdot g' \end{align*}

2.1.1. base case

proof by induction

for \(n = 1\) we get

\begin{align*} 1 \cdot g(x)^{1-1} \cdot g'(x) =& 1 \cdot 1 \cdot g'(x) \end{align*}

for \(n = 0\) we get

\begin{align*} (g(x)^0)' =& (1)' \\ =& 0 \\ \widehat{=}& (0 \cdot g(x)^{-1} \cdot g'(x)) \end{align*}

2.1.2. induction step

By the product rule for the formal derivation of a polynomial, it holds, that

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} g(x)^{n} =& \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(g(x)^{n-1} \cdot g(x)) \\ =& n - 1 \cdot g(x)^{n-2} \cdot g'(x) \cdot g(x) + g'(x) \cdot g(x)^{n-1} \\ =& n - 1 \cdot g(x)^{n-1} \cdot g'(x) + g'(x) \cdot g(x)^{n-1} \\ =& n \cdot g(x)^{n-1} \cdot g'(x) \end{align*}

2.2. reduction to monomials

follows from differentiation as homomorphism, as for \(f = \sum \alpha_i X^i\)

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} f(g(x)) =& \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} \sum \alpha_i (g(x))^i \\ =& \sum \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} \alpha_i g(x))^i \\ =& \sum \alpha_i \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} g(x))^i \\ =& \sum \alpha_i i \cdot g(x))^{i-1} \cdot g'(x) \\ =& f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x) \end{align*}
