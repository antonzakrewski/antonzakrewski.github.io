prime local ring and ring as union of units and nilradical

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring.

TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is prime local
  2. \(\mathcal{N}_A\) is a maximal ideal
  3. the ring is the union of the nilradical and the units
  4. the quotient ring \(A/\mathcal{N}_A\) is a field

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from

  • maximal ideal as prime ideal
  • Nilradical as intersection of prime ideals

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

follows from

  • maximal ideal and non-unit

where the maximal ideal is given by the set of nilpotent elements.

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 4)

the set of non-units is an ideal, hence by maximal ideal and non-units \(\mathcal{N}_A\) is maximal. Thus by maximal ideal of a commutative ring and quotient ring as field \(A/ \mathcal{N}_A\) is field.

2.4. 4) \(\implies\) 1)

follows by maximal ideal and quotient ring as field, as \(\mathcal{N}_A\) is a maximal ideal Then by nilradical as intersection of prime ideals, we conculde that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{N}_A = \bigcap \mathfrak{p} \subesteq \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

hence \(\mathcal{N}_A\) is the only prime ideal
