homology of the hom chain complex as abelian group of chain homotopy classes

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes and \(C,D\) chain complexes. Then the first homology of the hom chain complex

\begin{align*} H_1(\underline{Hom}(C,D)) \end{align*}

is isomorphic to the abelian group \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})}(C,D)/\sim\) modulo chain homotopy

2. Proof

2.1. cycles as chain maps

Let \([(\varphi_p)] \in H_1(\underline{Hom}(C,D))\) up to chain homotopy Note that by definition a boundary satisfies

\begin{align*} d_0(\varphi_p) =& d_{p}^D \circ \varphi_p + (-1)^{0 + 1} \varphi_{p-1} \circ d_p^C \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

or that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_{p} \arrow[]{r}{\varphi_{p}} \arrow[]{d}{d_p} & C_{p-1} \arrow[]{d}{\varphi_{p-1}}\\ D_{p} \arrow[]{r}{d_p} & D_{p-1} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

2.2. up to chain homotopy

Let \(\varphi, \psi\) be maps such that the cosets are equal

\begin{align*} (\varphi_p) + \mathrm{im}(d_1) = (\psi_p) + \mathrm{im}(d_1) \end{align*}

Then there exists an \((\mu_p) \in \prod \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(C_{p-1},D_p)\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_{p+1} \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{p} \arrow[]{ld}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{p-1} \arrow[]{ld}{}\\ D_{p+1} \arrow[]{r}{} & D_{p} \arrow[]{r}{} & D_{p-1} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

such that

\begin{align*} (\varphi_p) + d_{-1}^{\underline{Hom}} (\mu_p) = (\psi_p) \end{align*}

Calculating \(d_{-1}^{\underline{Hom}}\)

\begin{align*} d_{-1}^{\underline{Hom}}(\mu_p) =& d_{p+1}^D \circ \mu_p + (-1)^{-1 + 1} \mu_{p-1} \circ d_{p} \\ \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} (\varphi_p) + d_{-1}^{\underline{Hom}} (\mu_p) =& (\psi_p) & & \vert - ( \varphi_p) d_{p+1}^D \circ \mu_p + (-1)^{-1 + 1} \mu_{p-1} \circ d_{p} =z (\varphi_p - \psi_p) \end{align*}

so \(\mu_p\) is a chain homotopy
