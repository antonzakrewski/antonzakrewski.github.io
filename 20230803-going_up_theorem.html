Going up theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(A \subseteq B\) be rings such that \(B\) is integral over \(A\) and \(\mathfrak{p}_1 \subseteq ... \subseteq \mathfrak{p}_m ... \subseteq \mathfrak{p}_n \subseteq A\) be an ascending chain of prime ideals in \(A\).

If there are prime ideals \(\mathfrak{q}_1 \subseteq ... \subseteq \mathfrak{q}_m \subseteq B\) such that \(\mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{q}_i \cap A\), then there are prime ideals

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{q}_{m+1} \subseteq ... \subseteq \mathfrak{q}_m \subseteq B \end{align*}

with \(\mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{q}_i \cap A\)
