categorical product in Set

1. Proposition

Given the category set, \(\mathrm{Set}\) and sets \(S_i \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Set})\) for an indexing set \(i \in I\), the categorical product with the canonical projection is the Cartesian product \(\prod S_i\)

2. Proof

2.1. construction of a map

Let \(A\) be a set and

\begin{align*} f_i: A \rightarrow S_i \end{align*}

be maps

Then let

\begin{align*} f: A \rightarrow& \prod_{i \in I} S_i \\ a \mapsto& (f_i(a))_{i \in I} \end{align*}

Then by construction

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{rd}{f_i} \arrow[]{r}{f}& \prod S_i \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{pr}_i}\\ & S_i \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

2.2. uniqueness

Let \(g\) be a map making the diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{rd}{f_i} \arrow[]{r}{g}& \prod S_i \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{pr}_i}\\ & S_i \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute

Then for \(a \in A\) it follows, that for the \(i\)-th component

\begin{align*} (\mathrm{pr}_i \circ g) (a) = f_i(a) \end{align*}

hence by construction of \(\mathrm{pr}_i\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} g(a) =& (f_i(a))_{i \in I} \\ =& f(a) \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} g = f \end{align*}
