localization and polynomial ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S\) a multiplicatively closed subset. Then for the localization and the quotient ring, it holds that

\begin{align*} R[X_s]/(s \cdot X_s - 1) \cong S^{-1}R \end{align*}

2. Proof

Consider the ring-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: R[X_s] \rightarrow& S^{-1}R \\ r \mapsto& r X_s \mapsto& s^{-1} \end{align*}

It is by definition welldefined and surjective.

Suppose \(m = \sum \alpha_i g_i \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\), then

\begin{align*} 0 = \alpha_i \cdot \varphi(g_i) && \vert \cdot \end{align*}
