sheafification functor

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space Then the sheafification functor is defined as functor from category of presheaves to the category of sheaves

\begin{align*} \mathrm{sheafify}: \mathrm{Psh}(\mathrm{Op}(X)) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{Op}(X)) \\ \mathcal{F} \mapsto& \mathcal{F}^{\#} (\eta: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}) \mapsto& (\eta^{\#}: \mathcal{F}^{\#} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}^{\#}) \end{align*}

where \(\mathcal{F}^{\#}\) is the sheafification and \(\eta^\#\) is the unique morphism determined the universal property for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F} \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[dashed]{rd}{\iota \circ \eta} \arrow[]{r}{\eta} & \mathcal{G} \arrow[]{d}{\iota}\\ \mathcal{F}^{\#} \arrow[swap, dashed]{r}{\exists! \eta^{\#}} & \mathcal{G}^{\#} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
