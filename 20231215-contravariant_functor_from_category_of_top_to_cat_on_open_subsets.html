contravariant functor from Category of Top to Cat on open Subsets

1. Definition / Proposition

Given category Top and Category Cat, there exists the natural contravariant functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Op}: \mathrm{Top} \rightarrow \mathrm{Cat} \\ X \mapsto& \mathrm{Op}(X) (f: X \rightarrow Y) \mapsto& (\mathcal{F}_f: \mathrm{Op}(Y) \rightarrow \mathrm{Op}(X)) \end{align*}

where \(X\) is the Category of Open Subsets and \(\mathcal{F}_f\) is the contravariant functor from Category of Top to Cat on open Subsets.

2. Proof

2.1. identity

follows from \(\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{id}} = \mathrm{id}_{\mathrm{Op}(Y)}\) as identity functor

2.2. composition

follows from the definition, as for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} f: X \rightarrow Y \\ g: Y \rightarrow Z \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and \(O \substeq Z\)

\begin{align*} (\mathcal{F}_g \circ \mathcal{F}_f)[O] =& f^{-1} \circ g^{-1}[O] \\ =& (g \circ f)^{-1}[O] \\ =& \mathcal{F}_{(g \circ f)}[O] \end{align*}
