preimage of a multiplicatively closed subset as multiplicatively closed subset

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be rings, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) a ring-homomorphism and \(S \subseteq B\) a multiplicatively closed subset . Then the preimage \(T \coloneqq \varphi^{-1}[S] \subseteq A\) is also multiplicatively closed.

2. Proof

Note that \(\varphi(1) = 1 \in S\), hence \(1 \in T\). Suppose \(a,b \in T\), then it follows, that

\begin{align*} \varphi(a) \cdot \varphi(b) \in S \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \varphi(a \cdot b) \in S \end{align*}

and therefore also \(a \cdot b \in S\)
