affine space as irreducible space

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space. Then \(\mathbb{A}_K^n\) with the zariski topology is an irreducible topological space

2. Proof

2.1. proof a)

Let \(V(S), V(T)\) be algebraic set such that \(V(S) \cup V(T) = \mathbb{A}_K^n\). Then by union of algebraic sets we conclude, that

\begin{align*} V(S) \cup V(T) =& V(S \cdot T) \\ =& \mathbb{A}_K^n \\ =& V(0)$ \end{align*}

where \(0\) is unique up to radicals (see: Bijection of radical ideals and affine algebraic sets in an algebraically closed field) According to:

  1. polynomial ring over an integral domain as integral domain
  2. integral domain and nonzero product of ideals

we conclude, that w.l.o.g. \(S = 0\), hence \(V(S) = V(0) = \mathbb{A}_K^n\) Thus \(\mathbb{A}_K^n\) is irreducible

2.2. proof b)

follows from

  • polynomial ring over an integral domain as integral domain
  • zero ideal as prime ideal and integral domain
  • bijection between prime ideals and affine varieties

where

\begin{align*} I(\mathbb{A}_K^n) = (0) \end{align*}
