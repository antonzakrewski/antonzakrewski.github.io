von Neumann regular ring as reduced ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a von Neumann regular ring. Then \(R\) is reduced

2. Proof

Suppose there exists an \(a \in R\) such that \(a^n = 0\). w.l.o.g. let \(n = 2\) - e.g. by taking \(a' \coloneqq a^{\mathrm{ceil}(\frac{n}{2})}\) then we conclude

\begin{align*} a =& a^2 \cdot x \\ =& 0 \cdot x \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

for all \(x \in R\) and hence \(a = 0\)
