trivial presentation of a group

1. Definition

Let \(G\) be group, then the trivial presentation of \(G\) is defined as presentation

\begin{align*} \langle G \vert R_G\rangle \end{align*}

where \(R_G = g h \cdot k^{-1}\) for

\begin{align*} g \cdot h = k \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \iota: \langle G \vert R_G\rangle \rightarrow G g \mapsto& g \end{align*}

be the induced map,

2.1. welldefined

Suppose \(g_1 = g_2\), then there exist \(g_i \cdot h_i \cdot k^{-1}_i\) such that

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot \prod g_i \cdot h_i \cdot k^{-1}_i = g_2 \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} \iota(g_2) =& \iota(g_1 \cdot \prod g_i \cdot h_i \cdot k^{-1}_i) \\ =& \iota(g_1) \cdot \iota(\prod g_i \cdot h_i \cdot k^{-1}_i) \\ =& \iota(g_1) \cdot \prod \iota(g_i \cdot h_i \cdot k^{-1}_i) \\ =& \iota(g_1) \cdot \prod 1 \\ =& \iota(g_1) \end{align*}

2.2. homomorphism

follows from construction

2.3. surjective

follows from \(\iota(g) = g\)

2.4. injective

Note that

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot g_2 = g_1g_2 \end{align*}

as

\begin{align*} g_1g_2 =& 1 \cdot g_1g_2 \\ =& (g_1 \cdot g_2 \cdot (g_1g_2)^{-1}) \cdot g_1g_2 \\ =& g_1 \cdot g_2 \end{align*}

hence for \(\iota(g_1 \cdot g_2 ... \cdot g_n) = 1\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} g_1g_2...g_n = 1 \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot ... \cdot g_n = 1 \end{align*}

hence by minimal kernel and monomorphism, \(\iota\) is injective
