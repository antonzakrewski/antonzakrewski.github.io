functor from the categorified group induces homomorphism on Automorphism subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(BG\) the categorification and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: BG \rightarrow \mathcal{C} \end{align*}

a functor. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) induces an group-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: G \rightarrow \mathrm{Aut}_{\mathcal{F}(*)} \end{align*}

2. Proof

corollary of

  • functor as homomorphism for the automorphism group, thus
\begin{align*} G = \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) = \mathrm{Aut}(*) \rightarrow \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{F}(*) \end{align*}
