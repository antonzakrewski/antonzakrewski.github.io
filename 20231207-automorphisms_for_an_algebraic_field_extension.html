K-automorphisms for an algebraic field extension and simple field extension

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) an algebraic field extension with degree \(n\) and \(\alpha \in L\).

Suppose there exist \(n\) K-Automorphisms \(\sigma_i: L \rightarrow L\) with \(\sigma_i(\alpha) \neq \sigma_{j}(\alpha)\).

Then \(L = K(\alpha)\) is a simple field extension

2. Proof

Let \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{k} \lambda_i X^i\) be the minimal polynomial. Then we conclude, that for \(j \in \{1,...,n\}\)

\begin{align*} 0 =& \sigma_{j}( 0) \\ =& \sigma_j(\sum_{i=0}^{k} \lambda_i \alpha^i) \end{align*}

Since \(\sigma_j\) is \(K\)-linear, we conculde, that

\begin{align*} 0 =& ... \\ =& \sigma_j(\sum_{i=0}^{k} \lambda_i \alpha^i) \\ =& \sum_{i=0}^{k} \lambda_i \sigma_j(\alpha)^i \end{align*}

Hence \(f\) has at least \(\sigma_1(\alpha),...,\sigma_n(\alpha)\) as roots, therefore at least \(n\) roots.

Furthermore, by degree of a simple field extension and degree of the minimal polynomial, we conclude that

\begin{align*} [K(\alpha) : K] \geq n \end{align*}

By Gradlemma, since \(K(\alpha) \subseteq L\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} n =& [L : K] \\ =& [L : K(\alpha)] \cdot [K(\alpha) \cdot K] \\ \leq& [L : K(\alpha)] \cdot n \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} [L : K(\alpha)] = 1 \end{align*}

or \(L = K(\alpha)\)
