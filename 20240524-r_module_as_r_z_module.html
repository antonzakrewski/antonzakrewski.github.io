R-module as R,Z module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod and \(\mathrm{R-\mathbb{Z}-Mod}\) the category of bimodules Then there exists a fully faithful embedding

\begin{align*} \iota: \mathrm{RMod} \rightarrow& \mathrm{R-\mathbb{Z}-Mod} \\ M \mapsto& M \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

2.1.1. object

For \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) we define

\begin{align*} M \cdot_{\mathbb{Z}} n \coloneqq n_r \cdot_R M \end{align*}

where \(n_r = 1(n)\) for the initial morphism \(\mathbb{Z} \rightarrow R\) in category Ring (cf. Initial Object in Ring)

2.1.1.1. module structure

follows from restriction of scalars

2.1.1.2. compatibility

follows from integers as subring of the center: For \(m \in M\) and \(r \in R, n \in \mathbb{Z}\) we conclude

\begin{align*} (r \cdot_R m) \cdot_\mathbb{Z} n =& n_r \cdot_R r \cdot m \\ =& r \cdot_R n_r \cdot m \\ =& r \cdot (m \cdot_{\mathbb{Z}} n) \\ =& r \cdot_R (m \cdot_{\mathbb{Z}} n) \end{align*}

2.1.2. morphism

follows from

\begin{align*} \varphi(m \cdot_{\mathbb{Z}} n) =& \varphi(n_r \cdot_R m) \\ =& n_r \cdot_R \varphi(m) \\ =& \varphi(m) \cdot_{\mathbb{Z}} n \end{align*}

2.2. fully faithful

follows as this is an isomorphism onto the Image given by forgetting the right-\(\mathbb{Z}\) mod structure (cf. isomorphism in category cat as object-bijective, fully faithful functor)
