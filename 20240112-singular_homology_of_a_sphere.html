singular homology of a sphere

1. Proposition

Given the n-sphere for \(n \geq 1\) and the singular homology functor, it holds, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{H}_n(S^k) \cong \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z} & \mbox{if } n = 0,k \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(n,k = 1\)

Note that

\begin{align*} H_1(S^1) =& \mathrm{coker}(0 \rightarrow H_1(S^1)) \\ =& \mathrm{coker}(H_1(x) \rightarrow H_1(S^1)) \\ =& \tilde{H}_1(S^1) \\ \end{align*}

Hence by the suspension isomorphism, we get

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}_1(S^1) =& \tilde{H}_1(\Sigma (* \coprod *)) \cong \tilde{H}_0(* \coprod *) \end{align*}

Applying singular homology preserves coproducts, it holds, that

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}_0(* \coprod *) =& \mathrm{coker}(H_0(*) \rightarrow H_0(* \coprod *)) \\ =& \mathrm{coker}(H_0(*) \rightarrow H_0(*) \coprod H_0(*)) =& \mathrm{coker}(\mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z} \\ z \mapsto& (z,0) \end{align*}

is the group-homomorphism, since the singular homology preserevs canonicallness

Hence

\begin{align*} \mathrm{coker}(\mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}) = \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2.2. \(n = k\)

follows from induction and the suspension isomorphism, as

\begin{align*} \Sigma S^{n-1} = S^{n} \end{align*}

2.3. \(n \geq k, n \neq 0\)

\begin{align*} H_n(S^k) \cong \mathrm{coker}(0 \rightarrow H_n(S^k)) \\ =& \mathrm{coker}(H_n(*) \rightarrow H_n(S^k)) \\ =& \tilde{H}_n(S^k) \\ \cong& \tilde{H}_{n-1}(S^{k-1}) \\ ... \\ \cong& \tilde{H}_{n-k}(* \coprod *) \\ =& \mathrm{coker}(H_{n-k}(*) \rightarrow H_{n-k}(* \coprod *)) \\ =& \mathrm{coker}(H_{n-k}(*) \rightarrow H_{n-k}(*) \coprod H_{n-k}(*)) \\ =& \mathrm{coker}(0 \rightarrow 0 \coprod 0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
