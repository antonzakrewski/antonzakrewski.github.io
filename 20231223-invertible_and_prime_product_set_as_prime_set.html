invertible and prime product set as prime set

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain

\begin{align*} T = A^{\times} \cup \{p_1 \cdot ... \cdot p_n\} \end{align*}

the invertible and prime product set

Suppose \(\alpha \cdot \beta \in T\), then so are \(\alpha, \beta \in T\)

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(\alpha\)

2.1. unit

follows from product as unit and parts as unit

2.2. prime product

follows from prime decomposition of a product and factors for an integral domain
