singular boundary map and Yoneda Lemma

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{Top}\) be the category Top and

\begin{align*} d_n: C_{n}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{n-1}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \end{align*}

be the boundary map

Then \(d_n\) is a natural transformation given by

\begin{align*} \simga = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \iota(\partial \Delta_{\mathrm{top}}^n) \in C_{n-1}^{\mathrm{sing}}(\Delta_{n}) \end{align*}

see

  • natural transformation from covariant hom functor as elemente

2. Proof

Since

\begin{align*} C_n^{\mathrm{sing}}(-) = (\mathbb{Z}[\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Top}}(\Delta_n^{\mathrm{top}}, -)]) \end{align*}

where \(\mathbb{Z}[]\) is the free abelian group functor, we conclude, that the morphism \(d_n\) naturally corresponds to a morphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Top}}(\Delta_n^{\mathrm{top}}, -) \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}((\mathbb{Z}[\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Top}}(\Delta_{n-1}^{\mathrm{top}}, -)])) \end{align*}

using the yoneda lemma, this corresponds to a

\begin{align*} \sigma \in \mathrm{Set}(\mathbb{Z}[\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Top}}(\Delta_{n-1}^{\mathrm{top}}, \Delta_{n}^{\mathrm{top}}) \end{align*}

Writing the down morphism, this gives the element

\begin{align*} \sigma = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \iota(\partial \Delta_{\mathrm{top}}^n) \in C_{n-1}^{\mathrm{sing}}(\Delta_{n}) \end{align*}

As

\begin{align*} \eta_{X}(\tilde{\sigma}) =& \tilde{\sigma} \circ (\sum \iota(\partial \Delta_{n}^{\mathrm{top}} \\ =& \sum \partial \sigma \circ \Delta_n^{\mathrm{top}} =& \sum \partial \sigma \end{align*}

since taking the boundary of a simplex commutes with a map, as it depends on the domain.
