simplicial set as colimit over skeleta

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a simplicial set, then for the comparison map

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... & \mathrm{sk}_{n}(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{sk}_{n+1}(X) & .. \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

it holds that \(X\) is a colimit

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \mathrm{sk}_n(X) \simeq X \end{align*}

2. Proof

Follows from counit of a n skeleton as bijection up to degree n
