fixed subring

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(G \subseteq \mathrm{Aut}(R)\) be a subgroup of the automorphism group. Then the fixed subring is defined as subring

\begin{align*} R^G = \{r \in R \vert \varphi(r) = 0 \forall \varphi \in G\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. single automorphism

2.1.1. \(0,1\)

follows from the requirement, that \(\varphi(0) = 0, \varphi(1) = 1\)

2.1.2. additive, multiplicative closure

Let \(\alpha, \beta \in R^G\), then for \(\varphi \in G\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} \varphi( \alpha + \beta) =& \varphi(\alpha) + \varphi(\beta) \\ =& \alpha + \beta \end{align*}

hence \(\alpha + \beta \in R^G\), since \(\varphi\) was arbitrary

2.1.3. additive inverse

Let \(\alpha \in R^G\), then

\begin{align*} 0 =& \varphi(0) \\ =& \varphi(\alpha - \alpha) \\ =& \alpha + \varphi(-\alpha) \end{align*}

thus \(\varphi(- \alpha) = - \alpha\)

2.2. general case

follows from intersection of subrings as subring
