integral domain and multiplication with non-unit not associated

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(\alpha, \beta \in R\). Suppose \(\alpha \not\in R^{\times}\) is not a unit TFAE:

  1. \(\alpha \cdot \beta \sim \beta\) are assoziierte Elemente
  2. \(\alpha\) is a unit

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption there exists a unit \(\gamma\) such that

\begin{align*} \gamma \cdot \alpha \cdot \beta = \beta \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} (1 - \gamma \cdot \alpha) \cdot \beta = 0 \end{align*}

this shows that \(\gamma \cdot \alpha = 1\), since \(R\) is a unique factorization domain

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from multiplication with \(\alpha^{-1}\)
