additive functor preserving chain homotopies and null homotopies

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A}), \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{B})\) be the categories of chain complexes. Suppose \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{B})\) is an additive functor.

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{F}\) preserves chain homotopies
  2. \(\mathcal{F}\) preserves nullhomotopic chain map

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(f \cong g\) be chain homotopic maps

\begin{align*} f,g: A_* \rightarrow B_* \end{align*}

Then using addition of chain homotopic maps we get

\begin{align*} f - g \cong 0 f - g + \mathrm{id} \cong \mathrm{id} \end{align*}

Then we conclude

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(f - g + \mathrm{id}) \cong \mathcal{F} \end{align*}
