cellular approximation

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X), (Y, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(Y)))\) be CW-Complexes and

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

a continuous map such that there exists a \(k \geq -1\) with

\begin{align*} f[ \mathrm{SK}_k(X)] \subseteq f[ \mathrm{SK}_k(Y)] \end{align*}

Then there exists a cellular map \(f'\) and a homotopy relative to \(\mathrm{SK}_k(X)\) from \(f\) to \(f'\)
