power of a prime ideal as subset and radical

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a}\) an ideal and \(\mathfrak{p} \supseteq \mathfrak{a}\) a prime ideal such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p}^n \subseteq \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(p \in \mathfrak{p}\), then it follows that \(p^n \in \mathfrak{p}\). Therefore \(p \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{p}}\) showing \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\). Since \(\mathfrak{p}\) is prime and hence radical (cf. prime ideal as radical ideal) we also get by radical of subsets

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{p}} \supseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}
