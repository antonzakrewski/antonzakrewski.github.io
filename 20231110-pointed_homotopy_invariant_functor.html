pointed homotopy invariant functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{Top}_* \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be a functor from category Top*. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is said to be pointed homotopy invariant, if for pointed homotopic maps \(f \sim g, f,g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Top}_*}(X,Y)\), it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(f) =& \mathcal{F}(g) \end{align*}
