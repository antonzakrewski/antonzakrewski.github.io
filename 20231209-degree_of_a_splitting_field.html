degree of a splitting field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f \in K[X]\) a polynomial of degree \(n\) and \(L\) a splitting field. Then for the degree of the field extension, it holds, that

\begin{align*} [L : K] \mid n! \end{align*}

2. Proof

proof by strong induction

2.1. \(n = 1\)

for \(n = 1\), our polynomial splits by assumption, hence we have \(K\) as splitting field and thus degree \(1\) or \(1 \mid 1 = 1!\)

2.2. \(n \rightarrow n + 1\)

Let \(f\) be a polynomial of degree \(n + 1\).

2.2.1. irreducible

Then choose a root \(\alpha\) in an closure \(\overline{K}\). Then \(f\) is up to scalar the minimal polynomial, and hence \(K(\alpha)\) has degree \(n + 1\). Therefore,

\begin{align*} f = (X - \alpha) \cdot g \end{align*}

for some \(g\) with degree \(\mathrm{deg}(g) = n\) Hence by Gradlemma and induction hypothesis,

\begin{align*} [K(f) : K] =& [K(\alpha) : K] \cdot [K(g) : K(\alpha)] =& (n + 1) \cdot [K(g) : K(\alpha)] \mid (n + 1) \cdot n! = (n + 1)! \end{align*}

2.2.2. reducible

Let

\begin{align*} f =& g_1 \cdot ... \cdot g_r \\ =& g_1 \cdot f_2 \end{align*}

with \(g_1,...,g_r\) irreducible (cf. Polynomring über einem Körper als faktorieller Ring)

Then since

\begin{align*} K(f) = K(g_1)(f_2) \end{align*}

(cf. splitting field of a product of polynomials)

by Gradlemma , we conclude, that

\begin{align*} [K(f) : K] =& [K(g_1)(f_2) : K] \\ =& [K(g_1)(f_2) : K(g_1)] \cdot [K(g_1) : K] \end{align*}

where by induction hypothesis, \([K(g_1)(f_2) : K(g_1)] \mid (\mathrm{deg}(g_1))!, [K(g_1) : K] \mid \mathrm{deg}(f_2)!\) Therfore, by product of factorials divides factorial of the sum and Grad von Polynomen unter Multiplikation in einem Integritätsbereich we conclude, that

\begin{align*} (\mathrm{deg}(g_1))! \cdot \mathrm{deg}(f_2)! \mid ( \mathrm{deg}(g_1) + \mathrm{deg}(f_2))! =& (\mathrm{deg}(g_1 \cdot f_2))! = (n+1)! \end{align*}

Hence by transitivity of \(\mid\)

\begin{align*} [K(f) : K] =& [K(g_1)(f_2) : K(g_1)] \cdot [K(g_1) : K] \mid (n+1)! \end{align*}
