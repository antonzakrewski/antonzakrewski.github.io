free group action and unique action path

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(X\) a set and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a free group action. Then for \(x_1, x_2 \in X\), there exists at most one \(g \in G\) such that

\begin{align*} g \cdot x_1 = x_2 \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose there exists \(g_1,g_2 \in G\) such that \(g_1 x_1 = g_2 x_1 = x_2\) Then we conclude, that

\begin{align*} g_2^{-1} g_1 x_1 =& g_2^{-1} \cdot g_2 \cdot x_1 \\ =& e \cdot x_1 \\ =& x_1 \end{align*}

hence by freeness, it follows, that

\begin{align*} g_2^{-1} \cdot g_1 =& e \\ g_1 =& g_2 \end{align*}
