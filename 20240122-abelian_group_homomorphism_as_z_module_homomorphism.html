abelian group homomorphism as Z-module homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(M,N\) be abelian groups and \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) a group-homomorphism Then \(\varphi\) is a module-homomorphism for \(M,N\) as modules over the integers

see: abelian group as module over the integers

2. Proof

It remains to show linearity Let \(z \in \mathbb{Z}\), then

\begin{align*} \varphi(z \cdot m) =& \varphi( \sum_{i=1}^{\vert z \vert} \mathrm{sgn}(z) m) \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{\vert z \vert} \varphi(\mathrm{sgn}(z) m) \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{\vert z \vert} \cdot \mathrm{sgn}(z) \varphi(m) \\ =& z \cdot \varphi(m) \end{align*}
