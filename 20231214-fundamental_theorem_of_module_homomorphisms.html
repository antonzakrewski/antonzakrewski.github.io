fundamental theorem of module homomorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M\) a \(R\)-module \(N \subseteq M\) a submodule. Suppose

\begin{align*} \pi_N: M \rightarrow M/N \end{align*}

is the canonical projection to the quotient module

Then for each module-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: M \rightarrow P \end{align*}

such that \(N \subseteq \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\), then there exists a unique module-homomorphism \(\psi: M/N \rightarrow P\) such that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M \arrow[]{r}{\pi} \arrow[]{rd}{\varphi}& M/N \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists! \psi}\\ & P \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. uniqueness

Suppose we have \(\psi_1,\psi_2\) satisfying the condition. then we get

\begin{align*} \psi_1 \circ \pi = \psi_2 \circ \pi \end{align*}

Since \(\pi\) is surjective, by surjective map as epimorphism we conclude, that \(\pi\) is an epimorphism. Hence it follows, that

\begin{align*} \psi_1 = \psi_2 \end{align*}

2.2. existence

Let

\begin{align*} \psi: M/n \rightarrow& P \\ m + N \mapsto& \varphi(m) \end{align*}

2.2.1. welldefined map

Let \(m + N = m' + N\). Then there exists an \(n \in N\) such that \(m + n = m'\). Therefore,

\begin{align*} \psi(m + N) =& \varphi(m) \\ =& \varphi(m) + 0 \\ =& \varphi(m) + \varphi(n) \\ =& \varphi(m + n) \\ =& \varphi(m') \\ =& \psi(m' + N) \end{align*}

2.2.2. module homomorphism

2.2.2.1. additive
\begin{align*} \psi( m + N + m' + N) =& \psi(m + m' + N) \\ =& \varphi(m + m') \\ =& \varphi(m) + \varphi(m') \\ =& \psi(m + N) + \psi(m' + N) \end{align*}
2.2.2.2. multiplication
\begin{align*} \psi(r \cdot (m + N)) =& \psi( rm + N) \\ =& \varphi(rm) \\ =& r \cdot \varphi(m) \\ =& r \cdot \psi(m + N) \end{align*}

2.2.3. commutes

follows from construction
