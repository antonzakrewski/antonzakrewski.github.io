regular hausdorff space and compact subset as union of a finite cover

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a regular hausdorff space, \(U_i\) an arbitrary open cover and \(K\) a compact subset Then there exist finitely many compact subsets \(C_i \subseteq U_i\) such that

\begin{align*} K = \bigcup_{i \in \{1,...,n\}} C_i \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(U_1,...,U_n\) be finitely many open subsets such that

\begin{align*} K \subseteq \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i \end{align*}

Then for each point \(x \in U_i \cap K\) we may find a closed neighbourhood \(V_i \subseteq U_i\) of \(x_i\) (cf. equivalent characterization of a t3 space) Repeating this for each point \(x \in U_i \cap K\) for \(i \in \{1,..,n\}\) we get a cover with open interior of \(K\). Again by compactness we conclude that finitely many \(V_i\) suffice. Then for each \(U_i\) pick the subsets \(V_i \subseteq U_i\), take the finite union \(\bigcup V_i\), which is closed and intersect with \(K\). Denote this intersection by \(C_i\). It is closed and a subset of \(K\), hence compact (cf. closed subset of a compact space as compact set)

By construction

\begin{align*} K = \bigcup C_i \end{align*}
