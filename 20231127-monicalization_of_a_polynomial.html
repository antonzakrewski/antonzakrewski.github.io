simple monicalization of a multivariate polynomial over an infinite field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an infinite field and \(f \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) be a polynomial different from the zero polynomial. Then there exist \(\lambda, \alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1} \in K\) such that

\begin{align*} f' = \lambda f(y_1 + \alpha_1 y_n, y_2 + \alpha_2 y_n, ...,y_{n-1} + \alpha_{n-1} y_n, y_n) \in K[Y_1,...,Y_n] \end{align*}

is monic as an element of \((K[y_1,...,y_{n-1}])[y_n]\)

2. Proof

Let \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \beta_i \prod_{j=1}^n X_j^{\nu_{j,i}}\). Then evaluating \(y_1 + \alpha_i y_n, ..., y_{n-1} + \alpha_{n-1} y_n, y_n\) results in

\begin{align*} f(...) =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \beta_i \prod_{j=1}^n (\alpha_j y_j + y_n)^{\nu_{j,i}} \end{align*}

We can consider the monomials with highest degree \(d\) and then get

\begin{align*} f(...) =& \sum \beta (\prod_{i \neq n} \alpha_i^k) y_n^d \end{align*}

with \(\beta \neq 0\). let

\begin{align*} f_d(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1}) \coloneqq \sum \beta (\prod_{i \neq n} \alpha_{i}) \end{align*}

by construction, it is a homogeneous polynomial, hence by homogeneous polynomial over an infinite integral domain and non-root there exist \(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1}\) such that

\begin{align*} f_d(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1}) \neq 0 \end{align*}

Thus for choosing \(\lambda = f_d(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1})^{-1}\), we get

\begin{align*} f' = y_n^d + \sum_{i=0}^{d-1} (\gamma) y_n^d \in (K[X_1,...,X_{n-1}])[X_n] \end{align*}

as monic
