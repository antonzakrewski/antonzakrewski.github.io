fundamental groupoid of a space

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space. then the fundamental groupoid \(\pi_{\leq 1}(X)\) or \(\tau_{\leq 1}\) of \(X\) is the groupoid with

  1. points of \(x\) as objects
  2. paths between points up to homotopy rel endpoints, i.e. \(\pi_0(\Omega_{x,x'}(X))\)

where the composition of morphisms is defined as concatenation of paths (after reparametrization)
