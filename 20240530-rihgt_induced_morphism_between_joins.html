right induced morphism between joins

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(X,Y,Y'\) be simplicial sets, \(- \star -\) the join and \(\varphi: Y \rightarrow Y'\) a morphism of simplicial sets. Then there exists an induced morphism of simplicial sets

\begin{align*} X \star \varphi: X \star Y \rightarrow& X \star Y' \\ [n] \mapsto& \overline{\varphi}[n] \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \overline{\varphi}[n]: \coprod_{(I_n,J_n) \in \mathrm{Cut}([n])} X(I_n) \times Y(J_n) \rightarrow \coprod_{(I_n,J_n) \in \mathrm{Cut}([n])} X(I_n) \times Y'(J_n) \end{align*}

is the morphism induced by universal property of the coproduct and the universal property of the product for

\begin{align*} (\varphi([n],\mathrm{id}): X(I_n) \times Y(J_n) \rightarrow X(I_n) \times Y'(J_n) \end{align*}
