universal cover

1. Definition

Let \(B\) be a topological space, \(b \in B\) a point and \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map.

Then \(p\) is said to be a universal cover at \(b\), if it is an representing object for the topological fibre Set functor at \(b\)

i.e. for the topological fibre Set functor there exists a natural isomorphism for the contravariant hom-functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fib}_b(-) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Cov}(B)}((p: E \rightarrow B), -) \end{align*}

and by Yoneda by natural transformations from Hom-Sets to a presheaf, it is determined by an \((p: E \rightarrow B) \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Cov}(B))\), which is fixed, and an \(e \in \mathrm{Fib}_b(p: E \rightarrow B)\)
