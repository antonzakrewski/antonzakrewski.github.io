finite product of noetherian modules as noetherian module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M_1,...,M_n\) noetherian modules over \(R\). Then

\begin{align*} M \coloneqq \bigoplus_{i=1}^n M_i \end{align*}

is also a noetherian module

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(n = 2\), the general case follows from induction

Then this follows from noetherian modules satisfy short exact 2 out of 3 for the split exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & M_1 \arrow[]{r}{} & M_1 \oplus M_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & M_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
