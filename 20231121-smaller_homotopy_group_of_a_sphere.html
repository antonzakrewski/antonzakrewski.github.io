smaller homotopy group of a sphere

1. Proposition

Let \(n,k \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(k < n\). Then the \(k\)-th homotopy group of the n-sphere is the zero group

\begin{align*} \pi_k(S^n) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

let \(\gamma \in \pi_k(S^n)\), then using homotopy group and sphere one can represent \(\gamma\) by a pointed map

\begin{align*} f: S^k \rightarrow S^n \end{align*}

Using cellular approximation for

\begin{align*} f[\mathrm{Sk}_1(X)] = f[*] \subseteq \mathrm{Sk}_1(Y) = * \end{align*}

there exists a cellular map homotopic relative to \(*\)

\begin{align*} f': S^k \rightarrow S^n \end{align*}

Then it follows, that

\begin{align*} S^k = f[\mathrm{Sk}_k(X)] \subseteq \mathrm{Sk}_k(Y) = * \end{align*}

Thus \(f\) is nullhomotopic and therefore \(0\)
