product as unit and factors as unit

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(\alpha, \beta \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \beta \in A^{\times} \end{align*}

is a unit

Then so are \(\alpha, \beta\)

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists an \(c \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} (\alpha \cdot \beta) \cdot c = 1 \end{align*}

Hence \(\beta \cdot c = \alpha^{-1}\)

Symmetry of the argument follows from

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \beta \cdot c = c \cdot \alpha \cdot \beta \end{align*}
