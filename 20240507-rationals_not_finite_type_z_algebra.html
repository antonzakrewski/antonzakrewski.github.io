rationals not of finite type Z-algebra

1. Proposition

The \(\mathbb{Q}\) as abelian group is not of finite type over the integers \(\mathbb{Z}\)

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \frac{a_i}{b_i} \in \mathbb{Q} \end{align*}

be elemnets.

Then we show, that \(\langle \frac{a_i}{b_i}\rangle \subsetneq \mathbb{Q}\)

Let \(p_j\) be the finitely many prime numbers such that \(p_j \mid \prod b_i\). Then the generated subring is given by

\begin{align*} \langle \frac{a_i}{b_i}\rangle = \{ \frac{n}{\prod p_i^{e_i}} \vert e_i \geq 0, n \in \mathbb{Z}\} \end{align*}

Since there exists infinitely many prime numbers, there exists a prime number \(p\) such that \(p \neq p_i\). Hence

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{p} \not\in \langle \frac{a_i}{b_i}\rangle \end{align*}
