finite covering map as closed map

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be topological spaces and \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map, such that for \(b \in B\) the fibre \(p^{-1}[b]\) is finite. Then \(p\) is a closed map

2. Proof

using: openness and neighbourhood for all points

Let \(A \subseteq E\) be closed and \(x \not\in p(A)\). Choose a trivializing cover \(U\) of \(x\).

Then for each \(x_1,...,x_n \in p^{-1}[x]\) with \(\coprod_{i=1}^n U_i \simeq p^{-1}[U]\) choose

\begin{align*} V_i \eqqcolon \varphi[U_i] \setminus A \subseteq \varphi[U_i] \end{align*}

as neighbourhood of \(x_i\)

Since covering map is open, it follows, that

\begin{align*} V \coloneqq \bigcap_{i=1}^n p[V_i] \end{align*}

is open.

Furhtermore it is by construction disjoint to \(p[A]\)
