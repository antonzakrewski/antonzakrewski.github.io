1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a pointed category, (sufficiently) finitely bicomplete. then for a morphism \(f: A \rightarrow B\) there exists a canonical factorization

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{ker}(f) \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & \mathrm{coker}(f) \\ & \mathrm{im}(f) \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists!} \arrow[]{d}{=}& \mathrm{Coim}(f) \arrow[]{d}{=} & & \\ & \mathrm{coker}(\mathrm{ker}(f) \rightarrow A) \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists! }& \mathrm{ker}(B \rightarrow \mathrm{Coker}(f)) & & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
