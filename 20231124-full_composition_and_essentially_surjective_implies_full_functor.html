full composition and essentially surjective implies full functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{G}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{E}\) be functors Supopse that \(\mathcal{F}\) is essentially surjective and \(\mathcal{G} \circ \mathcal{F}\) be full.

Then \(\mathcal{G}\) is also full

2. Proof

By assumption, for each \(x^C, y^C \in \mathcal{C}\), there exist \(x,y \in \mathcal{D}\) and isomorphisms

\begin{align*} f: \mathcal{F}(x^C) \rightarrow& x \\ g: \mathcal{F}(y^C) \rightarrow& y \end{align*}

Consider

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{G} \mathcal{F}(x') \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}(f)}& \mathcal{G} \mathcal{F}(y') \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}(g)}\\ \mathcal{G}(x) & \mathcal{C}(y) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath} \begin{align*} \mathcal{GF}(g \circ \mathcal{F}(h) \circ f^{-1} = \mathcal{G} g \circ \mathcal{GF}(h) \circ \mathcal{G}(f^{-1} \\ =& h \end{align*}
