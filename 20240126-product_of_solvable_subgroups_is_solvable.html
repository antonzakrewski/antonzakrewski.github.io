product of solvable subgroups is solvable

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(H,K\) subgroups where \(H \trianglelefteq G\) is a normal subgroup.

TFAE:

  1. \(H,K\) are solvable.
  2. the product of subgroups \(HK\) is also solvable

see: product of a normal subgroup and subgroup as subgroup

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By the first isomorphism theorem for groups, we get

\begin{align*} HK/H \simeq K/(H \cap K) \end{align*}

Here \(K\) is solvable, hence also the quotient \(K/(H \cap K)\)

Thus by solvable group and quotient group, both \(HK/H\) and \(H\) are solvable and therefore also \(HK\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from subgroup of a solvable group as solvable group
