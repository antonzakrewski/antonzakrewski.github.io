convergence in a topological space

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \((a_i)_{i \in I}\) a sequence in \(X\). \(a_i\) converges against \(x \in X\), if for every neighbourhood \(U \in \mathcal{U}(x)\), almost all elements of \(a_i\) are points in the neighbourhood.
