chinese remainder theorem about injectivity

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a}_1,...,\mathfrak{a}_n\) be ideals and

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrow& \prod_{i} A/\mathfrak{a}_i \\ a \mapsto& (a + \mathfrak{a}_i) \end{align*}

the canonical morphism TFAE:

  1. \(\varphi\) is injective
  2. \(\bigcap \mathfrak{a}_i = \{0\}\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(\bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathfrak{a}_i \neq \{0\}\) then there exists an \(a \in \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathfrak{a}_i \setminus \{0\}\). Note that \(\mathfrak{a}_i \supseteq \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \mathfrak{a}_i\) hence

\begin{align*} \varphi(a) =& (a + \mathfrak{a}_i) \\ =& (0 + \mathfrak{a}_i) \end{align*}

Thus \(\varphi\) is not injective

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(\varphi\) is not injective, then by minimal kernel and monomorphism there exists an \(a \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\). Furthermore, by definition

\begin{align*} \varphi(a) = (0 + \mathfrak{a}_i) \end{align*}

hence \(a \in \mathfrak{a}_i\) for arbitrary \(i\)
