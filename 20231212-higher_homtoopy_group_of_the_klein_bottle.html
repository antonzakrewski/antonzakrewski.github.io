higher homotopy group of the klein bottle

1. Proposition

The klein bottle \(K\) and for \(n \geq 2\), the homotopy group is the zero group

\begin{align*} \pi_n(K,1) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

note, that the klein bottle is a a quotient of the covering like group action

\begin{align*} (\mathbb{Z} \rtimes \mathbb{Z}) \times \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow& \mathbb{R}^2 \\ \end{align*}

klein bottle as R2/Z2 for a semidirect product (missing: covering like)

hence by orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like, we get a covering map

\begin{align*} \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2/(\mathbb{Z} \rtimes \mathbb{Z}) \cong K \end{align*}

Thus since covering maps on higher homotopy groups induces isomorphisms, we get

\begin{align*} \pi_n(\mathbb{R}^2,1) \cong \pi_n(K,1) \end{align*}

furthermore, the homotopy group functor is homotopy invariant, hence since the euclidean space is contractible space and contractible spaces have trivial homotopy group we get

\begin{align*} 0 \cong \pi_n(*,*) \cong \pi_n(\mathbb{R}^2, 1) \cong \pi_n(K,1) \end{align*}
