isomorphic R-Algebras and isomorphic Algebras over the localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(B,C\) \(S^{-1}A\)-Algebras and \(S^{-1}A\) a localization for a multiplicatively closed subset \(S \subseteq R\).

TFAE:

  1. \(B,C\) as A-Algebras (after forgetting via \(A \rightarrow S^{-1}A\)) are isomorphic
  2. \(B,C\) as \(S^{-1}A\)-Algebras are isomorphic

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption there exists an iso \(f: B \rightarrow C\). Then this commutes.

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} B \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & C \\ & & \\ & \arrow[]{luu}{} A \arrow[]{ruu}{} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Consider the localization as epimorphism

\begin{align*} A \twoheadrightarrow S^{-1}A \end{align*}

Then this results in a diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} B \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & C \\ & SA^{-1}A & \\ & \arrow[]{luu}{} A \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{u}{\iota} \arrow[]{ruu}{} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which admits by the universal property of a ring localization an extension (since \(A \rightarrow B\) was given by forgetting via \(A \rightarrow S^{-1}A\)) and therefore the elements of \(S\) are mapped to units

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} B \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & C \\ & \arrow[dashed]{lu}{} SA^{-1}A \arrow[dashed]{ru}{}& \\ & \arrow[]{luu}{} A \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{u}{\iota} \arrow[]{ruu}{} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

It remains to show, that this diagram commutes, but this follows from the fact that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} B \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & C \\ & & \\ & \arrow[]{luu}{} A \arrow[]{ruu}{} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes and \(A \rightarrow S^{-1}A\) is an epimorphism

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from applying the forgetful functor from the coslice category
