simply connected covering and semi-locally simply connected space

1. Proposition

Let \(B\) be a locally and globally path-connected topological space.

TFAE:

  1. there exists a (surjective ?) simply connected covering \(\pi: \tilde{B} \rightarrow B\)
  2. \(B\) is semi-locally simply connected space

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(b \in B\) and \(U \subseteq B\) an open neighbourhood of \(b\) within the trivializing open cover. Then for \(\gamma \in \pi_1(U,b)\) take a fibre $b ∈ \(\tilde{B}\) and lift the path \(\gamma\) to \(p^{-1}[U]\) (cf. unique path lifting) Then it follows, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} p^{-1}[U] \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{\iota } & \tilde{B} \arrow[]{d}{}\\ U \arrow[]{r}{} & B \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes and therefore also

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \pi_1(p^{-1}[U],b') \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{\iota } & \pi_1(\tilde{B},b') \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \pi_1(U,b) \arrow[]{r}{} & \pi_1(B,b) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

since \(\pi_1(\tilde{B},b') = 0\) as \(\tilde{B}\) is simply connected, it follows, that \(\gamma \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Top}}([0,1], U)\) is nullhomotopic inside \(B\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.1. set

Let

\begin{align*} \tilde{B} \coloneqq \coprod_{x \in b} \mathrm{Hom}_{\tau_{\leq 1}(B)}(b,x) = \{\text{paths from b modulo homotopy rel endpoints}\} \end{align*}

be the underlying set.

2.2.2. topology

Let

\begin{align*} U \subseteq \tilde{B} \end{align*}

be open if for each \([\gamma] \in U\) there exists a path connected open space \(V_{\gamma} \subseteq B\) containing \(\gamma(1)\), such that for each path \(\gamma': [0,1] \rightarrow V\) with \(\gamma'(0) = \gamma(1)\) we get

\begin{align*} [\gamma' \cdot \gamma] \in U \end{align*}

Then \(\emptyset\) is open by vacuous truth and \(\tilde{B}\) is open by taking \(B\).

Furthermore, the union of open sets is open, as taking \(V_{\gamma}\) suffices, since \([\gamma' \cdot \gamma] \subseteq U_i \subseteq \bigcup_i U_i\)

Suppose \([\gamma] \in U_1 \cap U_2\) with \(U_1,U_2 \subseteq \tilde{B}\) open. then choose path connected open \(V_1,V_2 \subseteq B\).

Then choose a path connected open set \(V \subseteq V_1 \cap V_2\) containing \(\gamma(1)\)

Then for each \(\gamma': [0,1] \rightarrow V\) note that \(\gamma'\) is a path inside \(V_i\) and hence \([\gamma' \cdot \gamma] \in U_i\) and thus

\begin{align*} U_1 \cap U_2 \end{align*}

is open

2.2.3. covering

Let the projection be

\begin{align*} \pi: \tilde{B} \rightarrow& B \\ [\gamma] \mapsto& \gamma(1) \end{align*}

Let \(b' \in B\) and \(V\) be an open path connected neighbourhood of \(b'\) with the property, that for \(V \hookrightarrow B\) the induced map

\begin{align*} \pi_1(V,b') \rightarrow \pi_1(B,b) \end{align*}

is the zero morphism

This can be done, as \(B\) is locally path connected and semi-locally simply connected

Let

\begin{align*} F = \mathrm{Hom}_{\tau_{\leq 1}(B)}(b,b') \end{align*}

and \([\gamma] \in \pi^{-1}[V]\), i.e. \(\gamam(1) \in V\).

Choose a path \(\alpha\) from \(\gamma(1)\) to \(b'\) in \(V\). Then define

\begin{align*} \theta([\gamma]) = [\alpha \cdot \gamma] \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\tau_{\leq 1}(B)}(b,b') \end{align*}

First this map is independent of the choice of \(\alpha\), as

\begin{align*} \alpha' \cdot \alpha^{-1} \in \pi_1(V,b) = \{1\} \end{align*}

Therefore,

\begin{align*} [\alpha \cdot \gamma] =& [\alpha' \cdot \alpha^{-1}] \cdot [\alpha \cdot \gamma] \\ =& [\alpha' \cdot \gamma] \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \theta: \pi^{-1}[V] \rightarrow& V \times F^{\delta} \\ [\gamma] \mapsto& \alpha \cdot \gamma \end{align*}

is a homeomorphism
