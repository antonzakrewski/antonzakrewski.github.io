monomorphism in set as injective map

1. Proposition

Given category set \(\mathrm{Set}\), sets \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Set})\) and a morphism \(f: A \rightarrow B\).

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is injective
  2. \(f\) is a monomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of:

  • injective map as monomorphism

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(f\) is not injective, then there exist different \(x,y \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} f(x) = f(y) \end{align*}

Then for the map

\begin{align*} g: A \rightarrow& A \\ x \mapsto& y \\ y \mapsto& x \\ z \mapsto& \mathrm{id}(z) & \mbox{else } \\ \end{align*}

and the identity morphism, it follows that

\begin{align*} f \circ g =& f \circ \mathrm{id}_{A} \end{align*}

Nonetheless, by construction it holds, that

\begin{align*} g \neq \mathrm{id}_{A} \end{align*}

as \(x \neq y\) by assumption.
