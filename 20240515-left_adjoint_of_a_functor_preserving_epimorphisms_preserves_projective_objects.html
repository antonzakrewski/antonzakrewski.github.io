left adjoint of a functor preserving epimorphisms preserves projective objects

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor which is a left adjoint \(\mathcal{L} \dashv \mathcal{R}\). Suppose \(\mathcal{R}\) preserves epimorphisms Then \(\mathcal{L}\) preserves projective objects

2. Proof

Let \(p \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) be a projective object. Then by assumption

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(p), -) \simeq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(p,\mathcal{G}(-)) \end{align*}

The right hand side is given as functor composition

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(p,-) \circ \mathcal{G}(-) \end{align*}

where both \(\mathcal{G}(-)\) and \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(p,-)\) preserve epimorphisms (cf. projective object and covariant hom functor preserving epimorphisms)

Thus \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(p,\mathcal{G}(-))\) also preserves epis.
