ideal torsion submodule functor

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) a right ideal and \(M\) a left Module. Then then ideal torsion submodule functor is defined as endofunctor

\begin{align*} -[\mathfrak{a}]: \mathrm{AMod} \rightarrow& \mathrm{AMod} \\ M \mapsto& M[\mathfrak{a}] \coloneqq \{m \in M \vert \alpha \cdot m = 0 \forall \alpha \in \mathfrak{} \} (\varphi: M \rightarrow N) \mapsto& (\varphi_{\vert M[\mathfrak{a}]}: \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. object

using the Kriterium für ein Untermodul:

  1. \(0 \in M[\mathfrak{a}]\) hence \(M[\mathfrak{a}]\) is nonempty
  2. let \(r \in R\) then for arbitrary \(\alpha \in A\) it follows that \(\alpha \cdot r =: \alpha \in \mathfrak{a}\) showing
\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot r \cdot m =& \alpha' m \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

for \(m \in M[\mathfrak{a}]\)

2.2. morphism

it remains to show that this is welldefined: Suppose \(m \in M[\mathfrak{a}]\) then for arbitrary \(\alpha \in \mathfrak{a}\) it holds

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \varphi(m) =& \varphi(\alpha \cdot m) \\ =& \varphi(0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

hence \(\varphi(m) \in N[\mathfrak{a}]\)
