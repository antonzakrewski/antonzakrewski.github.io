long exact sequence of a pair of spaces

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, where \(A \subseteq X\) is a subspace. Then there exists the canonical long exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n}(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n}(X,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(X,A) \arrow[]{r}{} & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

follows from the fundamental theorem of short exact chain complexes for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X,A) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ & & & \mathrm{coker}(\iota) & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
