fp cohomology ring of a lens space

1. Proposition

Let \(p,q\) be coprime prime numbers with \(p \neq 2\) Then the \(\mathbb{F}_{p}\) singular cohomology is given by

\begin{align*} P_{\mathbb{F}_p}(\alpha,\beta)/ (\beta^2 = 0, \alpha^2 = 0) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a)

using universal coefficient Theorem for cohomology this results in

\begin{align*} H^n(L(p,q); \mathbb{F}_p) = \begin{cases} \mathbb{F}_p & \mbox{if } i \in \{0,1,2,3\} \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

And using cup product pairing non degenerate shows that \(X\) generates this, as

\begin{align*} H^l \otimes H^k \rightarrow H_0 \simeq H^3 \end{align*}

is nondegenerate.

Then as \(H^l, H^k, H_0\) are \(1\)-dimensional vector spaces

Then as \(\alpha\) is an odd degree element, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cup \alpha = - \alpha \cup \alpha \end{align*}

But since \(\mathrm{char}(\mathbb{F}_p) = p \neq 2\) we conclude that

\begin{align*} \alpha^2 = 0 \end{align*}
