trivial group action

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(X\) be a set, \(G\) a group. Then the trivial group action is defined as

\begin{align*} \varphi: G \times X \rightarrow& X \\ (g,x) \mapsto& x \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. neutral

follows from \(\varphi(e,x) = x\)

2.2. (pseudo-)associativity

follows from

\begin{align*} \varphi((g \cdot g'),x) =& x \\ =& \varphi(g',x) \\ =& \varphi(g, \varphi(g',x)) \end{align*}
