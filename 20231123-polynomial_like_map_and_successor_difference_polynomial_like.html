polynomial-like map and successor difference polynomial-like

1. Proposition

Let \(f: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}\) be a map.

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is polynomial-like on \(\mathbb{Z}\)
  2. \(\Delta f\) is polynomial like on \(\mathbb{N}\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

by assumption, there exist \(g \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) such that \(g(x) = f(x)\) for almost all \(x \in \mathbb{Q}\). Then

\begin{align*} g(x + 1) - g(x) \in \mathbb{Q}[x] \end{align*}

is a polynomial, such that

\begin{align*} \Delta f(x) =& f(x+1) - f(x) \\ =& g(x +1) - g(x) \end{align*}

for almost all \(x \in \mathbb{Z}\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption, there exists a polynomial \(g'(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \alpha_i X^i \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\). Then consider the matrix

\begin{align*} A \coloneqq& \begin{pmatrix} \binom{1}{1} & \binom{2}{2} & \binom{3}{m} & ... & \binom{n-1}{n-1} & \binom{n}{n} \\ 0 & \binom{2}{1} & \binom{3}{2} & ... & \binom{n-1}{n-2} & \binom{n}{n-1} \\ 0 & 0 & \binom{3}{1} & ... & \binom{n-1}{n-3} & \binom{n}{n-2} \\ \vdots & & & \ddots & & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \binom{n-1}{1} & \binom{n}{2} \\ 0& 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \binom{n}{1} \\ \end{pmatrix} \in \mathbb{Q}^{n \times n} \end{align*}

it is a vectorspace-isomorphism (cf. determinant, todo).

Therefore, for the vector

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} v \coloneqq \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_0 & \alpha_1 & ... & \alpha_{n-1} \end{pmatrix}^{T} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

there exists a unique solution \(x = (\beta_1, ...,\beta_{n}) \in \mathbb{Q}\)

\begin{align*} Ax = v \end{align*}

Let

\begin{align*} g = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_i X^i \end{align*}

observe, that by construction

\begin{align*} g(x + 1) - g(x) = g'(x) \end{align*}

By assumption, there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that for all \(m \geq n\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} g'(m) = \Delta f(m) \end{align*}

Let \(\beta_0 \coloneqq f(n) - g(n)\)

2.2.1. claim

\begin{align*} g(m) + \beta_0 =& f(m) && m \geq n \end{align*}
2.2.1.1. induction basis

for \(m = n\):

\begin{align*} g(n) + \beta_0 =& g(n) + f(n) - g(n) \\ =& f(n) \end{align*}
2.2.1.2. induction step
\begin{align*} g(x+1) + \beta_0 =& g(x+1) - g(x) + g(x) + \beta_0 \\ =& g'(x) + f(x) \\ =& \Delta f(x) + f(x) \\ =& f(x + 1) \end{align*}
