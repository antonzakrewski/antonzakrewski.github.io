trivial higher homotopy group of a 1-dimensional CW Complex

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a \(1\)-dimensional CW-Complex. then for \(n \geq 2\), the homotopy group

\begin{align*} \pi_n(X,1) = 0 \end{align*}

is the zero group

2. Proof

using classification of one dimensional CW Complexes up to homotopy, this reduces down to

\begin{align*} \pi_n(\coporod_{i \in I} S^1,1) \end{align*}

Using wedge of circles covered by a tree, we get a covering of

\begin{align*} p: T \rightarrow \bigvee S^1 \end{align*}

Hence by covering map on higher homotopy groups as isomorphism we get

\begin{align*} \pi_n(p): \pi_n(T,0) \cong \pi_n(\bigvee S^1, 1) \end{align*}

Note that a tree is contractible, hence by contractible space and trivial homotopy group we get

\begin{align*} \pi_n(T,0) = \cong \end{align*}
