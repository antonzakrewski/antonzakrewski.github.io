prime ideal in a finite ring as maximal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a finite commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p}\) a prime ideal.

Then \(\mathfrak{p}\) is maximal

2. Proof

by prime ideal and quotient ring as integral domain it follows, that

\begin{align*} A/\mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

is an Integral domain. Furthermore, since

\begin{align*} \vert A / \mathfrak{p} \vert \leq \vert A \vert < \infty \end{align*}

by finite integral domain as field we conclude, that \(A/\mathfrak{p}\) is a field. By maximal ideal of a commutative ring and quotient ring as field we conclude, that \(\mathfrak{p}\) is maximal
