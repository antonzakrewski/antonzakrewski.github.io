localization of a quotient module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(M\) an \(R\)-module and \(N \subseteq M\) a submodule. Then for the localization functor and quotient module it holds, that

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(M/N) \cong S^{-1}M/S^{-1}N \end{align*}

as \(S^{-1}R\)-modules

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi: S^{-1}(M/N) \rightarrow& S^{-1}M / S^{-1}N \\ \frac{m + N}{s} \mapsto& \frac{m}{s} + S^{-1}N \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

Let \(m_1,m_2 \in M\), \(s_1,s_2 \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} \frac{m_1 + N}{s_1} = \frac{m_2 + N}{s_2} \end{align*}

Then there exists an \(s \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} s \cdot (s_2 \cdot m_1 - s_1 \cdot m_2 + N) =& 0 + N \\ s \cdot s_2 \cdot m_1 + N =& s \cdot s_1 \cdot m_2 + N \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \left(\frac{m_1}{s_1} + S^{-1}N\right) - \left(\frac{m_2}{s_2} + S^{-1}N\right) =& \frac{m_1}{s_1} - \frac{m_2}{s_2} + S^{-1}N \\ =& 0 + S^{-1}N \end{align*}

2.2. injectiv

minimal kernel and monomorphism Let \(\frac{m + N}{s} \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\), then there exists an \(n \in N, s' \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} \frac{m}{s} = \frac{n}{s'} \end{align*}

Hence there exists an \(\overline{s} \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} \overline{s} \cdot (s' \cdot m - s \cdot n) =& 0 \\ \overline{s} \cdot s' \cdotm m =& \overline{s} \cdot s \cdot n \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \frac{m + N}{s} =& \frac{\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot m + N }{\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot s} \\ =& \frac{\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot m - \overline{s} \cdot s \cdot n + N}{\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot s} \\ =& \frac{0 + N}{\overline{s} \cdot s' \cdot s} \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

2.3. image

Let \(\frac{m}{s} + S^{-1}N \in S^{-1}M/S^{-1}N\), then by assumption

\begin{align*} \varphi( \frac{m+ N}{s}) =& \frac{m}{s} + S^{-1}N \in \mathrm{im}(\varphi) \end{align*}
