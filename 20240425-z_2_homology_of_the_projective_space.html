Z/2 homology of the projective space

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n\) be the real projective space, then the singular homology relative to \(\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\) is given by

\begin{align*} H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n; \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) = \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} & \mbox{if } 0 \leq k \leq n \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from

  1. universal coefficient formula for singular homology
  2. singular homology of the real projective space
  3. tor 1 functor for an integral domain and quotient
  4. pi-1 tensor submodule of a quotient ring of the integers and gcd

where there exists a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_k( \mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{r}{} & H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n, \mathbb{Z}/2) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n), \mathbb{Z}/2) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

by tor additive in both variables and additive functor preserves zero object we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(0, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \simeq 0 \end{align*}

2.1. \(k > n + 2\)

Then it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n), \mathbb{Z}/2) \simeq& \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(0, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \\ \simeq& 0 \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} 0 \simeq 0 \otimes \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \simeq H_k( \mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n, \mathbb{Z}/2) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

2.2. \(k = n + 1\), \(k\) even

Then Then \(H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) = H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) = 0\) hence both outer terms are zero and thus

\begin{align*} H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \simeq 0 \end{align*}

2.3. \(k = n + 1\), \(k\) odd

Then \(H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) = 0\) hence there exists an iso

\begin{align*} H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \simeq \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n), \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n), \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \simeq \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) = 0\) as \(\mathbb{Z}\) is flat (cf. tor functor for a flat module

2.4. \(k \leq n\)

2.4.1. \(k\) odd

Then

\begin{align*} H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) \simeq \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z} & \mbox{if } k = 1, k = n \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

where \(\mathbb{Z}, 0\) are flat module

2.4.2. \(k = 0\)

Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n), \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \simeq& \mathrm{Tor}_1^{0, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \\ \simeq& 0 \end{align*}

Thus \(\mu\) is an isomorphism and it remains to calculate

\begin{align*} H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) \otimes \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

which results in

\begin{align*} \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z} \otimes \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \simeq \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \\ \mathbb{Z}/2 \otimes \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \simeq& \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{cases} \end{align*}

2.5. \(k\) even, non zero

Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(H_{k-1}(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n), \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \simeq& \mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \\ \simeq& \mathbb{Z}/\mathrm{gcd}(2,2) \mathbb{Z} \\ \simeq& \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

Then \(H_k(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n) \simeq 0\) and hence the map

is an isomorphism
