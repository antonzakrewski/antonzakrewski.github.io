precomposition with an isomorphism as isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) a morphism

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is an isomorphism
  2. precomposition with \(f\) is an isomorphism on hom-sets

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of:

  • covariant hom functor of points
  • isomorphisms under functors

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption for each \(C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\)

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,C) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,C) \end{align*}

is an iso on hom sets

Hence for \(C = A\) we get a bijection

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,A) \end{align*}

by surjectivity an \(g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A)\) such that

\begin{align*} g \circ f = \mathrm{id}_{A} \end{align*}

We also get a bijection

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,B) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B) \end{align*}

Furthermore, since

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}(f \circ g) =& f \circ g \circ f \\ =& f \circ \mathrm{id}_{A} \\ =& f \\ =& \mathrm{id}_{B} \circ f \\ =& f^{ \rightarrow} (\mathrm{id}_{B} ) \\ \end{align*}

we get by injectivity, that \(\mathrm{id}_{B} = f \circ g\)
