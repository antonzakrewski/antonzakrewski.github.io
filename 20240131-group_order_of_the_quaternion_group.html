group order of the quaternion group

1. Proposition

the group order of the quaternion group is

\begin{align*} \vert Q \vert = 8 \end{align*}

2. Proof

We claim, that each element \(g \in Q\) is represented by

\begin{align*} \alpha^l \beta^k \end{align*}

for \(l \in \mathbb{Z} / 4 \mathbb{Z}\) and \(k \in \mathbb{Z} / 2 \mathbb{Z}\).

There we get by induction (note that the inverse follows from \(\alpha^{-1} = \alpha^3\))

\begin{align*} \alpha^l \beta^k \cdot \alpha = \begin{cases} \alpha^{l+1} & \mbox{if } k \equiv 1 \\ \alpha^{l-1} \beta & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

by applying \(\beta \alpha = \alpha^{-1} \beta\)

and

\begin{align*} \alpha^{l} \beta^{k} \beta =& \alpha^{l} \beta^{k+1} \end{align*}

where applying \(\alpha^{2} = \beta^{2}\) results in

\begin{align*} \alpha^{l} \beta^{k+1} =& \alpha^{l+2} \beta^{k-1} \end{align*}
