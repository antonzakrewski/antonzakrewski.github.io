polynomial continuous between affine spaces and affine line

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space and \(\mathbb{A}_K^1\) the affine line. Then a polynomial \(f \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) as mapification is a continuous map

\begin{align*} f: \mathbb{A}_K^n \rightarrow& \mathbb{A}_K^1 (a_1,...,a_n) \mapsto& f(a_1,...,a_n) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose \(A \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^1\) is closed. Then \(A\) is finite (cf. simple affine line has cofinite topology) and by preimage and union it suffices, to show that

\begin{align*} f^{-1}[ \{\alpha\}] \end{align*}

is closed for \(\alpha \in K\). (cf. Urbilder abgeschlossene Mengen unter stetigen Abbildungen)

But \(f^{-1}[ \{\alpha\}]\) is precisely the zero locus of the polynomial \(f(X_1,...,X_n) - \alpha\), hence closed in the zariski topology on an affine space
