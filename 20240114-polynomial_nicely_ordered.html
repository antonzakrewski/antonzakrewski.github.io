polynomial nicely ordered

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(R[X_i]\) the polynomial ring. Then for a polynomial \(f \in R[X_i]\) there exists a depiction

\begin{align*} f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i \prod X_j^{j(i)} \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(R[X]\), the general case follows from \(R[X_i][X_j] = R[X_i \cup X_j]\) and \(f \in R[X_1,...,X_n] \subseteq R[X_i]\)
