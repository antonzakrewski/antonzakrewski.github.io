composition of chain homotopic maps

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(X_*, Y_*, Z_*\) are chain complexes and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X_* \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{r}{f_1} \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{f_2} & Y_* \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{r}{g_1} \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{g_2}& Z_* \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

chain homotopic maps.

Then there exists a canonical chain homotopy

\begin{align*} g_1 \circ f_1 \sim g_2 \circ f_2 \end{align*}

2. Proof

By assumption, there exist

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{ld}{h_n} X_{n} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{f_1 - f_2} & X_{n-1} \arrow[]{ld}{h_{n-1}} \\ Y_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{ld}{h_n'} Y_{n} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{f_2 - g_2} & Y_{n-1} \arrow[]{ld}{h_{n-1}'}\\ Z_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{} & Z_{n} \arrow[]{r}{} & Z_{n-1} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then we get

\begin{align*} g_1 f_1 - g_2 f_2 =& (g_1 f_1 - g_1 f_2) - (g_2 f_2 - g_1 f_2) \\ =& g_1(f_1 - f_2) - (g_2 - g1)(f_2) \\ =& g_1(hd + dh) - (h'd + dh')(f_2) \\ =& g_1 hd + g_1 dh - h'df_2 - dh'f_2 \end{align*}

using the assumption that

\begin{align*} d g =& g d \\ d f =& f d \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} g_1 hd + g_1 dh - h'df_2 - dh'f_2 =& g_1 hd + d g_1 h - h' f_2d - d h' f_2 \\ =& d(g_1h - h' f_2) + (g_1 h - h' f_2)d \end{align*}

Hence \(g_1 h - h' f_2\) suffices as homotopy.
