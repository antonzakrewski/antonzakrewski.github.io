fundamental group of a torus

1. Proposition

Given the d-torus, the fundamental group is

\begin{align*} \pi_1(T^d, 1) = \mathbb{Z}^d \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from:

  • torus as Rn/Zn
  • euclidean space as contractible space
  • simply connected covering like group action and fundamental group
