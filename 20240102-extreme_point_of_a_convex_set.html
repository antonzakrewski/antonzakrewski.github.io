extreme point of a convex set

1. Definition

Let \(V\) be a real vector space and \(M \subseteq V\) a convex set. Then an extreme point is a point \(v \in M\) such for \(v_1,v_2 \in V\) and \(\lambda \in (0,1)\) with

\begin{align*} v = \lambda \cdot v_1 + (1 - \lambda) v_2 \end{align*}

it follows, that

\begin{align*} v_1 = v_2 = v \end{align*}
