representation of a contravariant functor unique up to unique isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a locally small category, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}\) a covariant functor. Suppose there exist representations \((A,a),(B,b)\).

Then \(A,B\) are unique up to unique isomorphism \(f: A \rightarrow B\)

2. Proof

follows from

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,A) \arrow[bend left]{rr}{\eta_b \circ \eta_a} \arrow[]{r}{\eta_a} & \mathcal{F} \arrow[]{r}{\eta_b} & \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,B) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus there exists a unique natural isomorphism (cf. covariant yoneda embedding)

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,A) \sim \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,B) \end{align*}

by natural transformation between contravariant hom functors as postcomposition, there exists an \(f\). Thus by hom functor as conservative functor, \(f\) is an isomorphism

Furthermore vlt zusammenhang zwischen \(a \in \mathcal{F}(A), b \in \mathcal{F}(B)\)
