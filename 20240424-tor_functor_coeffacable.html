tor functor coeffacable

1. Proposition

Let \(n \geq 1\), then the Tor Functor is coeffacable

2. Proof

Let \(M \in \mathrm{RMod}\). Then choose a free module and an algebraic module-epimorphism, e.g.

\begin{align*} \varphi: R^M \rightarrow& M \\ (r_m) \mapsto& m \end{align*}

Then \(R^M\) is flat module (cf. free module as flat module) Thus it follows from tor functor for a flat module, as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{rd}{0}& \mathrm{Tor}(R^m \times -) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{Tor}(f) & \mathrm{Tor}(M \times -) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
