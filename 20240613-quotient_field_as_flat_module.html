quotient field as flat module

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain and \(Q(A)\) the quotient field Then \(Q(A)\) is a flat module

2. Proof

using flat module and A-flat, let \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) be an ideal Then it remains to show the map

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathfrak{a} \otimes_{A} K \rightarrow& A \otimes_A Q(A) \cong Q(A) \\ a \otimes x \mapsto& a \cdot x \end{align*}

is injective.

Assume \(\mathfrak{a} \neq (0)\) as otherwise this is given by

\begin{align*} 0: 0 \rightarrow K \end{align*}

(cf. zero morphism as monomorphism and domain as zero object)

Let \(\beta \in \mathfrak{a} \setminus \{0\}\), then consider the map

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1}: Q(A) \rightarrow& A \otimes_A Q(A) = Q(A) \\ x \mapsto& b \otimes b^{-1} x \end{align*}

It follows that \(\varphi\) is split mono, as

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1} ( \varphi(a \otimes x)) =& \varphi^{-1}(ax) \\ =& b \otimes b^{-1}ax \\ =& b \cdot b^{-1} a \otimes x \\ =& a \otimes x \end{align*}
