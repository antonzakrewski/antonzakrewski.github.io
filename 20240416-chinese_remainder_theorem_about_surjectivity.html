chinese remainder theorem about surjectivity

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a}_1,...,\mathfrak{a}_n\) be ideals and

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrow& \prod_{i=1}^n A/\mathfrak{a}_i \\ a \mapsto& (a + \mathfrak{a}_i) \end{align*}

the canonical morphism TFAE:

  1. \(\varphi\) is surjective
  2. \(\mathfrak{a}_i, \mathfrak{a}_j\) are coprime for \(i \neq j\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(\mathfrak{a}_i, \mathfrak{a}_j\) are not coprime, then consider the element

\begin{align*} (1_i,0_j,...) \in \prod_{i=1}^n A/\mathfrak{a}_i \end{align*}

By surjectivity, there exists an element \(a \in A\) such that \(a = 1_i + \mathfrak{a}_i, a = 0_j + \mathfrak{a}_j\). Hence it follows that \(a \in \mathfrak{a}_j\) and \(1 - a \in \mathfrak{a}_i\) and therefore also

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a}_i + \mathfrak{a}_j = A \end{align*}

thus a contradiction

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
