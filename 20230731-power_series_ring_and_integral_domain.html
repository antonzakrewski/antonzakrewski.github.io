power series ring and integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(A[ [X] ]\) the power series ring. TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is an Integral domain
  2. \(A[ [X ] ]\) is an Integral domain

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(f,g \in A [ [ X ] ] \setminus \{0\}\). Then there exist minimal \(r,s \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} f =& \alpha_r \cdot X^r + \alpha_{r+1} X^{r+1} + ... \\ g =& \beta_{s} \cdot X^s + \beta_{s+1} X^{s+1} + ... \end{align*}

with \(\alpha_{r}, \beta_{s} \neq 0\). Hence

\begin{align*} f \cdot g = \alpha_{r} \beta_{s} \cdot X^{r+s} + r \end{align*}

with \(\pi_{r+s}(r) = 0\). Since \(\alpha_r, \beta_{s} \neq 0\) and \(A\) is an Integral domain, we conclude that \(\alpha_{r} \cdot \beta_{s} \neq 0\) and hence

\begin{align*} f \cdot g \neq 0 \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrowtail A[ [ X] ] \\ a \mapsto (a,0,0,...) \end{align*}

be the cananonical embedding. Then the statement is a corollary of monomorphism and integral domain
