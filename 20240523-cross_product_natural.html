cross product of singular cohomology as natural operation

1. Proposition

Let \(X,X',Y,Y'\) be topological spaces and \(f: X' \rightarrow X, g: Y' \rightarrow Y\) be continuous maps Suppose furthermore \(L,M\) are \(R\)-modules over a commutative ring \(R\) and \(\alpha \in H^p(X;L), \beta \in H^q(Y;M)\)

Then the cross product of singular cohomology is a natural transformation, i.e.

\begin{align*} f^* \alpha \times g^* \beta = (f \times g)^*(\alpha \times \beta) \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from the definition, as each map is a natural transformation, possibly after right whiskering
