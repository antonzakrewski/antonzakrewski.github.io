primary ideal in general not a power of a prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p}\) a prime ideal and \(\mathfrak{p}^n\) the product. Then \(\mathfrak{p}\) is in general not a primary ideal.

2. Proof

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K[X,Y]\) the polynomial ring and \(\mathfrak{q} = (X,Y^2)\) be \((X,Y)\) primary Then \((X,Y^2)\) is primary for \(\sqrt{\mathfrak{q}} = (X,Y)\) but not a power, as

\begin{align*} (x,y)^2 = (X^2, Y^2, XY) \supsetneq (X,Y^2)$ \end{align*}
