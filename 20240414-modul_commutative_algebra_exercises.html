Idea:

  • Multiplication with the element

Let \(\mathfrak{p}\) be an associated prime ideal for \(\varphi: A/\mathfrak{p} \rightarrow A\) Let \(x \coloneqq \varphi(1)\) Then it follows by functorality of the ideal torsion submodule that \(\varphi(1)\) has \(\mathfrak{p}\) torsion. Furthermore \(\varphi(1)\) has only \(\mathfrak{p}\) torsion, as for \(\alpha \in A\)

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \varphi(1) =& 0 \\ \varphi(\alpha \cdot 1) =& 0 \end{align*}

and by injectivity \(\alpha \cdot 1 = 0\) which by implies \(\alpha \in \mathfrak{p}\)
