surjective morphism in general not split epi

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a concrete category and \(f \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{C})\) a surjective morphism. Then \(f\) is in general not split epid

2. Proof

Given category Group \(\mathrm{Grp}\) and the group-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

then a morphism

\begin{align*} \psi: \mathbb{Z} / 2 \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

is, if it exists, defined by \(\psi(1)\), as \(\mathbb{Z} / 2 \mathbb{Z}\) is a cyclic group.

for \(\psi(1) \neq 0\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} \psi(1) + \psi(1) =& \psi(1 + 1) \\ =& \psi(0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

thus a contradiction.

Therefore, \(\psi(1) = 0\) and

\begin{align*} \psi \circ \varphi = 0 \end{align*}

is the zero morphism
