cofibration and retraction

1. Proposition

Let \(X,A\) be topological spaces.

TFAE:

  1. \(\iota: A \rightarrow X\) is a cofibration
  2. there exists a Retraction \(R: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow A \times [0,1] \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} X \times \{0\}\)
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \times [0,1] \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} X \times \{0\} \arrow[]{r}{f \coprod H} \arrow[]{d}{}& A \times [0,1] \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} X \times \{0\}\\ X \times [0,1] \arrow[dashed]{ru}{R}& \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let

\begin{align*} H: A \times [0,1] \rightarrow A \times [0,1] \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} X \times \{0\} \end{align*}

be the identity. Then we can extend \(\mathrm{id}_{A} = H(-,0)\) to \(\mathrm{id}_{X}: X \rightarrow X \times \{0\}\) and thus get a homotopy

\begin{align*} \overline{H}: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow& A \times [0,1] \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} X \times \{0\} \end{align*}

which by construction is our desired retraction

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose there exist a map \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) and a homotopy

\begin{align*} H: A \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}. By [[id:43361c3e-7e6f-4ffb-a0ec-7988d859da0d][glueing lemma for a locally finite, closed covering]] we conclude, that \begin{align*} H': A \times [0,1] \coprod_{A \times \{0\}} X \times \{0\} \rightarrow Y \\ (a,t) \mapsto& H(a,t) (x,0) \mapsto& f(x) \end{align*}

is continuous Then we can precompose with the Retraction and get

\begin{align*} H' \circ R: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow& Y \end{align*}
