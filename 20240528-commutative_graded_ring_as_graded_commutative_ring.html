commutative graded ring as graded-commutative ring for a stretch

1. Proposition

Let \(R = \bigoplus_{n \in \mathbb{N}_0} R_n\) be a N-graded ring Then \(R\) admits a graded-commutative ring structure via.

\begin{align*} S_k \coloneqq \begin{cases} 0 & \mbox{if } d \nmid k \\ R_{\frac{k}{d}} & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

for any even \(d \geq 2\) with the inherited multiplication defined on homogeneous elements

\begin{align*} \alpha_{k} \cdot_S \beta_{k'} = (\alpha_k \cdot_{R} \beta_k') \in R_{k + k'} \end{align*}

and extending via distributivity

2. Proof

2.1. graded commutativity

follows as even degrees commute by assumption and odd degrees are always zero
