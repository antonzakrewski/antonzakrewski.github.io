isomorphism on integral singular homology and isomorphism on general singular homology

1. Proposition

Let \(X,Y\) be topological spaces, \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map such that

\begin{align*} f_*: H_*(Y;\mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow H_*(X;\mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism for singular homology

Then the induced map on singular homology

\begin{align*} f_*: H_*(Y; \mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow H_*(X; \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

is also an isomorphism for any abelian group \(M\)

2. Proof

  • quasi isomorphism between chain complexes of projectives and homotopy equivalence
  • hom chain complex and precomposition preserves chain homotopy
