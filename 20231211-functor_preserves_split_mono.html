functor preserves split mono

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) be a functor. Suppose \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) is a split mono, then so is \(\mathcal{F}(f)\)

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists a left-inverse \(f^{-1}: B \rightarrow A\). Therefore we conclude since

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{A} = f^{-1} \circ f \end{align*}

that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{F}(A)} =& \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}_{A}) \\ =& \mathcal{F}( f^{-1} \circ f) \\ =& \mathcal{F}(f^{-1}) \circ \mathcal{F}(f) \end{align*}

hence \(\mathcal{F}^{-1}\) is a left inverse
