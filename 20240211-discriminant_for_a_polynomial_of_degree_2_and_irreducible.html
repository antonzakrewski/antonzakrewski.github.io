discriminant for a polynomial of degree 2 and irreducible

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field of \(\mathrm{char}(K) \neq 2\) and

\begin{align*} f = X^2 + \alpha_1 X + \alpha_0 \in K[X] \end{align*}

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is reducible
  2. there exists an \(k \in K\) such that \(k^2 = \Delta_f\) for the discriminant \(\Delta_f\)

2. Proof

using irreducible polynomial of degree less than 3 and root

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Then \(f\) splits and hence both roots of \(f\) \(\beta_1,\beta_2\) are elements of \(K\). Thus

\begin{align*} \Delta_f = \prod_{i < j} (\beta_i - \beta_j)^2 \\ =& (\beta_i - \beta_j)^2 \end{align*}

and hence \(\beta_i - \beta_j = k\) satisfies this.

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By pq-Formel / Quadratische Formel it holds:

\begin{align*} \beta_i = \frac{- \alpha_1 \pm \sqrt{p^2 - 4 \alpha_0}}{2} \end{align*}

Hence evaluating results in

\begin{align*} \Delta_f =& ( \frac{- \alpha_1 + \sqrt{p^2 - 4 \alpha_0}}{2} - \frac{-\alpha_1 - \sqrt{p^2 - 4 \alpha_0}}{2} )^2 \\ =& (\sqrt{p^2 - 4 \alpha_0})^2 \end{align*}

Suppose such an \(k \in K\) exists, then \(k = \pm \sqrt{p^2 - 4 \alpha_0}\). Hence

\begin{align*} \frac{p + k}{2} \in K \end{align*}

and therefore \(f\) has a root and splits
