orthogonal set under ring homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be rings and \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) a ring-homomorphism. Suppose \(\{e_i\}\) is an orthogonal set, then so is

\begin{align*} \varphi[ \{e_i\}] \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(a = \varphi(e_i),b = \varphi{e_j) \in \varphi[ \{e_i\}]\) with \(a \neq b\) and thus \(i \neq j\). Then it follows that

\begin{align*} a \cdot b =& \varphi(e_i) \cdot \varphi(e_j) \\ =& \varphi(e_i \cdot e_j) \\ =& \varphi(0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
