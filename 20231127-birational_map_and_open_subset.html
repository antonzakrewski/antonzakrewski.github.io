birational map and open subset

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a algebraically closed field, \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space and \(X \subseteq \mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) be an affine variety. Then for the function field of a variety \(K(X)\) and rational maps \(h,\tilde{h} \in K(X)\)

TFAE:

  1. \(h = \tilde{h}\)
  2. there exists a nonempty open set \(U \subseteq X\) such that \(h_{\vert U} = \tilde{h}_{\vert U}\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(r \coloneqq h - \tilde{h} \in K(X)\). Then we get

\begin{align*} r = \frac{f}{g} \end{align*}

for some \(f,g \in K[X]\). Furthermore, \(f_{\vert U} = 0\), hence

\begin{align*} U \subseteq V(f) \end{align*}

Taking the closure

\begin{align*} V(0) = X = \overline{U} = V(f) \subseteq X \end{align*}

we get by nullstellensatz

\begin{align*} V(0) =& V(f) \\ I(V(f)) =& \sqrt{(f)} \\ =& (0) \end{align*}

hence \(f = 0\) and thus \(r = 0\)
