element as product of irreducibles for a noetherian domain

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian Integral domain. Then for \(a \in A \setminus (A^{\times} \cup \{0\})\) there exist finitely many irreducibles \(p_i\) such that

\begin{align*} \prod p_i = a \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(a\) is reducible.

By element and irreducible dividing in a noetherian domain there exists an \(p_1\) such that \(p_1 \mid a\). Hence there exists an \(a_1\) with

\begin{align*} a_1 \cdot p_1 = a \end{align*}

inductively we may construct a chain

\begin{align*} (a_1) \subsetneq (a_2) ... \end{align*}
