isomorphism between R-S-Mod and A-Mod

1. Proposition

Let \(R,S\) be rings and \(\mathrm{R-S-Mod}\) the category of bimodules. Then for the tensor product \(A \coloneqq R \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} S^{\mathrm{op}}\) with the opposite ring there exists a canonical isomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{A-Mod} \simeq& \mathrm{R-S-Mod} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. object

2.1.1. A-Mod \(\rightarrow\) R-S-Mod

Define

\begin{align*} r \cdot_R m \coloneqq (r \otimes 1) \cdot_A m \\ m \cdot_S s \coloneqq (1 \otimes s) \cdot_A m \\ \end{align*}

Then

2.1.1.1. unit

follows as

\begin{align*} 1 \cdot_R m =& (1 \otimes 1) m \\ =& m =& (1 \otimes 1) \cdot m =& \cdot m =& m \cdot_S 1 \end{align*}
2.1.1.2. distributive

follows from distributivity of elementary tensors

\begin{align*} (r_1 + r_2) \cdot_R (m_1 + m_2) =& (r_1 + r_2 \otimes 1) \cdot_A (m_1 + m_2) \\ =& ((r_1 \otimes 1) + (r_2 \otimes 1)) \cdot_A (m_1 + m_2) \\ =& (r_1 \otimes 1) m_1 + (r_1 \otimes 1) m_2 + (r_2 \otimes 1) m_1 + (r_2 \otimes 1) m_2 \\ =& r_1 \cdot_R m_1 + r_1 \cdot_R m_2 + r_2 \cdot_R m_1 + r_2 \cdot_R m_2 \end{align*}

The case for \(S\) follows analagously

2.1.1.3. multiplicative

Follows as

\begin{align*} r_1 \cdot_R (r_2 \cdot m) =& r_1 \cdot_R ((r_2 \otimes 1) \cdot_A m) \\ =& ((r_1 \otimes 1) \cdot_A (r_2 \otimes 1) \cdot m) \\ =& (r_1 \otimes 1) \cdot_R (r_2 \otimes 1) \cdot m \\ =& (r_1 \cdot r_2 \otimes 1) \cdot m \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} (m \cdot_S s_1) \cdot_S s_2 =& ((1 \otimes s_1) \cdot m) \cdot_S s_2 \\ =& (1 \otimes s_2) \cdot_A ((1 \otimes s_1) \cdot m) \\ =& ((1 \otimes s_2) \cdot_A (1 \otimes s_1)) \cdot m \\ =& (1 \otimes s_2 \cdot_{S^{\mathrm{op}}} s_1) \cdot m \\ =& (1 \otimes s_1 \cdot_{S} s_2) \cdot m \\ =& m \cdot (s_1 \cdot s_2) \end{align*}
2.1.1.4. associative
\begin{align*} (r \cdot_R m) \cdot_S s =& ((r \otimes 1) \cdot_A m) \cdot s \\ =& (1 \otimes s) \cdot_A ((r \otimes 1) \cdot_A m) \\ =& ((1 \otimes s) \cdot_A (r \otimes 1)) \cdot_A m \\ =& (r \otimes s) \cdot_A m \\ =& ((r \otimes 1) \cdot_A (1 \otimes s)) \cdot_A m \\ =& (r \otimes 1) \cdot_A (1 \otimes s) \cdot_A m) \\ =& r \cdot_R ((1 \otimes s) \cdot_A m) \\ =& r \cdot_R (m \cdot_S s) \end{align*}

2.1.2. R-S-Mod \(\rightarrow\) A-Mod

Consider

\begin{align*} (\sum (r \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} s)) \cdot m \coloneqq \sum (r \cdot_R m \cdot_S s) \end{align*}
2.1.2.1. unit

follows from

\begin{align*} (1 \otimes 1) \cdot m =& 1 \cdot_R m \cdot_S 1 \\ =& m \end{align*}
2.1.2.2. distributivity

for

\begin{align*} ((r_1 \otimes s_1) + (r_2 \otimes s_2) ) \cdot m \end{align*}

this follows by definition

the other direction

\begin{align*} (r \otimes s) \cdot (m_1 + m_2) =& r \cdot (m_1 + m_2) \cdot s \\ =& (r m_1 + r m_2 ) \cdot s \\ =& r m_1 s + r m_2 s \\ =& (r \otimes s) \cdot m_1 + (r \otimes s) \cdot m_2 \end{align*}
2.1.2.3. multiplicative
  1. elementary tensor
    \begin{align*} (r_1 \otimes s_1) \cdot_{R \otimes S^{\mathrm{op}}} ((r_2 \cdot s_2) \cdot_{R \otimes S^{\mathrm{op}}} m ) =& (r_1 \otimes s_1) \cdot_{R \otimes S^{\mathrm{op}}} \cdot (r_2 \cdot_R m \cdot_S s_2) \\ =& (r_1 \cdot_R r_2 \cdot_R m \cdot_S s_2 \cdot_S s_1) \\ =& (r_1 \cdot_R r_2 \cdot_R m \cdot_S (s_1 \cdot_{S^{\mathrm{op}}} s_2)) \\ =& (r_1 \cdot_R r_2 \otimes s_1 \cdot_{S^{\mathrm{op}}} s_2) \cdot m =& ((r_1 \otimes s_1) \cdot_{R \otimes S^{\mathrm{op}}} (r_2 \otimes s_2)) \cdot_{R \otimes S^{\mathrm{op}}} m \end{align*}
  2. general case

    follows from distributivity, as

    \begin{align*} (\sum \alpha_i \cdot ((\sum \beta_j) \cdot m)) =& (\sum \alpha_i \cdot ((\sum \beta_i m))) \\ =& (\sum \alpha_i \cdot (\sum (\beta_i m))) \\ =& (\sum \alpha_i (\sum (\beta_i m))) \\ =& (\sum \sum (\alpha_i \cdot (\beta_i m))) \\ =& (\sum \sum (\alpha_i \cdot \beta_j) \cdot m) \\ =& (\sum \sum (\alpha_i \cdot \beta_j)) \cdot m \\ =& (\sum \alpha_i \cdot \sum \beta_j) \cdot m \end{align*}

2.2. morphism

2.2.0.1. R-S-Mod \(\rightarrow\) S-Mod

Let \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) be a bimodule homomorphism. Then

\begin{align*} \varphi((r \otimes s) \cdot -) =& \varphi(r \cdot - \cdot s) \\ =& r \cdot \varphi(- \cdot s) \\ =& r \cdot \varphi(-) \cdot s \\ =& (r \otimes s) \cdot \varphi(-) \end{align*}
2.2.0.2. A-Mod \(\rightarrow\) R-S-Mod

Let \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) be an A-mod homomorphism. Then

\begin{align*} \varphi(r \cdot_R - \cdot_S s) =& \varphi( (r \otimes 1) \cdot (1 \otimes s) \cdot_A -) \\ =& (r \otimes 1) \cdot_A (1 \otimes s) \cdot_A \varphi(-) \\ =& r \cdot_R \varphi(-) \cdot_S s \end{align*}
