topological space as colimit of an open covering

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(O_i\) an open covering of \(X\). Let \(\mathcal{O}\) be the full subcategory of category Top containing \(O_i\) and any finite intersection \(\bigcap_{i = 1}^n O_i\) with the subspace topology Then \(X\) is a filtered colimit of the inclusion functor \(\iota: \mathcal{O} \rightarrowtail \mathrm{Top}\)

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. let \(\mathrm{Set}(X) = \mathrm{Set}(\colim_I)\) (cf. forgetful functor from Top to Set admits left adjoints and left adjoint preserves colimits, limit in Set), as taking the set theoretic bijection and equipping \(X\) with the initial topology (cf. initial topology for a single bijection as homeomorphism)

by colimit of a space finer than the original space, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}: \colim_I \rightarrow& X \end{align*}

is continuous

by construction,

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{\vert C_i}: (C_i)_{X} \rightarrow (C_i)_{\colim} \end{align*}

is continuous.

Hence by glueing lemma for an open covering, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}: X \rightarrow& \colim_I \end{align*}

is also continuous
