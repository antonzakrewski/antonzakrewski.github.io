reduced singular homology and wedge product

1. Proposition

Let \((X_i,x_i)\) be based spaces, which admit a pointed contractible neighbourhood of \(x_i\), and \(\tilde{H}_n\) the reduced singular homology, then it holds for the wedge product

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}_n( \bigvee X_i) \simeq \bigoplus_{i \in I} \tilde{H}_n(X_i) \end{align*}

2. Proof

By definition it holds, that

\begin{align*} \tilde{H_n}(\bigvee_{i \in I} X_i) = H_n( \vee X_i, *) \end{align*}

Then for each \(i \in I\), let \(U \coloneqq \bigvee U_i\) be the wedge product. It is open by definition of the quotient topology and furthermore by wedge product of pointed contractible spaces as contractible space the chain map \(\mathrm{const}: C_*(\bigvee U_i) \rightarrow C_*(*)\) is a quasi-isomorphism (cf. singular homology as homotopy invariant functor)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_*(\bigvee U_i) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{d}{0} \arrow[shift left=1]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{const}} & C_*(*) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{d}{0} \arrow[shift left=1]{d}{\iota}\\ C_*(\bigvee X_i) & C_*(\bigvee X_i) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then applying the homology functor results in a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(\bigvee U_i) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{d}{0} \arrow[shift left=1]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{const}} & H_n(*) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{d}{0} \arrow[shift left=1]{d}{\iota}\\ H_n(\bigvee X_i) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_{} }& H_n(\bigvee X_i) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Hence it follows, that their cokernels are naturally isomorphic (cf. colim functor, isomorphisms under functors)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(\bigvee U_i) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{d}{0} \arrow[shift left=1]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{const}} & H_n(*) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{d}{0} \arrow[shift left=1]{d}{\iota}\\ H_n(\bigvee X_i) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_{} }& H_n(\bigvee X_i) \\ \mathrm{coker}(\iota) \arrow[]{r}{\exists! \varphi}& \mathrm{coker}(\iota) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Therefore we get

\begin{align*} H_n(\bigvee X_i, *) \simeq& H_n(\bigvee X_i, \bigvee U_i) \end{align*}

using excision theorem for \(*\) gives

\begin{align*} H_n(\bigvee X_i, \bigvee U_i) \simeq& H_n(\bigvee X_i \setminus *, \bigvee U_i \setminus *) \end{align*}

and the homeomorphism

\begin{align*} (\bigvee X_i) \setminus * \simeq \coprod (X_i \setminus *) \end{align*}

(cf. subspace of wedge product without point as coproduct) gives

\begin{align*} H_n(\bigvee X_i \setminus *, \bigvee U_i \setminus *) \simeq H_n(\coprod X_i \setminus *, \coprod U_i \setminus *) \end{align*}

applying singular homology preserves coproducts gives

\begin{align*} H_n(\coprod X_i \setminus *, \coprod U_i \setminus *) \simeq \bigoplus H_n(X_i \setminus *, U_i \setminus *) \end{align*}

again by excision, we get

\begin{align*} \bigoplus H_n(X_i \setminus *, U_i \setminus *) \simeq \bigoplus H_n(X_i, U_i) \end{align*}

and again by similar reasoning for the cokernel

\begin{align*} \bigoplus H_n(X_i, U_i) \simeq \bigoplus H_n(X_i, *) \end{align*}
