Transformation: Exponentialfunktion zur Winkelfunktion

1. Satz

\begin{align*} e^{i\theta} =& \cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta) \\ e^{-i\theta} =& \cos(\theta) - i\sin(\theta) \end{align*}

2. Herleitung

2.1. a)

  • siehe Eulersche Formel

2.2. b)

  • Quotient komplexer Zahlen
  • Trigonometrischer Pythagoras
\begin{align*} e^{i\pi} =& \cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta) && \vert ^{-1} \\ e^{-i\pi} =& \frac{1}{\cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta)} \\ e^{-i\theta} =& \frac{\cos(\theta) - \sin(\theta)}{(\cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta)) \cdot (\cos(\theta) - i\sin(\theta))} \\ e^{-i\theta} =& \frac{\cos(\theta) - \sin(\theta)}{\cos^2(\theta) + \sin^2(\theta)} \\ e^{-i\theta} =& \frac{\cos(\theta) - \sin(\theta)}{1} \\ e^{-i\theta} =& \cos(\theta) - \sin(\theta) \end{align*}
