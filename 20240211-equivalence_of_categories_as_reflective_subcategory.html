equivalence of categories as reflective subcategory

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be equivalent categories. Then \(\mathcal{C}\) is a reflective subcategory of \(\mathcal{D}\).

2. Proof

follows from:

  • adjointification of an equivalence
  • adjoint equivalence of categories and fully faithful and essentially surjective functor
  • definition of a reflective subcategorie
