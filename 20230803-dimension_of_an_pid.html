dimension of a PID

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a principal ideal domain. Then the dimension is given by

\begin{align*} \mathrm{dim}(A) =& \begin{cases} 0 & \mbox{if } A \textrm{ is field} \\ 1 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a)

  • dimension of a field

2.2. b)

Suppose \(A\) is not a field. Then there exists a nonzero maximal ideal resp.

\begin{align*} (0) \subseteq \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

Using prime ideal and maximal ideal for a Principal Ideal it follows that each prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\) with \(0 \subseteq \mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\) is also a maximal ideal. Therefore \(\mathfrak{p} = \mathfrak{m}\)
