relative singular homology and excision theorem

1. Proposition

The relative singular homology functor satisfies the Excision Axiom of Homology:

For a topological space \(X\) and subspaces \(U,A \subseteq X\) with \(\overline{U} \subseteq \mathrm{int}(A)\) (cf. closure, interior) it holds, that

\begin{align*} C_*^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U, A \setminus U) \rightarrow C_*^{\mathrm{sing}}(X,A) \end{align*}

is a quasi-isomorphism

2. Proof

Let \(C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \vert X \setminus U, A)\) be the image of the map of chain complexes

\begin{align*} C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U) \otimes C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) \rightarrow& C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \\ \end{align*}

induced by \(X \setminus U \hookrightarrow X, A \hookrightarrow X\) on the elementary tensors.

Let furthermore \(H_{*}(X \vert X \setminus U, A)\) be the the homology

Then

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) \cap C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \vert X \setminus U, A) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes and furthermore, the top left term is given by \(C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A \setminus U)\)

By applying levelwise the first isomorphism theorem for groups, this induces an isomorphism on the horizontal cokernels

\begin{align*} C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U) \oplus C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) / C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) \simeq C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U) / C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A) \cap C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \setminus U) = C_{*}^{X \setminus U} / C_{*}^\mathrm{sing}(A \setminus U) \end{align*}

For any \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\), the composition

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(X \setminus U, A \setminus U) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} H_n(C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X \vert X \setminus U, A)/ C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A)) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} H_n(C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X)/C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(A)) = H_n(X,A) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is an isomorphism, where the first morphism is given by the isomorphism of the cokernels described above and the second by the 5-Lemma / 5-Lemma for quasi isomorphisms0
