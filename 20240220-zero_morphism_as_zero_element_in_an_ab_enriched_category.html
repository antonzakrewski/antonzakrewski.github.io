zero morphism as zero element in an ab enriched category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a preadditive category \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{A})\) objects and \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(A,B)\) the hom object. Then the neutralelement is the zero morphism

2. Proof

Since

\begin{align*} \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(0,0) \vert = 1 \end{align*}

it follows, that \(\mathrm{id}_{0} = 0\) is the neutral element of \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(0,0)\).

Then pre and postcomposition with \(0: A \rightarrow 0, 0 \rightarrow B\) gives a homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(0,0) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{A}}(A,B) \end{align*}

where by assumption the neutral element is preserved.

Thus

\begin{align*} \varphi(0) =& 0_{0 \rightarrow B} \circ 0_{0 \rightarrow 0} \circ 0_{A \rightarrow 0} \\ =& 0_{A \rightarrow B} \end{align*}
