generation of an polynomial ideal and vectorspace

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) the polynomial ring and \(I \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) an ideal. Let \(I_{m} = I \cap K[X_1,...,X_n]_{\mathrm{deg} = n\) be the vectorspace of polynomials in \(I\) with degree \(m\)

Then \(I\) has at least

\begin{align*} \mathrm{dim}(I_m) - n \cdot \mathrm{dim}(I_{m-1}) \end{align*}

generators

2. Proof(sketch)

\(n \cdot \mathrm{dim}(I_{m-1})\) is the maximal number of polynomials generated by polynomials with degree \(m-1\) by multiplying with a variable \(X_i\). Furthermore, the generators of the ideal \(I\) also generate \(I\) after multiplying with \(X_i\) (right ?)
