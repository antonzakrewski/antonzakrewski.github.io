colimit preserves coeffacability

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be a sufficiently (cocomplete) abelian categories, \(I: \mathcal{I} \rightarrow \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B})\) Then the coproduct of the functors is also coeffacable.

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim} \mathcal{F}_n: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B} \end{align*}

is also coeffacable.

2. Proof

It follows from colimit of functors for a cocomplete codomain, that \(\coprod_n\) exists and is given by

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_n \mathcal{F}_n: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B} \\ A \mapsto& \mathrm{colim}_n \mathcal{F}_n(A) \end{align*}

Choose epimorphisms \(f_n: P_n \rightarrow A\) such that \(\mathcal{F}(f_n) = 0\). Then consider

\begin{align*} A =& \mathrm{colim}_n P_n \\ f =& \mathrm{colim}_n f_n \end{align*}

using colimit functor preserves epimorphisms it follows that \(f\) is an epimorphism.

Furthermore by colimit of initial morphisms as initial morphism, the map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}(\mathcal{F}_n(f)) = 0 \end{align*}

is the zero morphism
