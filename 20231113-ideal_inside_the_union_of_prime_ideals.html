prime avoidance theorem for unions

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p}_1,...,\mathfrak{p}_n\) be ideals where \(\mathfrak{p}_3,...,\mathfrak{p}_n\) are prime and \(\mathfrak{a}\) an ideal (resp. additive subgroup, multiplicative monoid), such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \bigcup_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{p}_i \end{align*}

then there exists an \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{p}_i \end{align*}

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

w.l.o.g. we may assume that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p}_i \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_j \end{align*}

for \(i \neq j\), otherwise we may consider $\bigcupl ≠ i \mathfrak{p}l = \bigcup \mathfrak{p}l $

Proof by induction

2.1. induction basis

for \(n = 1\) this is trivial. for \(n = 2\) we may choose \(a_i \in \mathfrak{a} \setminus \mathfrak{p}_i\) and consider

\begin{align*} (a_1 + a_2) \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

it follows that \((a_1 + a_2) \in \mathfrak{p}_i\) and hence \(a_i \in \mathfrak{p}_i\), contradiction

2.2. induction step

Suppose \(\mathfrak{a} \not\subseteq \mathfrak{p}_i\) for arbitrary \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\). Then choose an element \(a_i \in \mathfrak{a} \setminus \bigcup_{i \neq j} \mathfrak{p}_j \subseteq \mathfrak{p}_j\) and consider

\begin{align*} a = a_1 \cdot ... \cdot a_{n-1} + a_n \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

Suppose \(a \in \mathfrak{p}_n\), then it follows that \(a_1 \cdot ... a_{n-1} \in \mathfrak{p}_n\) or by repeated usage of primeness \((a_1) \cdot (a_2 ... a_{n-1}) \in \mathfrak{p}_n\) that some \(a_j \in \mathfrak{p}_n\), contradiction as \(a_j \in \mathfrak{a} \setminus \mathfrak{p}_n\)

Suppose \(a \in \mathfrak{p}_{l}\) for \(l < n\), then it follows that as \(a_{l} \in \mathfrak{p}_{l}\) we may conclude that \(a_n \in \mathfrak{p}_l\)
