freyd theorem of large products

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(\kappa\) a cardinal such that for each \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) it holsd, tht

\begin{align*} \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B) \vert \leq \kappa \end{align*}

If \(\mathcal{C}\) has categorical products (resp. coproducts) of size \(\kappa\), then \(\mathcal{C}\) is a posetal category.

2. Proof

Let \(g,f: A \rightarrow B\) be morphisms and let \(\prod_{\kappa} B\) be the \(\kappa\) sized product for the discrete diagram \(J_B: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) Then there exists \(2^{\kappa}\) different natural transformations from \(J_A\) to \(J_B\)
