locally finite type morphism

1. Definition

Let \(X,Y\) be schemes and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a morphism of schemes. Then \(f\) is said to be locally of finite type, if there exists an open cover of \(Y\) by affine subsets \(V_i = \mathrm{Spec}(B_i)\), such that

\begin{align*} U_i \coloneqq f^{-1}[V_i] \end{align*}

can becovered by open affine subsets \(U_{i,j} = \mathrm{Spec}(A_{i,j}\), where each \(A_{i,j}\) is a finitely generated \(B_i\)-Algebra
