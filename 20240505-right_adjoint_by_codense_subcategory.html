right adjoint by codense subcategory

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{D}_0 \hookrightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a dense subcategory Suppose \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{R}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) are functors, such that \(\mathcal{R}\) preserves limits formed by \(\mathcal{D}_0\)

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{L} \vdash \mathcal{R}\) are adjoints
  2. for \(D \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D}_0)\) there exists a natural isomorphism
\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{L}(-),D) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \mathcal{R}(D)) \end{align*}

here naturalness is required for both components.

2. Proof

follows analogously to

  • left adjoint by dense subcategory
  • using covariant hom-functor preserves small limits
