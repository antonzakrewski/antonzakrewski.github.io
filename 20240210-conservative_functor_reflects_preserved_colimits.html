conservative functor reflects preserved colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a conservative functor, which preserves colimits of a shape \(\mathcal{J}\). Suppose that \(\mathcal{C}\) admits colimits of this shape \(\mathcal{J}\).

Then \(\mathcal{F}\) reflects colimits of the shape \(\mathcal{J}\)

2. Proof

follows dually to conservative functor reflects preserved limits
