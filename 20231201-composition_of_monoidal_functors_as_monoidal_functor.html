composition of monoidal functors

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be monoidal categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) and \(\mathcal{G}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{E}\) be monoidal functors.

Then the composition of functors

\begin{align*} \mathcal{GF}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{E} \end{align*}

is also a monoidal functor
