compact subset of wedge product

1. Proposition

Let \((X_i,x_i)\) be based spaces and \(\bigvee X_i\) the wedge product of based spaces. Suppose \(K\) is compact, then

\begin{align*} K \cap X_i = \iota_{*}^{-1}[K] \end{align*}

is also compact for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X_i \arrow[bend left]{rr}{\iota_{*}} \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & \coprod X_i \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & \coprod X_i / \sim = \bigvee X_i \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

Let \(O_i\) be an open covering of \(K \cap X_i\). Then for

  1. \(x_i \not\in O_i\) it follows, that \(\iota_*[O_i] \usbseteq \bigvee X_i\) is open, as \(\iota[O_i] \subseteq \coprod O_i\) is open with \(\iota^{-1}[\iota[O_i]] = O_i\)
  2. \(x_i \in O_i\) take \(\pi[\bigcup X_i \cup O_i]\) as open subset. with \(K_i \cap \pi[\bigcup X_i \cup O_i] = \iota_*[O_i]\)

Then there exists a finite subcover in \(\bigvee X_i\), which can be taken as finite subcover in \(X_i\)
