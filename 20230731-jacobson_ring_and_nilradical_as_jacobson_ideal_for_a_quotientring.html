jacobson ring and nilradical as jacobson ideal for a quotientring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring. TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is a Jacobson ring
  2. for each ideal \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) and the quotient ring the nilradical is the Jacobson radical \(\mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{a}} = \mathcal{J}_{A/\mathfrak{a}}\)
  3. for each prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A\) and the quotient ring the nilradical is the Jacobson radical \(\mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{a}} = \mathcal{J}_{A/\mathfrak{a}}\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of

  • jacobson radical as nilradical for a jacobson ring
  • quotient ring of a jacobson ring as jacobson ring

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

special case

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

using prime ideal and quotient ring as integral domain and integral domain as reduced ring we get

\begin{align*} \{0\} =& \mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{p}} \\ =& \mathcal{J}_{A/\mathfrak{p}} \\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \text{ maximal}} \mathfrak{m} \\ \end{align*}

hence this follows by the correspondence theorem for maximal ideals and quotient ring and correspondence theorem for ideals and quotient rings
