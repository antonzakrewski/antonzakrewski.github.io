monoid as monoidal category

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a monoid, then \(M\) is a monoidal category

2. Proof

Let \(m \in M\) be the objects and \(M\) be the discrete category Let \(e \in M\) be the tensor unit.

Suppose

\begin{align*} m \otimes n = m \cdot n \end{align*}

and let the associator, left unitor and right unitor \(\lambda, \rho, \alpha\) be the identity morphisms
