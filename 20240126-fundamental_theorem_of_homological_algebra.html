fundamental theorem of homological algebra

1. Proposition

Suppose \(P \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})_{\geq 0}\) is a chain complex of projectives and \(M \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) an acyclic chain complex. Moreover, let \(f_0: P_0 \rightarrow M_0\) be a morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P_2 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists} & P_1 \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists} \arrow[]{r}{}& P_0 \arrow[]{d}{f_0} \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ M_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & M_1 \arrow[]{r}{} & M_0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then there exists a chain map \(f: P \rightarrow M\), unique up to chain homotopy

2. Proof

2.1. existence

follows from extension of a chain map

2.2. uniqueness up to chain homotopy

follows from extension of chain homotopies, as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{r}{} P_1 \arrow[swap]{d}{f_1,f_1'} & P_0 \arrow[]{ld}{0} \arrow[]{d}{f_0,f_0} \arrow[]{r}{} & P_{-1} \arrow[]{ld}{0} \\ M_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{r}{} M_1 & M_0 \arrow[]{r}{} & M_{-1} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where the induced morphism is the zero morphism, as \(M\) is acyclic and hence \(H_n(M) = 0\).
