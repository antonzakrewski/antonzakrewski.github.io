CW Pair as cofibration

1. Proposition

Let \((X,A)\) be a CW Pair. Then \(\iota: A \rightarrow X\) is a cofibration

2. Proof

Since \(\iota: S^{n-1} \rightarrow D^n\) is a cofibration (cf. sphere into disk as cofibration), by cofibrations under coproducts it follows, that

\begin{align*} \coprod \iota: S^{n-1} \rightarrow D^n \end{align*}

is also cofibration.

Since cofibrations are mirrored under pushouts it follows, that the attaching map

\begin{align*} \iota': \mathrm{SK}_{n-1}(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{SK}_n(X) \end{align*}

is also a cofibration

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \coprod S^{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{\coprod \iota} \arrow[]{r}{\coprod \alpha_i} & \mathrm{SK}_n(X) \arrow[]{d}{\iota'}\\ \coprod D^{n} \arrow[]{r}{\coprod \beta_i}& \mathrm{SK}_{n+1}(X) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Let \(Z\) be a topological space and \(A \rightarrow Z\) a homotopy Then since each inclusion from \(\mathrm{SK}_n(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{SK}_{n+1}(X)\) is a cofibration

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[bend right]{rrrd}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{rrd}{} \mathrm{Sk}_0(X) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathrm{Sk}_1(X) \arrow[]{rd}{}\arrow[]{r}{} & ... \\ & & & Z \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

we can inductively construct homotopies $A → Z, \mathrm{SK}0(X) → Z, \mathrm{SK}1(X) → Z, …$. Then we get a diagram, which commutes by construction and thus, by cofibration and currying lift, we get by universal property a map

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& \mathrm{Map}([0,1], Z) \arrow[]{d}{}& \\ \mathrm{SK}_0(X) \arrow[]{ru}{} \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{}& Z & \\ \mathrm{SK}_1(X) \arrow[]{ruu}{}\arrow[]{d}{}& & \\ \vdots & & \\ & \mathrm{colim}(X) \arrow[dashed, bend right]{uuuu}{} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
