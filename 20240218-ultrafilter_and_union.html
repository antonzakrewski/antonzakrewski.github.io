ultrafilter and union

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a set, \(\mathcal{F}\) an Ultrafilter and \(A,B \subseteq X\) be subsets such that

\begin{align*} A \cup B \in \mathcal{F} \end{align*}

Then \(A \in \mathcal{F}\) or \(B \in \mathcal{F}\)

2. Proof

using ultrafilter and complement it holds, that either \(A \in \mathcal{F}\) or \(X \setminus A \in \mathcal{F}\). w.l.o.g. suppose \(X \setminus A \in \mathcal{F}\), then it follows, that

\begin{align*} (X \setminus A) \cap (A \cup B) \in \mathcal{F} \end{align*}

where Distributivgesetz für Vereinigung und Schnitt

\begin{align*} (X \setminus A) \cap (A \cup B) =& ((X \setminus A) \cap A) \cup (B \cap X \setminus A) \\ =& \emptyset \cup (B \cap X \setminus A) \\ =& (B \cap X \setminus A) \\ \subseteq B \end{align*}

Hence it follows, that \(B \in \mathcal{F}\)
