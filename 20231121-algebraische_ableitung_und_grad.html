formal derivation and degree

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(R[X]\) the polynomial ring and \(f \in R[X]\) a polynomial.

Then for the formal derivation \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} f\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} f) < \mathrm{deg}(f) \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from construction.

Note that depending on \(\mathrm{char}(R)\) and the degree of \(f\), the degree of the derivation may be less than \(\mathrm{deg}(f) - 1\)
