general cayley-hamilton theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring and \(M\) a finitely generated module over \(A\). Suppose \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) is an ideal and

\begin{align*} \varphi: M \rightarrow M \end{align*}

an module-homomorphism with

\begin{align*} \varphi(M) \subseteq \mathfrak{a}M \end{align*}

Then there exists a monic polynomial \(f \in A[X]\) with coefficients in \(A\) such that

\begin{align*} f(\varphi) = 0 \in \mathrm{End}_A(M) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(m_1,...,m_n\) be generators of \(M\). Since

\begin{align*} \varphi[M] \subseteq \mathfrak{a}M \end{align*}

we may write every

\begin{align*} \varphi(m_i) = \sum_{j=1}^n a_{ij} m_j \end{align*}

with \(a_{i,j} \in \mathfrak{a}\)

Then \(M\) is also an \(A[X]\)-module (cf. module with an endomorphism as module over the polynomial ring)

Let

\begin{align*} \overline{A} = (a_{i,j}) \in A^{n\times n} \end{align*}

the matrix with entries \(a_{i,j} \in \mathfrak{a}\)

Set

\begin{align*} B \coloneqq XE_n - \overline{A} \in A[X]^{n \times n} \end{align*}

and \(f = \det(B) \in A[X]\).

It follows that \(f \in \mathfrak{a}[X]\) is a monic polynomial.

By construction, if \(m = (m_1,...,m_n)^t\), then

\begin{align*} Bm = (XE_n - \overline{A}) m = 0 \end{align*}

Let \(\tilde{B}\) be the adjunct matrix with

\begin{align*} \tilde{B} B = \mathrm{det}(B) E_n \end{align*}

Then it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{det}(B) E_n m =& \tilde{B}B m \\ =& \tilde{B}0 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

showing \(\mathrm{det}(B)m_i = 0\) for every generator \(m_i\)

Then \(f\) suffices as polynomial, as for arbitarry generator \(m_i\)

\begin{align*} 0 =& \mathrm{det}(B) m_i \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^m a_k X^k \cdot m_i \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^m a_k \varphi(m_i)^k \\ =& f(\varphi)(m_i) \end{align*}

showing that

\begin{align*} f(\varphi) = 0 \in \mathrm{End}(M) \end{align*}
