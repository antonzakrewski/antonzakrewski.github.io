hom-set out of a bimodule with module structure

1. Proposition

Let \(R,S\) be rings, \(M,N\) left \(R\)-module and \(M\) a right \(S\)-module. Then the hom-set

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{R-Mod}}(M,N) \end{align*}

admits a natural left-\(S\) module structure with

\begin{align*} s \cdot f = f(\cdot -s) \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \cdot: S \rightarrow& \mathrm{End}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{R-Mod}}(S,M)) \\ s \mapsto& (f(-) \mapsto f(s \cdot -)) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(1\)

follows as

\begin{align*} f(-) = f(1 \cdot -) \end{align*}

2.2. additive

follows as

\begin{align*} \cdot(s_1 + s_2) (f \mapsto f((s_1 + s_2) \cdot -)) =& (f \mapsto f(s_1 \cdot - + s_2 \cdot -)) \\ =& (f \mapsto f(s_1 \cdot -) + f(s_2 \cdot -)) =& (f \mapsto f(s_1 \cdot -)) + (f \mapsto f(s_2 \cdot -)) =& \cdot(s_1) + \cdot(s_2) \end{align*}

2.3. multiplicative

follows as

\begin{align*} \cdot(s_1 \cdot s_2) =& (f \mapsto f(s_1 \cdot s_2 \cdot -)) \\ =& (f \mapsto f(s_1 \cdot -) \circ (f \mapsto f(s_2 \cdot -)) \\ =& \cdot(s_1) \cdot \cdot(s_2) \end{align*}
