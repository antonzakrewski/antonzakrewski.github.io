stalk of a spectrum and localization

1. Proposition

let \(A\) be a ring and \((\mathrm{Spec}(A), \mathcal{O})\) the spectrum of \(A\). Then for \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\), the stalk of the structure sheaf of the spectrum is isomorphic to

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{\mathfrak{p}}(\mathcal{O}) =& \mathcal{A}_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{align*}

2.3. injective

Let \(s,s'\) be sections with

\begin{align*} s(\mathfrak{p}) = s'(\mathfrak{p}) \end{align*}

then choose a common neighbourhood \(V\) of \(\mathfrak{p}\) where

\begin{align*} s(\mathfrak{q}) =& \frac{a}{f} \\ s'(\mathfrak{q}) =& \frac{b}{g} \end{align*}

for \(f,g \not\in \mathfrak{q}\)

Thus by construction of a localization, there exists an \(h \in S^{-1}\) such that

\begin{align*} h \cdot ( a \cdot g - b \cdot f) = 0 \end{align*}

Take \(V' \coloneqq D(h) \cap V\) as neighbourhood, then we get

\begin{align*} s_{\vert V'} =& s'_{\vert V'} \\ =& (\mathfrak{q} \mapsto \frac{a}{f} = \frac{b}{g}) \end{align*}
