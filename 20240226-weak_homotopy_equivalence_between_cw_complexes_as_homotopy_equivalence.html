weak homotopy equivalence between CW complexes as homotopy equivalence

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X)), (Y, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(Y))\) be a CW-Complex and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a weak homotopy equivalence Then \(f\) is a homotopy equivalence

2. Proof

using Whitehead theorem, it follows, that for an arbitrary CW-Complex, the induced morphism

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{hTop}}(A,X) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{hTop}}(A,Y) \end{align*}

is a Bijection, hence an isomorphism in category set.

Thus taking the full subcategory of Category hTop containing CW-complexes and using postcomposition with an isomorphism as isomorphism, it follows, that

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

is an isomorphism in hTop, hence a homotopy equivalence
