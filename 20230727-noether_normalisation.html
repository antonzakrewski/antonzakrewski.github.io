simple Noether Normalisation

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an infinite field, \(R\) a finitely generated K-Algebra with generators \(x_1,...,x_n\). Then there exists an injective K-algebra-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \iota: K[z_1,...,z_r] \rightarrowtail R \end{align*}

such that \(R\) is a finitely generated module over \(K[z_1,...,z_r]\)

2. Proof

proof by induction

2.1. \(n = 0\)

Then we get

\begin{align*} K = K[\emptyset] = R \end{align*}

2.2. induction

2.2.1. \(x_1,...,x_n\) algebraically independent

Then by assumption

\begin{align*} K[x_1,...,x_n] \rightarrowtail R \end{align*}

is injective

2.2.2. \(x_1,...,x_n\) not algebraically independent

by assumption, there exists a polynomial \(f \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) different from the zero polynomial such that

\begin{align*} f(x_1,...,x_n) = 0 \end{align*}

by simple monicalization of a multivariate polynomial over an infinite field, there exist \(y_i \coloneqq y_i + \alpha_i y_n\) and a \(\lambda \in K^{\times}\) such that

\begin{align*} g \coloneqq \lambda \cdot f(y_1,...,x_n) \end{align*}

is monic in \(K[X_1,...,X_{n-1}][X_n]\)

Note that \(y_i\) generate \(x_1,...,x_n\).

Furthermore, \(y_n\) is integral over \(K[y_1,...,y_{n-1}]\), as

\begin{align*} f(y_1 - \alpha_1 y_n,...,y_{n-1} - \alpha_{n-1} y_n, y_n) = f(x_1,...,x_n) = 0 \end{align*}
