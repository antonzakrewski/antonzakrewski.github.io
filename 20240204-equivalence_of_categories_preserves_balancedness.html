equivalence of categories preserves balancedness

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be equivalent categories. TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{C}\) is balanced
  2. \(\mathcal{D}\) is balanced

2. Proof

wlog 1) \(\implies\) 2) note that by adjointification of an equivalence, w.l.o.g. we have a left and right adjoint \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) (resp. \(\mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\)). Thus a bimorphism \(f \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{D})\) is also a bimorphism in \(\mathcal{C}\) (cf. left adjoint preserves epimorphisms / right adjoint preserves monomorphisms)

Hence it follows by fully faithful functor as conservative functor and adjoint equivalence of categories and fully faithful and essentially surjective functor
