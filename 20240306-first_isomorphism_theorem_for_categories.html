first isomorphism theorem for categories

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor. Suppose \(\mathcal{G}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{E}\) is a functor and \(\mathrm{coim}(\mathcal{F})\) the coimage. TFAE:

  1. there exists a unique factorization
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C} \arrow[]{r}{\pi} \arrow[swap]{rd}{\mathcal{G}} & \mathrm{coim}(\mathcal{F}) \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists! }\\ & \mathcal{E} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
  1. for \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) and \(f_1,f_2 \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) it holds, that
\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(A) = \mathcal{F}(B) \Rightarrow& \mathcal{G}(A) = \mathcal{G}(B) \\ \mathcal{F}(f_1) = \mathcal{F}(f_2) \Rightarrow& \mathcal{G}(f_1) = \mathcal{G}(f_2) \end{align*}
