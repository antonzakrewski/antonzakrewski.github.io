balanced category and extremal epimorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category.

TFAE:

  1. each epimorphism is an extremal epimorphism
  2. \(\mathcal{C}\) is a balanced

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(f\) be a bimorphism. Then

\begin{align*} f = f \circ \mathrm{id} \end{align*}

where the LHS is an epimorphism and \(f\) on the RHS a monomorphism.

Thus by assumption, \(f\) is an isomorphism, hence \(\mathcal{C}\) is balanced

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(e\) be an epimorphism with \(e = m \circ g\) for \(m\) as monomorphism. Then we conclude by precomposition as epimorphism implies epimorphism, that \(m\) is also an epimorphism, hence bimorphism and by assumption an isomorphism
