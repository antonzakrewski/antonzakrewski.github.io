Gaußsche Summenformel

1. Satz

\begin{align*} \sum_{k=0}^n k = \frac{n\left(n+1\right)}{2} \end{align*}

2. Beweise

2.1. Beweis durch vollständige Induktion

2.1.1. Induktionsanfang

\begin{align*} \sum_{k=0}^0 k =& 0 = \frac{0 \left( 0 + 1\right)}{2} \end{align*}

2.1.2. Induktionsschritt

\begin{align*} \sum_{k=0}^{n+1} k =& (n + 1) + \sum_{k=0}^{n} \\ =& (n + 1) + \frac{n\left(n + 1\right)}{2} \\ =& \frac{2n + 2 + n\left(n + 1\right)}{2} \\ =& \frac{\left(n + 1\right) \left(\left(n + 1\right) + 1 \right)}{2} \end{align*}

2.2. Substition

2.2.1. \(n = 2j\)

Sei $h =& j - k ⇒ k =& $

\begin{align*} \sum_{k=0}^{n} k =& \sum_{k=0}^{2j} k \\ =& \sum_{k=1}^{j} k + \sum_{k=j+1}^{2j} k \\ =& \sum_{k=1}^{j} k + \sum_{k=1}^{j} (k + j) \\ =& (\sum_{k=1}^{j} j - k + 1) + \sum_{k=1}^{j} (k + j) \\ =& \sum_{k=1}^{j} (2j + 1) \\ =& j \cdot (2j + 1) \\ =& \frac{1}{2} n \cdot \left( 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} n + 1\right) \\ =& \frac{n ( n + 1)}{2} \end{align*}

2.2.2. \(n = 2j + 1\)

\begin{align*} \sum_{k=0}^{n} k =& \sum_{k=0}^{2j + 1} k \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{2j + 1} k \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{2j} k + 2j + 1 \\ =& \frac{(2j)^2 + 2j }{2} + 2j + 1 \\ =& \frac{(2j)^2 + 2j + 4j + 2}{2} \\ =& \frac{(2j)^2 + (2j+1) + 4j + 1}{2} \\ =& \frac{(2j + 1)^2 + (2j+1)}{2} \\ \end{align*}
