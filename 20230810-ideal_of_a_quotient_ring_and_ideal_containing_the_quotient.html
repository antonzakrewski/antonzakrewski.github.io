correspondence theorem for ideals and quotient rings

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) an ideal and \(A/\mathfrak{a}\) the quotient ring. Then the map

\begin{align*} \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}: \{\overline{\mathfrak{b}} \subseteq A \vert \overline{\mathfrak{b}} \text{ ideal such that } \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \overline{\mathfrak{b}}\} \rightarrow \{\mathfrak{b} \subseteq A/\mathfrak{a} \vert \mathfrak{b} \text{ ideal}\} \end{align*}

is an isomorphism between posets (see: Category Pos)

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

follows from:

  1. preimage of an ideal as ideal
  2. surjektives Bild eines Ideals als Ideal

where \(\pi: A \rightarrow A/\mathfrak{a}\) is a algebrabic ring-epimorphism

2.2. respecting posets

follows from:

  • image and subsets
  • preimage and subsets

2.3. Bijection

2.3.1. a)

  • surjective image of a preimage, where \(\pi: A \rightarrow A/\mathfrak{a}\) is injective

2.3.2. b)

  • preimage of an injective image

Suppose \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2\) are different ideals with \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2\). Then there exists an \(a_1 \in \mathfrak{a}_1 \setminus \mathfrak{a}_2\) and it follows, that

\begin{align*} a_1 + \mathfrak{a} \not\in \pi[\mathfrak{a}_2] \end{align*}

as otherwise, there would exist an \(a \in \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{a}_2\) and \(a_2 \in \mathfrak{a}_2\) such that

\begin{align*} a + a_2 = a_1 \end{align*}

and hence \(a_1 \in \mathfrak{a}_2\)
