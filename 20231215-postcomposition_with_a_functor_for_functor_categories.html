postcomposition with a functor for functor categories

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories, \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{E}) \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E})\) the functor categorieas Suppose \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) is a functor, then precomposition

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow}: \mathrm{Fun}( \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{E}) \\ \mathcal{G} \mapsto& \mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{G} \\ \eta(-) \mapsto& \mathcal{F}(\eta(-)) \end{align*}

is a contravariant functor.
