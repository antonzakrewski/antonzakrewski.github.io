inner hom inherits local object isomorphism

Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a closed symmetric monoidal infinity category and \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{R}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) a symmetric monoidal localization.
Then for each pair of objects \(C,D \in \mathcal{C}\), where \(D\) is a local object, the unit of the localization

\begin{align*} C \rightarrow \mathcal{RL}(C) \end{align*}

induces an equivalence

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{map}}(\mathcal{RL}(C), D) \rightarrow \underline{\mathrm{map}}(C,D) \end{align*}

Proof

a) horrible proof

Note that \(D \simeq \mathcal{R}(d)\) for some \(d \in \mathcal{D}\)

\begin{align*} \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\mathcal{RL}(c), D)) \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\mathcal{RL}(c), \mathcal{R}(d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\mathcal{RL}(c), \mathcal{R}(d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathcal{RL}(c), \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(-, \mathcal{R}(d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathcal{RL}(c), \mathcal{R}(\underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\mathcal{L}(-), d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(c, \mathcal{R}(\underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\mathcal{L}(-), d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(c, \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(-, \mathcal{R}(d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(c, \mathcal{R}(d))) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{map}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(c, D)) \\ \end{align*}

where we use the symmetry & monoidal adjunction and internal hom
