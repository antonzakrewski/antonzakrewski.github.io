pi-1 tensor submodule of a quotient ring of the integers and gcd

1. Proposition

Let \(k,l \geq 0\), then the pi 1 torsion module

\begin{align*} T_k^1(\mathbb{Z}/l \mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Z}/ (\mathrm{gcd}(k,l) \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

consider the morphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: T_k^1(\mathbb{Z}/l \mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/( \mathrm{gcd}(k,l) \mathbb{Z}) [n] \mapsto& [n/ \mathrm{gcd}(l,k)] \end{align*}

using the classification of finitely generated abelian groups it suffices to show this for

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathbb{Z}/(p_1^n \mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/(p_2^m \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

for \(p_1 \neq p_2\) it follows, that \(p_2\) is an element in the multiplicative group, hence \(T_{p_2}^1 = \{0\}\) and \(\mathbb{Z}/ \mathrm{gcd}(p_1^n,p_2^m) \mathbb{Z} = \mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z} = 0\)

So we may assume \(p_1 = p_2\) For \(n \leq m\) we get the identity as isomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}/ p_1^n \mathbb{Z} \cong& \mathbb{Z}/\mathrm{gcd}(p_1^n, p_1^m) \\ =& \mathbb{Z}/p_1^n\mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

For \(n < m\) it follows that

\begin{align*} T_{p_1}^1 = \{p^{n-m} \cdot (0,1,...,p^m - 1)\} \end{align*}

Hence the map

\begin{align*} \varphi: T_{p_1}^1 \rightarrow& \mathbb{Z}/p^m \mathbb{Z} \\ p^{n-m} \cdot k \mapsto& k \end{align*}

is an isomorphism
