homotopic map and restriction of the domain

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(f,f' X \rightarrow Y\) homotopic relative to \(A \subseteq X\).

Then for \(B \subseteq X\), the restrictions \(f_{\vert B}, f_{\vert B}'\) are homotopic relative to \(A \cap B\)

2. Proof

follows from choosing a homotopy

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times X \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

and since the restriction of a continuous map is continuous,

\begin{align*} H_{\vert [0,1] \times B}: [0,1] \times B \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

is a desired homotopy
