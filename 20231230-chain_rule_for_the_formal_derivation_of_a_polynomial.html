product rule for the formal derivation of a polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(f,g \in R[X]\) polynomials and \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\) the formal derivative of a polynomial. Then it holds, that

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(f \cdot g) = \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(f) \cdot g + \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(g) \cdot f \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for monomials \(g_j = \alpha_j X^j, f_i = \beta_i X^i\). The general case follows from applying

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(f \cdot g) =& \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(\sum \sum g_j f_i) \\ =& \sum \sum (g_j' f_i + g_j f_i') \\ =& \sum \sum g_j' f_i + \sum \sum g_j f_i' \\ =& \sum g_j' f + \sum g f_i' \\ =& \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(g) \cdot f + g \cdot \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}f \end{align*}

Suppose we have

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(\alpha_j X^j \cdot \beta_i X^i) =& \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}(\alpha_j \cdot \beta_i X^{j+i}) \\ =& (j + i) \cdot \alpha_j \beta_i \cdot X^{j+i-1} \\ =& j \alpha_j \cdot \beta_i X^{j+i-i} + i \cdot \alpha_j X^{j+i-1} \\ =& \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} (\alpha_j X^j) \cdot (\beta_i X^i) + (\alpha_j X^j) \cdot \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} (\beta_i X^i) \end{align*}
