colimit in a slice category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(X \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) an object and \(\mathcal{C} / X\) the slice category. Let \(I: \mathcal{I} \rightarrow \mathcal{C} / X\) be a diagram and \(\mathrm{colim}\) a colimit after right whiskering with the forgetful functor \(\mathrm{Forget} \circ \mu\).

Then \(\mathrm{colim}\) is also a colimit of the original diagram

\begin{align*} I: \mathcal{I} \rightarrow \mathcal{C} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. construction

Suppose the following diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_1 \arrow[]{rdd}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C_2 \arrow[]{dd}{} & C_3 \arrow[]{ddl}{}\\ & & \\ & \mathrm{colim} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is a colimit.

Then consider the original diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_1 \arrow[bend right]{dddr}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C_2 \arrow[]{ddd}{} & C_3 \arrow[]{dddl}{} \\ & & \\ & & \\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Since the diagram commutes we get by universal property a unique morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_1 \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[bend right]{dddr}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C_2 \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[bend left]{ddd}{} & C_3 \arrow[]{dl}{} \arrow[bend left]{dddl}{}\\ & \mathrm{colim} \arrow[dashed]{dd}{} & \\ & & \\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Note that this is by universal property the only morphism making this diagram commute. Therefore \(\mathrm{colim} \rightarrow X\) had to be the original object before applying the forgetful functor

Suppose furthermore there exists an object \(Y \rightarrow X\) and a diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_1 \arrow[bend right]{ddr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[bend right]{dddr}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[bend left]{dd}{} C_2 \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[bend left]{ddd}{} & \arrow[bend left]{ddl}{} C_3 \arrow[]{dl}{} \arrow[bend left]{dddl}{}\\ & \mathrm{colim} \arrow[bend right]{dd}{} & \\ & Y \arrow[]{d}{} & \\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then by universal property there exists a unique morphism \(Y \rightarrow \mathrm{colim}\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_1 \arrow[bend right]{ddr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[bend left]{dd}{} C_2 \arrow[]{d}{} & \arrow[bend left]{ddl}{} C_3 \arrow[]{dl}{} \\ & \mathrm{colim} \arrow[dashed]{d}{} & \\ & Y & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus there also exists diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C_1 \arrow[bend right]{ddr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[bend right]{dddr}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[bend left]{dd}{} C_2 \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[bend left]{ddd}{} & \arrow[bend left]{ddl}{} C_3 \arrow[]{dl}{} \arrow[bend left]{dddl}{}\\ & \mathrm{colim} \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[bend right]{dd}{} & \\ & Y \arrow[]{d}{} & \\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. commuting

It follows by uniqueness of the induced morphisms that every part, especially

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & \mathrm{colim} \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[bend right]{dd}{} & \\ & Y \arrow[]{d}{} & \\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

2.3. uniqueness

It follows again by uniqueness of the induced morphisms that the morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & \mathrm{colim} \arrow[dashed]{d}{} \arrow[bend right]{dd}{} & \\ & Y \arrow[]{d}{} & \\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is unique
