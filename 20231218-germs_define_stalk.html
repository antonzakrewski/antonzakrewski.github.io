germs uniquely define stalk for a sheaf

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{Op}(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}\) a sheaf and \(U \subseteq X\) open. Suppose \(s,t \in \mathcal{F}(U)\) are sections such that each germ is equal, i.e. for \(x \in U\)

\begin{align*} s_{x} = t_{x} \end{align*}

then

\begin{align*} s = t \end{align*}

2. Proof

By equal germs imply equality on neighbourhoods, for each \(x \in U\), there exists an neighbourhood \(V_x \subseteq U\) such that

\begin{align*} s_{\vert V_x} = t_{\vert V_x} \end{align*}

Therefore, there exists a covering \(V_x\) of \(U\) and hence by locality of a sheaf, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} s = t \end{align*}
