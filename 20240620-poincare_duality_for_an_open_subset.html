poincare duality lemma for an open subset

1. Proposition

Let \(U \subseteq \mathbb{R}^n\) be open subset, \(\sigma_{X} \in H_n^{\mathrm{lf}}(X;R)\) an R-Orientation Then

\begin{align*} - \cap \sigma_{X}: H_c^p(U;M) \rightarrow H_{n-p}^{\mathrm{lf}}(U;M) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

see:

  • locally finite homology
  • cohomology of a space with compact support
  • cap product

2. Proof

  • every open subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is a union of convex open balls
  • intersection of convex sets
  • poincare duality for an open cover
  • poincare duality for a chain of open subsets
  • poincare duality for Rn

Note that \(U\) is a countable union of convex open subsets \(C_n \cong \mathbb{R}^n\). Then the claim follows from \(\bigcup C_n = U \cap V\) and poincare duality for a chain of open subsets and poincare duality for Rn)
