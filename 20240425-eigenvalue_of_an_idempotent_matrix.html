eigenvalue of an idempotent matrix

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(R^{n \times n}\) the matrix ring and \(A \in R^{n \times n}\) a matrix. Suppose \(A\) is idempotent and \(\lambda \in R\) an eigenvalue then \(\lambda\) is also idempotent.

2. Proof

By assumption there exists an Eigenvektor \(m \in R^{n \times n}\). Then we conclude that

\begin{align*} \lambda \cdot m =& A \cdot m \\ =& A^2 \cdot m \\ =& A \cdot (\lambda \cdot m) \\ =& \lambda \cdot (A \cdot m) \\ =& \lambda^2 \cdot m \end{align*}
