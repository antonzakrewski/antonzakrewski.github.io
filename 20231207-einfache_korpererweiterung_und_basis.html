simple field extension and basis

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) a simple field extension for \(L = K(\alpha)\) of degree \(n\) Then

\begin{align*} \alpha^0,...,\alpha^{n-1} \end{align*}

is a basis of \(L\) as K-vector space

2. Proof

Suppose there exist \(\lambda_i\) such that

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \lambda_i \alpha^i = 0 \end{align*}

then \(f(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \lambda_i X\) is a polynomial of degree \(n-1\). contradiction

hence \(\alpha^0,...,\alpha^{n-1}\) are linearly independent and by Austauschsatz von Steinitz, \(\alpha^0,...,\alpha^{n-1}\) is a basis
