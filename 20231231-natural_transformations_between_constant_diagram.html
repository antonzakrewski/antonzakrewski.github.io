natural transformations between constant diagrams

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(\mathcal{I}\) a nonempty index category. Then for an object \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) constant diagram functors \(\underline{A}, \underline{B}\), a natural transformation is precisely given by morphism \(f: A \rightarrow B\)

2. Proof

2.1. morphism \(\Rightarrow\) natural transformation

naturality follows from

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} I \arrow[]{d}{g} & & \underline{A}(I) = A \arrow[]{d}{\underline{A}(g) = \mathrm{id}_{A}} \arrow[]{r}{f} & B = \underline{B}(I) \arrow[]{d}{\underline{B}(g) = \mathrm{id}_{B}}\\ J & & \underline{A}(I) = A \arrow[]{r}{f}& B = \underline{B}(J)\\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. natural transformation \(\Rightarrow\) morphism

Let \(\eta: \underline{A}(C) \rightarrow \underline{B}(C)\) be a natural transformation. By assumption, there exists an \(I \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{I})\). Then \(\eta(I) \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(\underline{A}(I), \underline{B}(I)) = \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\), hence \(\eta(I) = f\) for some \(f: A \rightarrow B\)

furthermore, for \(J \in \mathcal{I}\) we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} I \arrow[]{d}{g} & & \underline{A}(I) = A \arrow[]{d}{\underline{A}(g) = \mathrm{id}_{A}} \arrow[]{r}{f} & B = \underline{B}(I) \arrow[]{d}{\underline{B}(g) = \mathrm{id}_{B}}\\ J & & \underline{A}(I) = A \arrow[]{r}{\eta(J)}& B = \underline{B}(J)\\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus by naturality of the diagram, we get by evaluating \(\mathrm{id}\)

\begin{align*} \eta(J) = f \end{align*}
