discriminant of an inseparable polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(f \in K[X]\) be a inseparable polynomial. Then the discriminant is

\begin{align*} \Delta_f = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

definitions, as there exists a root \(\alpha_n = \alpha_m\) with \(n \neq m\). Hence

\begin{align*} \Delta_f =& \prod_{i < j} (\alpha_i - \alpha_j)^2 \\ =& (\alpha_n - \alpha_m)^2 \cdot ... \\ =& 0 \cdot ... \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
