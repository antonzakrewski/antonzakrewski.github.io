Galoisgroup of a polynomial as Subgroup of Sn

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f \in K[X]\) a separable polynomial with degree \(\mathrm{deg}(f) = n\) Then the galois group of \(f\) is a subgroup of the symmetric group \(S_n\)

2. Proof

Let \(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\) be the roots of \(f\) in \(K(f)/K\). Then \(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\) generates \(K(f)\) as K-vectorspace. Thus any automorphism \(\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(K(f)/K)\) is determined by \(\{\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\}\)

By root under A-Algebra homomorphisms, the map

\begin{align*} \sigma_{\vert \{roots\}}: \{\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\} \rightarrow \{\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\} \end{align*}

is welldefined and injective, hence also bijective (cf.Äquivalenz von Injektivität und der Komposition zu einer Identitätsabbildung)

Therefore, \(\sigma_{\vert \{\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\}} \in S_n\)
