singular homology degree of the antipodal map

1. Proposition

Given the n-sphere and the antipodal map

\begin{align*} A: S^n \rightarrow S^n \end{align*}

the singular homological degree is

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(A) = (-1)^{n+1} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Note that

\begin{align*} A = r_{1} \circ ... \circ r_{n+1} \end{align*}

where \(r_i\) are the reflections.

Then by the multiplicativity of the singular homological degree on the sphere and singular homological degree of a reflection, we conclude

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(A) =& \mathrm{deg}(r_1 \circ ... \circ r_{n+1}) \\ =& \prod_{i = 1i^{n+1} \mathrm{deg}(r_i) \\ =& (-1)^{n+1} \end{align*}
