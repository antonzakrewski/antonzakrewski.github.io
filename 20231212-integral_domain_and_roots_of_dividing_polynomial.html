integral domain and roots of dividing polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(R[X]\) the polynomial ring and \(f,g \in R[X]\) polynomials. Suppose \(f \mid g\), then a root \(\alpha \in R\) of \(f\) is also a root of \(g\)

2. Proof

By assumption there exists an \(h \in R[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} f \cdot h = g \end{align*}

Applying the evaluation homomorphism for \(\alpha\) results thus in

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\alpha}(g) =& \varphi_{\alpha}(f \cdot h) \\ =& \varphi_{\alpha}(f) \cdot \varphi_{\alpha}(g) \\ =& 0 \cdot \varphi_{\alpha}(g) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
