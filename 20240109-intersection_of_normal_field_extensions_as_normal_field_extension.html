intersection of normal field extensions as normal field extension

1. Proposition

Let \(L/K\) be an algebraic field extension and \(E,F \subseteq L\) subfields, such that \(E/K, F/K\) are normal field extensions. Then the intersection \(E \cap F\) is a normal field extension over \(K\).

2. Proof

Suppose \(\alpha \in E \cap F\), then by assumption, we get a minimal polynomial \(f \in K[X]\). By assumption, \(f\) splits in \(E\) and \(F\), hence

\begin{align*} f = \prod (X - \alpha_i) \end{align*}

with \(\alpha_i \in E\) and \(\alpha_i \in F\).

Thus \(\alpha_i \in E \cap F\) and hence \(f\) splits in \(E \cap F\)
