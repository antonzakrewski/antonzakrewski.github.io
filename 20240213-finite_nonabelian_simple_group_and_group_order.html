finite nonabelian simple group, index subgroup and group order

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a finite non abelian simple group and \(H \subseteq G\) a subgroup of index \(n\). Then

\begin{align*} \vert G \vert \mid \frac{n!}{2} \end{align*}

2. Proof

As stated in finite nonabelian simple group and index of subgroup

there exists an algebraic group-monomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: G \rightarrow S_n \end{align*}

with \(n \geq 5\).

Then \(G \subseteq A_n\), as otherwise by intersection of a normal subgroup with a subgroup as normal subgroup of the subgroup it would hold, that

\begin{align*} G \cap A_n \trianglelefteq G \end{align*}

with \(G \cap A_n \subsetneq G\)

Therefore \(G\) would be not simple
