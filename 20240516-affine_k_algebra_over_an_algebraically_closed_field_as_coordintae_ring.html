affine k-algebra over an algebraically closed field as coordintae ring

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field, \(A\) an affine k-algebra. Then there exists an affine variety \(V\) such that the coordinate ring is isomorphic to \(A\)

\begin{align*} A \cong \mathcal{O}(V) \end{align*}

2. Proof

By assumption there exists an algebrabic ring-epimorphism

\begin{align*} \pi: K[X_1,...,X_n] \twoheadrightarrow A \end{align*}

with \(a_i \coloneqq \pi(X_i)\).

Let \(\mathfrak{a} = \mathrm{ker}(\pi)\) such that \(K[X_1,...,X_n] \cong A/\mathfrak{a}\) (cf. Homomorphiesatz für A-Algebren)

Using the characterization of a radical ideal and reduced quotient ring we conclude that \(\mathfrak{a} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\) is radical.

Let \(V \coloneqq \mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a})\). Then by Hilbert's Nullstellensatz

\begin{align*} \mathcal{I}(V) =& \mathcal{I}(\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a})) \\ =& \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \\ =& \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

Therefore by definition of the coordinate ring we obtain

\begin{align*} A(V) =& K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathcal{I}(V) \\ =& K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathcal{I}(\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a})) \\ =& K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathfrak{a} \\ \cong& A \end{align*}
