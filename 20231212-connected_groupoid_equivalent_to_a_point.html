connected groupoid equivalent to an object

1. Proposition

Let \(\tau\) be a connected groupoid. Then for \(A \in \mathrm{Ob}(\tau)\)

\begin{align*} \tau \cong A \end{align*}

where \(A\) is full subcategory containing \(A\)

Uses AC

2. Proof

using adjoint equivalence of categories and fully faithful and essentially surjective functor

2.1. construction

Let

\begin{align*} \iota: A \rightarrowtail& \tau \\ \end{align*}

be the natural inclusion.

Furthermore by connectedness, for each \(B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\), choose a morphism \(f_B: B \rightarrow A\) or \(f_B: A \rightarrow B\) (here AC is used) w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(f_B: A \rightarrow B\) and \(f_B\) is an isomorphism, as \(\tau\) is a groupoid.

Then we define a functor by

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \tau \rightarrow& A \\ B \mapsto& A \\ (g: C \rightarrow B) \mapsto& (f_{B}^{-1} \circ g \circ f_C) \end{align*}

2.1.1. identity

for the identity \(\mathrm{id}_{C}\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}_{C}) =& f_C^{-1} \circ \mathrm{id}_{C} \circ f_C \\ =& f_C^{-1} \circ f_C \\ =& \mathrm{id}_{A} \end{align*}

2.1.2. composition

Suppose we have \(g: B \rightarrow C\) and \(h: C \rightarrow D\). Then we get

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(h \circ g) =& f_D^{-1} \circ h \circ g \circ f_B \\ =& f_D^{-1} \circ h \circ f_C \circ f_C^{-1} \circ g \circ f_B \\ =& \mathcal{F}(h) \circ \mathcal{F}(g) \end{align*}

2.2. essentially surjective

follows immediatly, as each pair of objects is isomorphic in a connected groupoid

2.3. fully faithful

follows from:

  • precomposition with an isomorphism as isomorphism
  • postcomposition with an isomorphism as isomorphism

hence we have an isomorphism on hom-sets
