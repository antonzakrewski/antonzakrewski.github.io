product of factorials divides factorial of the sum

1. Proposition

Let \(a,b \in \mathbb{N}_0\), then

\begin{align*} a! \cdot b! \mid (a + b)! \end{align*}

2. Proof

Given the symmetric groups \(S_a, S_b\) and \(S_{a+b}\), the group order is \(a!, b!, (a + b)!\) (cf. Grad einer symmetrischen Gruppe) Furthermore, the group order of the direct product

\begin{align*} \vert S_a \times S_b \vert = a! \cdot b! \end{align*}

cf. group order of a product

By Satz von Lagrange, it suffices to write a algebraic group-monomorphism

\begin{align*} S_a \times S_b \rightarrow& S_{a+b} \end{align*}

as the image is a subgroup (cf. <Bild eines Gruppenhomomorphismus als Untergruppe>)

Here

\begin{align*} (\varphi_a, \varphi_b) \mapsto& \varphi \end{align*}

for

\begin{align*} \varphi(n) = \begin{cases} \varphi_a(n) & \mbox{if } n \in \{1,...,a\} \\ a + \varphi_b(n) & \mbox{if } n \in \{a + 1,...,a+b\} \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

is injective and welldefined (todo)
