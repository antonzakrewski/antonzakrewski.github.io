center of a p-group nontrivial

1. Satz

Let \(G\) be a group of group order \(\vert G \vert = p^k\) for a prime number \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) and \(k \in \mathbb{N}^+\). Then the center

\begin{align*} Z(G) \neq \{1\} \end{align*}

2. Beweis

w.l.o.g. suppose that \(Z(G) \neq G\). Then there exists an \(x \in G \setminus Z\) and therefore also \(\vert \mathcal{O}_x \vert > 1\) for the conjugation as group action and hence by Klassengleichung it follows for the orbit

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_x > 1 \end{align*}

Note that by orbit isomorphic to group modulo stabilizer it holds, that

\begin{align*} \vert \mathcal{O}_x \vert = \vert G : G_x \vert \end{align*}

and hence by Lagrange,

\begin{align*} \vert G : G_x \vert \mid \vert G \vert = p^k \end{align*}

and therefore \(1 < \vert G : G_x \vert = p^l\) for some \(l \in \{1,...,k\}\)

Hence by the Klassengleichung it follows, that

\begin{align*} p \mid \sum_{\vert \mathcal{O}_x \vert > 1}^{} \vert \mathcal{O}_x \vert \end{align*}

and since \(\vert Z \vert \geq 1\), it follows that

\begin{align*} \vert Z \vert = p^i \end{align*}

for some \(i \in \{1,...,k\}\)
