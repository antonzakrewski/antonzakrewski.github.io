connected covering map and collapse to the deck as covering map

1. Proposition

Let \(B\) be a topological space, \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a connected covering map and \(\mathrm{Deck}(p)\) the deck transformation.

Then there exists a factorization

\begin{align*} E \rightarrow E/\mathrm{Deck}(p) \rightarrow B \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} f: E/\mathrm{Deck}(p) \rightarrow& B \\ [e] \mapsto& p(e) \end{align*}

it is a welldefined set-theoretic map, as \(\mathrm{Deck}(p)\) maps within the fibre. Furthermore, it is continuous by universal property of the quotient topology, as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} E \arrow[swap]{rd}{p} \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & E/\mathrm{Deck}(p) \arrow[]{d}{f}\\ & E \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
