nilradical as jacobson radical for a polynomial ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(A[X_i]\) a polynomial ring. Then it holds for the nilradical and the Jacobson radical

\begin{align*} \mathcal{N}_{A[X_i]} = \mathcal{J}_{A[X_i]} \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(A[X]\) as

\begin{align*} A[X_i \vert i \neq j][X_j] = A[X_i] \end{align*}

2.1. \(\subseteq\)

nilradical as subset of the jacobson radical

2.2. \(\supseteq\)

suppose \(a \not\in \mathcal{N}\). Then \(a\) is not nilpotent, hence by units in a polynomial ring we conclude, that

\begin{align*} a \cdot x + 1 \end{align*}

is not a unit.

therfore, there exists a maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m}\) with \(a \cdot x + 1 \in \mathfrak{m}\) (cf. maximal ideal and non-unit) Note, that \(a \not\in \mathfrak{m}\), as otherwise \(a \cdot x \in \mathfrak{m}\) and therefore

\begin{align*} a \cdot x + 1 - a \cdot x = 1 \in \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

contradiction to \(\mathfrak{m}\) being maximal (cf. von einer Einheit erzeugtes Ideal und triviales Ideal)
