homotopy group of a totally disconnected space as zero group

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a totally disconnected space with a basepoint \(x_0\) Then the homotopygroup \(\pi_n((X,x_0)) = 0\) is the zero group

2. Proof

By assumption

\begin{align*} \pi_n(X) = \pi_0(\mathrm{Map}_{*}(S^n, (X,x_0))) \end{align*}

Furthermore, since the image of a connected set is connected, it follows, that for each \(f \in \mathrm{Map}_{*}(S^n, (X,x_0))\) the image \(f[S^n]\) is connected. By assumption, \(x_0 \in f[S^n]\), thus \(f[S^n] = \{x_0\}\). Hence the mapping space has cardinality \(1\).

The zero group is the only group with cardinality one
