ring as module over a group ring

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(R\) a ring. Then \(R\) is an module over the group ring \(RG\) by

\begin{align*} (\sum_{g\in G}^{} r_g g ) \cdot r = \sum_{g,g' \in G} (r_g r_{g'}) \cdot (g \cdot g') \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from

  • ring epimorphism from the group ring to the ring

and

  • restriction of scalars of a module for a ringhomomorphism
