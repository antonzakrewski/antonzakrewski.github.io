disjoint open cover of a clopen set induces clopen sets

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(T\) a clopen Set and

\begin{align*} \sqcup_{i \in I} O_i = T \end{align*}

a disjoint, open cover. Then each union

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in J} O_i \end{align*}

is also clopen

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(I = \{1,2\}\) (cf. Openness of a union of open sets)

Let \(S \coloneqq X \setminus T\). Then

\begin{align*} X \coloneqq S \sqcup T \\ =& S \sqcup O_1 \sqcup O2 \end{align*}

Then we get, that

\begin{align*} X \setminus O_1 = S \sqcup O_2 \end{align*}

is closed and furthermore, \(S \sqcup O_2\) as union of open sets is also open

Thus \(X \setminus O_1, O_1\) are clopen
