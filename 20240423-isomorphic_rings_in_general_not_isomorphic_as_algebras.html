isomorphic rings in general not isomorphic as algebras

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B,C\) be commutative rings such that \(B \simeq C\) are ring-isomorphic. Then \(A \rightarrow B, A \rightarrow C\) are in general not isomorphic as A-Algebras

2. Proof

Consider \(A = \mathbb{Z}[X]\) and \(B,C = \mathbb{Z}\). Then by Initial Object in Ring it follows that there exists only the identity

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

Furthermore by the universal property of a polynomial ring consider the maps

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathbb{Z} & & \mathbb{Z}\\ & \arrow[]{ru}{x \mapsto 1} \arrow[]{lu}{x \mapsto 2} \mathbb{Z}[X] & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which dont commute
