dihedral group and permutations

1. Proposition

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), then the \(n\)-th dieder group is the subgroup of the symmetric group \(S_n\) generated by

\begin{align*} \alpha =& \begin{pNiceMatrix} 1 & 2 ... & n \end{pNiceMatrix} \beta =& \begin{pNiceMatrix} 1 & 2 & ... & n - 1 & n \\ 1 & n & ... & 3 & 2 \\ \end{pNiceMatrix} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Note that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ord}(\alpha) =& n \\ \mathrm{ord}(\beta) =& 2 \end{align*}

hence \(\alpha^n, \beta^2\) are relations.
