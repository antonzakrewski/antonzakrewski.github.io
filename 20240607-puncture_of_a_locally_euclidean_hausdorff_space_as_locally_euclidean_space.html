puncture of a locally euclidean hausdorff space as locally euclidean space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a locally euclidean topological space and \(S \subseteq X\) a finite subset. Then the puncture \(X \setminus S\) is also locally euclidean.

2. Proof

Let \(x \in X \setminus S\). Then there exists an open neighbourhood \(U\) of \(x\) in \(X\) homeomorphic to \(\mathbb{R}^n\). w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(U\) is disjoint from \(S\), since \(X\) is hausdorff.
