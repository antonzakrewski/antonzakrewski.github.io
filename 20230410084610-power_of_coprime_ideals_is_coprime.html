power of coprime ideals is coprime

1. Theorem

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(I_i,I_j\) coprime ideals. Then for \(s,t \in \mathbb{N}\) follows

\begin{align*} I_i^t + I_j^s = R \end{align*}

2. Proof

proof by induction using the equivalence of ideal with a unit and \(R\):

2.1. basis

for \(t,s = 1\) follows by assumption

2.2. step

w.l.o.g. for \(I_i^{t+1} + I_j^{s}\) since the sum of ideals is symmetric. We know, that \(a \in I_{i}^t\) and \(b \in I_{j}^s\) exist such that \(1 = a + b\) Therefore we conclude, that \(1 = 1 \cdot 1 = (a + b)(a + b) = a^2 + b(a + b^2)\). It follows, that \(a^2 \in I_i^{2t} \subseteq I_i^{t + 1}\) and \(b(a + b^2) \in I_{j}^s\)
