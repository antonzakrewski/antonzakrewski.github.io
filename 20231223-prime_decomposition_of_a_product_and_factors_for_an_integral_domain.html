prime decomposition of a product and factors for an integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain, \(\alpha, \beta \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \beta = p_1 \cdot ... \cdot p_r \end{align*}

for prime \(p_1,...,p_r\).

Then after renumbering

\begin{align*} \alpha =& \lambda_1 \cdot p_1 \cdot ... p_{k} \beta =& \lambda_2 \cdot p_{k+1} ... \cdot p_{r} \end{align*}

for \(k \in \{0,...r\}\) and units \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2 \in A^{\times}\)

2. Proof

note that

\begin{align*} p_1 \mid \alpha \cdot \beta \end{align*}

and hence w.l.o.g. \(p_1 \cdot \alpha_{1} = \alpha\) by integral domain and cancellable ring, it follows, that

\begin{align*} p_2 \cdot ... \cdot p_r =& \alpha_1 \cdot \beta \end{align*}

and repeating this results in

\begin{align*} \alpha =& \alpha_1 \cdot p_1 \\ =& \alpha_2 \cdot p_1 \cdot p_2 \\ =& ... \\ =& \lambda_1 \cdot p_1 \cdot ... \cdot p_r \end{align*}
