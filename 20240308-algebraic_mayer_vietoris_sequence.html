algebraic mayer-vietoris sequence

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \arrow[]{r}{} ... & C_{n+1} \arrow[]{d}{\varphi_{n+1}} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & A_n \arrow[]{r}{\partial} \arrow[]{d}{\psi_n} & B_n \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_n} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & C_n \arrow[]{r}{\partial} \arrow[]{d}{\varphi_n} & A_{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{\psi_{n-1}} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & ... \\ \arrow[]{r}{} ... & C_{n+1}' \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & A_n' \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & B_n' \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & C_n' \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & B_{n-1}' \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & .. \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a diagram, where both rows are exact and \(\varphi_(-)\) are isomorphisms

Then there exists an exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{rr}{\partial \varphi_{n+1} \partial} & & A_n \arrow[]{r}{\psi_n \oplus \partial} & A_n' \oplus B_n \arrow[]{r}{\partial \oplus - \sigma_n} & B_n' \arrow[]{rr}{\partial \varphi_{n}^{-1} \partial} & & A_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{} & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. R-Mod

2.1.1. exact at \(A_n' \oplus B_n\)

2.1.1.1. zero map

follows from commutativity of the square

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_n \arrow[]{r}{\partial} \arrow[]{d}{\psi_n} & B_n \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_n} \\ A_n' \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & B_n' \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

hence

\begin{align*} (\partial \oplus -\sigma_n) \circ (\psi_n \oplus \partial) = 0 \end{align*}
2.1.1.2. image

2.1.2. exact at \(B_n'\)

2.1.2.1. zero map
2.1.2.2. image

2.1.3. exact at \(A_{n-1}\)

2.1.3.1. zero map
2.1.3.2. image
