monomorphism and integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) commutative rings, \(\iota: A \rightarrowtail B\) a monomorphism and \(B\) an Integral domain. Then \(A\) is also an integral domain

2. Proof

Let \(a_1,a_2 \in A \setminus \{0\}\), then \(\iota(a_1) \neq 0, \iota(a_2) \neq 0\) (see: minimal kernel and monomorphism) and

\begin{align*} \iota(a_1 \cdot a_2) =& \iota(a_1) \cdot \iota(a_2) \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} \iota(a_1) \cdot \iota(a_2) \neq 0 \end{align*}

since \(B\) is integral Therefore \(a_1 \cdot a_2\) can't be \(0\)
