contractible space and identity map

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space. TFAE:

  1. \(X\) is contractible
  2. the identity map is homotopic to a constant map,

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

by assumption, there exists a continuous map

\begin{align*} f: * \rightarrow X \end{align*}

and a homotopy inverse \(g: X \rightarrow *\) with a homotopy

\begin{align*} f \circ g \sim \mathrm{id}_{X} \end{align*}

here \(f \circ g\) is a constant map, since it factors through the single point \(*\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption, there exists a continous, constant map

\begin{align*} c: X \rightarrow X \\ \end{align*}

Then we define

\begin{align*} g: X \rightarrow * \end{align*}

as usual and

\begin{align*} f: * \rightarrow& X \\ * \mapsto& c(x) \end{align*}

for an \(x \in X\)

then we get

\begin{align*} g \circ f = \mathrm{id}_{*} \end{align*}

hence trivially homotopy equivalent to the identity and by construction

\begin{align*} f \circ g = c \end{align*}

which by assumption is homotopy equivalent to \(\mathrm{id}_{X}\)

Hence \(*\) and \(X\) are homotopy equivalent spaces
