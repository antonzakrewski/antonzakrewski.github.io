reflective subcategory inherits colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories such that \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) is a reflective subcategory. Suppose \(J: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) is a diagram and \(\mathrm{colim}_{\mathcal{F} \circ J}\) the colimit in \(\mathcal{D}\). Then \(\mathrm{R}(\mathrm{colim}_{\mathcal{F} \circ J}\) is a colimit of \(J\) in \(\mathcal{C}\)
