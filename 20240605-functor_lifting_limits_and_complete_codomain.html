functor lifting limits and complete codomain implies functor preserving limit

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor, which Suppose \(\mathcal{I}\) is an indexing category, \(I: \mathcal{I} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) a diagram and \(\eta: \mathrm{const}(C) \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) making \((I, \eta)\) a limit. Assume furthermore, that \(\mathcal{D}\) admits a limit of the diagram \(\mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{I}\) and that \(\mathcal{F}\) lifts \(\mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{I}\).

Then \(\mathcal{F}\) preserves \((I, \eta)\)

2. Proof

Choose a limit \(C_{D}\) of \(\mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{I}\). Then we may lift it by assumption to a limit of \(\mathcal{I}\). This is isomorphic to the original limit \(C\) and then applying \(\mathcal{F}\) preserves this isomorphism (todo), such that \(\mathcal{F}(D)\) and \(\mathcal{F}(C)\) are isomorphic, compatible with the projections.
