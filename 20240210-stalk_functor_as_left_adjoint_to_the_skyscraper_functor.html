stalk functor as left adjoint to the skyscraper functor

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(x \in X\) and \(\mathcal{C}\) be a (complete) category admitting (sufficiently many) filtered colimits Then the stalk functor is a left adjoint to the skyscraper sheaf functor (for the category of presheaves reps. the category of sheaves)

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for the category of presheaves, the other case follows analogously (or some reflector blabla)

Let \(A \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) and \(\mathcal{F} \in \mathrm{PSh}(X)\). Then it remains to show, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathrm{Stalk}_x(\mathcal{F}), A) \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{PSh}(X)}() \end{align*}

Consider

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathrm{Stalk}_x(\mathcal{F}), A) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{PSh}(X)}() (f: \mathcal{F}_x \rightarrow A) \mapsto& (f': \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathrm{Sky}(A)) \varphi^{-1}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{PSh}(X)}() \rightarrow& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathrm{Stalk}_x(\mathcal{F}), A) (f: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathrm{Sky}(A)) \mapsto& (f: \mathcal{F}_x \rightarrow \mathrm{Sky}(A)_x) \end{align*}

where \(f'\) is defined by

\begin{align*} f'(U) = \begin{cases} f \circ \iota: U \rightarrow \mathcal{F}_x \rightarrow A & \mbox{if } x \in U \\ 1: U \rightarrow 1 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}
