group homomorphism and construction as fundamental group

1. Proposition

Let \((X,x)\) be a based space, \(G\) a group and \(\varphi: \pi_1(X,x) \rightarrow G\) a group-homomorphism (cf. fundamental group). Then there exists a topological space \(Y\) and a continuous map

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

such that

\begin{align*} \pi_1(Y,f(x)) \simeq G \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} \pi_1(f): \pi_1(X,x) \rightarrow \pi_1(Y,f(x)) \simeq G \end{align*}

is \(\varphi\) up to canonical automorphism

2. Proof

using factorization of a group homomorphism through a coproduct

2.1. injective + free group

Let

\begin{align*} \iota:wa G \rightarrow G \coprod \langle h_i\rangle \end{align*}

Then using the generators \(h_i\), consider the wedge \(\bigvee_{i \in I} S^1\) of 1-spheres.

Then attach the 1-disk \(D^1\) at the point, to get \(H \coloneqq D^1 \coprod_* \bigvee_{i \in I} S^1\) and choose the opposite point of \(D^1\) as base point It follows, that \(H\) is homotopy equivalent to \(\bigvee_{i \in I} S^1\) (cf. wedge product with deformation retractible space homotopy equivalent)

Furthermore, consider the pushout \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd}

[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]

3. \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & X \arrow[]{d}{} \\

H \arrow[]{r}{} & ~{X} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here \(\bigvee S^1 \vee [0,\frac{3}{4})\) and \(X \vee (\frac{1}{4},1]\) suffice as open cover with connected intersection, namely \((\frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4})\) Thus by Seifert-Van kampen we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \pi_1(\tilde{X},*) = \pi_1(\bigvee S^1 \vee [0,\frac{3}{4}) \coprod_* \pi_1(X \vee \left(\frac{1}{4}\right, 1] \end{align*}

here \(( \frac{1}{4}, \frac{3}{4})\) is contractible, hence has trivial homotopy.

Furthermore, \(X \vee (\frac{1}{4}, 1]\) is homotopy equivalent to \(X\) by using product functor of topological spaces for a locally compact space as left adjoint and left adjoint preserves colimits for

\begin{align*} [0,1] \times X \vee ( \frac{1}{4}, 1] \simeq [0,1] \times X \copro_{* \times [0,1]} ( \frac{1}{4}, 1] \times [0,1] \end{align*}

and thus defining the maps by universal property through

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}(\pi_2): [0,1] \times X \rightarrow X (t,x) \mapsto x \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} H': (\frac{1}{4},1] \times [0,1] \rightarrow& (\frac{1}{4}, 1] \\ (t,x) \mapsto& x + (1 - x) \cdot t \end{align*}

for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} (\frac{1}{4},1] \times [0,1] \arrow[]{r}{H'}& (\frac{1}{4}, 1] \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & D^1 \arrow[]{r}{} & \tilde{X} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

3.1. surjective

Choose generators \(g_i\) of the kernel. Then consider the pushout

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \bigvee S^1 \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{} & X \arrow[]{d}{} \\ \bigvee D^1 \arrow[]{r}{} & \tilde{X} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Let \(c\) denote the center of \(D^1\)

Then we get a suitable covering by using \(\tilde{X} \setminus (\coprod c)\) and \(\bigvee D^1\).

Here \(\bigvee D^1\) is contractible.

Furthermore \(\tilde{X} \setminus (\coprod c)\) is homtopoy equivalent to \(\tilde{X}\) (todo)

Thus we get the pushout of groups

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \langle g_i\rangle \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \pi_1(X,*) \arrow[dashed]{d}{} \\ 0 \arrow[dashed]{r}{} & H \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

since \(g_1\) as gets identified with \(0\).
