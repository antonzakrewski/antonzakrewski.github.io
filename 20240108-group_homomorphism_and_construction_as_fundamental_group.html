group homomorphism and construction as fundamental group

1. Proposition

Let \((X,x)\) be a based space, \(G\) a group and \(\varphi: \pi_1(X,x) \rightarrow G\) a group-homomorphism (cf. fundamental group). Then there exists a topological space \(Y\) and a continuous map

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

such that

\begin{align*} \pi_1(Y,f(x)) \simeq G \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} \pi_1(f): \pi_1(X,x) \rightarrow \pi_1(Y,f(x)) \simeq G \end{align*}

is \(\varphi\) up to canonical automorphism
