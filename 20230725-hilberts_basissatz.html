Hilbert's basis theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring. TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is a noetherian ring
  2. \(A[X_1,...,X_n]\) is noetherian.

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

w.l.o.g. for \(A[X]\), the general case follows from induction and polynomialring in multiple variables and inductive construction

Let \(I \subseteq A[X]\) be an ideal, then \(J_n \coloneqq \{\alpha \in A \vert \exists f \in I \vert f = \alpha X^n + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_i X^i \}\) is an ideal of \(A\) (see: leading coefficients of an ideal as ideal) Because of the inclusion of ideals of leading coefficients $J0 \subseteq J1 \subseteq …$ and since \(A\) is noetherian (see: equivalent characterization of a noetherian ring) , there exists a \(J_m\) such that $J = Jm+1 = Jm+2 …$. Furthermore each \(J_i\) is finitely generated by \(\alpha_{i,1},...,\alpha_{i,n}\) (as \(A\) is a noetherian ring).

For each $αi,j$$ choose an element \(\beta_{i,j} = \alpha_{i,j} + \sum_{k=0,...,i-1} \lambda_{k} X^k\). Let \(G \coloneqq \{\beta_{i,j} \vert i \leq m, j \leq n\}\).

Then it follows that \(\langle G\rangle = I\): Let \(\alpha X^n + \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \lambda'_{k} \in I\). Then since \(\alpha \in J_n\) there exists an element of \(\sum \gamma_{i,j} \beta_{i,j} \in \langle G\rangle\) such that the leading coefficients agree.

Since \(G \subseteq I\) it follows that

\begin{align*} \alpha X^n + \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \lambda'_k - \sum \gamma_{i,j} \beta_{i,j} \in I \end{align*}

Furthermore

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(\alpha X^n + \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \lambda'_k - \sum \gamma_{i,j} \beta_{i,j}) < n \end{align*}

Therefore after iterating it remains to show that each \(\alpha \in I\) with \(\mathrm{deg}(\alpha) \leq 0\) is in \(\langle G\rangle\). Here the leading coefficient is precisely an element \(J_0\) and therefore generated by some \(\beta_{i,j}\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from the algebrabic ring-epimorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: A[X_1,...,X_n] \rightarrow& A \\ X_i \mapsto& 0 \end{align*}

and image of a noetherian ring as noetherian ring
