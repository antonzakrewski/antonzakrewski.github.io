lifting functors for kucera theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be locally small categories and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D} \end{align*}

be a functor, which is injective on objects

Then there exist concrete categories

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{C}},\mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{D}} \end{align*}

with full and surjective on object functors to \(\mathcal{C}\) resp. \(\mathcal{D}\) and a functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}': \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{C}} \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{D}} \end{align*}

making

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{C}} \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{} \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists \mathcal{F}'}& \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{D}} \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{}\\ \mathcal{C} \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}} & \mathcal{D} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute

2. Proof

Choose a well order \(<_{\mathcal{D}}\) with sets as bounded subclasses for \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D})\). Then let

\begin{align*} A <_{\mathcal{C}} B \Leftrightarrow \mathcal{F}(A) <_{\mathcal{D}} \mathcal{F}(B) \end{align*}

be a well order on \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\).

Then by the steps in Kucera's Theorem, there exist the concrete categories \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{C}}, \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{D}}\)

Furthermore, we can define

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}': \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{C}} \rightarrow& \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{D}} \\ A \mapsto& \mathcal{F}(A) \\ (f_1,f_2) \mapsto& (\mathcal{F}(f_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2)) \end{align*}

It follows, that this is a welldefined functor, as it preserves the identity

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}'( \mathrm{id}_{A}) =& \mathcal{F}'(\mathrm{id}_{A}, \mathrm{id}_{A}) \\ =& (\mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}_{A}), \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}_{A}) ) \\ =& (\mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{F}(A)}, (\mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{F}(A)}) \\ \end{align*}

and that it is compatible with composition, since \(\mathcal{F}'\) preserves the wellorder, hence

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}((f_1,f_2) \circ (g_1,g_2)) =& (\mathcal{F}(f_1 \circ g_2 \circ g_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2))) \\ =& (\mathcal{F}(f_1) \circ \mathcal{F}(g_2) \circ \mathcal{F}(g_1)), \mathcal{F}(f_2))) \\ =& (\mathcal{F}(f_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2)) \circ (\mathcal{F}(g_1), \mathcal{F}(g_2)) \\ =& \mathcal{F}'(f_1,f_2) \circ \mathcal{F}'(g_1,g_2) \end{align*}

or <insert cases>

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}((f_1,f_2) \circ (g_1,g_2)) =& (\mathcal{F}(g_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2 \circ f_1 \circ g_2))) \\ =& (\mathcal{F}(g_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2) \circ \mathcal{F}(_1)) \mathcal{F}(g_2))) \\ =& (\mathcal{F}(f_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2)) \circ (\mathcal{F}(g_1), \mathcal{F}(g_2)) \\ =& \mathcal{F}'(f_1,f_2) \circ \mathcal{F}'(g_1,g_2) \end{align*}

Here it follows, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{C}} \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{\pi_{\mathcal{C}}} \arrow[dashed]{r}{\exists \mathcal{F}'}& \mathcal{L}_{\mathcal{D}} \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{\pi_{\mathcal{D}}}\\ \mathcal{C} \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}} & \mathcal{D} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes, as by construction

\begin{align*} \pi_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}'(A)) = \mathcal{F}(\pi_\mathcal{C}(A)) \end{align*}

is true.

Furthermore we have

\begin{align*} \pi_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}'(f_1,f_2) =& \pi_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{F}(f_1), \mathcal{F}(f_2)) \\ =& \mathcal{F}(f_1) \circ \mathcal{F}(f_2) \\ =& \mathcal{F}(f_1 \circ f_2) \\ =& \mathcal{F}(\pi_{\mathcal{C}}(f_1,f_2)) \end{align*}
