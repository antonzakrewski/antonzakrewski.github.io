surjection on homotopy groups between skeleton and CW complex

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex and \(x \in \mathrm{sk}_0(X)\). Then for \(k \leq n\) the induced map

\begin{align*} \pi_k(\mathrm{SK}_n(X),x) \rightarrow \pi_k(X,x) \end{align*}

is a surjection on homotopy group

2. Proof

Let \(g \in \pi_k(X,x)\) then using element of a homotopy group and sphere we may assume

\begin{align*} g': S^k \rightarrow X \end{align*}

Then as

\begin{align*} g'[\mathrm{Sk}_0(S^n,0)] = g'[*] = x = \mathrm{Sk}_0(X,x) \end{align*}

there exists a cellular map \(f'\) homotopic relative to \(*\).

Then as \(f'\) is a cellular map, we conclude that

\begin{align*} f'[S^k] = f'[\mathrm{Sk}_k(S^k)] \subseteq X \end{align*}

hence using restricting to the image gives \(f'_{\mathrm{im}\) where applying \(\iota\) gives

\begin{align*} \iota \circ f'_{\mathrm{im}} = f' \end{align*}

which is homotopic to \(g'\)
