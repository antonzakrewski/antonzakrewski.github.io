tensor product of Algebras as categorical coproduct in CAlg

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathrm{CALg}_A\) the Category of commutative algebras, then the categorical coproduct is the tensor product.

2. Proof

Let \(B_1, B_2\) be A-Algebras.

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & A \arrow[]{rd}{f_2} \arrow[]{ld}{f_1}& \\ B_1 & & B_2 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then the tensor product is an A-Algebra via

\begin{align*} \iota_1 \otimes \iota_2: A \rightarrow& M_1 \otimes M_2 \\ (a) \mapsto& (f_1(a), f_2(a)) \end{align*}

Given an A-algebra \(Z\) and morphisms of A-Algebras

\begin{align*} g_1: B_1 \rightarrow& Z \\ g_2: B_2 \rightarrow& Z \\ \end{align*}

Then define

\begin{align*} b_1 \otimes b_2 \mapsto& g_1(b_1) \cdot g_2(b_2) \end{align*}
