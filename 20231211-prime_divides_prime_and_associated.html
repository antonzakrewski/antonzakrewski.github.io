prime divides prime and associated

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain, \(p,p'\) be primes such that

\begin{align*} p \mid p' \end{align*}

Then \(p \sim p'\) are associated.

2. Proof

By assumption there exists an \(x \in A\) with

\begin{align*} p \cdot x =& p' \end{align*}

Therefore, \(p' \cdot 1 = p \cdot x\) and thus

\begin{align*} p' \mid p \cdot x \end{align*}

Since \(p'\) is prime, it follows, that \(p' \mid p\) or \(p' \mid x\).

Suppose \(p' \mid x\), then by assumption, there exists an \(x'\) such that \(p' \cdot x' = x\). Therefore by integral domain and cancellable ring

\begin{align*} p \cdot x =& p' \\ p \cdot x' \cdot p' =& p' \cdot 1 \\ p \cdot x' =& 1 \end{align*}

it follows, that \(p\) is a unit, contradiction to the definition of a prime element
