natural transformation and morphisms as epimorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, where \(\mathcal{D}\) admits pushouts and \(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})\) the functor category Suppose \(\eta \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{C})\) is a natural transformation

TFAE:

  1. \(\eta(A)\) is an epimorphism for all \(A \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\)
  2. \(\eta\) is an epimorphism

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

natural transformation containing only epimorphisms as epimorphism

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

analogously to natural transformation and morphisms as monomorphism for epimorphism and pushout and colimit of functors for a cocomplete codomain
