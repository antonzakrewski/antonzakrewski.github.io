equivalence creates colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) part of an equivalence. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) creates colimits

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • adjointification of an equivalence
  • adjoint equivalence of categories and fully faithful and essentially surjective functor
  • fully faithful functor reflects colimits
  • left adjoint preserves colimits
