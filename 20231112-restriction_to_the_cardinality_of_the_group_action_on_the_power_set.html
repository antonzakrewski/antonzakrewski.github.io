restriction to the cardinality of the group action on the power set

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(\mathcal{P}(G)\) the power set of the underlying-set, \(\Pi\) a cardinal and \(\mathcal{P}_{\Pi}(G) \subseteq \mathcal{P}(G)\) the subset of sets with cardinality \(\Pi\) Then

\begin{align*} \varphi: G \times \mathcal{P}_{\Pi}(G) \rightarrow& \mathcal{P}_{\Pi}(G) \\ (g, A) \mapsto gA \end{align*}

is a group action

2. Proof

follows from

  • group action on the power set

where multiplication with \(g\) is a Bijection, since multiplication with \(g^{-1}\) is the inverse map, hence \(\vert gA \vert = \vert A \vert\). Therefore, it is welldefined
