CW-Complex

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(A \subseteq X\) a subspace. A CW-complex on \(X\) consists of \(( \mathrm{SK}_n)\) a skeletal filtration

\begin{align*} (\mathrm{Sk}_{n(X))_{n \geq -1} \end{align*}

for \(\mathrm{SK}_{-1}(X) = A\) and a natural homeomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{n \rightarrow \infty} \mathrm{SK}_n(X) \simeq X \end{align*}

such that for each \(\mathrm{SK}_n\) there exist attaching maps of a CW complex for \(i \in I\)

\begin{align*} g_{i}^k: \partial [0,1]^k \rightarrow \mathrm{SK}_{n+1} \end{align*}

, i.e. there exists a pushout

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| S^n \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{SK}_n \arrow[]{d}{} \\ D^n \arrow[]{r}{g_{i}^k} & \mathrm{SK}_{n+1} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(D^n\) is the disk

Note that \(A\) is usually empty
