hom chain complex functor as left exact functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes Then the hom chain functor complex is a left exact functor

2. Proof

by definition we get

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(A,B)_{-} = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathcal{A}}(A[-]_p,B_p) \end{align*}

using left exact functor on chain complexes and degreewise left exact we may assume that there exists a fixed \(n\) resp. show that

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(A,B)_{n} = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathcal{A}}(A[n]_p,B_p) \end{align*}

Then as limit of left exact functors is left exact functor and limit functor as left exact functor, it suffices to show that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})}(-[n]_p,-_p) \end{align*}

is left exact for a fixed \(p\)

Since the shift functor is exact, we may further assume that \(n = 0\) or show that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathcal{A}}(-_p,-_p) \end{align*}

is left exact.

Since the p-th component functor of chain complexes is exact this reduces to

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathcal{A}}(-,-) \end{align*}

Hence the statement follows from ab-hom functor as left exact functor
