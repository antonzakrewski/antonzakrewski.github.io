radical as maximal ideal and primary ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p}\) an ideal, \(\mathfrak{m}\) a maximal ideal

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathfrak{p}\) is \(\mathfrak{m}\) primary ideal
  2. \(\mathfrak{p} = \sqrt{\mathfrak{m}}\)

2. Proof

radical of an ideal as intersection of prime ideals

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{q}} = \bigcap_{I \supseteq \mathfrak{q}} I = \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

Thus \(\mathfrak{m}\) is the only prime ideal with \(\mathfrak{q} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\). Hence for the canonical projection

\begin{align*} \pi: A \rightarrow& A/\mathfrak{q} \\ m \mapsto& m/\mathfrak{q} \end{align*}
