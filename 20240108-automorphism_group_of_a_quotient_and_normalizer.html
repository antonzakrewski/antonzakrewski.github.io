automorphism group of a G-Set quotient and normalizer

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(H \subseteq G\) a subgroup. Then there exists an group-isomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Aut}_G(G/H) \cong N_G(H)/H \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{Aut}_G(G/H)\) is the automorphism group of \(G/H\) as G-Set and \(N_G(H)\) the Normalizer.

see: quotient of a group and subgroup as subset

2. Proof

Consider the map

\begin{align*} \psi: \mathrm{Aut}_G(G/H) \rightarrow& N_G(H)/H \\ \varphi \mapsto& \varphi(H) \\ \left(\varphi: gH \mapsto gxH \right) \mapsfrom& xH \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

2.1.1. welldefined automorphism

Let \(x \in N_G(H)/H\) and \(g_1H = g_2H\), then there exists an \(h \in H\) such that \(g_2 = g_1 \cdot h\) Thus

\begin{align*} \varphi(g_1 H) =& g_1 \varphi(H) \\ =& g_1 x H \\ \varphi(g_2 H) =& g_2 x H \\ =& g_1 \cdot h \cdot x \cdot H \end{align*}

Furthermore, since \(x \in N_G(X)\), it holds, that \(x^{-1} \cdot h^{-1} \cdot x \in H\), hence

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot h \cdot x \cdot H =& g_1 \cdot h \cdot x \cdot (x^{-1} \cdot h^{-1} \cdot x) \cdot H \\ =& g_1 \cdot x \cdot H =& \varphi(g_1 H) \end{align*}

2.1.2. element in \(N_G(H)\)

Let \(\varphi(H) = xH\). Then for \(h \in H\), it holds, that

\begin{align*} \varphi(hH) =& \varphi(H) h x H =& xH \end{align*}

hence by assumption, there exists an \(h' \in H\) such that

\begin{align*} hx =& x h' \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} x^{-1} h x =& h' \end{align*}

showing that for arbitrary \(h\)

\begin{align*} x^{-1} h x \in H \end{align*}

2.2. bijection

2.2.1. a)

note that a automorphism \(\varphi\) is determined by \(\varphi(H)\), as for \(gH\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} \varphi(gH) =& g \cdot \varphi(H) \\ \end{align*}

2.2.2. b)

follows from construction
