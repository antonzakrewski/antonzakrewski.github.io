polynomial automorphism on the affine space and determinant as constant

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(\mathbb{A}_K^n\) the affine space and

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathbb{A}_K^n \rightarrow& \mathbb{A}_K^n \\ (a_1,...,a_n) \mapsto& (f_1(a_1,...,a_n),...,f_n(a_1,...,a_n)) \end{align*}

a regular function / polynomial map.

Then for the algebraic jacobian determinant, it holds that

\begin{align*} \det( \frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}) \in K^{\times} \end{align*}

is a constant polynomial different from the zero polynomial

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists an inverse morphism

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1}: \mathbb{A}_K^n \rightarrow \mathbb{A}_K^n \end{align*}

choose an open set \(U \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\) with polynomials $g1,…,gn such that

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1}_{\vert U} = (g_1,...,g_n) \end{align*}

and furthermore

\begin{align*} \varphi \circ \varphi^{-1} =& \mathrm{id}_{U'} \end{align*}

for some open \(U' \subseteq U\)

Then by the multidimensional chain rule for the formal derivative of a polynomial, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \left(\frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\partial g_i}{\partial x_j}\right) =& ( \frac{\partial \mathrm{id}_{x_i}}{\partial x_j }) \\ =& E_n \end{align*}

Furthermore, applying the determinant and the multiplicativity of the determinant results in Polynomials

\begin{align*} \det(\frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}) \cdot \det(\frac{\partial g_i}{\partial x_j}) = 1 \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} f' \coloneqq \det(\frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}), g' \coloneqq \det(\frac{\partial g_i}{\partial x_j}) \in K[X] \end{align*}

Hence by degree of polynomials under multiplication, it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(f') + \mathrm{deg}(g') = 0 \end{align*}

and therfore also \(\mathrm{deg}(f') = 0\) or \(f \in K\)
