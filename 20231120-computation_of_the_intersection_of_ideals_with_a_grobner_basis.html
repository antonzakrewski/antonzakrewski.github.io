computation of the intersection of ideals with a gröbner basis

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) the polynomial ring and \(I,J\) ideals. Then the Intersection of the ideals \(I,J\) is computed by:

  1. adding another variable \(t\) to \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) and taking the ideal \(\mathfrak{a} \coloneqq t \cdot I + (1 - t) \cdot J\)
  2. finding a Gröbner basis \(G_{\mathfrak{a}}\) with regard to the monomial order lex \(t > X_1 > ... > X_n\) (cf. Buchberger-Algorithmus)
  3. intersecting \(\mathfrak{a}\) with \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) (cf. gröbner basis and restriction)
