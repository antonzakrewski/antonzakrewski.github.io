submodule of a finitely generated free module over a PID as free module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain and \(M \subseteq R^n\) a submodule of a finitely generated free module. Then \(M\) is free.

2. Proof

Proof by strong induction over the \(n\)

2.1. induction basis

By Untermodul als Ideal and \(R\) as PID, we conclude that

\begin{align*} M \simeq (\alpha) \end{align*}

for some \(\alpha\). If \(\alpha = 0\), then \(M = (0) = 0 \simeq R^0\)

Else we get the module-isomorphism

\begin{align*} R \rightarrow& M \\ 1 \mapsto& \alpha \end{align*}

2.2. induction step

Consider the submodule \(R^{n-1} \hookrightarrow R^n\). Then for the map

\begin{align*} \pi: R^n \twoheadrightarrow R^n/R \simeq R^{n-1} \end{align*}

with kernel \(\mathrm{ker}(\pi) \simeq R\)

Thus for the map

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M \arrow[]{r}{} & R^{n} \arrow[]{r}{} & R^{n-1} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

we get \(M \cap R\) as kernel. By assumption it further holds that \(M \cap R \simeq R^{ \{0,1\}}\)

Therefore we also get a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & M \cap R \arrow[]{r}{} & M \cap R^n \arrow[]{r}{} & (M \cap R^{n})/R \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where by assumption \((M \cap R^{n})/R \simeq R^{n-1}\).

Thus by splitting lemma for modules we conclude that

\begin{align*} M \cap R^{n} \simeq& (M \cap R) \oplus ((M \cap R^n)/R) \simeq& R^{0,1} \oplus R^k \\ \simeq& R^{k, k+1} \end{align*}

(see exactification of a homomorphism)
