natural isomorphism between diagrams induce isomorphism between limits

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(J: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) and \(J': \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be diagrams and

\begin{align*} \eta: J \rightarrow J' \end{align*}

a natural isomorphism.

Then if the limit \(\mathrm{lim}_{J}\) exists, then also \(\mathrm{lim}_{J'}\) exists

2. Proof

2.1. a) speical case

corollary of

  1. isomorphisms under functors
  2. restriction, to those diagrams that admit a limit
  3. lim functor (is then welldefined)

2.2. b) general

is dual to natural isomorphism between diagrams induce isomorphism between colimits
