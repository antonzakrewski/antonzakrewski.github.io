algebraic field extension and subring as field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(L/K\) a field extension.

TFAE:

  1. \(L/K\) is an algebraic field extension
  2. each subring \(R \subseteq K\) is a subfield

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(R \subseteq L/K\) be a subring and \(\beta \in R \setminus \{0\}\). Since \(\beta \in L\) is an algebraic element, there exists a minimal polynomial \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i\). It is irreducible, hence \(\alpha_0 \neq 0\).

Furthermore, we get

\begin{align*} f(\beta) = 0 \sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_i \beta^i =& - \alpha_0 \end{align*}

thus, since \(\alpha_0 \in K^{\times}\)

\begin{align*} \beta \cdot - \frac{1}{\alpha_0}(\sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \alpha_{i+1} \beta^i) = 1 \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{\alpha_0}(\sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \alpha_{i+1} \beta^i) \in R \end{align*}

since \(R\) is closed under taking sums and multiplicatio"a

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition suppose \(L/K\) is not algebraic, hence there exists a transcendental element \(\tau\). Therefore, \(K[X] \cong K[\tau]\)
