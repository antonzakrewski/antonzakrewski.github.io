natural transformation between categorified groups and factorization through inner automorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups, \(BG, BH\) the categorification and \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}: BG \rightarrow BH\) functors. Then a natural transformation \(\eta\) is precisely given by an element \(h \in H\) such that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} G \arrow[swap]{r}{\mathcal{G}} \arrow[swap]{rd}{\mathcal{F}} & H \arrow[]{d}{ \left(-\right)^h} \\ & H \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes, where \(\left(-\right)^h\) denotes the inner automorphism

2. Proof

By functor as homomorphism for the automorphism group, we note, that \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}\) are group homomorphisms. Furhtermore, \(\eta\) is given by \(\eta(*) \in \mathrm{Hom}_{BH}(*,*)\), i.e. an element \(g \in H\)

Furthermore, we get for arbitrary \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*)\)

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(f) \circ \eta(*) =& \eta(*) \circ \mathcal{G}(f) \\ \mathcal{F}(f) =& \eta(*) \circ \mathcal{G}(f) \circ \eta(*)^{-1} \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(f) = h \cdot \mathcal{G}(f) \cdot h^{-1} \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(-) = \mathcal{G}(-)^{h} \end{align*}
