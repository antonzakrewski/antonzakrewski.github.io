degree of a splitting field of complex roots of unities for a prime

1. Proposition

Let \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) be a prime number, \(f = X^p - 1 \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) and \(L = K(f)\) the splitting field. then

\begin{align*} [L : \mathbb{Q}] = \begin{cases} 1 & \mbox{if } p = 2 \\ p & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(p = 2\)

follows from \(L = \mathbb{Q}\), as \(x^2 - 1 = (x + 1) (x - 1)\)

2.2. \(p \neq 2\)

Note that each root is an element

\begin{align*} \mathrm{e}^{\frac{n}{p} 2 \pi \mathrm{i}} \end{align*}

with \(n \in \{0,...,p-1\}\)
