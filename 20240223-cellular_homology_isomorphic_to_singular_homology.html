cellular homology isomorphic to singular homology

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex. Then we have for the cellular homology and singular homology

\begin{align*} H_n^{\mathrm{cell}}(X;M) \simeq H_n(X;M) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Consider the the exact columns

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_{n+1}(X_{n+1}, X_n; M) \arrow[]{d}{}& H_{n-1}(X_{n-2};M) = 0 \arrow[]{d}{}\\ H_n(X_n; M) \arrow[]{d}{}& H_{n-1}(X_{n-1};M) \arrow[]{d}{} \\ H_{n}(X_{n+1}(M) \arrow[]{d}{}& H_{n-1}(X_{n-1},X_{n-2};M) \\ H_{n}(X_{n+1}, X_n;M) & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

an the exact row

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 = H_n(X_{n-1};M) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X_n;M) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X_n,X_{n-1};M) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(X_{n-1};M) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

these combine to a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & H_{n+1}(X_{n+1},X_n;M) \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{rd}{\partial_{n+1}^M}& & H_{n-1}(X_{n-2};M) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ H_{n}(X_{n-1};M) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X_n;M) \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{\beta} & H_n(X_{n}, X_{n-1}; M) \arrow[]{r}{\gamma} \arrow[]{rd}{\partial_n^M}& H_{n-1}(X_{n-1};M) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ & H_n(X_{n+1};M) \arrow[]{d}{} & & H_{n-1}(X_{n-1},X_{n-2};M) \\ & H_n(X_{n+1}, X_n;M) & & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then by definition

\begin{align*} H_{n}^{\mathrm{cell}}(X;M) = \mathrm{ker}(\partial_n^M)/ \mathrm{im}(\piartial_{n+1}^M) \end{align*}

As \(H_{n-1}(X_{n-2};M) = 0\) and the column is exact, it follows, that \(\mathrm{ker}(\partial_{n}^{M}) = \mathrm{ker}(\gamma)\) (cf. exact sequence and monomorphism, kernel and postcomposition with a monomorphism) As \(H_n(X_{n-1};M)\) it follows, that \(\beta\) is also a monomorphism, hence \(\mathrm{im}(\beta) \simeq H_{n}(X_n;M)\) (cf. image of a monomorhpism in an abelian category)

Thus by exactness

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\gamma) = \mathrm{im}(\beta) \simeq H_n(X_n;M)$ \end{align*}

Using this isomorphism, \mathrm{im}(∂n+1M)$ corresponds to \(\mathrm{im}(\alpha)\)

Therefore

\begin{align*} H_{n}^{\mathrm{cell}}(X;M) \simeq H_{n}(X_n;M) / \mathrm{im}(\alpha) \\ \simeq H_n(X_{n+1};M) \end{align*}

Moreover, the inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota: X_{n+1} \rightarrow X \end{align*}

induces by n-th homology determined by n+1 Skeleton
