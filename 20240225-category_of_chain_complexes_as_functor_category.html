category of chain complexes as functor category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an additive category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) the category of chain complexes. Then \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) is a functor category

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{I}, \mathcal{A}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Take \(\mathcal{I}\) as Ab-enriched category with objects

  1. \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\)

and hom objects

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{I}}(n,m) = \begin{cases} \{ k \cdot \mathrm{id} \vert k \in \mathbb{Z} \} & \mbox{if } n = m \\ \{ k \cdot \partial \vert k \in \mathbb{Z}\} & \mbox{if } n = m - 1 \\ \{0 \} & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} \partial \circ \partial = 0 \end{align*}

then a chain complex is given precisely by an enriched functor
