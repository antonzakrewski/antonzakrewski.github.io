open covering of the disk induces closed decomposition

1. Proposition

Let \([0,1]^n\) for \(n \in \overline{N}\) be the disk and \(U_i\) an open cover.

Then there exists an \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} \prod_{i = 1}^n [\frac{j_i}{m}, \frac{j_i+1}{m}] \subseteq U_k \end{align*}

for suitable \(k\) is a closed covering of \([0,1]^n\)

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. choose the metric

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{d(-,-)}{2^i} \end{align*}

Then by lebesgue's number lemma, choose an \(\delta > 0\). Then since \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{d(-,-)}{2^i}\) converges for a bounded \(d(-,-)\), choose a \(\frac{1}{n} > 0\) such that

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{n \cdot 2^i} < \delta \end{align*}

Note that such an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) exists by archimedian axiom

Hence

\begin{align*} d(\prod_{i = 1}^n [\frac{k_i}{n}, \frac{k_i}{n+1}] ) < \delta \end{align*}

and therefore, there exists an Ui$ with

\begin{align*} \prod_{i = 1}^n [\frac{k_i}{n}, \frac{k_i}{n+1}] \subseteq U_i \end{align*}
