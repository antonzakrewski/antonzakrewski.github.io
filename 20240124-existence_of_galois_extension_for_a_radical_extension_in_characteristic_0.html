existence of galois extension for a radical extension in characteristic 0

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field of characteristic \(0\) and \(L/K\) a radical field extension. Then there exists a field extension \(M/L\) such that \(M/K\) is both

  1. radical
  2. galois field extension

2. Proof

proof by induction for the degree of the field extension \([L : K]\) (cf. radical field extension as finite field extension)

2.1. base

for \([L : K] = 1\) we get \(L = K\) and hence \(L\) is the splitting field over the empty set

2.2. step

2.2.1. \(r = 2\)

Suppose \(L = K(\sqrt[n]{a})\), then \(X^n - \alpha\) is separable (cf. field of characteristic 0 as perfect field) Furthermore, the roots are given by \(\sqrt[n]{a} \zeta^j\) for \(j \in \{1,...,n\}\) for a primitive root of unity \(\zeta\).

Then \(M = L(\zeta)\) is by construction a radical field extension and furthermore the splitting field of \(X^n - a, X^n - 1\)

2.2.2. \(r \geq 3\)

By assumption, we get

\begin{align*} K \subseteq ... \subseteq K_{r-1} \subsetneq K_r = L \end{align*}

where \(K_{r-1}\) has degree less than \(\vert L : K]\). Therefore, by induction <> there exists an radical, galois field extension \(N'/K\) with \(N'/K_{r-1}\).

Furthermore, \(K_{r-1}/L\) is by assumption a radical field extension, i.e. \(L = N(\sqrt[m]{c}\) for a \(c \in N\).

Consider the polynomial.

\begin{align*} f = \prod_{\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(N'/K)} (X^m - \sigma(c)) \in N'[X] \end{align*}

Then for arbitrary \(\tau \in \mathrm{Gal}(N'/K)\) i follows, that

\begin{align*} \tau(f) =& \prod_{\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(N'/K)} (X^m - \tau(\sigma(c))) \\ =& \prod_{\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(N'/K)} (X^m - \sigma(c)) \\ =& f \end{align*}

hence \(f \in K[X]\)

Let \(M\) be the splitting field of \(f\) over \(N'\). Thne by construction \(N'\) is galois.

Furthermore, \(M\) is given by adjunction of \(m\)-th roots of elements of \(N'\), hence \(M/N'\) is radical and thus also \(M/K\)

At last, for \(\sigma = \mathrm{id}\) it follows, that \(\sqrt[m]{c}\) is a root of \(M\), hence by construction \(L = N(\sqrt[m]{c} \subseteq M\)
