coeffacable

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be preadditive categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) a functor. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is said to be coeffacable, if for every object \(A \in \mathcal{A}\) there exists an \(P \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{A})\) and an epimorphism

\begin{align*} p: P \twoheadrightarrow A \end{align*}

such that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(p) = 0 \end{align*}

is the zero morphism
