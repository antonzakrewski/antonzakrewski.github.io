transitivity of normal subgroups for a sylow subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(H \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup and \(H \trianglelefteq N\) a p-Sylow-group. Then \(H \trianglelefteq G\)

2. Proof

Let \(g \in G\) be a arbitrary. Then since conjugation is a group isomorphism, it follows that \(g H g^{-1}\) is a p-Sylow group. Furthermore, since \(N\) is a normal subgroup, it follows from \(H \subseteq N\)

\begin{align*} g H g^{-1} \subseteq g N g^{-1} = N \end{align*}

that \(g H g^{-1}\) is a p-sylow-group of \(G\) Then by sole sylow group as normal subgroup it follows, that \(H = g H g^{-1}\) and hence \(H\) is normal
