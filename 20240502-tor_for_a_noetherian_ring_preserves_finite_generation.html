tor for a noetherian ring preserves noetherian moduels

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring, \(\mathrm{A-Mod}\) the category AMod and \(\mathrm{Tor}_n^A(-,-)\) the Tor Functor Then \(\mathrm{Tor}_n(-,-)\) preserves finitely generated modules, i.e. if \(M,N\) are finitely generated, so is

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_n(M,N) \end{align*}

(and therefore also noetherian)

2. Proof

by noetherian ring and finitely generated projective resolution for a finitely generated module we may choose a projective resolution

\begin{align*} (\bigoplus A^{n})_* \rightarrow M \end{align*}

where each component is finitely generated, hence noetherian.

Then taking the tensor product preserves noetherianness (cf. tensor product of noetherian modules as noetherian module) Furthermore the homology of a noetherian module is also noetherian module showing that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_n(M,N) \end{align*}

is notherian
