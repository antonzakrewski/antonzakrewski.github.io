1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a polynomial ring, \(I \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) an ideal, such that the origin \((0,...,0) \in V(I)\) (see: zero locus) Then for the initial ideal of the radical, \(\mathrm{in}(\sqrt{I})\), the map

\begin{align*} K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathrm{in}(\sqrt{I}) \rightarrow \mathrm{G}_{\mathfrak{m}}(K[X_1,...,X_n]) x_i \mapsto& x_i \end{align*}

is an ring-isomorphism, where \(\mathfrak{m} = \langle x_1,...,x_n\rangle\)

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

2.2. injective

2.3. surjective
