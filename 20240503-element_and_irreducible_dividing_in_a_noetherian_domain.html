element and irreducible dividing in a noetherian domain

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian domain and \(a \in A \setminus (A^{\times} \cup \{0\})\). Then there exists an irreducible element \(b\) such that

\begin{align*} b \mid a \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(a\) is not irreducible, hence there exist \(a_1, b_1\) such that

\begin{align*} a_1 \cdot b_1 = b \end{align*}

with \(a_1,b_1 \not \in A^{\times}\)

Then iteratively we may find a chain of ideals

\begin{align*} (b) \subseteq (b_1) \subseteq (b_2) \subseteq ... \end{align*}

Such that if it stabilizes at \(n\), then \(b_n\) is irreducible: Otherwise there would exist \(a_n,b_n\), which are not units such that \(a_n \cdot b_n = b\), hence

Here we also get \(\subsetneq\) for proper \(a_n,b_n\) since otherwise there would exist an \(\alpha\) such that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot b = b_n \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot a_n \cdot b_n = 1 \cdot b_n \end{align*}

and since integral domain and cancellable ring we would get \(1 = \alpha \cdot a_n\)

Since \(A\) is noetherian, each chain stabilizes, hence there exists an irreducible \(b_n \mid a\)
