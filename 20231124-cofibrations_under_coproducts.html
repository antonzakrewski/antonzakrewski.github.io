cofibrations under coproducts

1. Proposition

Let \(X_i,A_i\) be topological spaces and \(\iota_i: A_i \rightarrow X_i\) be cofibration. Then \(\coprod_i \iota: \coprod_i A_i \rightarrow \coprod_i X_i\) is also a cofibration.

2. Proof

Suppose there exists a map

\begin{align*} \coprod f_i: \coprod A_i \rightarrow X \end{align*}

and a homotopy

\begin{align*} H: (\coprod_i A_i) \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

since \([0,1]\) is locally regular compact, using product functor of topological spaces for a locally compact space as left adjoint and left adjoint preserves colimits, we get a canonical isomorphism

\begin{align*} (\coprod A_i) \times [0,1] \cong \coprod (A_i \times [0,1]) \end{align*}

restricting to \(A_i \times [0,1]\), we get a natural homotopy

\begin{align*} H_i: A_i \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

and using the individual Homotopy Extension Property, this results in homotopies

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}_i: X_i \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

Thus taking the coproduct we get a map

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}': \coprod (X_i \times [0,1]) \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

which naturally gives

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}: (\coprod X_i) \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}
