homotopy relative to a map as equivalence relation

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow X\) a continuous map for \(A \subseteq X\). Then homotopy relative to \(\varphi\) is an equivalence relation

2. Proof

2.1. reflexive

  • constant map

2.2. symmetry

follows from

\begin{align*} \overline{H}: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow& Y \\ (x,t) \mapsto& H(x,1-t) \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \overline{H} = H \circ (\mathrm{id}, (1 - t)) \end{align*}

2.3. transitivity

  • glueing lemma for a locally finite, closed covering
