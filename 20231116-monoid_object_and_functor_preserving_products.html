monoid object and functor preserving finite products and terminal object

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor preserving finite products and the terminal object

Then for a monoid object \(G \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\), the \mathcal{F}(G) ∈ \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D})\) is also a group object.

2. Proof

follows from the definition, as the product is preserved and furthermore commuting diagrams are also preserved
