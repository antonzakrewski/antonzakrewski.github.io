radical of an ideal and intersection of rabinowitsch spectrum

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) a strict ideal, then it holds for the radical

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{rab}}(A) \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(\subseteq\)

corollary of radical of an ideal as intersection of prime ideals

2.2. \(\supseteq\)

using again radical of an ideal as intersection of prime ideals we may assume that \(\mathfrak{a}\) is prime as

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \supseteq \mathfrak{a}} \mathfrak{p} \\ \overset{!}{=}& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \supseteq \mathfrak{a}} \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{rab}}(A) \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

it follows that \(A/\mathfrak{p}\) is an Integral domain (cf. quotient ring of an integral domain as integral domain)

Then by nilradical as jacobson radical for a polynomial ring and polynomial ring over an integral domain as integral domain we get

\begin{align*} \{0\} =& \mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{p}[X]} \\ =& \mathcal{J}_{A/\mathfrak{p}[X]} \\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(A/\mathfrak{p}[X])} \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

using the correspondence theorem for ideals and quotient rings it follows that

\begin{align*} \{0\} \leftrightarrow \mathfrak{p}[X] \end{align*}

showing that \(\mathfrak{p}[X] = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \supseteq \mathfrak{p}[X] \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}(A[X])}\)

Then taking the preimage of the inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota^{-1}: A \hookrightarrow A[X] \end{align*}

gives

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} =& \iota^{-1}[\mathfrak{p}[X]] \\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \supseteq \mathfrak{p}[X] \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}(A[X])} \iota^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}]\\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \supseteq \mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{rab}(A[X])} \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}
