product of normal subgroups as normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(N_1,N_2\) normal subgroups. Then the product \(N_1 \cdot N_2\) is also a normal subgroup.

2. Proof

Let \(g \in G\) and \(n_1 \cdot n_2 \in N_1 \cdot N_2\) for \(n_1 \in N_1, n_2 \in N_2\). Then we get

\begin{align*} g \cdot n_1 \cdot n_2 \cdot g^{-1} =& \left( g \cdot n_1 \cdot g^{-1} \right)\cdot \left( g \cdot n_2 \cdot g^{-1} \right) \end{align*}

since \(N_1,N_2\) are normal subgroups, we conclude, that \(g \cdot n_i g^{-1} = n_i' \in N_i\). Hence

\begin{align*} g \cdot n_1 \cdot n_2 \cdot g^{-1} =& \left( g \cdot n_1 \cdot g^{-1} \right)\cdot \left( g \cdot n_2 \cdot g^{-1} \right) \\ =& n_1' \cdot n_2' \in N_1 \cdot N_2 \end{align*}
