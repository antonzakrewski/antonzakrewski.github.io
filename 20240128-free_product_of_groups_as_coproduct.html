free product of groups as coproduct

1. Proposition

Given category Group and groups \(G_i\), the free product of groups is the categorical coproduct

2. Proof

Suppose there exists a group \(H\) and group-homomorphisms

\begin{align*} \varphi_i: G_i \rightarrow H \end{align*}

Then we define

\begin{align*} \varphi: \coprod G_i \rightarrow& H \\ g_i \mapsto& \varphi_i(g_i) \end{align*}

2.1. group homomorphism

Note that for \(g_i, g_i' \in G_i\) and \(g_j \in G_j\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} \varphi(g_i \cdot g_i' \cdot g_j) =& \varphi_i(g_i \cdot g_i') \cdot \varphi_j(g_j) \\ =& \varphi_i(g_i) \cdot \varphi_i(g_i') \cdot \varphi_j(g_j) \\ =& \varphi(g_i) \cdot \varphi(g_i') \cdot \varphi_j(g_j) \end{align*}

2.2. welldefined

w.l.o.g. for the relation \(g \cdot h \cdot k^{-1}\) for \(g \cdot h = k\) in \(G_i\) the general case follows from repeated application

\begin{align*} \varphi(g \cdot h \cdot k^{-1}) =& \varphi_i(g \cdot h \cdot k^{-1}) \\ =& \varphi(1) \\ =& 1 \end{align*}

2.3. uniqueness

Note that \(\varphi: \coprod G_i \rightarrow H\) is determined by the generators \(\iota_i[g_i]\) for \(g_i \in G_i\) (cf. group homomorphism determined by generators)
