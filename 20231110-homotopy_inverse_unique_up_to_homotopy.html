homotopy inverse unique up to homotopy

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a Homotopy equivalence Then a homotopy inverse \(g: Y \rightarrow X\) is unique up to homotopy relative to \(\emptyset\).

2. Proof

Suppose \(g_1,g_2: Y \rightarrow X\) are homotopy inverses. Then

\begin{align*} g_1 =& g_1 \circ \mathrm{id}_{Y} \\ \sim& g_1 \circ f \circ g_2 \\ \sim& \mathrm{id}_{X} \circ g_2 \\ =& g_2 \end{align*}
