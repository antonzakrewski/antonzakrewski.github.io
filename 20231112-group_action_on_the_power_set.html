group action on the power set

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(\mathcal{P}(G)\) the powerset of the underlying-set. Then

\begin{align*} \varphi: G \times \mathcal{P}(G) \rightarrow& \mathcal{P}(G) \\ (g, A) \mapsto gA \end{align*}

is a group action

2. Proof

2.1. identity

follows from

\begin{align*} \varphi(e, A) =& eA \\ =& \{e a \vert a \in A\} \\ =& \{a \vert a \in A\} \\ =& A \end{align*}

2.2. associativity

follows from

\begin{align*} \varphi(h,\varphi(g,A)) =& h(gA) \\ =& h \{g a \vert a \in A\} \\ =& \{h \cdot g \cdot a \vert a \in A\} \\ =& (hg)A \end{align*}
