naturality of an adjunction with respect to natural transformations

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_1,\mathcal{L}_2: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow& \mathcal{D} \\ \mathcal{R}_1,\mathcal{R}_2: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow& \mathcal{C} \end{align*}

be adjoint functors with \(\mathcal{L}_i \dashv \mathcal{R}_i\) with unit \(\eta_i\) and counit \(\epsilon_i\)

Suppose \(\tau_{\mathcal{L}}: \mathcal{L}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_2\) is a natural transformation and

\begin{align*} \tau_\mathcal{R}: \mathcal{R}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{R}_1 \end{align*}

the (cf. equivalence of subcategories of left and right adjoints)

Then for \(S \in \mathcal{C}, X\in \mathcal{D}\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\tau_{\mathcal{L}}(S)} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{f \circ \tau_{\mathcal{L}}(S)}& \mathcal{L}_2 \arrow[]{r}{f} & X \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

this results in a

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} S \arrow[]{r}{\overline{f}} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{\overline{f \circ \tau_{\mathcal{L}(S)}}} & \mathcal{R}_2(X) \arrow[]{r}{\tau_{\mathcal{R}}(X)} & \mathcal{R}_1(X) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(\overline{f}, \overline{f \circ \tau_{\mathcal{L}(S)}\) are the corresponding morphisms.Suppose \(\tau_{\mathcal{L}}: \mathcal{L}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_2\) is a natural transformation and

\begin{align*} \tau_\mathcal{R}: \mathcal{R}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{R}_1 \end{align*}

the (cf. equivalence of subcategories of left and right adjoints)

Then for \(S \in \mathcal{C}, X\in \mathcal{D}\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\tau_{\mathcal{L}}(S)} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{f \circ \eta_{\mathcal{L}}(S)}& \mathcal{L}_2 \arrow[]{r}{f} & X \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

this results in a

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} S \arrow[]{r}{\overline{f}} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{\overline{f \circ \tau_{\mathcal{L}(S)}}} & \mathcal{R}_2(X) \arrow[]{r}{\eta_{\mathcal{R}}(X)} & \mathcal{R}_1(X) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(\overline{f}, \overline{f \circ \tau_{\mathcal{L}(S)}\) are the corresponding morphisms.
