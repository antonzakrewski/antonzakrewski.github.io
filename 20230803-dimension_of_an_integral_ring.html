dimension of an integral extension

1. Proposition

Let \(A \subseteq B\) rings such that \(B\) is integral over \(A\). Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{dim}(A) = \mathrm{dim}(B) \end{align*}

see dimension

2. Proof

follows from

  • Going up theorem
  • contraction of an ideal and integral extension
