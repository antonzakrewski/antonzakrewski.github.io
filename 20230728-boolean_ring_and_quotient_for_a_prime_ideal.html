boolean ring and quotient for a prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a Boolean ring, \(\mathfrak{p} \subsetneq R\) a prime ideal. Then

\begin{align*} R / \mathfrak{p} \cong \mathbb{Z} / 2\mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Since \(\mathfrak{p}\) is prime, it follows that \(A/\mathfrak{p}\) is an Integral domain (cf. prime ideal and quotient ring as integral domain). Then for \(x \in A/\mathfrak{p}\) it follows by definiton that

\begin{align*} (x + \mathfrak{p})^2 = x^2 + \mathfrak{p} \\ =& x + \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

showing that \(x + \mathfrak{p}\) is idempotent. By idempotent element in an integral domain we conclude that \(x + \mathfrak{p} \in \{0,1\}\) showing htat

\begin{align*} A/\mathfrak{p} = \mathbb{F}_2 \end{align*}
