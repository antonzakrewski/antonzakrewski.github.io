height of a prime ideal and local ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A\) a prime ideal. Then for computing the height

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{a}) \end{align*}

we may w.l.o.g. assume that \(A\) is a local ring by passing to the localization of a ring at a prime ideal (cf. localization at a prime ideal as local ring)

2. Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) be a minimal prime ideal containing \(\mathfrak{a}\) resp. \(a\) Then consider the local ring (cf. localization, localization at a prime ideal as local ring)

\begin{align*} A_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{align*}

by the correspondence theorem for a localization at a prime ideal

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(A_{\mathfrak{p}}) \simeq \{\mathfrak{q} \vert \mathfrak{q} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A), \mathfrak{q} \subseteq \mathfrak{p} \} \end{align*}

using height of a prime ideal in a localization we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(S^{-1}\mathfrak{p}) = \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{p}) \end{align*}

There we may w.l.o.g. assume that \(A\) is a local ring by replacing with \(A_{\mathfrak{p}}\)
