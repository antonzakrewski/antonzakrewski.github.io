group order of the dihedral group

1. Proposition

Let \(n \geq 3\), then the group order of the dieder group is \(\vert D_n \vert = 2n\)

2. Proof

Note that for the product

\begin{align*} \alpha^{l} \cdot \beta^k, \alpha^{l'} \cdot \beta^{k'} \end{align*} \begin{align*} (\alpha^{l} \cdot \beta^{k}) \cdot (\alpha^{l'} \cdot \beta^{k'})^{-1} =& (\alpha^{l} \cdot \beta^{k}) \cdot \beta^{-k'} \cdot \alpha^{-l'} \\ =& \alpha^{l} \cdot \beta^{k - k'} \cdot \alpha^{-l'} \\ =& \begin{cases} \alpha^{l - l'} & \mbox{if } k - k' \equiv 0 \mod 2 \\ \alpha^{l + l'} \beta & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

hence a) for \((k,l) \neq (k',l')\) it follows by \(k \in \{0,1\}\) that \(k = k' \Leftrightarrow k - k' \equiv 0 \mod 2\), that \(l - l' \neq 0\) or \(\alpha^{l - l'} \neq 1\) b) for each \(g \in D_n\), by iterative application, there exists a representation

\begin{align*} g = \alpha^{k} \cdot \beta^{l} \end{align*}
