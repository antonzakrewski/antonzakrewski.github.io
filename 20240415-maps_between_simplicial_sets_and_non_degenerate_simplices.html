maps between simplicial sets and non degenerate simplices

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a concrete category and \(X,Y: \Delta \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) simplicial objects. Suppose \(f,g: X \rightarrow Y\) are morphisms in \(X\) such that

\begin{align*} f_{\vert \textrm{non degenerate}} = g_{\vert \textrm{non degenerate} \end{align*}

Then it follows that

\begin{align*} f = g \end{align*}

2. Proof

corollary of degenerate simplex as degeneration of a unique non degenerate simplex, as for each \(x \in X_k\) there exists a unique \(y \in X_{k'}\) and degeneracy maps \(\Sigma \coloneqq \sigma \circ .. \sigma\) such that

\begin{align*} x = \Sigma(y) \end{align*}

Using naturality of a morphism between functors (see: natural transformation) results in

\begin{align*} f(x) =& f(\Sigma(y)) \\ =& \Sigma(f(y)) \end{align*}

and therefore also

\begin{align*} \Sigma(f(y)) =& \Sigma(g(y)) \\ =& g(\Sigma(y)) \end{align*}
