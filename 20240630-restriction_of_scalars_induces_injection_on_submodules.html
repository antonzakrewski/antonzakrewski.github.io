restriction of scalars induces injection on submodules

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be rings, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) a surjective ring-homomorphism and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Res}_{\varphi}: \mathrm{B-Mod} \rightarrow \mathrm{A-Mod} \end{align*}

the restriction of scalars functor

Then there exists a natural injection

\begin{align*} \{A-\mathrm{submodules}\} \simeq \{B-\mathrm{submodules}\} \end{align*}

given by the identity on the underlying subsets

2. Proof

Let \(M\) be a \(B\)-module follows from functorality as

\begin{align*} N \hookrightarrow M \end{align*}

gets send to

\begin{align*} N \hookrightarrow M \end{align*}

while letting the underlying sets untouched
