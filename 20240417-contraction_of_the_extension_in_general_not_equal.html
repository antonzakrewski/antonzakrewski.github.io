contraction of the extension in general not equal

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be a commutative ring, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) be a ring-homomorphism and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq B\) be an ideal. Then for the contraction and extension is

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a} \subsetneq (\mathfrak{a}^{e})^{c} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a)

Consider

\begin{align*} \pi: R \twoheadrightarrow 0 \end{align*}

then

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{a}^{e})^{c} = R \end{align*}

regardless of \(\mathfrak{a}\)

2.2. b)

Consider

\begin{align*} \iota: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q} \end{align*}

and \(\mathfrak{a} = (2)\). Then

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{a}^{e})^{c} =& (2)^{c} \\ =& \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}
