group action on the orbit

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(X\) a set and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a group action.

Then for \(x \in X\), the group action on the orbit \(\mathcal{O}_x\) is a welldefined group action

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\mathcal{O}_x}: G \times \mathcal{O}_x \rightarrow \mathcal{O}_X \\ (g,x) \mapsto& \varphi(g,x) \end{align*}

2. Proof

compatibility with the group follows from the restriction.

furthermore, by definition of an orbit, the Codomain of \(\varphi_{\mathcal{O}_x}\) is welldefined.
