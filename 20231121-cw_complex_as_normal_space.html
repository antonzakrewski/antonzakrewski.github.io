CW complex as normal space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex.

Then \(X\) is a normal space

2. Proof

2.1. a)

  • CW complex as paracompact space
  • CW complex as hausdorff space
  • Parakompakte Hausdorff-Raum als normaler Raum

2.2. b)

Let \(A,B \subseteq X\) be closed.

using the unviersal property of the colimit we will inductively construct a continuous map

\begin{align*} f: X \rightarrow [0,1] \end{align*}

such that \(f^{-1}[0] = A, f^{-1}[1] = B\)

Then \(f^{-1}[(\frac{2}{3}, 1]), f^{-1}[[0,\frac{1}{3})]\) suffice as open neighbourhoods

Let \(A_n \coloneqq \mathrm{Sk}_n(X) \cap A, B_n \coloneqq \mathrm{Sk}_n(X) \cap B\).

2.2.1. induction basis

Then it follows that \(\mathrm{Sk}_0(X) = I^{\Delta}\) is T4 (and discrete), hence there exists a continuous map

\begin{align*} f_{0}: \mathrm{Sk}_0(X) \rightarrow [0,1] \end{align*}

with \(f_0^{-1}[0] = A_0, f^{-1}[1] = B_0\).

2.2.2. induction step

Suppose there already exists a map \(f_{n}\). Then for each $n+1$-cell we may use that \(D^{n+1}\) is a metric space, hence einfaches Urysohnsches Lemma für metrische Räume / Urysohnsches Lemma applies.

Furthermore \(\iota^{-1}[A_n] \subseteq D^{n+1}\) is closed by closed sets in a quotient topology

Thus we may extend the map on each cell, compatible with the n-skeleton. This induces a map on the \(n+1\) skeleton
