zero as unit and zero ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring with \(0 \in R^{\times}\) a unit Then \(R = \{0\}\)

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists an inverse \(0^{-1}\)

Let \(a \in R\), then

\begin{align*} a =& a \cdot 1 \\ =& a \cdot 0 \cdot 0^{-1}\\ =& 0 \cdot 0^{-1} \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

(cf. Multiplikation mit 0 (Ring))
