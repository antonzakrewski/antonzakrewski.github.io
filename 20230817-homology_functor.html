homology functor

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes for an abelian category \(\mathcal{A}\), then the homology functor is defined as

\begin{align*} H_n: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow& \mathcal{A} \\ (B_*) \mapsto& H_n(B_*) \\ (f: B_* \rightarrow C_*) \mapsto& (f': H_n(B_*) \rightarrow H_n(C_*)) \end{align*}

where

  1. \(H_n(B_*)\) is given as colimit
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{im}(\partial_1) \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{r}{\iota} \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{0} & \mathrm{ker}(\partial_n) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(\partial_1)/ \mathrm{im}(\partial_2) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
  1. the morphism is given as morphism induced by
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & A_{n} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& A_{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{} \\ B_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{} & B_{n} \arrow[]{r}{} & B_{n-1} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
