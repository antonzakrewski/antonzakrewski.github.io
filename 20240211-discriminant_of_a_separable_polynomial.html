discriminant of a separable polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f \in K[X]\) a separable polynomial. Then the discriminant is

\begin{align*} \delta_f \in K \setminus \{0\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(\in k\)

By Fixkörper der Galoisgruppe gleich der Galoiserweiterung it remains to show, that for an \(\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} \sigma(\delta_f) \overset{!}{=} \delta_f \end{align*}

Thus by galois group acts on roots we conclude, that \(\sigma\) permutates the roots \(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n\).

Hence

\begin{align*} \sigma(\delta_f) =& \sigma(\prod_{i < j} (\alpha_i - \alpha_j)^2) \\ =& \prod_{i < j} \sigma((\alpha_i - \alpha_j)^2) \\ =& \prod_{i < j} (\sigma(\alpha_i - \alpha_j))^2 \\ =& \prod_{i < j} (\sigma(\alpha_i) - \sigma(\alpha_j))^2 \end{align*}

here after renumbering and possible multiplication with \((-1)^2 = 1\) we get

\begin{align*} \prod_{i < j} (\sigma(\alpha_i) - \sigma(\alpha_j))^2 =& \prod_{i < j} (\alpha_i - \alpha_j)^2 \end{align*}

2.2. \(\neq 0\)

follows from field as integral domain and \(\alpha_i \neq \alpha_j\) for \(i \neq j\)
