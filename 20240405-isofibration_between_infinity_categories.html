isofibration between infinity categories

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be infinity categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) an inner fibration. Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is said to be a isofibration, if for every lifting

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \{0\} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & \mathcal{C} \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \Delta^1 \arrow[]{r}{f} \arrow[dashed]{ru}{f'} & \mathcal{D} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(f'\) is an equivalence of \(\mathcal{C}\)
