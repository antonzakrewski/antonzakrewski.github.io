finite simple group and subgroup of index p

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a finite simple group and \(H \subseteq G\) a subgroup of index prime \(p \in \mathbb{P}\). Then \(p^2 \nmid \vert G \vert\)

2. Proof

Consider the group action on the cosets. By group action as group homomorphism and simple group and group homomorphism we get a group-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: G \rightarrow \mathrm{Aut}_{\mathrm{Set}}(G/H) \simeq S_p \end{align*}

Then by construction, for \(g \not\in H\), which exists, as \(\vert G : H \vert = p > 1\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} g \cdot H = gH \neq H \end{align*}

and hence \(\varphi(g) \neq \mathrm{id} = 1\). Therefore, we conclude, that \(\varphi\) is injective, as \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) = 1\).

Thus \(G \subseteq S_p\) can be regarded as subgroup and by Satz von Lagrange and group order of a symmetric group

\begin{align*} \vert G \vert \mid \vert S_p \vert = p! \end{align*}

Since \(p\) is prime, \(p^2 \nmid p!\) and hence \(p^2 \nmid p!\)
