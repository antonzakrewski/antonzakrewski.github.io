ext 1 of two finitely generated abelian groups

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{Z}/k, \mathbb{Z}/l \mathbb{Z}\) be two cyclic groups Then Ext is given

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Ext}_{\mathbb{Z}}^1(A,B) \simeq \begin{cases} 0 & \mbox{if } k = 0 \\ \mathbb{Z}/\mathrm{gcd}(k,l) & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(k = 0\)

follows as \(\mathbb{Z}\) is free and hence projective (cf. free module as projective module)

  • covariant ext for a fixed projective module

2.2. else

follows from covariant ext 1 for an integral domain and quotient for a principal ideal as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Ext}_{\mathbb{Z}}^{1}(\mathbb{Z}/k \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}/l \mathbb{Z}) \simeq& (\mathbb{Z}/l \mathbb{Z})/(l \cdot \mathbb{Z}/k) \\ \simeq& \mathbb{Z}/(\mathrm{gcd}(k,l)) \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

where the latter isomorphism follows from quotient of a principal ideal by another principal ideal (?)
