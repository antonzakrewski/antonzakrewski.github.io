n-functor preserving objectwise limits and currying

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}_i\), \(\mathcal{D}\) be categories and

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: \prod \mathcal{C}_i \rightarrow \mathcal{D} \end{align*}

be a functor such that for each \((c_i)_{i \neq j} \in \mathrm{Ob}(\prod_{i \neq j}\mathcal{C}_i)\) the functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(-,(c_i)_{i \neq j}): \mathcal{C}_i \rightarrow \mathcal{D} \end{align*}

preserves limits of a shape \(J\).

Then so does the functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{curry}_{\mathcal{F}}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathrm{Fun}(\prod_{i \neq j} \mathcal{C}_{i}, \mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from limit of functors for a complete domain, as it is a sufficient condition that a diagram of functors is objectwise a limit.
