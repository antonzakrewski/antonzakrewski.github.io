primitive polynomial and product

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X_i, g = \sum_{i=0}^{m} \beta_i X^i\) be Polynomials.

TFAE:

  1. \(f,g\) are primitive
  2. the product \(f \cdot g\) is primitive

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

\begin{align*} (\alpha_n \beta_m, \alpha_n \cdot \beta_{m-1} + \alpha_{n-1} \beta_{m-1}, ..., \alpha_0 \beta_0) \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(f \cdot g\) is primitev, then there exists a sum

\begin{align*} 1 = \sum \lambda \alpha_i \beta_i \end{align*}

(cf. von einer Einheit erzeugtes Ideal und triviales Ideal

Therefore, we conclude, that there especially exists sums

\begin{align*} 1 =& \sum (\lambda \cdot \alpha_i) \beta_i \\ 1 =& \sum (\lambda \cdot \beta_i) \alpha_i \end{align*}
