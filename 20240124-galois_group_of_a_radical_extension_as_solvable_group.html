galois group of a radical extension as solvable group

1. Proposition

Let \(L/K\) be a radical and galois extension for a field \(K\) of characteristic \(0\). Then the galois group \(\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) is a solvable group

2. Proof

Suppose we have a tower of fields

\begin{align*} K = K_1 \subseteq ... \subseteq K_r = L \end{align*}

with \(K_{i+1} = K_i(\sqrt[n_i]{\alpha_i})\).

Let \(n = \prod n_i\) and \(\zeta\) a primitive n-root of unity. Then we define \(K_i = Ki(\zeta)\)
