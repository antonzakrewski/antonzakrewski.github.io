composition map for hom chain complexes

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(U,R,S,T\) be rings, \(C,D,E\) be chain complexes of bimodules, where

  1. \(C\) is a $(S,R) bimodule
  2. \(D\) is a \((T,R)\) bimodule
  3. \(E\) is a \((U,R)\) bimodule

Then for the hom chain complexes there exists a chain map of \(U,S\)-bimodules

\begin{align*} \kappa: \underline{\mathrm{Hom}_R}(D,E) \otimes_T \underline{\mathrm{Hom}_R}(C,D) \rightarrow& \underline{\mathrm{Hom}_R}(C,E) \\ (\varphi_k)_k \otimes (\psi_k)k \mapsto& (\varphi_{k+q} \oplus \varphi_k)_k \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined domain

Note that by hom chain complex of bimodules

  1. \(\underline{\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{R-Mod}}}(D,E)\) is a chain complex of \((T,R)\) modules
  2. \(\underline{\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{R-Mod}}}(C,D)\) is a chain complex of \((S,T)\) modules

2.2. proof

There is a chain map $ϕ = (ϕk)k

\begin{align*} (\kappa \circ d^{\otimes})((\psi_k)_k \otimes (\varphi_k)_k) =& \kappa(d^{\underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(D,E)(\psi) \otimes \varphi + (-1)^p \psi \otimes d^{\underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C,D)}(\varphi) \\ =& \kappa((d^E \circ \psi_k + (-1)^{p+1} \psi_{k-1} \circ d^D) \otimes (-1)^p \psi \otimes (d^D \circ \varphi_k + (-1)^{q + 1} \varphi_{k-1} \circ d^C)_k) \\ =& \kappa(d^E \circ \psi_k + (-1)^{p+1} \psi_{k-1} \circ d^D)_k \otimes \varphi + (-1)^p \psi \otimes (d^D \circ \varphi_k + (-1)^{q + 1} \varphi_{k-1} \circ d^C)_k) \\ =& (d^E \circ \psi_{k+q} + (-1)^{p+1} \psi_{k+q - 1} d^D) \varphi_k + (-1)^{p} \psi_{k + q - 1} (d^D \circ \varphi_k + (-1)^{q+1} \varphi_{k-1} d^C)_k =& (d^E \circ \psi_{k+q} \circ \varphi_k + (-1)^{p + q + 1} \psi_{k+q-1} \varphi_{k-1} d^C))_k \\ =& d^{\underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C,E)}(\kappa(\psi \otimes \varphi)) \end{align*}
