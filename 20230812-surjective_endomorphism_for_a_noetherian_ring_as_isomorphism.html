surjective endomorphism for a noetherian module as isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) a noetherian module over \(R\) Suppose \(\varphi: M \rightarrow M\) is an epimorphism. Then \(\varphi\) is also an isomorphism

2. Proof

Proof by contradiction Assume that \(\varphi\) is not a monomorphism, then by the characterization of a minimal kernel and monomorphism there exists an

\begin{align*} a \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \setminus \{0\} \end{align*}

Consider the chain

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \subseteq \mathrm{ker}(\varphi^2) \subseteq \mathrm{ker}(\varphi^3) \subseteq ... \end{align*}

since \(\varphi\) is surjective, there exists - by induction - for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) an \(r_n \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} \varphi^n(r_n) = a \end{align*}

Hence \(r_n \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi^{n+1}), r_n \not\in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi^n)\) Therefore the Chain does not stabilize, hence \(M\) is not noetherian.
