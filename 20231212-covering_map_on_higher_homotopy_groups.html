covering map on higher homotopy groups as isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be topological spaces and $p: E → \(B\) a covering map, wtih \(E \neq \emptyset\) Then for \(n \geq 2\), the map

\begin{align*} p_{*}: \pi_n(E,e) \rightarrow \p_n(B,p(e)) \end{align*}

is an group-isomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. surjective

Let α: (Sn,1) → (B,b)$ be a representative of an element of $πn(B,b)$d Then since

\begin{align*} \pi_1(S^n,1) = 0 \end{align*}

(cf. ) we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \alpha_*(\pi_1(S^n,1)) \subseteq p_*(\pi_1(E,e)) \end{align*}

since \(S^n\) is locally path connected nad globally pathconnected, we can lift \(\alpha\) to

\begin{align*} \overline{\alpha}: (S^n, 1) \rightarrow (E,e) \end{align*}

such that \(p \overline{\alpha} = \alpha\) hnece

\begin{align*} p_*[\overline{\alpha}] = [\alpha] \end{align*}

and therefore $p*$is surjective

2.2. injective

assume \(\alpha,\alpha': (S^n,1) \rightarrow (E,e)\) such that

\begin{align*} [p(\alpha)] = [p(\alpha')] \in \pi_1(B,b) \end{align*}

Then choose a path homotopy

\begin{align*} H: S^n \times [0,1] \rightarrow B \end{align*}

Since

\begin{align*} \pi_1(S^n \times [0,1]) = 1 \end{align*}

by <homotopy theorem>, there exists a homotopy

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}: S^n \times [0,1] \rightarrow E \end{align*}

with \(\tilde{H}(1,0) = e\)

Therefore

\begin{align*} \tilde{H}(1,-) \end{align*}

is a lift of the constant path at \(b\)

therefore it is contsant at \(e\) by uniqueness. thus \(\tilde{H}(1,1) = e\)

Furthermore, \(\tilde{H}(-,0) = \alpha\), hence by uniqueness it folosws, that \(\tilde{H}(-,1) = \alpha'\)
