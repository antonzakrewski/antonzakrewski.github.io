formal derivation in characteristic p as zero of an irreducible polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain with characteristic \(\mathrm{char}(R) = p\), \(f \in R[X]\) be an irreducible polynomial

TFAE:

  1. the formal derivative is the zero polynomial \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}f = 0\)
  2. Then there exists a polynomial \(g \in R[X]\), such that
\begin{align*} g(X^p) = f \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(g = \sum \alpha_i X^i\), then

\begin{align*} f =& g(X^p) \\ =& \sum \alpha_i X^{i \cdot p} \end{align*}

and hence for each monomial \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} \alpha_i X^{i \cdot p} = 0\).
