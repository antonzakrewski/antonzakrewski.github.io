functor between 2-pullbacks of categories preserves essentially surjectivity under fully faithfullness

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}_0, \mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2\) and \(\mathcal{D}_0, \mathcal{D}_1, \mathcal{D}_2\) be categories and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}_1} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_1} & \mathcal{C}_0 \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_0} & \mathcal{C}_2 \arrow[swap]{l}{\mathcal{F}_2} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_2}\\ \mathcal{D}_1 \arrow[swap]{r}{\mathcal{G}_1}& \mathcal{D}_0 & \arrow[]{l}{\mathcal{G}_2}\mathcal{D}_2 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a diagram, such that \(\mathcal{L}_1, \mathcal{L}_2\) are essentially surjective and \(\mathcal{L}_0\) is fully faithful

Then the canonical functor for the 2-categorical pullback of a functor diagram (cf. diagram of 2-categorical pullbacks of functors implies functor)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C}_1 \times_{\mathcal{C}_0} \mathcal{C}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{D}_1 \times_{\mathcal{D}_0} \mathcal{D}_2 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is also essentially surjective

2. Proof

Let \((X_1',X_2',\alpha)\) be objects. Then since \(X_i' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D}_i)\) and \(\mathcal{L}_i\) is essentially surjective, there exist \(X_1,X_2\) such that \(\mathcal{L}_i(X_i) \simeq X_i'\). Pick isomorphisms \(f_i: X_i' \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_i(X_i)\), then we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{G}_1(X_1) \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{G}_1(f_1)} \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & \mathcal{G}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}_2(f_2)}\\ \mathcal{G}_1(\mathcal{L}_1(X_1)) & \mathcal{G}_2(\mathcal{L}_2(X_2)) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then we define an isomorphism

\begin{align*} \beta \coloneqq \mathcal{G}_2(f_2) \circ \alpha \circ \mathcal{G}_1(f_1^{-1}): \mathcal{G}_1(\mathcal{L}_1(X_1)) \rightarrow \mathcal{G}_2(\mathcal{L}_2(X_2)) \end{align*}

(cf. composition of isomorphisms as isomorphism). Therefore

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{G}_1(X_1) \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{G}_1(f_1)} \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & \mathcal{G}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}_2(f_2)}\\ \mathcal{G}_1(\mathcal{L}_1(X_1)) \arrow[]{r}{\beta}& \mathcal{G}_2(\mathcal{L}_2(X_2)) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes by construction.

By commutativity of

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}_1} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_1} & \mathcal{C}_0 \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_0} & \mathcal{C}_2 \arrow[swap]{l}{\mathcal{F}_2} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_2}\\ \mathcal{D}_1 \arrow[swap]{r}{\mathcal{G}_1}& \mathcal{D}_0 & \arrow[]{l}{\mathcal{G}_2}\mathcal{D}_2 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

it holds, that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{G}_i \circ \mathcal{L}_i = \mathcal{L}_0 \circ \mathcal{F}_i \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \beta: \mathcal{L}_0(\mathcal{F}_1(X_1)) \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_0(\mathcal{F}_2(X_2)) \end{align*}

Therefore, since \(\beta\) is fully faithful and hence conservative (cf. fully faithful functor as conservative functor), there exists an isomorphism \(\gamma \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}_0}(\mathcal{F}_1(X_1), \mathcal{F}_2(X_2))\), such that \(\mathcal{L}_0(\gamma) = \beta\). Thus

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{G}_1(X_1) \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{G}_1(f_1)} \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & \mathcal{G}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}_2(f_2)}\\ \mathcal{G}_1(\mathcal{L}_1(X_1)) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_0(\gamma)}& \mathcal{G}_2(\mathcal{L}_2(X_2)) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes and hence we have a morphism

\begin{align*} (f_1,f_2): (X_1,X_2,\alpha) \rightarrow (\mathcal{L}_1(X_1), \mathcal{L}_2(X_2), \mathcal{L}_0(\gamma)) \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}(X_1,X_2,\gamma) = (\mathcal{L}_1(X_1), \mathcal{L}_2(X_2), \mathcal{L}_0(\gamma)) \end{align*}

Furthermore, we have \((f_1^{-1}, f_2^{-1})\) as inverses, as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{G}_1(X_1) \arrow[swap, bend right=85]{dd}{\mathrm{id}} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{G}_1(f_1)} \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & \mathcal{G}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}_2(f_2)} \arrow[bend left=85]{dd}{\mathrm{id}}\\ \mathcal{G}_1(\mathcal{L}_1(X_1)) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_0(\gamma)} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{G}_1(f_1^{-1})} & \mathcal{G}_2(\mathcal{L}_2(X_2)) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}_2(f_2^{-1})} \\ \mathcal{G}_1(X_1) \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & \mathcal{G}_2(X_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and analogously for the other side
