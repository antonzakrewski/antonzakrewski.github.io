ring with subgroups as ideal and integers

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, such that every subgroup is already an ideal. Then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} R \simeq \mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(1 \in R\). Consider the generated cyclic group by \(1\). By assumption, it is an ideal, furthermore, it contains the unit \(1\), hence by unit and trivial ideal it is the whole ring.

Therefore, each \(r \in R\) is \(1 \cdot n\) for some \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\).

Thus the algebrabic ring-epimorphism

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z} \twoheadrightarrow R \end{align*}

(cf. Initial Object in CRing)

Note, that since \(\mathbb{Z}\) is an principal ideal domain, each ideal is generated by some \(n \in \mathbb{N}_0\) (w.l.o.g. for \(\mathbb{N}_0\), cf. Assoziiertheit und Ideale) Thus by Homomorphiesatz für Ringe, we get a canonical ring-isomorphism

\begin{align*} R \simeq \mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}
