subset of coinciding stalks as open set

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{Op}(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}\) a set-valued sheaf. Suppose \(U,V \subseteq X\) are open and \(t \in \mathcal{F}(U), s \in \mathcal{F}(V)\) are sections.

Then the set

\begin{align*} W \coloneqq \{x \in U \cap V \vert s_x = t_x \in \mathcal{F}_x\} \end{align*}

is an open set.

2. Proof

using: openness and neighbourhood for all points

Let \(x \in W\), then by assumption, there exists an open neighbourhood \(U_x\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U_x}(t) =& \mathrm{res}_{U_x}(s) \end{align*}

Then for \(y \in U_x\), we get \(U_x\) as open neighbourhood, such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U_x}(t) =& \mathrm{res}_{U_x}(s) \end{align*}

Hence \(s_y = t_y\) for \(y \in U_x\) and thus \(U_x \subseteq W\)
