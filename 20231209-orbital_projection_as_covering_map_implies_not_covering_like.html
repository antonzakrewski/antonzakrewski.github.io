orbital projection as covering map implies in general not covering-like

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a topological group, \(X\) a topological space and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a topological group action Suppose the orbital projection

\begin{align*} p: X \rightarrow X/G \end{align*}

is a covering map.

Then \(\varphi\) is in general not covering-like (unless it is free, cf. orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like)

2. Proof

Consider the trivial group action, then the map

\begin{align*} p: X \rightarrow X/G \cong X \end{align*}

is the identity (cf. identity as covering map).

Nonetheless, for \(G \neq 0\) the zero group, the group action is not free and hence by contraposition not covering-like (cf. covering like group action as free group action)
