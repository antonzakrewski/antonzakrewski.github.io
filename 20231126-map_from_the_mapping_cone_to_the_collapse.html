map from the mapping cone to the collapse

1. Definition

Let \(X,A\) be topological spaces, \(\iota: A \rightarrow X\) a continuous map. Then the canonical map from the mapping cone is defined as map

\begin{align*} p: C(i) \rightarrow X/A \end{align*} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| A \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{} & C(A) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& * \arrow[]{d}{}\\ X \arrow[]{r}{} & C(i) \arrow[]{r}{p} & X/A \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(C(A)\) is the Cone on \(A\)

2. welldefined

Note that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| A \arrow[]{d}{}\arrow[]{r}{}& C(A) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ X \arrow[]{r}{}& C(i) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& * \arrow[]{d}{} \\ X \arrow[]{r}{}& X/A \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

are pushouts

Hence by glueing lemma for pushouts, we conclude, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| C(A) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{}& * \arrow[]{d}{} \\ C(i) \arrow[]{r}{}& X/A \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is a pushout
