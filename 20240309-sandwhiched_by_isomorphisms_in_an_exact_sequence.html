sandwhiched by epi- and monomorphism in an exact sequence

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{r}{h} & A' \arrow[]{r}{f} & X \arrow[]{r}{g} & B \arrow[rightarrowtail, ]{r}{h'} & B' \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

an exact sequence, where \(h\) is an epimorphism and \(h'\) a monomorphism

Then \(X = 0\) is the zero object

2. Proof

By kernel and monomorphism and image of an epimorphism we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(f) =& \mathrm{im}(h) = A' \\ \mathrm{im}(g) =& \mathrm{ker}(h') = 0 \end{align*}

hence by

  • isomorphic kernel and zero morphism

and

  • zero object as image and zero morphism

we get \(f = 0\) resp. \(g = 0\).

Furthermore, note that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(0) = \mathrm{im}(0) = 0 \end{align*}

and hence we are done by zero morphism as monomorphism and domain as zero object
