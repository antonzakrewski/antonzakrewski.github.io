n-th homology of a n-dimensional CW complex as free abelian

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be an \(n\)-dimensional CW-Complex Then the \(n\)-th singular homology of \(X\) is a free abelian group

2. Proof

Consider the exact sequence given by exact sequence of homology for a pushout of cells

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(\mathrm{Sk}_{n-1}) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & \bigoplus_{i \in I} \tilde{H}_{n-1}(S^{n-1}) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

here \(\mathrm{Sk}_{n-1}\) is a \(n-1\) dimensional cw complex, hence by n-th homology of an k-dimensional cw-complex for n > k this reduces to

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & \bigoplus_{i \in I} \tilde{H}_{n-1}(S^{n-1}) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

by singular homology relative to a contractible subspace (?) this gives

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & \bigoplus_{i \in I} \mathbb{Z} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

thus by exact sequence and monomorphism it follows, that \(H_n(X)\) is a subgroup of \(\bigoplus_{i \in I} \mathbb{Z}\).

Therefore by subgroup of a free group as free group, \(H_n(X)\) is free.
