category of elements of a functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a locally small category and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}} \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}\) be a contravariant functor. Then the category of elements is defined as comma category containing

  1. natural transformations \(\mu: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{\mathcal{C}}}(-,c) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}(-)\) for \(c \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\)
  2. natural transformations
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,c) \arrow[]{rr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{f^{ \leftarrow}: c \rightarrow c'}& & \arrow[]{ld}{} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,c') \\ & \mathcal{F}(-) & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Note

Note that by contravariant Yoneda a morphism

\begin{align*} \mu: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-,c) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}(-) \end{align*}

is given by an element \(x \in \mathcal{F}(c)\) and a morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(,c) \arrow[]{rr}{} \arrow[]{rd}{x}& & \arrow[]{ld}{x'} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(,c') \\ & \mathcal{F}(-) & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is given by a morphism \(f: c \rightarrow c'\) such that \(\mathcal{F}(f)(x') = x\)

Therefore an object can be seen as \((X,x)\) for \(x \in \mathcal{F}(x)\) and a morphism is \(\mu: (X,\mathcal{F}(f)(y)) = (X,x) \rightarrow (Y,y)\) since \(\mathcal{F}(f)(y) = x\)
