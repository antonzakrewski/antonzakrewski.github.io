field extension of a power of 2 degree and polynomial of degree 3

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) a field extension of degree \(2^k\) for some \(k \in \mathbb{N}_0\) and \(f \in K[X]\) a polynomial with \(\mathrm{deg}(f) = 3\) such that \(f\) is reducible in \(L\).

Then \(f\) is reducible in \(K\)

2. Proof

irreducible polynomial of degree less than 3 and root

Let \(\alpha \in L\) be a root \(f(\alpha) = 0\). Then for the minimal polynomial \(g\) there exist 3 Possibilities

  1. \(\mathrm{deg}(g) = 1\), then \(X + \alpha\), hence \(\alpha \in K[X]\)
  2. \(\mathrm{deg}(g) = 2\), then by Polynomring über einem Körper als euklidischer Ring, there exists a polynomial \(h\) with \(h \cdot g = f\).

by Grad von Polynomen unter Multiplikation in einem Integritätsbereich, it follows, that \(\mathrm{deg}(h) = 1\), hence \(h\) has a root in $K[X]

  1. \(\mathrm{deg}(g) = 3\), then contradiction to the Gradlemma, as \(3 \not\mid 2^n\)
