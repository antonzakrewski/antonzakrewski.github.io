abelianized space of space in general not unique up to homotopy equivalence

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a topological space and \(X_A\) an abelianized space. Then \(X_A\) is not unique up to Homotopy equivalence.

2. Proof

since the path component functor as homotopy invariant functor, we conclude, that the amount of path component is invariant under homotopy equivalence. Nonetheless, for \(X_A\), assume there exist \(\kappa\) many path components (cardinal).

Then consider

\begin{align*} \coprod_{\kappa + 1} * \sqcup X_A \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} A \rightarrow X_A \hookrightarrow \coprod_{\kappa + 1} * \sqcup X_A \end{align*}

Here the set of path components of \(\coprod_{\kappa + 1}* \sqcup X_A\) has a greater cardinality (hence not isomorphic in category set) and thus both spaces are not homotopy equivalent.

Furthermore, the fundamental group only sees the path component of the base point, hence

\begin{align*} \iota_*: \pi_1(X_A) \rightarrow \pi_1(\coprod_{\kappa + 1} * \sqcup X_A) \end{align*}

is the identity
