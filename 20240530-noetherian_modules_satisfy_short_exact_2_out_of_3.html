noetherian modules satisfy short exact 2 out of 3

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod Then noetherian modules satisfy short exact 2 out of 3

2. Proof

corollary of:

2.1. a)

  • quotient of a noetherian module as noetherian module
  • submodule of a noetherian module as noetherian module

2.2. b)

Let

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & B/A \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be short exact sequence such that \(A,B/A\) are noetherian.

Let $C0 \subseteq C1 …$ be a chain of modules in \(B\) Then consider

\begin{align*} D_i \coloneqq& \iota^{-1}[C_i] \\ E_i \coloneqq& \pi[C_i] \\ \end{align*}

as chains of morphism.

Then \(D_i,E_i\) stabilize for sufficiently large \(n\)

Suppose the chain \(C_i\) does not stabilize. Then for each \(i \in I\) there exists an \(x \in C_i\) such that \(x \not\in C_{i-1}\).

Let \(k\) be sufficiently large, i.e. \(D_i,E_i\) stabilize and \(x \in C_{k} \setminus C_{k-1}\) Then it follows that \(x \not\in A\) as otherwise \(x \in D_{k} = D_{k-1} \subseteq C_{k-1}\).

As \(E_{k} = E_{k-1}\) it follows that there exists an element \(c_{k-1} \in C_{k-1}\) such that \([c_{k-1}] = [x]\) Hence

\begin{align*} c_{k-1} - x \in A \end{align*}

As \(C_{k}\) is closed under addition, it follows that

\begin{align*} c_{k-1} - x \in C_{k} \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} c_{k-1} - x \in D_i = D_{i-1} \end{align*}

But then

\begin{align*} c_{k-1} - x \in C_{k-1} \end{align*}

showing that \(c_{k-1} - (c_{k-1} - x) \in C_{k-1}\), contradiction
