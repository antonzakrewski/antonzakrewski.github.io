group homomorphism of quotient groups induces group epimorphism on quotients of images

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups, \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup and \(\varphi: G \rightarrow H\) a group-homomorphism. Then there exists a canonical algebraic group-epimorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi': G/N \rightarrow& \varphi[G] / \varphi[N] \\ gN \mapsto& \varphi(g) \cdot \varphi[N] \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

2.1.1. welldefined domain

follows from intersection of a normal subgroup with a subgroup as normal subgroup of the subgroup

2.1.2. independent of representatives

Suppose \(gN = g'N\), then there exists an \(n \in N\) such that \(g = g' \cdot n\). Hence we get

\begin{align*} \varphi(g) \cdot \varphi[N] =& \varphi'(gN) \\ =& \varphi(g' \cdot n \cdot N) \\ =& \varphi(g' \cdot n) \varphi[N] \\ =& \varphi(g') \cdot \varphi(n) \cdot \varphi[N] \\ =& \varphi(g') \cdot \varphi[N] \end{align*}

2.1.3. homomorphism

follows from

\begin{align*} \varphi'(gN \cdot g'N) =& \varphi'(gg'N) \\ =& \varphi(gg') \cdot \varphi[N] \\ =& \varphi(g)\varphi[N] \cdot \varphi(g') \varphi[N] =& \varphi'(gN) \cdot \varphi'(g'N) \end{align*}

2.2. surjective

follows from construction, as for \(h \varphi[N] \in \varphi[G]/ \varphi[N]\), it follows, that \(h \in \varphi[G]\) hence there exists an \(g \in G\) such that \(\varphi(g) = h\)
