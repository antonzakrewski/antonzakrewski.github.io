left adjoint of the constant diagram functor and colimit

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{J}\) be categories, \(\mathrm{Fun}_{\mathrm{colim}}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{C}) \subseteq \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{C})\) the full subcategory of the functor category containing all diagrams, where the colimit exists.

Then the constant diagram functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{const}_J: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow fun \end{align*}

is precisely a right adjoint to the colim functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim} \vdash \mathrm{const}_{J} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.2. colimit \(\Rightarrow\) right adjoint

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(\mathrm{colim}(J), B) \simeq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{FunColim}}(J, \mathrm{const}_(J){B}) \end{align*}
