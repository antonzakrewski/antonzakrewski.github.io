sphere into disk as cofibration

1. Proposition

The canonical map

\begin{align*} \iota: S^{n-1} \rightarrow D^n \end{align*}

is a cofibration

2. Proof

by cofibration and retraction it suffices to show, that there exists a retraction

\begin{align*} D^{n} \times [0,1] \rightarrow S^{n-1} \times [0,1] \cup D^n \times \{0\} \end{align*}

we parametrize \(D^n\) with spherical coordinates \((\alpha, \beta, ..., r)\)

Then we can construct a continuous map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ret}: D^n \times [0,1] \rightarrow& S^{n-1} \times [0,1] \cup D^n \times \{0\} \\ ((\alpha,\beta,...,r),t) \mapsto& (\alpha, \beta,..., R(r,t), T(r,t)) \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} R(r,t) \coloneqq& \mathrm{min}(1, r + rt) \\ T(r,t) \coloneqq& \mathrm{min}(1, \mathrm{max}(0, r + rt - 1)) \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

w.l.o.g. Suppose \(r + rt < 1\), then we get \(\mathrm{max}(0, r + rt -1) = 0\) and hence \(T(r,t) = 0\).

2.2. continuous

it is continuous, as composition of continuous maps (multiplication, addition , Minimumsfunktion als stetige Abbildung / Maximumsfunktion als stetige Abbildung)

2.3. retraction

for \(r = 1\) we get

\begin{align*} R(1,t) =& \mathrm{min}(1, 1 + 1 \cdot t) \\ =& 1 T(1,t) =& \mathrm{min}(1, \mathrm{max}(0, 1 + 1 \cdot t - 1)) \\ =& \mathrm{min}(1, t) \\ =& t \end{align*}

For \(t = 0\) we get

\begin{align*} R(r,0) =& \mathrm{min}(1, r + r \cdot 0) \\ =& \mathrm{min}(1, r + r \cdot 0) \\ =& r \\ T(0,t) =& \mathrm{min}(1, \mathrm{max}(0, r + r \cdot 0 - 1)) \\ =& \mathrm{min}(1,0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
