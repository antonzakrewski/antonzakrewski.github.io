Schanuels lemma

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}_1 \arrow[]{r}{c_1} & P_1 \arrow[]{r}{p_1} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}_2 \arrow[]{r}{c_2} & P_2 \arrow[]{r}{p_2} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be short exact sequences where \(P_1,P_2\) are projective objects

Then there exists an isomorphism of short exact sequences

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}_1 \oplus P_2 \arrow[]{r}{(c_1,\mathrm{id})} \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}& P_1 \oplus P_2 \arrow[]{r}{(p_1,0)} \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}& M \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{=} & 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & P_1 \oplus \mathrm{ker}_2 \arrow[]{r}{(\mathrm{id}, c_2) } & P_1 \oplus P_2 \arrow[]{r}{(0,p_2)} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

using surjection from the kernel sum of a projective object and section to the projective object we get a sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 & \mathrm{ker}(p_1 + p_2) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & P_1 \oplus P_2 \arrow[]{r}{p_1 + p_2}& M \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
