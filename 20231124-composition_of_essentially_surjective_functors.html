composition of essentially surjective functors

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{G}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{E}\) be essentially surjective functors.

Then the composition \(\mathcal{G} \circ \mathcal{F}\) is also essentially surjective

2. Proof

Let \(e \in \mathcal{E}\), then there exists an object \(d \in \mathcal{D}\) such that \(\mathcal{G}(e) \cong d\) Analogously, there exists an \(c \in \mathcal{C}\) such that \(\mathcal{F}(c) \cong d\) Hence

\begin{align*} \mathcal{FG}(c) \cong e \end{align*}
