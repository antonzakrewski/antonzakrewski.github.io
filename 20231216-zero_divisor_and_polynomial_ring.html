zero divisor and polynomial ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(A[X]\) the polynomial ring and \(f \in A[X]\) a polynomial. 1 = ∑ (λ ⋅ αi) βi

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is a zero divisor
  2. there exists an \(\alpha \in A \setminus \{0\}\) such that \(\alpha \cdot f = 0\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Choose a \(g \in A[X]\) with smallest degree. Note, that by assumption, there exists one and furthermore, \(\mathbb{N}\) is well-ordered, hence there exists a minimal \(n\).

Let

\begin{align*} g = \sum_{i=0}^{m} \beta_i X^i \end{align*}

then it follows, that the \(m + n\)-th component is zero, and thus

\begin{align*} \beta_m \cdot \alpha_n = 0 \end{align*}

Therefore, it follows, that \(\alpha_n \cdot g\) has degree \(\leq m-1\) and furthermore

\begin{align*} (\alpha_n \cdot g) \cdot f = 0 \end{align*}

By minimality, it follows, that \(\alpha_n \cdot g = 0\), \(\mathrm{deg}(g) = 0\). (Otherwise reduce it)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
