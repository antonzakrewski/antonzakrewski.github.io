n-th homology determined by n+1 Skeleton

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex and \(l \geq n+1\) for \(n,l \in \mathbb{N}\) Then it holds for the homology functor

\begin{align*} H_{n}(\mathrm{Sk}_l) \simeq H_n(X) \end{align*}

2. Proof

consider the isomorphism of homology for a pushout of cells

\begin{align*} H_{n}(\mathrm{Sk}_l) \simeq H_n(\mathrm{Sk}_{l+1}) \end{align*}

Then by homology of a cw complex as colimit of skeletons it follows, that

\begin{align*} H_n(\mathrm{Sk}_l) \end{align*}

suffices together with the inverses of

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(\mathrm{Sk}_l) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(\mathrm{Sk}_{l+1}) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & ... \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(\mathrm{Sk}_{l+2}) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
