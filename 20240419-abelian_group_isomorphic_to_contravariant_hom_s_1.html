finite abelian group isomorphic to contravariant hom S1

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a finite abelian group. Then

\begin{align*} A \simeq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(A, S^1) \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} S^1 \simeq \mathbb{R}/ \mathbb{Z} \simeq \{z \in \mathbb{C} \vert \vert z \vert = 1\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

using the classification of finitely generated abelian groups we get

\begin{align*} A \simeq \bigoplus \mathbb{Z}/(n_i \mathbb{Z}) \end{align*}

as the contravariant hom-functor dualizes small colimits to limits it suffices to show that

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z} \simeq \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z}, S^1) \end{align*}

A group homomorphism \(\mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z}\) is uniquely determined by the generator \(1\). Furhtermore the image \(\varphi(1)\) is also Torsionselement hence a root of unity. Thus \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z})\) restricts to \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z})\) and there exist \(n\) different morphisms.

By abelian group homomorphisms as abelian group this is an abelian group. Furthermore \(\mathrm{id}\) generates this group, as

\begin{align*} n \cdot \mathrm{id}(1) = n \end{align*}

and each morphism \(f\) is determined by \(f(1)\)

Therefore

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z} \simeq& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z}, S^1) \\ n \mapsto& \{1 \mapsto& n\} \end{align*}
