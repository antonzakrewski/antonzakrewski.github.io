homology of a pair of spaces and factorization through suspension

1. Proposition

Let \((X,A)\) be a pair of spaces and \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\). Then there exists a factorization

\begin{align*} H_n(X,A) & H_n(\Sigma(A), *) \\ & H_{n-1}(A,*) \end{align*}

where \(\Sigma\) is the suspension and \(H_n(-,-)\) the relative singular homology functor

2. Proof

Consider the following commutative diagram of pairs of spaces

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} (C(\iota), C^+(A)) \arrow[]{r}{} & (\Sigma(A), C^+(A)) \\ (\mathrm{Cyl}(\iota), A) \arrow[]{u}{\iota_1} \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{u}{\iota_2} (C^{-}(A), A) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

for the inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota_1: \mathrm{Cyl}(\iota) \hookrightarrow \mathrm{Cyl}(\iota) \coprod_{A} C^+(A) \simeq C(\iota) \\ \iota_2: C^{-}(A) \hookrightarrow C^{-}(A) \coprod_{A} C^+(A) \Sigma(A) \end{align*}

and the collapse

\begin{align*} \pi: \mathrm{Cyl}(\iota) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Cyl}(\iota)/X \simeq C^{-}(A) \\ \pi: C(\iota) \rightarrow& C(\iota)/X \simeq \Sigma(A) \end{align*}

Applying the relative singular homology functor results in

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(C(\iota), C^+(A)) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(\Sigma(A), C^+(A)) \\ H_n(\mathrm{Cyl}(\iota),A) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{u}{\simeq} & H_n(C^-(A), A) \arrow[]{u}{\simeq}\\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

these are isomorphisms by excision theorem, as ???

By mapping cylinder of a subspace inclusion homotopy equivalent this results in the isomorphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(X,A) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(\mathrm{Cyl}(\iota), A) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Therefore, we get the commutative square

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & H_n(C(\iota), C^+(A)) \arrow[]{r}{}& H_n(\Sigma(A), C^+(A)) \\ H_n(X,A) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} \arrow[]{d}{\partial} & H_n(\mathrm{Cyl}(\iota), A) \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{u}{\simeq} \arrow[]{d}{\partial} & H_n(C^-(A), A) \arrow[]{u}{\simeq} \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ H_{n-1}(A,*) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}} & H_{n-1}(A,*) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}} & H_{n-1}(A,*) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here the lower map is the long exact sequence of a pair of spaces (?)

then, as \(C^+(A)\) is contractible, compatible with \(C(\iota), \Sigma(A)\), it follows that since the suspension isomorphism of singular homology is given by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(\Sigma(A) , *) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[bend left=70]{ddd}{\simeq} \\ H_n(\Sigma(A), C^+(A)) \arrow[]{d}{\simeqd} \\ H_n(C^{-}(A), A) \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ H_{n-1}(A,*) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

This gives then a factorization

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & & H_{n(\Sigma(A), *) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \\ & H_{n}(C(\iota),C^+(A)) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(\Sigma(A), C^+(A)) \arrow[]{d}{} \\ H_n(X,A) \arrow[swap]{rrd}{\partial} \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_{n}(\mathrm{Cyl}(\iota), A) & H_n(C^n(A), A) \arrow[]{d}{\partial}\\ & & H_{n-1}(A,*) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
