slice category associated to a functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(x \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D})\) and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor. Then the slice category associated to \(\mathcal{F}\) is defined as pullback

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\lrcorner}]| \mathcal{F}\X \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{D}/X \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{Forget}}\\ \mathcal{C} \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}} & \mathcal{D} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(\mathcal{D}/X\) is the slice category and \(\mathrm{Forget}\) is the forgetful functor from the slice category

2. Explicit

The category \(\mathcal{F}/X\) contains

  1. pairs \((c, \alpha: \mathcal{F}(c) \rightarrow X)\) as objects
  2. and \(\beta: c \rightarrow c'\) as morphisms, where
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(c) \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{\mathcal{F}(\beta)}& & \mathcal{F}(c') \arrow[]{ld}{}\\ & X & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes
