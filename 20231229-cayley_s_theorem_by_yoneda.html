Cayley's Theorem by Yoneda

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group. Then there exists a algebraic group-monomorphism

\begin{align*} G \rightarrow \mathrm{Bij}(G,G) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(BG\) be the categorification of a group. Then \(BG\) is locally small, as \(\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) = G\).

By natural transformations of a hom-set as postcomposition, there exists a natural bijection

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(A,B) \cong \mathrm{NatTrans}(\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(-,A) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(-,B))) g \mapsto& g^{ \leftarrow} \end{align*}

Note that \(\mathrm{Ob}(BG) = \{*\}\), hence a natural transformation is given by \(\eta(*)\) satisfying naturality.

Thus for \(A,B = *\) and \(- = *\) as only possible choice, we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \cong \mathrm{NatTrans}(\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*))) \end{align*}

or an injection

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \rightarrowtail \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}((\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*))) \end{align*}

By postcomposition with an isomorphism as isomorphism note that each natural transformation is an natural isomorphism, hence an isomorphism on all objects. Thus \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}((\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(-,A) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(-,B)))\) is an automorphism is category set, hence a bijection.

Therefore we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \rightarrowtail& \mathrm{Bij}((\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*))) \\ g \mapsto& g^{ \leftarrow} \end{align*}

Note that this gives the contravariant hom-functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}: BG \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set} \\ * \mapsto& \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(-,*) \\ (g: * \rightarrow *) \mapsto& (g^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*)) \end{align*}

and hence by functor from the categorified group induces homomorphism on Automorphism subgroup, we get a group-homomorphism

\begin{align*} G \rightarrow \mathrm{Aut}(\mathrm{Hom}_{BG}(*,*)) \end{align*}
