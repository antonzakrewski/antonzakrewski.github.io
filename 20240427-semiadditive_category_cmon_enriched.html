semiadditive category CMon enriched

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{M}\) be semiadditive category. Then \(\mathcal{M}\) is CMon enriched

2. Proof

2.1. commutative monoid

Let

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[shift right=1, swap]{r}{f} \arrow[shift left=1]{r}{g} & B \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be two parallel morphism

Then we define the sum

\begin{align*} f + g: A \rightarrow B \end{align*}

as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{\Delta_A} & A \oplus A \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus g} & B \oplus B \arrow[]{r}{\nabla} & B \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where \(\Delta_A\) is the diagonal morphism and \(\nabla_B\) the codiagonal morphism

2.2. netural element

take the zero morphism: This by assumption factors through the zero object.

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{rd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{0}& & B \\ & 0 \arrow[]{ru}{}& \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Therefore we obtain by application of universal properties a diagram of the form

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{rdd}{0} \arrow[]{r}{\Delta_A} & A \oplus A \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus 0} & B \oplus B \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{\nabla} & B \\ & A \oplus 0 \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus 0}& B \oplus 0 \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}& \\ & A \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{ruu}{0}& \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where the latter isomorphisms are given by coproduct with the initial object canonically iso / product with the terminal object canonically iso

2.3. associativity

follows from

  • associativity of the categorical product / associativity of the categorical coproduct
  • diagonal morphism commutes with associativity / codiagonal morphism commuets with associativity

2.4. commutativity

follows from

  • diagonal morphism commutes with permutation / codiagonal morphism commutes with permutations
  • commutativity of a coproduct / commutativity of a coproduct
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & A \oplus A \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus g} & B \oplus B \arrow[]{rd}{\nabla} \arrow[swap]{d}{(1,2)} & \\ A \arrow[bend left=70]{rrr}{f + g} \arrow[bend right]{rrr}{g + f} \arrow[]{r}{\Delta} \arrow[]{ru}{\Delta} & A \oplus A \arrow[]{r}{g \oplus f} \arrow[swap]{u}{(1,2)} & B \oplus B \arrow[]{r}{\nabla} & B \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

which commutes

2.5. bilinear

It suffices to show that

\begin{align*} h \circ (f + g) = h \circ f + h \circ g \end{align*}

since the \((f + g) \circ h\) follows from passing to the opposite category

Consider

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{\Delta} & A \oplus A \arrow[swap]{rd}{hf \oplus hg} \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus g} & B \oplus B \arrow[]{d}{h \oplus h} \arrow[]{r}{\nabla} & B \arrow[]{r}{h} & C \\ & & C \oplus C \arrow[]{rr}{\nabla}& & C \arrow[]{u}{\overset{!}{=} } \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here the left triangle commutes by uniqueness of the universal property of the coproducts, which is obtained by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A_1 \arrow[]{d}{\iota_1} \arrow[]{r}{f} & B_1 \arrow[swap]{d}{\iota_1} \arrow[bend left=50]{ddd}{h}\\ A \coprod A \arrow[swap, dashed]{rd}{hf \oplus hg} \arrow[]{r}{f \oplus g, dashed} & B \coprod B \arrow[dashed]{d}{h \oplus h} \\ & C \oplus C \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathrm{pr}_1}\\ & C_1 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and the right triangle commutes by the uniqueness of the universal property of the coproduct, which is obtained by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \arrow[bend right]{ddd}{h} B_1 \arrow[]{d}{\iota_1} \arrow[]{rd}{}& & \\ B \coprod B \arrow[dashed]{d}{h \oplus h}\arrow[]{r}{\nabla}& B \arrow[]{r}{h} & C \arrow[]{d}{=}\\ C \oplus C \arrow[]{rr}{\nabla} & & C \\ C_1 \arrow[]{u}{\iota_1} \arrow[]{rru}{\mathrm{id}} & & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here \(B_1 = B ,C_1 = C\) denote the first component of \(B \oplus B, C \oplus C\)
