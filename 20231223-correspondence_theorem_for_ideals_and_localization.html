correspondence theorem for prime ideals and localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed subset. Then there exists an order preserving Bijection

\begin{align*} \{\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A \vert \mathfrak{p} \cap S = \emptyset, \mathrm{prime}\} \cong \mathrm{Spec}(S^{-1}A) \end{align*}

given by the contraction and extension

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

2.1.1. contraction

prime ideal follows from contraction of a prime ideal

Suppose the intersection is nonempty, then there exists an \(s \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} \frac{s}{1} \in \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

but since \(\frac{s}{1}\) is a unit (cf. units in a localization) \(\mathfrak{p}\) is not strict (cf. unit and trivial ideal)

2.1.2. extension

2.1.2.1. strict

Proof by contraposition Suppose \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A\) is an ideal, such that the extension is \(S^{-1}A\). Then there exists a unit \(\frac{\alpha}{1} \in \iota[\mathfrak{p}]\). Then by units in a localization, \((\alpha) \cap S\) with \(\alpha \in \mathfrak{p}\), thus \(\alpha \in S \cap \mathfrak{p} \neq \emptyset\)

2.1.2.2. prime

Suppose

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{s_1} \cdot \frac{\beta}{s_2} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

Then it is equivalent to show, that for i\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} ⋅ \frac{\beta}{1} ∈ S-1\mathfrak{p} \end{align*} w.l.o.g.

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

because multiplication with \(s_1\) resp. \(\frac{1}{s_1}\) results in the general case.

by elements in the extension of an ideal, it follows, that each element

\begin{align*} \frac{\gamma}{s'} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

is given by

\begin{align*} \gamma \cdot a = \gamma' \end{align*}

for some \(s \in S, \alpha' \in \mathfrak{p}\)

since \(S \cap \mathfrak{p} = \emptyset\) and \(\mathfrak{p}\) is prime, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \gamma \in \mathfrak{p} \Leftrightarrow \frac{\gamma}{s} \in S^{-1} \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

Hence \(\alpha \cdot \beta \in \mathfrak{p}\) and by primeness, wlog \(\alpha \in \mathfrak{p}\), hence

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

2.3.1. a) first contraction, then extension
