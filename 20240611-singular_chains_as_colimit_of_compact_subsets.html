singular chains as colimit of compact subsets

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(\mathcal{K}_X\) the directed poset of compact subspaces, \(M\) an abelian group

Then there exists an isomorphism of chain complexes

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{C \in \mathcal{K}_X} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C;M) \cong C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \end{align*}

2. Proof

using natural isomorphism and isomorphism on the objects it suffices to show that the indeced morphism (cf.

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{C \in \mathcal{K}_X} H_*(C;M) \rightarrow H_*(X) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

2.1. surjective

Let

\begin{align*} a = \sum_{i=1}^r \sigma_i \otimes m_i \in \end{align*}

for \(\sigma_i: \Delta^k_{\mathrm{Top}} \rightarrow X\)

since \(\Delta^n\) is compact, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{im}(\sigma_n) \end{align*}

is compact (cf. image of compact space as compact space)

Furthermore the finite union of compact sets is compact and the image commutes with the union Thus for

\begin{align*} C \coloneqq \bigcup \mathrm{im}(\sigma_n) \in \mathcal{K}_X \end{align*}

it follows that

\begin{align*} a \in C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C) \end{align*}

2.2. injective

Suppose given an element \(\alpha \in \mathrm{ker}(\mathrm{colim}_{C \in \mathcal{K}_X} c^{\mathrm{sing}}(C) \rightarrow C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X))\). Then it is represented by a finite sum of

\begin{align*} \sum \alpha_i \end{align*}

for \(\alpha_i \in C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C_i)\). Using the upward directedness we may consider

\begin{align*} \sum \alpha_i \in C^{\mathrm{sing}}(\bigcup C_i) \end{align*}

Therefore it is represented by a single element \(\alpha \in C^{\mathrm{sing}}(\bigcup C_i)\) As \(C_i \hookrightarrow X\) is an injection it follows that so is

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(\bigcup C_i) \rightarrow C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \end{align*}

Thus it follows that \(\alpha = 0\)
