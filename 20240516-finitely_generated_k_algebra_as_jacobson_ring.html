finitely generated k-algebra as jacobson ring

1. Proposition

Let \(k\) be a field, \(A\) an finite type k-Algebra. Then \(A\) is a Jacobson ring

2. Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{p}\) be prime. Then using radical of an ideal and intersection of rabinowitsch spectrum we get

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{rab}}(A)} \mathfrak{m} \\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A[X])} A \cap \mathfrak{m} \\ =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(A[X])} \iota^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}] \end{align*}

for the inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota: A \rightarrow A[X] \end{align*}

since the preimage of a maximal ideal is maximal for finite type k-algebras we conclude that

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(A[X])} \iota^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}] \supseteq \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(A)} \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

and thus equality, as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{rab}}(A) \supseteq \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(A) \end{align*}
