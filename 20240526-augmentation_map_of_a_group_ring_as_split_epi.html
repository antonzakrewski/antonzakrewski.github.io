augmentation map of a group ring as split epi

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(G\) a group and \(R[G]\) the group ring Then the augmentation map of a group ring as ring-homomorphism is split epi with right-inverse

\begin{align*} \iota: R \rightarrow R[G] r \mapsto& r \cdot e \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. inverse

follows as

\begin{align*} \varphi(\iota(r)) =& \varphi(r \cdot e) \\ =& r \end{align*}

2.2. unit

\begin{align*} \iota(1) = 1e \end{align*}

2.3. additive

follows as

\begin{align*} \iota(r + r') =& (r + r')e \\ =& re + r'e \end{align*}

2.4. multiplicative

\begin{align*} \iota(r \cdot r') =& (r \cdot r')e \\ =& (r \cdot e ) (r' \cdot e) \\ =& \iota(r) \cdot \iota(r') \end{align*}
