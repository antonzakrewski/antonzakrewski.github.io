singular chain complexes for a covering like group action and module over the group ring

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(G\) a group acting covering-like on \(X\). Then for the singular chain complexes there exists a natural isomorphism

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z} G} \mathbb{Z} \cong C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X/G) \end{align*}

where \(\mathbb{Z}G\) is the group ring and \(X/G\) is the topological orbital collapse

2. Proof

Consider the map defined on each component by

\begin{align*} (n \cdot \Delta^{k} \otimes l) \mapsto& n \cdot l \pi \circ \Delta^k \end{align*}

it is surjective by orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like and lifting theorem for a covering

Furthermore, it is injective, as

\begin{align*} (n \cdot l) \cdot \pi \circ \Delta^k \mapsto& \Delta^k \otimes (n \cdot l) \end{align*}

is welldefined, since for different \(\Delta^k, \Delta^k'\) with \(\pi \circ \Delta^k = \pi \circ \Delta^k'\) it follows, that there exists an \(g \in G\) such that

\begin{align*} g \cdot \Delta^k = \Delta^k' \end{align*}
