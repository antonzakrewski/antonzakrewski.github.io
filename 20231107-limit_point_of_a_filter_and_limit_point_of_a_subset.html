convergent filter and limit point of a subset

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(A \subseteq X\) a subset.

TFAE:

  1. there exists a convergent filter \(\mathcal{F} \rightarrow x\) with \(A \in \mathcal{F}\)
  2. \(x \in \overline{A}\) is a limit point

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By definition, there exists a filter \(\mathcal{F}\) such that for the system of neighbourhoods, \(\mathcal{U}_x \subseteq \mathcal{F}\) and furthermore \(A \in \mathcal{F}\). Suppose \(U\) is a neighbourhood of \(x\), then by assumption \(U \in \mathcal{F}\) and since \(\mathcal{F}\) is welldefined, it follows that

\begin{align*} U \cap A \neq \emptyset \end{align*}

hence by definition of a closure, \(x \in \overline{A}\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(\mathcal{F} = \langle \mathcal{U}_x, A \rangle\) be the generated setfilter: Suppose \(O_1,...,O_n \in \mathcal{U}_x\), then w.l.o.g. \(O \coloneqq \bigcap_{i =1}^n O_i\) is a neighbourhood and hence

\begin{align*} O \cap A \neq \emptyset \end{align*}
