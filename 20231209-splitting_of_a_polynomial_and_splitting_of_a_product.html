splitting of a polynomial and splitting of a product

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(f \in R[X]\) a polynomial with \(f = g_1 \cdot g_2\).

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) splits
  2. \(g_1,g_2\) spilt

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. \(R\) is a field (cf. quotient field)

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows inductively from factoring roots of a polynomial, as a root of \(f\) is a root of \(g_1\) or \(g_2\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from

\begin{align*} f =& g_1 \cdot g_2 \\ =& \lambda_1 \cdot \prod (X - \alpha) \cdot \lambda_2 \cdot \prod (X - \beta) \\ =& (\lambda_1 \cdot \lambda_2) \cdot \prod (X - \alpha) (X - \beta) \end{align*}
