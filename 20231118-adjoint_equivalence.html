adjoint equivalence

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{G}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be functors.

Then \(\mathcal{F}, \mathcal{G}\) are an adjoint equivalence, if \(\mathcal{F} \dashv \mathcal{G}\) are adjoints and both the unit and counit are natural isomorphisms.

\begin{align*} \eta: \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{C}} \rightarrow& \mathcal{G} \circ \mathcal{F} \\ \epsilon: \mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{G} \rightarrow& \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{D}} \end{align*}

where \(\mathcal{F}\) is the left adjoint and \(\mathcal{G}\) the right adjoint

i.e. the (co-)units especially satisfy the triangle identities.
