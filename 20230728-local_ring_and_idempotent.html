local ring and idempotent

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a local ring and \(x \in A\) with

\begin{align*} x^2 = x \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} x \in \{0,1\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose \(x \not\in \{0,1\}\), then by idempotent and trivial unit, we conclude, that \(e,1-e \in \mathfrak{m}\) in the maximal ideal. (cf. 1 minus idempotent element, local ring and non-units as maximal ideal) thus contradiction, as \(1 - e + e = 1 \in \mathfrak{m}\)
