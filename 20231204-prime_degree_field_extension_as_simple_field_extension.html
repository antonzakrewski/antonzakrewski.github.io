prime degree field extension as simple field extension

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(L/K\) a field extension of degree \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) for a prime number \(p \in \mathbb{P}\).

Then \(L/K\) is a simple field extension

2. Proof

follows from Gradlemma, as there exist linearly independent \(a_1,...,a_m\) such that

\begin{align*} L = K(a_1,...,a_m) \end{align*}

Then we conclude that

\begin{align*} [L/K] =& [K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_m): K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{m-1})] \cdot ... [K(\alpha_1) : K] \\ =& p \end{align*}

since \(p\) is prime, it follows, that there exists an \(\alpha_n\) such that

\begin{align*} [K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n) : K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1}] = p \end{align*}

and the rest is

\begin{align*} [K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{j} : K(\alpha_{1},...,\alpha_{j-1})] = 1 \end{align*}

or equivalently

\begin{align*} K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_j) = K(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{j-1}) \end{align*}
