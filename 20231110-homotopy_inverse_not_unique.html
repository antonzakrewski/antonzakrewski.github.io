homotopy inverse in general not unique

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a Homotopy equivalence. Then the homotopy inverse is in general not unique.

2. Proof

Let \(X = \mathbb{R}\) and \(Y = *\) with the usual topology. E.g. choose morphisms \(f: * \mapsto 1, g: * \mapsto 0\) and a path \(\gamma: 0 \leadsto 1\). Note that \(t: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow *\) is uniquely determined, since \(*\) is a terminal object in Top

Then we get

  1. the constant homotopy as homotopy from \(\mathrm{id}_{*}\) to \(t \circ f = \mathrm{id}_{*} = t \circ g\)
  2. the homotopies
\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times \mathbb{R} \rightarrow& \mathbb{R} \\ (t,x) \mapsto& t \cdot x \\ H': [0,1] \times \mathbb{R} \rightarrow& \mathbb{R} \\ (t,x) \mapsto& t \cdot (x-1) + 1 \end{align*}

as homotopies from \(g\) resp. \(f\) to \(\mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}}\)
