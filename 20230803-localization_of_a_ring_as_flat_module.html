localization of a ring as flat module

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S^{-1}A\) the localization for a multiplicatively closed subset \(S \subseteq A\).

Then the localization as module

\begin{align*} S^{-1}A \end{align*}

is a flat module

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • localization as exact functor

Let \(N_i\) be a finite diagram. Then it remains to show that

\begin{align*} (S^{-1}A \otimes_A \mathrm{lim} N_i) \cong \mathrm{lim}(S^{-1}A \otimes_A N_i) \end{align*}

are canonically isomorphic

By localization and tensor functor commute we conclude

\begin{align*} (S^{-1}A \otimes_A \mathrm{lim} N_i) \cong& S^{-1}(A \otimes_A \mathrm{lim} N_i) \end{align*}

By tensor product with the Ring isomorphic we get

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(A \otimes_A \mathrm{lim} N_i) \cong S^{-1}(\mathrm{lim} N_i) \end{align*}

As localization is exact we get

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(\mathrm{lim} N_i) \cong \mathrm{lim}(S^{-1} N_i) \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \mathrm{lim}(S^{-1} N_i) \cong& \mathrm{lim}(S^{-1}(A \otimes_A N_i)) \\ \cong& \mathrm{lim}(S^{-1}A \otimes_A N_i) \end{align*}
