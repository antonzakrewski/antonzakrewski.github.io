Yoneda Lemma for equivalences

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) be a functor, such that precomposition

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow} : \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}', \mathcal{D}) \cong \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

induces an equivalence of categories

2. Proof

Let \(D = \mathcal{C}\) pick identity functor

By essential surjectivity of \(\mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow }\) there exists a functor \(\mathcal{G}: \mathcal{C}' \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow}(G) = \mathcal{G} \circ \mathcal{F} \cong \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{C}} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow} (\mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{G}) = \mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{G} \circ \mathcal{F} \\ \cong \mathcal{F} \\ = \mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow}(\mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{C}'}) \end{align*}

since \(\mathcal{F}^{ \rightarrow}\) is fully faithful it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F} \circ \mathcal{G} \cong \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{C}'} \end{align*}
