monodromy functor

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be topological spaces, \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map Then the monodromy action functor is defined as

\begin{align*} \tau: \tau_{\leq 1}(B) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set} \\ b \mapsto& \mathrm{Fib}_b^p \\ [\gamma] \mapsto& \tau_{\gamma}(1) \end{align*}

where \(\tau_\gamma(1)\) is the monodromy action of \(\gamma\)

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

Suppose \([\gamma] = [\gamma']\). Then by assumption, \(\gamma\), and \(\gamma'\) are homotopic relative to their endpoints. by path lifting preserving homotopy, we conclude, that the endpoints of the lifted \(\gamma, \gamma'\) stay the same.

Since the construction of the monodromy action only depends on the endpoints, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \tau_{\gamma} = \tau_{\gamma'} \end{align*}

2.2. identity

Given the identity of \(b\) in \(\tau_{\leq 1}(B)\), it is the homotopy class of the constant path. Thus lifting it with respect to \(e \in \mathrm{Fib}_b^p\) results uniquely (cf. unique path lifting for a covering map) in the constant path on \(e\). Hence

\begin{align*} \tau_{\gamma}(e) =& \overline{\gamma_e}(1) \\ =& c_{e}(1) \\ =& e \end{align*}

hence \(\tau_{\gamma}(e) = e\) and since \(e\) was arbitrary, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \tau_{\gamma} = \mathrm{id}_{\mathrm{Fib}_b^p} \end{align*}

2.3. composition

Given paths \(\gamma,\gamma'\), it remains to show, that

\begin{align*} \tau_{\gamma * \gamma'} = \tau_{\gamma} * \tau_{\gamma'} \end{align*}
