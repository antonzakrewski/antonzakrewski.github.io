Functor Spec

1. Definition / Proposition

The map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(-): \mathrm{CRing} \rightarrow& \mathrm{Top} \\ (f: A \rightarrow B) \mapsto (\mathrm{Spec}(f): \mathrm{Spec}(B) \rightarrow \mathrm{Spec}(A)) \end{align*}

is a contravariant functor (see: induced continuous map between spectra) category CRing

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

see: induced continous map between spectra

2.2. identity

Suppose \(\mathrm{id}: A \rightarrow A\) is the identity map, then the induced continous map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(\mathrm{id}_{}): \mathrm{Spec}(A) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Spec}(A) \\ \mathfrak{p} \mapsto \mathrm{id}^{-1}(\mathfrak{p}) = \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

2.3. composition

Suppose \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{CRing}}(A,B), g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{CRing}}(B,C)\), then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(g \circ f)(\mathfrak{p}) =& (g \circ f)^{-1}(\mathfrak{p}) \\ =& f^{-1} \circ g^{-1} (\mathfrak{p}) \\ =& \mathrm{Spec}(f) \circ \mathrm{Spec}(g) \mathfrak{p} \\ \end{align*}
