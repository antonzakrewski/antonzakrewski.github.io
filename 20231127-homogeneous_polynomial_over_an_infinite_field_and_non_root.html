homogeneous polynomial over an infinite integral domain and non-root

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an infinite Integral domain, \(f \in R[X_1,...,X_n]\) a polynomial different from the zero polynomial. then there exist \(a_1,...,a_{n-1} \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} f(a_1,...,a_{n-1},1) \neq 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

induction on \(n\)

2.1. base case

for \(n = 1\) it holds, that \(f = \alpha X^d\) for some \(d \in \mathbb{N}\), \(\alpha \neq 0\). Hence \(f(1) = \alpha \neq 0\)

2.2. induction step

We can choose an \(X_j\), such that \(\mathrm{deg}_{X_j}(f) \neq 0\). Let w.l.o.g. \(X_j = X_1\). Then we can write

\begin{align*} f = \sum_{i=0}^{d} f_i X_1^i \end{align*}

Since \(f\) is nonzero, there exists an \(f_i \neq 0\) and by induction assumption, there exist \(\alpha_2,...,\alpha_{n-1}\) such that

\begin{align*} f_i(\alpha_2,...,\alpha_{n-1},1) \neq 0 \end{align*}

Hence we have a nonzero polynomial

\begin{align*} g(X) \coloneqq f(X_1,\alpha_2,...,\alpha_{n-1},1) \in K[X] \end{align*}

and by degree and maximal number of zeros, there only exist finitely many \(\alpha_1 \in K\) with \(g(\alpha_1) = 0\). hence there exists an \(\alpha_1\) with \(g(\alpha_1) \neq 0\) and therefore also

\begin{align*} f(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{n-1},1) \neq 0 \end{align*}
