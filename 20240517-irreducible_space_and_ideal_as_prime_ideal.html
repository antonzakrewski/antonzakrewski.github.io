irreducible space and ideal as prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the zariski spectrum with the Zariski topology and \(\mathcal{V} \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(V)\) a subset

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{V}\) is a closed irreducible topological space
  2. the ideal associated to a vanishing set \(\mathcal{I}(V)\) is a prime ideal

ideal associated to a subset of spec

2. Proof

using the bijection between radical ideals and closed subsets of spec

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(\mathcal{I}(V) = \mathfrak{p}\) is not prime, then by prime ideal and product there exist ideals \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2\) such that \(\mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2 \subseteq \mathcal{I(V)}\) but \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2 \not\subseteq \mathcal{I(V)}\). Furthermore we may assume that \(\mathfrak{p}\) is radical and therefore also \(\mathfrak{a}_1 \setminus \mathfrak{a}_2, \mathfrak{a}_2 \setminus \mathfrak{a}_1 \neq \empytset\)

Using vanishing set of a product of ideals we conclude

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2) = V(\mathfrak{a}_1) \cup V(\mathfrak{a}_2) \end{align*}

and by Vanishing set and order reversing furthermore

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{a}_1), V(\mathfrak{a}_2) \subseteq& V(\mathfrak{p}) \\ V(\mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2) \supseteq V(\mathfrak{p}) \end{align*}

showing the equality

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{p}) = V(\mathfrak{a}_1) \cup V(\mathfrak{a}_2) \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Since vanishing set of a product of ideals Suppose \(\mathcal{I(V)}\) is not prime, then by prime ideal and product there exist ideals \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2\) such that \(\mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2 \subseteq \mathcal{I(V)}\) but \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2 \not\subseteq \mathcal{I(V)}\). Hence using the closed, nonempty irreducible set is generated by a prime ideal
