prime element and localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed set, \(S^{-1}A\) the localization and \(\alpha \in A\) such that \(\alpha \not\mid s \forall s \in S\) Suppose \(\alpha \in A\) is prime, then so is \(\frac{\alpha}{1} \in S^{-1}A\).

2. Proof

Suppose

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} \mid \frac{r_1}{s_1} \cdot \frac{r_2}{s_2} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \frac{\alpha}{1} \cdot s_1 \cdot s_2 \cdot c =& r_1 \cdot r_2 \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} \alpha \mid r_1 \cdot r_2 \end{align*}

Thus since \(\alpha\) is prime, w.l.o.g. \(\alpha \mid r_1\) and thus

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot c' = r_1 \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot \frac{c'}{s_1} = \frac{r_1}{s_1} \end{align*}

or

\begin{align*} \alpha \mid \frac{r_1}{s_1} \end{align*}
