compact open topology for a discrete space homeomorphic to the product

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(I\) a set and \(I^{\delta}\) the discrete topology on \(I\). Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{eval}: \mathrm{Map}(I^{\delta}, X) \rightarrow& \prod_{i \in I} X \\ (f: I^{\delta} \rightarrow X) \mapsto& (f(i))_{i} \end{align*}

is a homeomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. Bijection

follows from the Inverse map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{eval}^{-1}: (x)_{i} \mapsto& \{f: i \mapsto x_i\} \end{align*}

2.2. continuous

continuous map and subbasis Let \(O_j \times \prod_{i \neq j} X \subseteq \prod_{i \in I} X\) be open. Then we conclude

\begin{align*} \mathrm{eval}^{-1}[O_j \times \prod_{i \neq j} X] =& \{g \in \mathrm{Map}(I^{\delta}, X) \vert g(j) \in O\} \\ \mathcal{O}_{j, O} \end{align*}

2.3. open map

Since each compact set in the discrete topology is finite (finite set and compact in the discrete topology) we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}_{K,O} =& \mathcal{O}_{ \{k_1,...,k_n\},O} \\ =& \{f \in \mathrm{Map}(I^{\delta}, X \vert \forall i \in \{1,..,n\} : f(k_i) \in O\} \\ =& \bigcap_{i=1}^n \{f \in \mathrm{Map}(I^{\delta}, X \vert f(k_i) \in O\} \\ =& \bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathrm{eval}^{-1}[O_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X] \end{align*}

By intersection and preimage, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathrm{eval}^{-1}[O_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X] =& \mathrm{eval}^{-1}[\bigcap_{i=1}^n O_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X] \end{align*}

where \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n O_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X\) is open as finite intersection of open sets.

Thus by surjective preimage of open sets implies open map, we conclude, that \(\mathrm{eval}\) is an open map
