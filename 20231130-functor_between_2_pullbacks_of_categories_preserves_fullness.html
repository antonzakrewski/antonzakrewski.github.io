functor between 2-pullbacks of categories preserves fullness

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}_0, \mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2\) and \(\mathcal{D}_0, \mathcal{D}_1, \mathcal{D}_2\) be categories and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C}_1 \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{F}_1} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_1} & \mathcal{C}_0 \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_0} & \mathcal{C}_2 \arrow[swap]{l}{\mathcal{F}_2} \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_2}\\ \mathcal{D}_1 \arrow[swap]{r}{\mathcal{G}_1}& \mathcal{D}_0 & \arrow[]{l}{\mathcal{G}_2}\mathcal{D}_2 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a diagram, such that \(\mathcal{L}_1,\mathcal{L}_2\) are full and \(\mathcal{L}_0\) is faithful

Then for the canonical functor for the 2-categorical pullback of a functor diagram (cf. diagram of 2-categorical pullbacks of functors implies functor)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C}_1 \times_{\mathcal{C}_0} \mathcal{C}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{D}_1 \times_{\mathcal{D}_0} \mathcal{D}_2 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is also full

2. Proof

Let \(\mathcal{L}_1(X_1), \mathcal{L}(X_2), \mathcal{L}_0(\alpha)\) and \(\mathcal{L}_1(Y_1), \mathcal{L}_2(Y_2), \mathcal{L}_2(\beta)\) be objects in \(\mathcal{D}_1 \times_{\mathcal{D}_0} \mathcal{D}_2\) and

\begin{align*} (f_1,f_2): \mathcal{L}_1(X_1), \mathcal{L}(X_2), \mathcal{L}_0(\alpha) \rightarrow \mathcal{L}_1(Y_1), \mathcal{L}_2(Y_2), \mathcal{L}_2(\beta) \end{align*}

be a morphism. Then by assumption, \(f_i \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}_i}(\mathcal{L}_i(X_i),\mathcal{L}_i(Y_i))\), hence by fullness of \(\mathcal{L}_i\) there exist \(f_i': X_i \rightarrow Y_i\) with \(\mathcal{G}_i \circ \mathcal{L}_i (f_i') = \mathcal{G}_i(f_i)\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{G}_1\mathcal{L}_1(X_1) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_0(\alpha)} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{G}_1(\mathcal{L}_1(f_1'))} & \mathcal{G}_2\mathcal{L}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}_2(\mathcal{L}_2(f_2'))} \\ \mathcal{G}_1\mathcal{L}_1(Y_1) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_0(\beta)} & \mathcal{G}_2\mathcal{L}_2(Y_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Furthermore, since \(\mathcal{L}_0 \circ \mathcal{F}_i (-) = \mathcal{G}_i \circ \mathcal{L}_i (-)\), we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{L}_0\mathcal{F}_1(X_1) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_0(\alpha)} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{L}_0(\mathcal{F}_1(f_1'))} & \mathcal{L}_0\mathcal{F}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{L}_0(\mathcal{F}_2(f_2'))} \\ \mathcal{L}_0\mathcal{F}_1(Y_1) \arrow[]{r}{\mathcal{L}_0(\beta)} & \mathcal{L}_0\mathcal{F}_2(Y_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

since \(\mathcal{L}_0\) is faithfull, the preimage

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}_1(X_1) \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} \arrow[swap]{d}{\mathcal{F}_1(f_1')} & \mathcal{F}_2(X_2) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}_2(f_2')}\\ \mathcal{F}_1(Y_1) \arrow[]{r}{\beta} & \mathcal{F}_2(Y_2) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

also commutes (cf. faithful functor and preimage as diagram) Hence

\begin{align*} (f_1',f_2'): (X_1,X_2, \alpha) \rightarrow (Y_1,Y_2,\beta) \end{align*}

is a welldefined morphism, such that by construction

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}(f_1',f_2') = (f_1,f_2) \end{align*}
