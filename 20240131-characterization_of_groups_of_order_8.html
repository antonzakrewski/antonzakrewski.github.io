classificaiton of groups of order 8

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group of group order \(\vert G \vert = 8\). Then

\begin{align*} G \simeq \begin{cases} (\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}^{3} \\ ( \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}) \times (\mathbb{Z} / 4 \mathbb{Z}) \\ ( \mathbb{Z}/ 8 \mathbb{Z}) \\ D_4 \\ Q \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

where

  1. \(D_4\) is the dihedral group
  2. \(Q\) is the quaternion group

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. \(G\) non abelian. The abelian cases are precisely given by the first three cases (cf. Hauptsatz über endlich erzeugte abelsche GRuppen

Furthermore, by dihedral group not isomorphic to quaternions it holds, that \(D_4 \not\simeq Q\)

We may assume, that there exists an element \(g \in G\) with group order \(4\):

  1. since the order of an element divides group order, there exist \(1,2,4,8\) as possibilities
  2. \(1\) is reserved for the neutral element
  3. if there exists an element \(g\) with \(\mathrm{ord}(g) = 8\) then \(G \simeq \mathbb{Z}/8 \mathbb{Z}\), thus contradiction to the assumption \(G\) non abelian
  4. by each element of order 2 and abelian group it follows, that not all remaining \(7\) elements can have order \(2\)

By Normalteiler einer Untergruppe mit Index 2 / Untergruppe mit Index gleich 2 als Normalteiler it follows, that

\begin{align*} \langle g\rangle \trianglelefteq G \end{align*}

Then choosing an element \(h \in G\) gives \(h \langle g\rangle \sqcup \langle g\rangle\) hence

\begin{align*} G = \langle g, h\rangle \end{align*}

Then since \(\langle g\rangle\) is a normal subgroup, it follows, that

\begin{align*} h g h^{-1} \in \langle g\rangle \end{align*}

Furthermore, since

\begin{align*} (g h g^{-1})^n = g h^n g^{-1} \end{align*}

it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ord}(g h \cdot g^{-1}) = 4 \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} = \begin{cases} g \\ g^{-1} \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

Suppose \(g h g^{-1} = g\), then it follows, that \(h g = g h\) and \(G\) is abelian, contradiction.

Hence it follows

\begin{align*} h \cdot g =& g^{-1} \cdot h \end{align*}

By \(\vert G / \langle g\rangle \vert = 2\) it follows, that \(G/ \langle a\rangle = \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\) or

\begin{align*} h^2 \langle g\rangle =& e \\ h^2 \in& \langle g\rangle \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ord}(h^2) = \begin{cases} 2 \\ 1 \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

since from \(\mathrm{ord}(h^2) = 4\) it would follow, that \(\mathrm{ord}(h) = 8\) by \(\mathrm{ord}(h),\mathrm{ord}(h^2) \mid 8\)

hence

\begin{align*} 1 =& \begin{cases} h^2 \\ h^4 \end{cases} \end{align*}

in case \(h^2 = 1\) we conclude, that \(G \simeq D_4\), otherwise for \(h^4 = 1\) it follows from \(h^2 \in \langle g\rangle\) that

\begin{align*} h^2 = a^2 \end{align*}

and therefore \(G \simeq Q\)
