radical as intersection of finitely many prime ideals in a noetherian ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subsetneq A\) a strict ideal Then there exists finitely many prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}_1,...,\mathfrak{p}_n\) such that

\begin{align*} \bigcap \mathfrak{p}_i = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

2. Proof

using

  1. quotient ring of a noetherian ring as noetherian ring
  2. correspondence theorem for ideals and quotient rings

it suffices to show this for the nilradical

by radical of an ideal as intersection of minimal prime ideals and finite amount of minimal prime ideals for a noetherian ring we are done
