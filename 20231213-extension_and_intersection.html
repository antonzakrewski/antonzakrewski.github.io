extension and intersection

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be a commutative ring, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) be a ring-homomorphism and \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2 \subseteq A\) ideals

Then it holds for the extension and intersection

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{a}_1 \cap \mathfrak{a}_2)^e \subseteq \mathfrak{a}_1^e \cap \mathfrak{a}_2^ \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose \(a \in (\mathfrak{a}_1 \cap \mathfrak{a}_2)^e\). Then it is generated by \(r_i \cdot \varphi(a_i)\) with \(a_i \in \mathfrak{a}_1, \mathfrak{a}_2\). Thus it remains to show that \(\varphi(a_i) \in \mathfrak{a}_1, \mathfrak{a}_2\). this holds by definition
