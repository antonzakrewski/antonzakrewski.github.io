finitely presented group

1. Definition

Let \(G\) be a group.

Then \(G\) is said to be finitely presented, if there exists a presentation of a group \((S,R)\) of a set \(S\) and relations \(R\), such that

\begin{align*} \vert S \vert <& \infty \\ \vert R \vert <& \infty \end{align*}

are finite
