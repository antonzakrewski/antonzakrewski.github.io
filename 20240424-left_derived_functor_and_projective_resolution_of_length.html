left derived functor and projective resolution of length

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) a right exact functor. Suppose \(A \in \mathcal{A}\) is an object, which admits a projective resolution of length \(\leq k\), then for the left derived functor

\begin{align*} \mathcal{LF}_n(A) = 0 \end{align*}

for \(n > k\)

2. Proof

follows from the construction of the left derived functor, as by assumption there exists a projective resolution

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & P_{k} \arrow[]{r}{} & P_{k-1} \arrow[]{r}{} & ... & P_0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

since the right exact functor preserves zero morphisms it follows that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{L}(P_{k}) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{L}(P_{k-1}r \arrow[]{r}{} & ... & \mathcal{L}(P_1) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{L}(A) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where

\begin{align*} \mathcal{LF}_n(A) =& H_n(\mathcal{L}(P_* \rightarrow A)) \\ =& H_n(0 \rightarrow 0 \rightarrow ) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
