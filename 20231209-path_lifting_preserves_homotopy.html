path lifting preserves homotopy

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be topological spaces, \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map. Suppose \(\gamma,\gamma': [0,1] \rightarrow B\) are paths , homotopic relative to \(\gamma(0), \gamma(1)\), with \(\gamma(0) = b\) and \(e \in \mathrm{Fib}_b\).

Then the lifted paths \(\gamma_e, \gamma_e': [0,1] \rightarrow E\) for \(e\) (cf. unique path lifting for a covering map) are homotopic relative to \(e\) and \(\gamma_e(1)\)
