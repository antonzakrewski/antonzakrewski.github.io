Snake Lemma

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & M_1 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{f_1} & M_2 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{f_2} & M_3 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{f_3}& 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & N_1 \arrow[]{r}{} & N_2 \arrow[]{r}{} & N_3 & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a diagram, where the rows are exact

Then there exists a natural boundary map \(\partial: \mathrm{ker}(f_3) \rightarrow \mathrm{coker}(f_1)\) such that following sequnece

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathrm{ker}(f_1) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathrm{ker}(f_2) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(f_3) \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & \mathrm{coker}(f_1) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{coker}(f_2) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{coker}(f_3) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact
