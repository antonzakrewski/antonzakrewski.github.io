flat module as torsion free module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) a flat \(R\)-module Then \(M\) is a torsion-free module

2. Proof

Let \(r \in R\) such that \(r\) is not a zero divisor. Consider the exact sequence of modules

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{r}{}R & R \arrow[]{r}{\cdot r}& R/(r) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

tensoring results in the exact sequnece

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & M \otimes_{R} R/(r) \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

especially \(\cdot r: M \hookrightarrow M\) is injective

Hence \(M\) is torsion free
