p-Multiplicity of a root of an irreducible polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain of \(\mathrm{char}(R) = p > 0\). Suppose \(f \in R[X]\) is an irreducible polynomial such that for an integral element \(\alpha \in S\) for an Extension \(S\) Suppose \(\alpha\) is a root, then it follows for the multiplicity \(\nu_{\alpha}\), that

\begin{align*} \nu_{\alpha} = p^r \end{align*}

for some \(r \in \mathbb{N}\).

2. Proof

Let \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i\).

suppose w.l.o.g. that \(\nu_{\alpha} > 1\), as \(p^0 = 1\). Then by algebraische Ableitung eines irreduziblen Polynoms und mehrfache Nullstelle it follows, that

\begin{align*} f' = 0 \end{align*}

By definition, we get

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} (\sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i) =& \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} i \cdot \alpha_i X^{i-1} \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

Here it holds, that

\begin{align*} i \cdot \alpha_i = 0 \end{align*}

and since \(R\) is an integral domain, it follows, that \(\alpha_i = 0\) or \(i = 0\). In the first case, we may ignore that coefficient, in the latter, we conclude, that \(p \mid i\).

Hence we conclude, that there exists an \(g \in K[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} g(X^p) = f \end{align*}

by defining each monomial

\begin{align*} \lambda_i = \begin{cases} \alpha_{\frac{i}{p}} & \mbox{if } p \mid i \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

where the second case is compatible, since \(\alpha_{i} = 0\) for \(p \not\mid i\)
