maximal ideal and addition with 1 as unit and local ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(\mathfrak{m}\) a maximal ideal such that \(m + 1\) is a unit for \(m \in \mathfrak{m}\). Then \(A\) is a local ring

2. Proof

Assume there exist more than one maximal ideals, \(\mathfrak{m}, \mathfrak{m_}2\). Then by maximality

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{m}, \mathfrak{m}_1) = A \end{align*}

Hence there exists \(m \in \mathfrak{m}, m_1 \in \mathfrak{m}_1\) such that

\begin{align*} m + m_1 = 1 \end{align*}

Then it follows that

\begin{align*} m_1 =& 1 - m \\ =& (1 + (-m)) \end{align*}

where by assumption \((1 + (-m))\) is a unit. Hence \(m_1\) is a unit and by unit and trivial ideal it follows that \(\mathfrak{m}_1\) is not strict, contradiction
