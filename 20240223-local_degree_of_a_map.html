local degree of a map

1. Definition

Let \(f: S^n \rightarrow S^n\) be a continuous map and \(n \geq 1\). Suppose \(x \in S^n\) such that there exists an open neighbourhood \(U\) of \(x\) and \(V\) of \(f(x)\) with

\begin{align*} f_{\vert U}: U \rightarrow V \end{align*}

as homeomorphism

Then the local degree of \(f\) at \(x\) is defined as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(S^n) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{x\}) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(U,U \setminus \{x\}) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(V, V \setminus \{f(x)\}) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{f(x)\}) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & H_n(S^n) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq} \\ \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{rrrrr}{\cdot \mathrm{deg}_x(f)} & & & & & \mathbb{Z} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where

\begin{align*} H_n(S^n) \simeq H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{x\}) \end{align*}

is the isomorphismus by singular homology relative to a contractible subspace

\begin{align*} H_n(U, U \setminus \{x\}) \simeq H_n(V, V \setminus \{f(x)\}) \end{align*}

is given by the isomorphism in category of topological pairs

\begin{align*} f: U \rightarrow V \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{x\}) \simeq& H_n(U, U \setminus \{x\}) \\ H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{x\}) \simeq& H_n(V, V \setminus \{x\}) \end{align*}

are given by excision for

\begin{align*} X = S^n, A = S^n \setminus \{x\}, U = S^n \setminus U \end{align*}
