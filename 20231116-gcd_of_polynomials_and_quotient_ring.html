gcd of polynomials as non unit and quotient ring for a UFD

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a unique factorization domain \(R[X]\) the polynomial ring and \(f,g \in R[X]\) polynomials.

Suppose for the quotient ring \(Q(R)\) and the polynomial ring \(Q(R)[X]\) with \(f,g \in Q(R)[X]\), that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{gcd}_{Q(R)[X]}(f,g) \not\in Q(R)[X]^{\times} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{gcd}_{R[X]}(f,g) \not\in (R[X])^{\times} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose there exists a divisor \(c \in Q(R)[X]\), that is not a unit. Hence \(\mathrm{deg}(c) > 0\) and there exist \(f',g' \in Q(R)[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} f =& c \cdot f' \\ g =& c \cdot g' \end{align*}

By assumption

\begin{align*} f' =& \frac{p(x)}{r_f} \\ g' =& \frac{q(x)}{r_g} \\ c =& \frac{c'}{r_c} \end{align*}

for some \(r_f,r_g,r_c \in Q(R)\) (note that one can represent \(f' \in Q(R)[X]\) by expanding each denominator) and \(p(x),q(x),c'(x) \in R[X,Y]\)

thus we get

\begin{align*} f \cdot r_f \cdot r_g \cdot r_c =& c' \cdot r_g \\ g \cdot r_f \cdot r_g \cdot r_c =& c' \cdot r_f \end{align*}

where we can cancel \(r_f,r_g\)

\begin{align*} f \cdot r_f \cdot r_c =& c' \\ g \cdot r_g \cdot r_c =& c' \end{align*}

Since \(\mathrm{deg}(r_f), \mathrm{deg}(r_g) = 0\) by construction and \(\mathrm{deg}(c') > 0\). Taking a prime factorization of \(c' \in R[X,Y]\) (see: Satz von Gauß über einen faktoriellen Polynomring), we get a prime factor \(\tilde{c}\) of \(c'\) with \(\mathrm{deg}_Y(c') \geq 0\). Therefore, since \(c' \mid f \cdot r_f \cdot r_c\), it follows, that \(c'\) divides at least one of \(f,r_f,r_c\) in \(R[X,Y]\) Comparing degrees (cf. Grad von Polynomen unter Multiplikation in einem Integritätsbereich) results in

\begin{align*} c \mid& f \\ c \mid& g \end{align*}

where \(c \mid g\) follows analogously
