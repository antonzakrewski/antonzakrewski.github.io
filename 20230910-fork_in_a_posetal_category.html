fork in a thin category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a thin category, then there exist forks.

2. Proof

Let \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) and \(f,g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\). Then \(\mathrm{id}_{A}\) is the fork, because by assumption of \(\mathcal{C}\) being thin we conclude

\begin{align*} f =& g \\ f \circ \mathrm{id}_{A} =& g \circ \mathrm{id}_{A} \end{align*}
