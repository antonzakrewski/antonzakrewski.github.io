prime multiplied with unit as prime

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(p \in A\) be prime and \(\alpha \in A^{\times}\) a unit. Then

\begin{align*} p \cdot \alpha \end{align*}

is also prime

2. Proof

Suppose there exist \(r_1,r_2 \in A\) such that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot p \mid r_1 \cdot r_2 \end{align*}

Then by transitivity of divides and \(p \mid \alpha \cdot p\), we conclude, that

\begin{align*} p \mid r_1 \cdot r_2 \end{align*}

Hence since \(p\) is prime, w.l.o.g. \(p \mid r_1\). Furthermore, since

\begin{align*} \alpha^{-1} \cdot (\alpha \cdot p) = p \end{align*}

or \(\alpha \cdot p \mid p\), we conclude again by transitivity, that

\begin{align*} \alpha \cdot p \mid r_1 \end{align*}
