intermediate field extension of a normal extension and separability degree

1. Proposition

Let \(L/K\) be a normal field extension, \(M\) an intermediate field extension. Then for the separability degree, it holds

\begin{align*} [M : K]_s \coloneqq& \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M, \overline{K}) \vert \\ =& \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,L) \vert \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(\overline{K}\) be an algebraic closure of \(L\). Then there exists an injection \(\iota: L \hookrightarrow \overline{K}\), hence also a monomorphism

2.1. \(\vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,L) \vert \leq \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,\overline{K}) \vert\)

Therefore by postcomposition with a morphism as injection on hom sets and monomorphism we get an injection

\begin{align*} \iota^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,L) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M, \overline{K}) \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,L) \vert \leq \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,\overline{K}) \vert \end{align*}

2.2. \(\vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,L) \vert \geq \vert \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,\overline{K}) \vert\)

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,\overline{K}) \leftrightarrow& \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M, L) \\ (\sigma: M \rightarrow \overline{K}) \mapsto& (\sigma: M \rightarrow L) \\ \end{align*}

is welldefined:

Suppose \(\sigma \in \mathrm{Hom}_{K}(M,\overline{K})\) and \(m \in \alpha\). Then for the minimal polynomial \(f_{\alpha}\), all roots are by definition elements of \(L\), since \(L/K\) is a normal field extension.

By root under A-Algebra homomorphisms, it follows, that \(\sigma(\alpha) \in L\), as \(L\) contains all roots of the minimal polynomial
