connected component as subset of intersection of clopen sets

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(x \in X\). then for the connected component \(C(x)\) and the intersection of clopen sets \(\bigcap C_i\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} C(x) \subseteq \bigcap C_i \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose \(y \in C(x)\) and \(C_i\). Then if \(y \not\in C_i\), it follows, that \(C(x)\) is not connected, as \(C_i\) and \(X \setminus C_i\) are a disjoint open cover. Contradiction to \(y \in C(x)\)
