group order of a triangular matrices subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(n \in \mathbb{N}\). Then the group of triangular matrices with diagonal 1 as subgroup

\begin{align*} \begin{pNiceMatrix} 1 & \alpha_{1,2} & \dots & \alpha_{1,n} \\ 0 & 1 & \alpha_{2,3} & \alpha_{2,n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \dots & 1 \\ \end{pNiceMatrix} \end{align*}

has group order

\begin{align*} \vert K \vert^{ \frac{n (n-1)}{2}} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Note that \(\alpha_{i,j}\) may be choosen arbitrary. Hence we get

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} i = \frac{n \cdot (n-1)}{2} \end{align*}
