zeroth left derived functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) a right exact functor. Then for the left derived functor it holds that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{LF}_0 \simeq \mathcal{F} \end{align*}

up to natural isomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. object

note that by construction

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{r}{} I_1 & I_2 ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact and as \(\mathcal{F}\) is right exact, so is

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(0) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[]{r}{} & \arrow[]{r}{} \mathcal{F}(I_1) & \mathcal{F}(I_2) ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

here \(\mathcal{F}(0) \simeq 0\) (see: right exact functor preserves zero morphisms, functor preserving zero morphism and preserving zero object)

Thus by Homology of a chopped short exact sequence it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(A) \simeq \mathcal{LF}_0(A) \end{align*}
