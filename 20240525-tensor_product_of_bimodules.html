tensor product of bimodules

1. Proposition

Let \(R,S_1,S_2\) be rings, \(M\) an \(S_1,R\) and \(N\) an \(R,S_2\) bimodules Then the tensor product

\begin{align*} M \otimes_{R} N \end{align*}

is a \(S_1,S_2\) bimodule via

\begin{align*} s_1 \cdot_{S_1} (m \otimes_{R} n) \cdot_{S_2} s_2 =& (s_1 \cdot m \otimes_{R} n \cdot s_2) \end{align*}

on elementary tensors and distributive extension

Note that any left (right) \(R\) module is a \(R,\mathbb{Z}\) (reps. \(\mathbb{Z}, R\)) module (cf. R-module as R,Z module)

2. Proof

2.1. associativity

follows as

\begin{align*} ((s_1 \cdot_{S_1} (m \otimes_R n)) \cdot_{S_2} s_2) =& (s_1 \cdot_{S_1} m \otimes_R n) \cdot_{S_2} s_2 \\ =& (s_1 \cdot_{S_1} m \otimes_R n \cdot_{S_2} s_2) \\ =& s_1 \cdot_{S_1} (m \otimes_R n \cdot_{S_2} s_2) \\ =& s_1 \cdot_{S_1} ((m \otimes_R n) \cdot_{S_2} s_2) \end{align*}

2.1.1. unit

follows from

\begin{align*} 1 \cdot_{S_1} M \otimes_R N \cdot_{S_2} 1 = (1 \cdot m \otimes_R n \cdot 1) \\ =& (m \otimes_R n) \end{align*}

2.1.2. multiplicativity

follows as

\begin{align*} s \cdot (s' \cdot (m \otimes_R n)) =& s \cdot (s' \cdot m \otimes_R n) \\ =& (s \cdot s' \cdot m \otimes_R n) \\ =& ((s \cdot s') \cdot m \otimes_{R} n ) \\ =& (s \cdot s') \cdot (m \otimes_R n) \end{align*}

and analogously for \(S_2\)

2.1.3. distributivity

follows as

\begin{align*} (s_1 + s_2) \cdot (m \otimes_R n) =& ((s_1 + s_2) \cdot m \otimes_R n) \\ =& (s_1 \cdot m \otimes_R n) + (s_2 \cdot m \otimes_R n) \end{align*}
