right derived functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) a left exact functor Suppose \(\mathcal{A}\) admits enough injectives, then the right derived functor \(\mathcal{R}_i\mathcal{F}\) for \(i \in \mathbb{N}\) is defined as following

For an object \(A \in \mathcal{A}\) choose an injective coresolution

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} & I_0 \arrow[]{r}{} & I_1 ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then consider the diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(I_0) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(I_1) & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{R}_i\mathcal{F}(A) = H_i(... \mathcal{F}(I_{i-1}) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}(I_i) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}(I_{i+1}) ...) \end{align*}

For

\begin{align*} f: A \rightarrow B \end{align*}

we get after choosing injective coresolutions a diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}(f)} \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(I_0) \arrow[]{r}{}\arrow[]{d}{} & \mathcal{F}(I_1) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathcal{F}(B) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathcal{F}(I_0) \arrow[]{r}{}& \mathcal{F}(I_1) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
