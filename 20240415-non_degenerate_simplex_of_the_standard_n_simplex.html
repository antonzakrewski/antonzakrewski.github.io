non degenerate simplex of the standard n-simplex

1. Proposition

Let \(\Delta^n\) be the represented simplicial set, then for \([k] \in \mathrm{Ob}(\Delta)\) the set of non-degenerate simplices is given by

\begin{align*} \{f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\Delta}([k],[n]) \vert f \textrm{non degenerate} \} =& \{ f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\Delta}([k],[n]) \vert f \textrm{injective}\} \simeq& \{\textrm{strict monotone sequences}\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\Delta}([k],[n])\)

2.1. \(f\) non degenerate

Proof by contraposition Let \(f\) be injective and suppose that \(f\) degnerate

2.2. \(f\) degenerate \(\Rightarrow\) not injective

Suppose there exists a degeneracy map \(\sigma: [n] \rightarrow [n-1]\) and an element \(g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\Delta}([k-1],[n])\) such that

\begin{align*} \sigma^{ \rightarrow}(g) =& g \sigma \\ =& f \end{align*}

then it follows that there exist \(i,i+1 \in [n]\) such that σ(i) = σ(i+1)$ and hence

\begin{align*} f(i) =& g \sigma(i) \\ =& g \sigma(i+1) \\ =& f(i+1) \end{align*}
