isomorphic object and factorization

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) objects.

TFAE:

  1. each morphism \(g: C \rightarrow B, g': C \rightarrow A\) for \(C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) factorizes through \(A\) resp. \(B\)
  2. each morphism \(g: B \rightarrow B, g': A \rightarrow A\) factorizes through \(A\) resp. \(B\)
  3. \(A \cong B\) are isomorphic
  4. each morphism \(g: B \rightarrow C, g': A \rightarrow C\) for \(C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) factorizes through \(A\) resp. \(B\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

by assumption, both identities factorizes through it. Hence

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

by assumption, there exists an isomorphism \(f: A \rightarrow B\). Hence taking

\begin{align*} f \circ f^{-1} \circ g =& \mathrm{id}_{B} \circ g \\ =& g \end{align*}

2.4. 4) \(\implies\) other
