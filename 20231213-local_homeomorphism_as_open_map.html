local homeomorphism as open map

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces.

Suppose \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) is a local homeomorphism, then \(f\) is an open map

2. Proof

corollary of

  • glueing lemma for open maps on an open covering
  • homeomorphism as continuous, open / closed Bijection,

Note that by definition, each local restriction

\begin{align*} f_{\vert V} V \rightarrow f[V] \end{align*}

maps \(V\) homeomorphic to an open set \(f[V] \subseteq Y\), hence by inclusion from an open set as open map and composition of open maps, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \iota_{f[V]} \circ f_{\vert V}: V \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

is an open map
