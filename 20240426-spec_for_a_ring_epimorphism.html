spec for a ring epimorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be rings, \(\varphi: A \twoheadrightarrow B\) a ring-homomorphism and \(\mathrm{Spec}()\) the Spec Then \(\mathrm{Spec}(f)\) is a closed embedding

2. Proof

by homomorphism theorem for rings we may assume that

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrow A/\mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

for some ideal \(\mathfrak{a}\)

Then using the correspondence theorem for prime ideals and quotient ring there exists a bijection between a subset of ideals of \(A\) and the ideals \(A/\mathfrak{a}\)

2.1. injective

Hence this gives an injection.

2.2. open

Let \(V(\mathfrak{b})\) be closed Then consider \(\pi^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]\). Furthermore \(V(\pi^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}])\) is by definition closed and again by the correspondence theorem it follows that \(\mathfrak{c} \in V(\mathfrak{b})\) iff and only if \(\pi^{-1}[\mathfrak{c}] \in V([\mathfrak{b}])\).

Therefore \(\mathrm{Spec}(f)\) maps closed subsets to closed subsets
