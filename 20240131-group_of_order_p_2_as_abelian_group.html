classification of groups of order p2

1. Proposition

Let \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) be a prime number and \(G\) a group of group order \(\vert G \vert = p^2\) Then

\begin{align*} G \simeq& \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z}/ p^2 \mathbb{Z} \\ (\mathbb{Z} / p \mathbb{Z} )^2 \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. abelian

by center of a p-group nontrivial it follows, that

\begin{align*} \vert Z(G) \vert =& \begin{cases} p \\ p^2 \end{cases} \end{align*}

w.l.o.g. we may assume, that \(\vert Z(G) \vert = p\). Then there exists an \(x \in G \setminus Z\). Furthremore, for the stabilizer \(G_x\) it holds, that \(Z \subseteq G_x\), \(x \in G_x\) and therefore also

\begin{align*} Z \subsetneq G_x \end{align*}

hence by Satz von Lagrange \(G = G_x\), but that is equivalent to

\begin{align*} g x g^{-1} =& x \forall g \in G g x =& x g \forall g \in G \end{align*}

or \(x \in Z\), contradiction

2.2. classification

then this follows as corollary of classification of finitely generated abelian groups
