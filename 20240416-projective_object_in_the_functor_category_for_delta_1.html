projective object in the arrow category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category, \([1]\) the interval category and \(f: Q \rightarrow P \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Fun}([1],\mathcal{A})\) an object in the functor category TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is a projective object in \(\mathrm{Fun}([1],\mathcal{A})\)
  2. \(Q\) is a projective object and there exists a natural isomorphism
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{r}{f} & Q \arrow[]{d}{}\\ P \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & Q' \oplus P \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Note that \(P\) is also a projective object

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.1.1. q projective

Suppose there exists

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{}{}g \circ \iota& N \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}}& M \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}} \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{ll}{} \\ Q \arrow[]{r}{g} & N & \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{ll}{p} M \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Projectiveness implies that there exists a morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{d}{}\\ Q \arrow[]{r}{f} & M \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

hence \(Q\) is projective

consider

Since \(\iota\) is projective, there exists a lift

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}} & P \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}}\\ Q \arrow[]{r}{r} & P \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let $p,Q be projective with

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[]{r}{g_0} & N_0 \arrow[]{d}{h}& \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{l}{p_0} M_0 \arrow[]{d}{f}\\ P \oplus Q \arrow[]{r}{g_1} & N_1 & \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{l}{p_1} M_1 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Since \(P\) is projective, there exists a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & M_0 \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{} \\ P \arrow[]{ru}{\exists f} \arrow[]{r}{} & N_0 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Since \(Q\) is projective, there exists a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & & M_0 \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{d}{} \\ P \arrow[]{rru}{\exists f} \arrow[]{r}{} & P \oplus Q \arrow[]{r}{} & N_0 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Define

\begin{align*} f g_0 + g_1: P \oplus Q \rightarrow M_1 \end{align*}

Then

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[]{r}{g_0} & M_0 \arrow[]{d}{f}\\ P \oplus Q \arrow[]{r}{f g_0 + g_1} & M_1 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & M_1 \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \arrow[]{ru}{f g_0 + g_1} P \oplus Q \arrow[]{r}{g_1} & N_1 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute
