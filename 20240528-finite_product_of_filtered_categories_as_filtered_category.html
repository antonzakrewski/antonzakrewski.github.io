finite product of filtered categories as filtered category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}_i\) be filtered categories. Then the product category

\begin{align*} \prod \mathcal{C}_i \end{align*}

is also filtered

2. Proof

definitions

2.1. nonempty

follows from \((c_i)\) for \(c_i \in \mathcal{C}_i\)

2.2. arrow

for \((c_i), (d_i)\) choose \(f_i: c_i \rightarrow e_i, g_i: d_i \rightarrow e_i\) and then

\begin{align*} (f_i): (c_i) \rightarrow& (e_i) \\ (g_i): (d_i) \rightarrow& (e_i) \\ \end{align*}

suffices

2.3. fork

anlogously choose a fork for each component and then consider the product.
