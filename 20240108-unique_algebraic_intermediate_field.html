unique maximal algebraic intermediate field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(L/K\) a field extension. Then there exists a unique intermediate extension \(E\) such that

  1. \(E/K\) is algebraic
  2. \(L/E\) is purely transcendental

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} E = \{\alpha \in L \vert \alpha \text{ algebraic over K}\} \end{align*}

Then by set of algebraic elements as subfield, \(E\) is a subfield. Furthermore \(E/K\) is algebraic.

Suppose \(\alpha \in L\) is algebraic over \(E\). Then \(E(\alpha)/E\) is an algebraic field extension. Note, that by transitivity of algebraic field extensions, \(\alpha\) is algebraic over \(K\). Thus \(\alpha \in E\) and hence \(E(\alpha) = E\) and therefore, \(L/E\) is purely transcendental.
