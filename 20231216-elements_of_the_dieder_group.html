1. Proposition

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), then for an element \(x \in D_n\) in the dieder group, there exists unique \(i \in \{0,...,n-1\}\) and \(j \in \{0,1\}\) such that

\begin{align*} x = s^{j} \cdot r^i \end{align*}
