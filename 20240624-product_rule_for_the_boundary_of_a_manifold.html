product rule for the boundary of a manifold

1. Proposition

Let \(W\) be a \(k\)-dimensional and \(V\) an \(l\)-dimensional topological manifold with boundary. Then the boundary of the product is given by

\begin{align*} \partial(V \times W) \cong \partial V \times W \cup V \times \partial W \end{align*}

2. Proof

using boundary and interior of a manifold as disjoint union it suffices to treat a case distinction

2.1. interior

  • follows from
\begin{align*} U \times V \simeq \mathbb{R}^k \times \mathbb{R}^l \simeq \mathbb{R}^{k +l} \end{align*}

hence not in the boundary

2.2. interior + boundary

follows from

\begin{align*} U \times V \simeq (\mathbb{R}^{k-1} \times \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}) \times \mathbb{R}^l \simeq \mathbb{R}^{k+l-1} \times \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \end{align*}

with \((\varphi \times \psi)(x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^{k+l-1} \times [0]\), hence a point in the boundary

2.3. boundary + boundary

follows from proudct of half open lines as half open plane up to homeomorphism
