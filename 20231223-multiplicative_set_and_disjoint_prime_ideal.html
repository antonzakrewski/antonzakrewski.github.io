multiplicative set and disjoint prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed subset with \(0 \not\in S\). Then for \(p \in A\) such that \((p) \cap S = \emptyset\) there exists a prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\) with

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} \cap S = \emptyset \end{align*}

and \(p \in \mathfrak{p}\)

Note that for \(p = 0\) there always exists a \(\mathfrak{p}\), since \(1 \in S\) and thus \(A\) is different from the zero ring

2. Proof

By construction

\begin{align*} \frac{p}{1} \not\in (S^{-1}A)^{\times} \end{align*}

is not a unit.

Hence the generated Ideal

\begin{align*} ( \frac{p}{1}) \subseteq S^{-1}A \end{align*}

is strict (cf. unit and trivial ideal)

Therefore there exists a maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq S^{-1}A\), which is prime (cf. maximal ideal as prime ideal).

Hence by correspondence theorem for prime ideals and localization
