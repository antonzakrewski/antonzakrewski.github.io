connected component functor from Cat

1. Definition

Given Category Cat, the connected component functor is defined as functor

\begin{align*} \pi_0: \mathrm{Cat} \rightarrow& \mathrm{Cat} \\ \mathcal{C} \mapsto& \mathcal{C}_{\pi_0} (\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}) \mapsto& \pi_0(\mathcal{F}): \pi_0(\mathcal{C}) \rightarrow \pi_0(\mathcal{D}) \end{align*}

where \(A_{\pi_0}\) is the discrete category containing the equivalence class of objects, such that there exists a zigzag from \(A\) to \(B\) and

\begin{align*} \pi_0(\mathcal{F}): \pi_0(\mathcal{C}) \rightarrow \pi_0(\mathcal{D}) \\ (f: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}) \mapsto& \pi_0(f) \end{align*}

where \(\pi_0(f)\) is the functor sending each object to its connected component and each morphism to the identity morphism

2. welldefined

by assumption, for

\begin{align*} f: A \rightarrow B \end{align*}

it follows, that there exists a zigzag from \(\mathcal{F}(A)\) to \(\mathcal{F}(B)\), hence they are in the same equivalence class. Thus \(\mathcal{F}(f) = \mathrm{id}_{[A]}: [A] \rightarrow [B]\) is welldefined
