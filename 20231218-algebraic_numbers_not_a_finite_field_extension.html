algebraic numbers not a finite field extension

1. Proposition

The algebraic numbers are not a finite field extension.

2. Proof

Suppose \(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}\) is finite, then the degree is finite, \(\vert \overline{\mathbb{Q}} : \mathbb{Q} \vert = n\)

by

  1. primes Polynom eines faktoriellen Körpers und prim im Quotientenkörper
  2. Eisenstein

it follow, that

\begin{align*} X^{n+1} - p \end{align*}

is irreducible

Furthermore, by assumption \(X^{n+1} - p\) splits in \(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}\). By degree of a simple field extension and degree of the minimal polynomial, it follows, that for \(\sqrt[n+1]{p}\) we get

\begin{align*} \vert \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[n+1]{p} : \mathbb{Q} \vert = n +1 \end{align*}

hence \(\sqrt[n+1]{p} \not\in \overline{\mathbb{Q}}\), contradiction
