2.2. union of chain

Let \(T_i\) be a chain of algebraically independent subsets. Consider the union \(T = \bigcup T_i\).

Let

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \lambda_i \alpha_i^{\nu(i)} \end{align*}

be an algebraic combination for \(\lambda_i \in K\) and \(\alpha_i \in T\).

Then since \(n\) is finite, there exists an \(T_i\) such that \(\alpha_i \in T_i\)

Since \(T_i\) is algebraically independent, it follows, that \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_i^{\nu(i)} \neq 0\) and thus \(T\) is also algebraically independent
