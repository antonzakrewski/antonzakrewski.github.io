inclusion of multiplicative closed subsets and inclusions of localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathcal{S}\) the partially ordered set containing multiplicatively closed subsets. Then the localization is a functor

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(-):\mathcal{S} \rightarrow \mathrm{A-Alg} \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from the universal property, as for \(S \subseteq S'\) and the inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota: A \rightarrow S'^{-1} A \end{align*}
