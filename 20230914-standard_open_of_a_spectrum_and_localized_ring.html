standard open of an element and spectrum and localization along the element

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \((\mathrm{Spec}(A), \mathcal{O})\) the spectrum. Then for \(f \in A\) and the standard open \(D((f))\) is ring-isomorphic to the localization along an element

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}(D(f)) \cong A_f \end{align*}

2. Proof

Consider

\begin{align*} \varphi: A_f \rightarrow& \mathcal{O}(D(f)) \\ \frac{\alpha}{f^n} \mapsto& (s: \mathfrak{q} \mapsto \frac{\alpha}{f^n}) \end{align*}

2.1. injective

Suppose

\begin{align*} \varphi(\frac{a}{f^n}) = \varphi(\frac{b}{f^m}) \end{align*}

then for each \(\mathfrak{p} \in D(f)\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} \frac{a}{f^n} = \frac{b}{f^m} \in A_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{align*}

Thus there exists an \(h \in A \setminus \mathfrak{p}\) with

\begin{align*} h \cdot (f^m a - f^n b) = 0 \end{align*}

Consider the Annihilator \(\mathrm{Ann}\) of \((f^m a - f^n b)\). Then \(h \in \mathrm{Ann}\) and thus \(\mathrm{Ann} \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}\) Since \(\mathfrak{p}\) was arbitrary, this holds for each \(\mathfrak{p} \in D(f)\) and thus

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{a}) \cap D(f) = \empytset \end{align*}

Therefore \(f \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\), so \(f^l \in \mathrm{Ann}\) for some \(l \in \mathrm{Ann}\).

Thus

\begin{align*} f^l (f^m a - f^n b) = 0 \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} \frac{a}{f^n} = \frac{b}{f^m} \end{align*}

in \(A_f\)

2.2. surjective
