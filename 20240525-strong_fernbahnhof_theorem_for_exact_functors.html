strong Fernbahnhof theorem for exact functors

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories, \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A}), \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{B})\) the category of chain complexes and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{B})\) a left exact functor, which preserves trivial boundary chain complexes

Then for the homology chain complex functor, there exists a natural isomorphism

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}H_*(-) \simeq H_*(\mathcal{F}(-)) \end{align*}

of functors

Note that \(\mathcal{F}\) may be induced by an exact functor \(\mathcal{F}': \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) (cf. exact functor induces exact functor on chain complexes)

2. Proof

2.1. objects

follows, as \(\mathcal{F}^*\) resp. \(\mathcal{F}\) preserve kernels / cokernels and hence commute with the homology functor

2.2. morphism

follows from:

  • isomorphism of chain complexes and isomorphism of points
