palindromic polynomial and inverse as roots

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i\) a palindromic polynomial and \(\beta, \beta^{-1} \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} f(\beta) = 0 \end{align*}

then

\begin{align*} f(\beta^{-1}) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

Follows from

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i \beta^{i} =& 0 && \vert \cdot \beta^{-n} \\ \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i \beta^{i-n} =& 0 \\ =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_{n-i} \beta^{-i} \\ =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_{i} \beta^{-i} \end{align*}
