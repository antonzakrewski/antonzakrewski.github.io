singular chain complex of a cw complex as colimit of skeletons

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex, then the canonical map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}_{n \geq 0} \mathrm{C}_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(\mathrm{sk}_n(X)) \rightarrow C^{\mathrm{sing}}_*(X) = C^{\mathrm{sing}}_{*}(\mathrm{colim}_{n \geq 0}(X)) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

see:

  • induced morphism between colimits and functor

2. Proof

As \(\Delta_{\mathrm{top}^n\) is compact and image of compact space as compact space, it follows by compact subset of a cw-complex as subset of a finite subcomplex, that

\begin{align*} \Delta_{\mathrm{top}}^n \subseteq \mathrm{Sk}_k(X) \end{align*}

for some \(k \geq 0\)

Thus an element

\begin{align*} \sigma \in C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \end{align*}

is an element of \(C_*^{\mathrm{sing}}(\mathrm{Sk}_k(X))\) for a sufficiently large \(k \in \mathbb{N}\)
