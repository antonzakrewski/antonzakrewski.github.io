nilpotent map between homology and mapping torus

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(f: X \rightarrow X\) a continuous map and

\begin{align*} f_n: H_n(X) \rightarrow H_n(X) \end{align*}

the induced map by the singular homology functor.

Suppose \(f_n\) is nilpotent, i.e. \(f_l^k = 0\) for some \(k \in \mathbb{N}^+\) and \(l = n,n-1\), then it follows, that the homology of the mapping torus

\begin{align*} H_{n}(T_f) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

Note that by hom set of an ab-enriched category as ring and unit in the center plus nilpotent element as unit it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_* - f_l \end{align*}

is an isomorphism

By long exact sequence of homology for a mapping torus there exists the exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_{*} - f_n} }& H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n}(T_f) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(X) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_{*} - f_{n-1} } & H_{n-1}(X) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_{n-1}(X) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

sandwhiched by isomorphisms in an exact sequence
