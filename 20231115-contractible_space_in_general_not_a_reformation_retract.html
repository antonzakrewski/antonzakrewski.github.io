contractible space in general not a reformation retract

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, that is contractible. Then \(X\) is in general not deformation retract

2. Proof

Consider

\begin{align*} S = \{(x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \vert \frac{y}{x} \in \mathbb{Q}\} \end{align*}

Then \((1,0)\) is contractible / but not a deformation retract
