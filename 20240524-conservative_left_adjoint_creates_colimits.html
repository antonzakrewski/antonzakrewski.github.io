conservative left adjoint creates colimits for sufficiently cocomplete domain

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a conservative left adjoint, where \(\mathcal{C}\) admits (enough) colimits of a shape \(\mathcal{J}\) Then \(\mathcal{F}\) creates colimits of the shape \(\mathcal{J}\)

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • conservative functor reflects preserved colimits
  • left adjoint preserves colimits
