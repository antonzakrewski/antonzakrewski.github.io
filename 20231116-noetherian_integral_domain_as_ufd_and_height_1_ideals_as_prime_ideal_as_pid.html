noetherian integral domain as UFD and height 1 ideals as prime ideal as principal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian Integral domain.

TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is an unique factorization domain
  2. each ideal with height \(1\) is a Principal ideal

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(\mathfrak{p}\) be a prime ideal with height \(1\).

then by noetherian ring and prime ideal as minimal prime ideal over height many generators there exists an \(\alpha\) such that \(\mathfrak{p}\) is minimal over \(\alpha\). Take a prime decomposition of \(\alpha\), i.e.

\begin{align*} \alpha = \prod p_i \end{align*}

Then it follows that \(p_i \in \mathfrak{p}\) for some \(i\), as \((p_1) \cdot \prod_{i=2}^n p_i = \alpha \in \mathfrak{p}\). Hence we have a chain

\begin{align*} (0) \subsetneq (p_i) \subseteq \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

As \(\mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{p}) = 1\) we conclude that \(\mathfrak{p} = (p_i)\).

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(a \in A \setminus (A^{\times} \cup \{0\})\) be an element. Then consider the principal ideal \((a)\). By the height theorem we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(a) = 1 \end{align*}

Choose a chain

\begin{align*} (0) \subsetneq \mathfrak{p} \subseteq (a) \end{align*}

with \(\mathfrak{p}\) prime. Then by assumption \(\mathfrak{p}\) has height \(1\), since \((a)\) has heigth \(1\).

Then by assumption \(\mathfrak{p} = (p)\) for some prime \(p\). Note that

\begin{align*} p \mid a \end{align*}

using element as product of irreducibles for a noetherian domain we get

\begin{align*} p \mid a_0 \cdot ... a_n \end{align*}

for irreducibles \(a_i\).

As \(p\) is prime (cf. Primelement und erzeugte Primideal) there exists an \(a_i\), w.l.o.g. \(a_n\) such that \(p \mid a_n\). Hence this \(a_n\) is prime (cf. irreducible element and division by prime)

Let \(\alpha_1 \coloneqq a_0 \cdot ... a_{n-1}\).

Then inductively construct a chain

\begin{align*} (a) \subseteq (\alpha_1) \subseteq (\alpha_2) \subseteq ... \end{align*}

By noetherianness this chain stabilizes. Therefore we conclude that

\begin{align*} p = a_0 \cdot ... \cdot a_n \end{align*}

is a decomposition into prime elements (cf. prime decomposition, uniqueness of a prime decomposition
