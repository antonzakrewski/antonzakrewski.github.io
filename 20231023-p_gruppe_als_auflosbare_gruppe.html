p group as solvable group

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a p-group with group order \(\vert G \vert = p^k\) Then \(G\) is solvable

2. Proof

induction

2.1. \(k = 0\)

note that then \(G = 1\) and hence abelian and thus solvable (cf. abelian group as solvable group)

2.2. \(k > 0\)

Consider the center \(Z(G) \subseteq G\). It is nontrivial, an abelian subgroup and by center as normal subgroup we get

\begin{align*} 1 \trianglelefteq Z(G) \trianglelefteq G \end{align*}

Hence \(G/Z(G)\) is a group of order \(\vert G : Z(G) \vert = \vert G \vert / \vert Z(G) \vert = p^l\) for some \(l < k\)

Therefore by induction assumption, \(G/Z(G)\) is solvable.
