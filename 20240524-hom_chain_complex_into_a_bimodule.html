hom chain complex into a bimodule

1. Proposition

Let \(R,S\) be rings, and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod, \(M,N\) chain complexes of left (right) \(R\)-modules where \(N_*\) is also equipped with a right (left) \(S\)-module structure. Then the hom chain complex

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(M,N) \end{align*}

is also a right (left) \(S\)-module

2. Proof

by definition

\begin{align*} \underline{\mathrm{Hom}(M,N)}_n \coloneqq \prod_{p \in \mathbb{Z}} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{RMod}}^{\mathrm{Ab}}(M_p, N_{p+n}) \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{Hom}^{\mathrm{Ab}}_{\mathrm{RMod}}(M_p,N_{p+n})\) admits a right \(S\)-structure. Thus using limit of bimodules we conclude that the categorical product also admits a right \(S\)-structure.

Furthermore by hom into bimodules bifunctor the bimodule homomorphisms \(\partial^N\) and module homomorphisms \(\partial^{M}\) are \(S\)-module homomorphisms.
