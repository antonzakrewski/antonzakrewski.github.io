galois group acts transitive on roots and irreducible separable polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(f \in K[X]\) be a nonconstant separable polynomial and \(\mathrm{Gal}(f)\) the galois group.

TFAE:

  1. \(\mathrm{Gal}(f)\) acts transitively on the roots (cf. galois group acts on roots) in an algebraic closure \(\overline{K}\)
  2. \(f\) is irreducible

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Proof by contraposition: Suppose \(f\) is reducible, then there exist \(g,h \in K[X]\) with \(\mathrm{deg}(g), \mathrm{deg}(h) \geq 1\) with

\begin{align*} f = g \cdot h \end{align*}

furthermore, since \(f\) is separable, the roots of \(g\) and \(h\) are disjoint.

Therefore by root under A-Algebra homomorphisms, it follows, that for a root \(x\) of \(g\), \(\sigma(x) \not\in \{\text{roots of }h\}\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
