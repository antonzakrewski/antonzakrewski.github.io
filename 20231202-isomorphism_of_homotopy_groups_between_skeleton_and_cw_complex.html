isomorphism of homotopy groups between skeleton and CW complex

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \emptyset, \mathrm{SK}_n(X))\) be a CW-Complex and \(x \in \mathrm{sk}_0(X)\). Then the induced map for \(k < n\)

\begin{align*} \pi_k(\mathrm{SK}_n(X),x) \rightarrow \pi_k(X,x) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism on homotopy group

2.2. injection

using element of a homotopy group and map out of the sphere Suppose \(f,g: S^k \rightarrow X\) are pointed maps, such that

\begin{align*} \iota \circ f, \iota \circ g: S^k \rightarrow X \end{align*}

are the same element, i.e. homotopic rel \(*\)

Then there exists a pointed homotopy

\begin{align*} H: [0,1] \times S^k \rightarrow X \end{align*}

As a product of a sphere with the interval is a cw complex with

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Sk}_k(S^k \tiems [0,1] = S^k \times \{0,1\} \cup \{1\} \times [0,1] \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} H_{\vert [0,1] \times 1} = \mathrm{const} \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} H[\mathrm{Sk}_k([0,1] \times S^k) = \mathrm{Sk}_k(X) \end{align*}

By cellular approximation, \(H\) is homotopic relative to \(S^k \times \{0,1\} \cup \{1\} \times [0,1]\) to a cellular map \(H'\) with

\begin{align*} H'[[0,1] \times S^k] = H'[\mathrm{Sk}_{k+1}([0,1] \times S^k) \subseteq \mathrm{Sk}_{k+1}(X) \end{align*}

Thus \(H'\) restricts naturally to \(\mathrm{Sk}_n(X) \supseteq \mathrm{Sk}_{k+1}(X)\) and therefore \(f,g\) are already homotopic.
