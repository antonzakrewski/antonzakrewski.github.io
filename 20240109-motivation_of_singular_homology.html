Motivation of singular homology

1. Aspects

Should be a functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{H}_n(-): \mathrm{Top} \rightarrow \mathrm{Ab} \end{align*}

such that

  1. \(\mathrm{H}_n(\emptyset) = 0 \forall n \in \mathbb{Z}\)
\begin{align*} \mathrm{H}_n(*) = \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z}z & \mbox{if } n = 0 \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}
  1. \(\mathrm{H}_n\) is a homotopy invariant functor
  2. for an arbitrary family of topological spaces, the canonical morphism
\begin{align*} \coprod_{i \in I} \mathrm{H}_n(X_i) \cong H_n(\coprod X_i) \end{align*}
\begin{align*} H_n(X \vee Y) \cong H_n(X) \coprod H_n(Y) \end{align*}
\begin{align*} \mathrm{H}_n(S^k) \cong \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z} & \mbox{if } n = 0, k \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}
