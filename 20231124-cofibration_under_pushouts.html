cofibration mirrored under pushouts

1. Proposition

Let \(X,X',A,A'\) be topological spaces and \(\iota: A \rightarrow X\) be a cofibration. Then for a pushout

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\iota}& A \arrow[]{d}{\iota'} \\ X \arrow[]{r}{}& X' \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

it follows, that \(\iota'\) is also a cofibration

2. Proof

using: cofibration and currying lift Suppose there exists a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A' \arrow[]{d}{\iota'} \arrow[]{r}{H'}& \mathrm{Map}([0,1], Y) \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{ev}_0}\\ X' \arrow[]{r}{f}& Y \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\iota}& A \arrow[]{d}{\iota'} \arrow[]{r}{H'}& \mathrm{Map}([0,1], Y) \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{ev}_0}\\ X \arrow[]{r}{}& X' \arrow[]{r}{f}& Y \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and since \(\iota: A \rightarrow X\) is a cofibration, there exists a lift

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\iota} & A' \arrow[]{r}{H'}& \mathrm{Map}([0,1], Y) \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{ev}_0}\\ X \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[dashed]{rru}{\overline{H}} & X' \arrow[]{r}{f} & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Thus we get a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{} & A' \arrow[]{d}{\iota'} \arrow[bend left]{rdd}{H'} & \\ X \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[bend right]{rrd}{\overline{H}} & X' \arrow[dashed]{rd}{\exists \overline{H}'}& \\ & & \mathrm{Map}([0,1],Y) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and by universal property there exists a unique map

\begin{align*} \overline{H}': X' \rightarrow \mathrm{Map}([0,1],Y) \end{align*}

Furthermore, by construction

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A' \arrow[]{d}{\iota'} \arrow[]{r}{H'}& \mathrm{Map}([0,1], Y) \\ X' \arrow[]{ru}{\overline{H}'} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes, as well as

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \arrow[bend left]{rr}{} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[bend right]{rrd}{} & X' \arrow[]{r}{f} \arrow[]{rd}{} & Y \\ & & \mathrm{Map}([0,1],Y) \arrow[]{u}{\mathrm{ev}_0}\\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
