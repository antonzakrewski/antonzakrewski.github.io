based product and pointed homotopic maps

1. Proposition

Let \((X_1,x_1), (X_2,x_2), (Y_1,y_1), (Y_2,y_2)\) be based spaces and \(f,f': X_1 \rightarrow X_2\) and \(g,g': Y_1 \rightarrow Y_2\) be based pointed homotopic maps.

Then for the product topology and the maps

\begin{align*} (f \times g), (f' \times g'): X_1 \times Y_1 \rightarrow X_2 \times Y_2 \end{align*}

it follows, that

\begin{align*} f \times g \sim f' \times g' \end{align*}

are pointed homotopies

2. Proof

follows from the construction of product and homotopic maps, as the base point is preserved.
