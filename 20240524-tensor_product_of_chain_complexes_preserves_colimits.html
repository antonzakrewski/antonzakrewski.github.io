tensor product of chain complexes preserves colimits

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod. Then the tensor product of chain complexes functor

\begin{align*} (- \otimes_R -): \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}) \times \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{ModR}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{Ab})d \end{align*}

preserves colimits

2. Proof

Let \(\mathrm{colim} A^i\) be a colimit of chain complexes. Then it is given by

\begin{align*} (\mathrm{colim} A^i)_n \end{align*}

(cf. colimit of chain complexes)

Hence it also suffices to show that \(- \otimes_{R} -\) preserves colimits degreewise.

Using the definition we obtain

\begin{align*} ( \mathrm{colim}_i A^i \otimes_R \mathrm{colim}_j B^j)_n \coloneqq \bigoplus_{p + q = n} (\mathrm{colim}_i A^i)_p \otimes_R (\mathrm{colim}_j B^j)_q \end{align*}

as the tensor product preserves colimits and colimits commute with colimits we get

\begin{align*} \bigoplus_{p + q = n} (\mathrm{colim}_i A^i)_p \otimes_R (\mathrm{colim}_j B^j)_q =& \bigoplus_{p + q = n} \mathrm{colim}_i \mathrm{colim}_j (A^i \otimes_R B^j) \\ =& \mathrm{colim}_{i} \mathrm{colim}_j (\bigoplus_{p + q =n} A^i \otimes_R B^j) \end{align*}
