connected codomain and surjective covering map

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be topological spaces and \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map. Suppose \(B\) is connected. Then \(p\) is surjective or \(p\) is the empty covering map

2. Proof

Suppose w.l.o.g. \(p\) is not the empty covering map.

Let \(U_i\) be the open covering from the covering map. Then we divide those \(U_i\) into

  1. \(U_e\) defined as union of \(p^{-1}[U_i] \cong \emptyset \times U_i = \emptyset\)
  2. \(U_n\) defined as union of \(p^{-1}[U_i] \cong F \times U_i\) for \(\vert F \vert \geq 1\).

This induces an disjoint covering \(U_e \sqcup U_n\):

  1. covering as \(U_i\) is already a covering
  2. disjoint, as \(\emptyset = p^{-1}[U_i \cap U_j] = U_j \times F\) is a contradiction

But since \(E \neq \emptyset\), there exists an \(z \in E\) and hence also an \(p(z) \in U_j\) for some \(j\). Therefore, \(U_n \neq \emptyset\) and by connectedness, we conclude, that \(U_e = \emptyset\), hence \(U_n = B\).

Thus \(p\) is surjective
