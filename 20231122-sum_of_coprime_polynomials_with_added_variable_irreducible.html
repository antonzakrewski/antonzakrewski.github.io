sum of coprime polynomials with added variable irreducible

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f(X),g(X) \in K[X]\) be nonconstant, coprime elements. Then

\begin{align*} f(X) + g(X) \cdot Y \in K(Y)[X] \end{align*}

is irreducible over the quotient field \(K(Y)\)

2. Proof

Suppose \(g(X) = 0\), then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{gcd}(g(X), f(X)) = f(X) \in K[X]^{\times} = K \end{align*}

contradiction to \(f,g\) nonconstant.

Hence \(\mathrm{deg}_Y(f(X) + g(X) \cdot Y) = 1\)

Thus it follows, that

\begin{align*} f(X) + g(X) \cdot Y \in K(X)[Y] \end{align*}

is irreducible and as \(\mathrm{gcd}(f(X), g(X)) = 1\), we conclude, that

\begin{align*} f(X) + g(X)Y \in K[Y,X] \end{align*}

is irreducible, since it is also primitive

Hence it is also irreducible in

\begin{align*} f(X) + g(X) Y \in K(Y)(X) \end{align*}
