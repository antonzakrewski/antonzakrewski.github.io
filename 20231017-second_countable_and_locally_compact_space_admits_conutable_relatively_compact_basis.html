secondcountable and locally relatively compact space admits countable, relatively compact basis

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a secondcountable and locally relative compact space topological space. Then there exists a countable basis containing only relatively compact sets.

2. Proof

Let \(\mathcal{B}\) be a countable basis. Consider

\begin{align*} \mathcal{B}' \coloneqq \{O \in \mathcal{B} \vert \overline{O} \text{ compact} \} \subseteq \mathcal{B} \end{align*}

it is countable.

Suppose \(V \subseteq X\) is open. Then, since \(V\) is locally compact, for all \(x \in V\), there exist relatively compact, open neighbourhoods \(V_x\), w.l.o.g. \(V_x \subseteq V\) (cf. Intersection of a relatively compact set) Since \(\mathcal{B}\) is a basis, there exists \(U_{x,i} \in \mathcal{B}\) such that

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} U_{x,i} = V_x \end{align*}

Furthermore, subsets of a relatively compact set are relatively compact set, hence \(U_{x,i} \in \mathcal{B}'\)
