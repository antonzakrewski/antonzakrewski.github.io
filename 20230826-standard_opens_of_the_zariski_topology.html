standard opens of the zariski topology

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{Spec}(R)\) the zariski spectrum equipped with the Zariski topology. Then

\begin{align*} D(f) = \{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(R) \vert f \not\in \mathfrak{p}\} \end{align*}

form a basis of open sets

2. Proof

2.1. open set

follows from Vanishing set and definition of closed sets

\begin{align*} D(f) = X \setminus V(f) \end{align*}

2.2. basis

2.2.1. intersection

follows from intersection of standard opens

2.2.2. subset

Let \(O = \mathrm{Spec}(A) \setminus V(\mathfrak{a})\) an open set and \(\mathfrak{p} \in O\) Then it follows that \(\mathfrak{a} \not\subseteq \mathfrak{p}\) and hence there exists an element \(f \in \mathfrak{a} \setminus \mathfrak{p}\).

Then consider

\begin{align*} D(f) = \mathrm{Spec}(A) \setminus V(f) \end{align*}

where \((f) \not\subseteq \mathfrak{p}\), therefore \(\mathfrak{p} \not \in V(f)\)

\(D(f)\) is by definition open and since

\begin{align*} (f) \subseteq \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

it follows by Vanishing set and order reversing that \(V(f) \supseteq V(\mathfrak{a})\) and furthermore by basic set theory

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(A) \setminus V(f) \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(A) \setminus V(\mathfrak{a}) \end{align*}
