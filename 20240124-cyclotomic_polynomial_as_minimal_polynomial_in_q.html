cyclotomic polynomial as minimal polynomial in Q

1. Proposition

Let \(\varphi_n \in \overline{\mathbb{Q}}[X]\) be the cyclotomic polynomial and \(\zeta\) a primitive root of unity. Then \(\varphi_n\) is the minimal polynomial of a primitive root of unity \(\zeta_i\)

2. Proof

Let \(f_{\zeta_i}\) be the minimal polynomial of an algebraic element.

Note that \(\zeta_i\) is a root of \(X^n - 1\), hence

\begin{align*} f_{\zeta_i} \mid X^{n} - 1 \end{align*}

Therefore, there exists an \(h \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} f_{\zeta_i} \cdot h = X^n - 1 \end{align*}

by gauss lemma for monic polynomials it follows, since \(h\) is monic, that

\begin{align*} h, f_{\zeta_i} \in \mathbb{Z}[X] \end{align*}

Let \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) be a prime number with \mathrm{gcd}(p,n) = 1$.

Suppose \(\zeta_i^p\) is not a root of \(f_{\zeta_i}\). Then since \((\zeta_i)^{p}^n - 1 = ((\zeta_i)^n)^p - 1 = 1^p - 1 = 0\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} h(\zeta_i^p) = 0 \end{align*}

and thus \(\zeta_i\) is a root of \(h(X^p)\)

Hence it follows, that

\begin{align*} f \mid h(X^p) \end{align*}

and thus there exists a polynomial \(g\) such that

\begin{align*} h(X^p) = f \cdot g \end{align*}

Again aplying the gauss lemma we conclude, that \(g\) is monic with \(g \in \mathbb{Z}[X]\)

Consider the induced ring-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \pi: \mathbb{Z}[X] \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/p \mathbb{Z}[X] \end{align*}

then applying Freshman's Dream

\begin{align*} \pi_p(f) \cdot \pi_p(g) =& \pi_p(h(X^p)) \\ =& \pi_p(h(X)^p) \\ =& \pi_p(h(X))^p \end{align*}
