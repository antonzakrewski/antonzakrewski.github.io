one point compactification may refine the topology

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(X^+\) the canonical one point compactification. Then \(X'\) as subspace of \(X^+\) is a finer topology and not necessary homeomorphic

2. Proof

2.1. finer

follows from the construction, as \(\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathcal{T}^+\)

2.2. in general not homeo

consider a space, where \(A \subseteq X\) is compact but not closed (e.g. a finite set with the indiscrete topology). Then by construction, \(X^+ \setminus A\) is open in \(X^+\), hence \(A\) is closed in \(X^+\) and thus also in \(X\).
