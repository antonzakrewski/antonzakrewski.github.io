finite dimensional vector space and artinian module

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(V\) a vector space

TFAE:

  1. \(V\) is finite dimensional
  2. \(V\) is an Artinian module

2. Proof

special case of

  • finitely generated module over an artinian ring as artinian module

Alternatively

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

  • finitely generated module over an artinian ring as artinian module
  • field as artinian ring

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(V\) is artinian, but not finite dimensional Choose a basis \(\{v_i\}\), choose a countable subset \(v_n\) Then consider the chain

\begin{align*} \langle \{v_i \} \setminus \{v_1\}\rangle \supseteq \langle \{v_i \} \setminus \{v_1, v_2\}\rangle \supseteq \langle \{v_i \} \setminus \{v_1,v_2,v_3\}\rangle \supseteq \end{align*}

which does not stabilize, contradiction
