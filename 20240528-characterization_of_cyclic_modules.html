characterization of cyclic modules

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module

TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is a cyclic module
  2. there exists an algebraic module-epimorphism
\begin{align*} \varphi: A \twoheadrightarrow M \end{align*}
  1. \(M \cong A/\mathfrak{a}\) is a quotient module

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Define

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrow& M \\ 1 \mapsto& a \\ \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

follows from

  • Kernel of a module homomorphism as submodule
  • Homomorphiesatz für Moduln

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from
