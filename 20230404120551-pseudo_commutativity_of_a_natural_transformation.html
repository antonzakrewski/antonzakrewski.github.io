pseudocommutativity of a natural transformation

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}\) be functors and \(\eta: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}\) a natural transformation Then \(\eta\) pseudo-commutes with \(\mathcal{F}\) and \(\mathcal{G}\), i.e. for \(f: A \rightarrow B\)

\begin{align*} \theta(B) \circ \mathcal{F}(f) =& \mathcal{G}(f) \circ \theta(A) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Definition of naturality, where

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{d}{f} & & \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{F}(f)} \arrow[]{r}{\eta(A)} & \mathcal{G}(A) \arrow[]{d}{\mathcal{G}(f)}\\ B & & \mathcal{F}(B) \arrow[]{r}{\eta(B)} & \mathcal{G}(B) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
