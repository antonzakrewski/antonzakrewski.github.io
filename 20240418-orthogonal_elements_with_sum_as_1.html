orthogonal elements with sum as 1

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring. TFAE:

  1. \(A \simeq A_1 \times ... \times A_n\) for commutative rings \(A_i\)
  2. there exist a finite set of idempotent, orthogonal elements \(e_i\) with
\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} e_i = 1 \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption there exists a ring-homomorphism \(\varphi: A \rightarrow A_1 \times ... \times A_n\). Then consider \((1,...,0), (0,1,...,0), ..., (0,0,...,1)\). Those elements are idempotent and a orthogonal set (cf. orthogonal set under ring homomorphism) and by idempotent elements under ring homomorphisms it follows that \(\varphi(1,...,0),...,\varphi(0,...,1)\) is both idempotent and orthogonal. Furthermore

\begin{align*} 1 =& \varphi((1,1,1,..,1) \\ =& \varphi(1,0...,0) + ... + (0,...,1)) \\ =& \varphi(1,...,0) + ... + \varphi(0,...,1) \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(A_i \coloneqq e_i A\). Then it follows that the ring-homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: A \rightarrow& e_1A \times ... \times e_nA \\ a \mapsto& (a e_1,...,a e_n) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism with inverse given by

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1}: e_1A \times ... \times e_nA \rightarrow A \\ (a_1 e_1,...,a_n e_n) \mapsto& \sum a_i e_i \end{align*}
