finitely generated ideal as principal ideal in a boolean ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a Boolean ring, \(I\) a finitely generated ideal. Then \(I\) is a principal ideal

2. Proof

Proof by induction

2.1. induction basis

follows for \(n = 1\) by definition

2.2. induction step

Consider \((a_1,...,a_n,a_{n+1})\). Byinduction assumption \((a_1,...,a_n)\) is generated by an element \(a\).

Then consider

\begin{align*} a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1} \end{align*}

by multiplication

\begin{align*} (a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1}) \cdot (1 + a) =& a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1} + a^2 \cdot a_{n+1} + a^2 + a \cdot a_{n+1} \\ =& a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1} + a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a \cdot a_{n+1} \\ =& a \cdot a_{n+1} + a_{n+1} \end{align*}

(cf. boolean ring and additive inverse)

This shows that

\begin{align*} (a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1}) + (a \cdot a_{n+1} + a_{n+1}) =& a \end{align*}

hence \(a \in (a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1})\)

By symmetry of the argument we also conclude that \(a_{n+1} \in (a \cdot a_{n+1} + a + a_{n+1})\)
