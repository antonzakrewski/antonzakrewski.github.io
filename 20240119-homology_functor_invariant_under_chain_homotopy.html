Homology functor invariant under chain homotopy

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(M_n,M_n' \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A}))\) are chain complexes and \(f,g: M_n \rightarrow M_n'\) are chain homotopic chain maps.

Then for the homology functor, it holds, that

\begin{align*} H_n(f) = H_n(g) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. RMod

Let \([x] \in H_n(M)\) for \(x \in \mathrm{ker}(d_n)\).

Then it holds, that \(h_{n-1}d_n(x) = h_{n-1}(0) = 0\) and furthermore

\begin{align*} 0 =& g_n(x) - f_n(x) + d_{n+1}h_n(x) + h_{n-1} d_n(x) \\ f_n(x) =& g_n(x) + d_{n+1}h_n(x) \\ \end{align*}

since \(d_n(x) = 0\), as \(x \in \mathrm{ker}(d_n)\)

here \(d_{n+1}h_n(x) \in \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1})\), thus

\begin{align*} [f_n(x)] = [g_n(x)] \end{align*}
