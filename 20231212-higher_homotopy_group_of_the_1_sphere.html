higher homotopy group of the 1-sphere

1. Proposition

Given the 1-sphere \(S^1\), the homotopy group for \(n \geq 2\) is the zero group

\begin{align*} \pi_n(S^1, 1) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

note, that the 1-sphere is a a quotient of the covering like group action

\begin{align*} \mathrm{trans}: \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{R} \rightarrow& \mathbb{R} \\ n,x \mapsto& n + x \end{align*}

hence by orbital projection of a free action as covering map and covering-like, we get a covering map

\begin{align*} \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} \cong S^1 \end{align*}

Thus since covering maps on higher homotopy groups induces isomorphisms, we get

\begin{align*} \pi_n(\mathbb{R},1) \cong \pi_n(S^1,1) \end{align*}

furthermore, the homotopy group functor is homotopy invariant, hence since the euclidean space is contractible space and contractible spaces have trivial homotopy group we get

\begin{align*} 0 \cong \pi_n(*,*) \cong \pi_n(\mathbb{R}, 1) \cong \pi_n(S^1,1) \end{align*}
