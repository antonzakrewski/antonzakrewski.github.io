units in Z/pn Z

1. Proposition

Let \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) be a prime number, then the units of \(\mathbb{Z}/ p^n \mathbb{Z}\) are

\begin{align*} \left(\mathbb{Z}/ p^n \mathbb{Z} \right)^{\times} =& \{np + k \vert n \in \mathbb{N}, k \in \{1,...,p-1\}\} \\ =& \mathbb{Z} / p^n \mathbb{Z} \setminus \{0,p,2p,...,(p^{n-1}-1)p\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a)

Note that \(0,...,(p^{n-1}-1) \cdot p\) are nilpotent and thus zero divisors, hence not a unit (cf. unit is not a zero divisor)

2.2. b)

Let \(np + k\). Then \(k \in \mathbb{Z}/p \mathbb{Z}\) is a unit, hence there exists an \(l \in \{1,...,p-1\}\) such that \(l \cdot k = l'p + 1\). thus

\begin{align*} (np + k) \cdot l = p \cdot (nl + l') + 1 \end{align*}

where by Sum of a nilpotent and a unit as unit, this is a unit. Hence also \(np + k\) by multiplication with \(l \cdot (...)^{-1}\).
