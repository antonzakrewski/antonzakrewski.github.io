simple, coprime field extensions independent

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K(a), K(b)\) be simple, algebraic field extensions for \(a,b \in L\) in some field extension \(L/K\), such that f

\begin{align*} \mathrm{gcd}(m,n) = 1 \end{align*}

for

\begin{align*} m \coloneqq& [K(a), K] \\ n \coloneqq& [K(b), K] \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} [K(a,b) : K] = m \cdot n \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. $[K(a,b)] : K] ≤ m ⋅ n$d

Since \([K(a,b) : K] = [K(a, b) : K(a)] \cdot [K(b) : K]\) with \([K(a,b) : K(a)] \leq [K(b), K]\), as the maximal linear independence doesn't change under field extensions.

2.2.

Suppose

\begin{align*} [K(a,b) : K] = mn - d \end{align*}

Then we conclude, that by Gradsatz

\begin{align*} [K(a,b) : K(a)] [K(a) : K] = [K(a,b) : K] = [K(a,b) : K(b)] [K(b) : K] \\ (n - k) \cdot m = mn - d = (m - k') \cdot n \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} k \cdot m = d = k' \cdot n \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} m,n \mid d \end{align*}

since \(m,n\) are coprime, it follows, that \(d \geq \mathrm{lcd}(m,n) = mn\) or \(d = 0\). But \(d \geq m \cdot n\) is a contradiction, as \([K(a,b) : K] \geq [K(a) : K]\)
