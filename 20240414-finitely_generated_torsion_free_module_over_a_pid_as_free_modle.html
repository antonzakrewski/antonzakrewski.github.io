finitely generated, torsion free module over a PID as free module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain and \(M\) a finitely generated torsion-free module. Then \(M \simeq R^k\) is free for some \(k \in \mathbb{N}\)

2. Proof

By assumption there exist \(m_1,...,m_n\) such that \(M = \langle m_1,...,m_n\rangle\).

Furthermore \(\langle m_1\rangle \simeq R\) is free

Choose the maximal \(s \geq 1\) such that after renumbering

\begin{align*} F = \langle m_1,...,m_s\rangle \simeq R^s \end{align*}

Then for \(s < j\) and \(m_j\) we get

\begin{align*} \cap F \neq \{0\} \end{align*}

as otherwise $ ⟨ m1,…,ms,mj⟩ ≅ F ⊕ ⟨ mj⟩ ≅ Rs+1$, contradiction to maximality of \(s\).

Therefore there exists an \(r_j \neq 0\) with \(r_j \cdot m_j \in F\).

Let

\begin{align*} r = \prod_{j > s} r_j \end{align*}

Then \(r \cdot m_j \in F\) for arbitrary \(j > s\). Therefore

\begin{align*} rM = \{r \cdot m \vert m \in M\} \subseteq M \end{align*}

is a submodule of a free module (cf. submodule of a finitely generated free module over a PID as free module)

Here we get

\begin{align*} rM = \mathrm{im}(r \cdot -) \end{align*}

for the scalar multiplication as endomorphism and the short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(r \cdot - ) \arrow[]{r}{} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & rM \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ & & & R^k \arrow[]{u}{\simeq}& \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where by splitting lemma for modules

\begin{align*} M \simeq& \mathrm{ker}(r \cdot -) \oplus rM \\ \simeq& 0 \oplus rM \\ \simeq& rM \end{align*}

Here \(\mathrm{ker}(r \cdot -) \simeq 0\) is trivial, as \(r \cdot -\) is a monomorphism since \(M\) is torsion free
