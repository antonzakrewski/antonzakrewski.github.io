composition of left adjoints as left adjoint

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{L}_1: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D},\mathcal{L}_2: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{E}\) left adjoints with \(\mathcal{R}_1,\mathcal{R}_2\) as right adjoints Then the composition

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_2 \circ \mathcal{L}_1 \end{align*}

is also a left adjoint for \(\mathcal{R}_2 \circ \mathcal{R}_1\), even canonically if there exist canonical natural isomorphisms between \(\mathcal{L}_1 \vdash \mathcal{R}_1, \mathcal{L}_2 \vdash \mathcal{R}_2\)

2. Proof

follows from composition of natural transformations and composition of isomorphisms as isomorphism in a (bigger) functor category (?), as by assumption

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{E}}(\mathcal{L}_2 \circ \mathcal{L}_1(-), -) \cong& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{D}}(\mathcal{L}_1(-), \mathcal{R}_2(-)) \\ \cong& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, \mathcal{R}_2 \circ \mathcal{R}_1(-)) \end{align*}
