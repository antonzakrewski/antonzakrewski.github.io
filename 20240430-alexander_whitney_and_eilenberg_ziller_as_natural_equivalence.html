alexander whitney and eilenberg-zilber chain homotopic to the identity

1. Proposition

Given the alexander-whitney transformation and the eilenberg-zilber transformation, their composition

\begin{align*} \mathrm{AW} \circ \mathrm{EZ}(-) \simeq& \mathrm{id}(-) \\ \mathrm{EZ} \circ \mathrm{AW}(-) \simeq& \mathrm{id}(-) \end{align*}

is objectwise chain homotopic to the identity morphism

2. Proof

using equivalence class of chain maps from the tensored singular chain complexes to itself the composition

\begin{align*} \mathrm{EZ} \circ \mathrm{EZ} \end{align*}

corresponds to an element \(\mathbb{Z}\), which is given by evaluation at \(\emptyset\)

Here by construction both AW and EZ map \(1\) to \(1\).

Furthermore the \(\mathrm{id}\) also maps \(1\) to \(1\), hence they lie in the same equivalence class
