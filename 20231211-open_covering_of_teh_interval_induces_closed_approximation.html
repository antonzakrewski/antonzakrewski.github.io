open covering of the interval induces closed decomposition

1. Proposition

Let \([0,1]\) be the unit interval and \(U_i\) an open cover. Then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} [0,1] = [\frac{0}{n}, \frac{1}{n}] \cup [\frac{1}{n}, \frac{2}{n}] \cup ... \cup [\frac{n-1}{n}, \frac{n}{n}] \end{align*}

with \([ \frac{0}{n}, \frac{1}{n}] \subseteq U_i\) for some \(i \in I\)

2. Proof

corollary of lebesgue's number lemma and archimedian axiom: By assumpiton, there exists an \(\delta > 0\). Then choosing an sufficiently large \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) with \(\frac{1}{n} < \delta\) results in

\begin{align*} \mathrm{diam}([\frac{k}{n}, \frac{k+1}{n}]) = \frac{1}{n} < \delta \end{align*}

and thus \([\frac{k}{n}, \frac{k+1}{n}] \subseteq U_i\) for some \(i \in I\)
