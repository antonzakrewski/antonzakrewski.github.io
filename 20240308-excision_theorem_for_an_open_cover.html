excision theorem for an open cover

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(X_1,X_2 \subseteq X\) be subspaces with \(\mathrm{int}(X_1) \cup \mathrm{int}(X_2) = X\). Then the induced map by

\begin{align*} \iota: (X_1,X_1 \cap X_2) \rightarrow (X,X_2) \end{align*}

is an isomorphism on homology

\begin{align*} H_n(X_1,X_1 \cap X_2) \simeq H_n(X,X_2) \end{align*}

2. Proof

excision of homology

2.1. excision \(\Rightarrow\) open cover

Let \(A = X_2\) and \(U = X \setminus X_1\). Then it follows, that \(U = X \setminus X_1 \subseteq X \setminus \mathrm{int}(X_1)\), where \(X \setminus \mathrm{int}(X_1)\) is closed. Furthermore, \(\mathrm{int}(X_2) \supseteq X \setminus \mathrm{int}(X_1)\), as \(\mathrm{int}(X_1) \cup \mathrm{int}(X_2)\) covers \(X\) Then it follows, that

\begin{align*} H_n(X_1, X_2 \cap X_1) =& H_n(X \setminus (X \setminus X_1), X_2 \setminus (X \setminus X_1)) \simeq H_n(X, X_2) \end{align*}
