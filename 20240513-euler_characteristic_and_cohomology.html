euler characteristic and cohomology

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a homologically finite topological space. Then the homological euler characteristic is given by

\begin{align*} \chi(X) =& \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \mathrm{rk}(H^n(X)) \\ =& \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \mathrm{dim}_{\mathbb{Q}}(H^n(X;\mathbb{Q})) \end{align*}

see:

  • singular cohomology
  • rank of an abelian group
  • dimension

2. Proof

2.1. a)

follows from the universal coefficient Theorem for cohomology, where

  1. ext 1 of two finitely generated abelian groups has always rank \(0\)
  2. contravariant hom preserves finite rank of an abelian group

2.2. b)

see:

  • rank over an integral domain and tensor product
  • contravariant hom preserves finite rank of an abelian group
  • again universal coefficient theorem
