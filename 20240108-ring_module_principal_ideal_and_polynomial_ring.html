ring module principal ideal and polynomial ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(f \in A[X]\) a polynomial and \(\alpha \in A\). Then

\begin{align*} R[X]/(f,X - \alpha) \simeq R/(f(\alpha)) \end{align*}

are canonically isomorphic

2. Proof

Consider the ring-homomorphisms

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R[X] \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{r}{\mathrm{eval}_{\alpha}} & R \arrow[twoheadrightarrow]{r}{\pi} & R/(f(\alpha)) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then the composition is surjective, as each morphism is surjective.

We show, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\pi \circ \mathrm{eval}_{\alpha}) = (f(\alpha), X - \alpha) \end{align*}

2.1. \(\supseteq\)

Follows from

\begin{align*} \mathrm{eval}_{\alpha}(X - \alpha) =& 0 \\ \pi (f(\alpha)) =& 0 \end{align*}

2.2. \(\subseteq\)

Suppose \(g \in \mathrm{ker}(\pi \circ \mathrm{eval}_{\alpha})\), then by polynomial division for a leading unit there exists a polynomial \(h \in R[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} g = h \cdot (X - \alpha)^d + c \end{align*}

where \(c\) has smaller degree than \((X - \alpha)\).

Hence \(c\) is constant and since by assumption,

\begin{align*} g(\alpha) = 0 = h(\alpha) \cdot (X - \alpha)^d + c(\alpha) \end{align*}

it follows, that \(c(\alpha) = 0 \in R/(f(\alpha))\), showing \(c \in (f(\alpha))\)

Hence showing

\begin{align*} g \in (X - \alpha, f(\alpha)) \end{align*}

Thus by Homomorphiesatz für Ringe, we have shown, that

\begin{align*} R[X]/(f(\alpha),X - \alpha) \simeq R/(f(\alpha)) \end{align*}
