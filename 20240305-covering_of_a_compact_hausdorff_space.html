covering of a compact hausdorff space

1. Proposition

Let \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map, where \(B\) is compact and hausdorff.

TFAE:

  1. \(E\) is compact and hausdorff
  2. \(p^{-1}(b)\) is finite for \(b \in B\).

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(p^{-1}[b]\) is infinite, then choose a trivializing cover \(U_i\). Choose an open set \(U_i\) with \(b \in U_i\). Then we get

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U_i] \simeq \coprod_{j \mathrm{infinite}} U_i^j \end{align*}

For \(b' \neq b\) choose an open set \(U_{b'}\) with \(b \not\in U_{b'}\). Then

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U_{b'}], U_i^j \end{align*}

is an open cover of \(E\). Nonetheless, by construction each \(U_i^j\) is necessary for a covering, as \(U_i^j, U_i^{j'}\) are disjoint and for \(e \in p^{-1}[b]\) it holds, that \(e \not\in p^{-1}[U_{b'}]\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.2. compact

covering of a compact space with finite fibres and maximal number of points in a fibre

Choose a trivializing cover \(U_i\) of \(B\). By compactness of \(B\) we may assume, that \(U_i\) is finite.

Then by shrinking of a finite open cover for a t4 space we get an open covering \(V_i \subseteq O_i\) with \(\overline{V_i} \subseteq O_i\). As \(\overline{V_i}\) is a closed subset, it is compact (cf. closed subset of a compact space as compact set) Furthermore, we get

\begin{align*} \coprod_{i=1}^n U_i \simeq p^{-1}[U_i] \end{align*}

and thus \(\coprod_{i=1}^n \overline{V_i} \simeq p^{-1}[\overline{V_i}]\)

Iterating over all \(V_1,..,V_n\) we get a cover \(\overline{V}_i^j\) of \(E\), as \(p^{-1}[V_i]\) is a cover of \(E\).

hence by finite union of compact sets, \(E\) is compact
