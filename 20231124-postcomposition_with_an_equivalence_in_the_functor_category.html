postcomposition with an equivalence in the functor category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{E}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) be an equivalence of categories

Then postcomposition

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{C}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{C}) \end{align*}

is an equivalence between functor categories

2. Proof

Let \(\mathcal{U}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be the equivalence inverse and

\begin{align*} \eta_{\mathcal{C}}: c \rightarrow \mathcal{U} \mathcal{F}c \epsilon_{\mathcal{D}}: \mathcal{FU} \rightarrow d \end{align*}

be the unit and counit

Then postcomposition

\begin{align*} \mathcal{U}^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{C}) \eta_{\mathcal{G}, a} \coloneqq \eta_{\mathcal{G}(a)}: \mathcal{G}a \Rightarrow \mathcal{UFG}a \end{align*}
