stereographic projection

1. Definition

Let \(S^n\) be the n-sphere and \(N = (0,...,1) \in S^{n}\) the North Pole (resp. south pole). Then the stereographic projection is defined as continuous map

\begin{align*} \varphi_N: S^n \setminus N \rightarrow& \mathbb{R}^n \times \{0\} \\ (\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n) \mapsto& (0,...,1) + \frac{1}{1 - \alpha_n} (\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n - 1) \end{align*}

2. Proofsketch

multiplication and addition of continous maps

note that \(\alpha_n < 1\), hence \(\frac{1}{1 - \alpha_n}\) is continuous
