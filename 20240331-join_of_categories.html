join of categories

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories. Then the join of \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\) is defined as category containing

  1. \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C} * \mathcal{D}) = \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C}) * \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D})\) as objects
  2. the hom-set
\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C} * \mathcal{D}}(X,Y) =& \begin{cases} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,Y) & \mbox{if } X,Y \in \mathcal{C} \\ \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,Y) & \mbox{if } X,Y \in \mathcal{D} \\ \{*\} & \mbox{if } X \in \mathcal{C}, y \in \mathcal{D} \\ \emptyset & \mbox{if } x \in \mathcal{D}, y \in \mathcal{C} \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}
