classification of finitely generated modules over a pid

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain and \(M\) a finitely generated module. Then there exists a \(k \in \mathbb{N}_0\), irreducible \(p_1,...,p_r\) and natural numbers \(n(i,1) \geq ... \geq n(i,s_i) \geq 1\) for \(i = 1,...,r\) such that

\begin{align*} M \simeq R^k \oplus T_{p_i}(M) \oplus ... \oplus T_{p_r}(M) \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} T_{p_i}(M) \simeq R/(p_i^{n(i,1)}) \oplus ... \oplus R/(p_i^{n(i,s_i)}) \end{align*}

and \(k,r,n(i,s_i)\) and \(T_{p_i}(M)\) are unique

2. Proof

using finitely generated module over a PID as direct sum of free and torsion module we get

\begin{align*} M \simeq R^k \oplus T(M) \end{align*}

Thus using torsion module as sum over pi torsion modules for a finitely generated module over a PID we get

\begin{align*} T(M) \simeq \bigoplus T_{p_i}(M) \end{align*}

where by finitely generated pi torsionmodule and decomposition we conclude

\begin{align*} T_{p_i}(M) \simeq \bigoplus_{l} (R/p_i^{n(i,l)} \end{align*}
