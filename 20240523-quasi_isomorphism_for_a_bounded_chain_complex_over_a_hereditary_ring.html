quasi isomorphism for a bounded below chain complex over a hereditary ring for a chain complex of projectives

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a hereditary ring, \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod and \(C\) bounded below chain complex in \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod})\). Then there exists a bounded below chain complex of projectives \(P\) and quasi-isomorphisms

\begin{align*} P \overset{\cong}{\rightarrow}& C \\ P \overset{\cong}{\rightarrow}& H(C) \end{align*}

where \(H(C)\) is the chain complex of homology

2. Proof

using the proof of quasi isomorphism for a chain complex over a hereditary ring we may choose

\begin{align*} p_n: P_n = 0 \twoheadrightarrow H_n(C) \end{align*}

for allmost all negative numbers.

Then as the chain complex is given degreewise as

\begin{align*} Q_n \oplus P_{n+1} \end{align*}

where \(Q_n = \mathrm{ker}(p_n)\) and hence again \(0\) for almost all negative numbers it follows that we may construct \(P\) as bounded below chain complex
