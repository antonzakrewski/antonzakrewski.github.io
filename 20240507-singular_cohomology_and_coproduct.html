singular cohomology dualizes coproducts to products

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a group, then the singular cohomology functor dualizes coproducts to products

\begin{align*} H^n(\coprod (-,-),M) \simeq \prod H^n((-,-),M) \end{align*}

2. Proof

by singular homology preserves coproducts (?)

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(\coprod_{i \in I}(X_i,A_i)) \simeq \bigoplus_{i \in I} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X_i,A_i) \end{align*}

hence after applying the

\begin{align*} C_{\mathrm{sing}}(\coprod (X_i,Y_i);M) =& \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\coprod C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X_i;Y_i), M[0]) \\ \simeq& \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(\bigoplus C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X_i,Y),M[0]) \simeq& \prod \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X_i;Y_i)) \\ \simeq \prod C_{\mathrm{sing}}(Y;M) \end{align*}

as category RMod opp as AB4 category using Fernbahnhof theorem for exact functors we get

\begin{align*} H_n(\prod C_{\mathrm{sing}}(Y;M)) \simeq& \prod H_n(C_{\mathrm{sing}}(Y;M)) \end{align*}
