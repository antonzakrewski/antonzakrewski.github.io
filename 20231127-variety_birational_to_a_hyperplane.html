variety birational to a hyperplane

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(V\) a variety. Then there exist an affine hyperplane \(P\) and a birational map

\begin{align*} f: P \rightarrow V \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. affine case, characteristic \(0\)

Let \(X \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\).

Then the function field of \(K(X)\) is a (?) with \(x_1,...,x_n \in K[X]\)

Let \(d = \mathrm{dim}(X)\) by Noether Normalisation, we get a transcendental field extension

\begin{align*} L \coloneqq K(t_1,...,t_d) \end{align*}

and a finite algebraic field extension

\begin{align*} K(X) = K(t_1,...,t_d)(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_k) \end{align*}

by <Satz vom primitiven Element> and characteristic \(0\), we may assume, that

\begin{align*} K(X) = K(t_1,...,t_d)(\alpha) \end{align*}

Then \(\alpha \in K(x)\) is a root of an irreducible polynomial \(f \in L[Y]\) (see: Minimalpolynom eines algebraischen Elements)

Multiplication of \(f\) with the common denominator results w.l.o.g. in

\begin{align*} f \in K[t_1,...,t_d][y] \end{align*}

By gcd of polynomials as non unit and quotient ring for a UFD, we conclude, that \(f\) is irreducible in \(K[t_1,...,t_n][Y]\) and w.l.o.g. primitive

Then let

\begin{align*} Y = V(f) \subseteq \mathbb{A}^{d+1} \end{align*}

By construction, we get

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Qout}(K[t_1,...,t_d][Y]/ \langle f\rangle) = K(Y) = K(X) = K(t_1,...,t_d)[y]/ \langle f\rangle \end{align*}

as field, where \(f\) is irreducible and hence \(\langle f\rangle\) maximal ideal (cf. irreducible and prime element in an UFD, prime ideal and maximal ideal for a Principal Ideal.

Hence by contravariant functor from affine varieties to the function fields as fully faithfull functor fully faithful functor as conservative functor

we conclude, that \(X,Y\) are birational varieties
