height of an ideal in a localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq A\) a multiplicatively closed subset and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq S^{-1}A\) a strict ideal Then the height of \(\mathfrak{a}\) is given by the heigth in the contraction

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{a}) = \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{a}^{c}) \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from the correspondence theorem for prime ideals and localization where by assumption \(\mathfrak{a} \cap S = \emptyset\) as otherwise \(\mathfrak{a} = A\)
