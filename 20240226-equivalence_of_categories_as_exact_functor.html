equivalence of categories as exact functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor, which forms an equivalence Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is an exact functor

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • adjointification of an equivalence
  • left adjoint as right exact functor
  • right adjoint as left exact functor
