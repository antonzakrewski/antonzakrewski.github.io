tor 1 functor for an integral domain and quotient

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(r \in R\) and \((r)\) the ideal and \(R/(r)\) the quotient module. Then for the Tor Functor it holds for a module \(N\)

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_1^R(R/(r), N) \simeq \{n \in N \vert r \cdot n = 0\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Consider the short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & R \arrow[]{r}{\cdot r} & R \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & R/(r) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

as a projective resolution

Then it follows that after tensoring with \(N\) this results in

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{}& N \arrow[]{r}{\cdot r} & N \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & R/(r) \otimes_R N \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and taking the first homology results in

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\cdot r)/\mathrm{coker}(0) \simeq \mathrm{ker}( \cdot r) \\ =& \{n \in N \vert r \cdot n 0 \} \end{align*}
