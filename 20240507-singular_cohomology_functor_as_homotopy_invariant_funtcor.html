singular cohomology functor as homotopy invariant functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{TopPairs}\) the category of topological pairs, \(M\) an abelian group.

Then the singular cohomology functor is a homotopy invariant functor

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} f,g: (X_1, Y_1) \rightarrow (X_2, Y_2) \end{align*}

be homotopic maps of pairs

Then this follows

  • the relative singular chain complex functor preserves homotopies (cf. relative singular chain complex functor preserves homotopies)
  • hom chain complex and preserved chain homotopy

hence the map

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}, g^{ \rightarrow}: \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C_{\mathrm{sing}}(X_2,Y_2),M[0]) \rightarrow \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}(C_{\mathrm{sing}}(X_1,Y_1),M[0]) \end{align*}

are chain homotopic.

Therefore

\begin{align*} H_n(f) = H_n(g) \end{align*}
