regular elements as multiplicatively closed set

1. Proposition

Let \(A \neq 0\) be a nonzero ring, \(A_{\mathrm{reg}\) the regular elements. Then \(A_{\mathrm{reg}}\) is multiplicatively closed

2. Proof

2.1. a)

note that \(1 \in A_{\mathrm{reg}} \supseteq A^{\times}\) (cf. units multiplicatively closed, unit is not a zero divisor)

2.2. b)

Let \(\alpha_1,\alpha_2 \in A_{\mathrm{reg}}\). Suppose \(\alpha_1 \cdot \alpha_2\) is not regular. Then there exists an element \(\beta \neq 0\) such that (w.l.o.g. from the <left/right zero divisor>) \(\beta \cdot \alpha_1 \cdot \alpha_2 = 0\) But then \(\beta \cdot \alpha_1 \neq 0\), as \(\alpha_1\) is regular and hence \(( \beta_1 \cdot \alpha_1) \cdot \alpha_2 = 0\), contradiction to \(\alpha_2\) regular.
