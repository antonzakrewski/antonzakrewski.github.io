localization and tensor functor commute

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M,N\) \(R\)-modules and \(S \subseteq R\) a multiplicatively closed subset. then the localization functor commutes with the tensoring functor

\begin{align*} (S^{-1}M) \otimes_{S^{-1}A} (S^{-1}N) \cong S^{-1}(M \otimes_A N) \end{align*}

2. Proof

using:

  • associativity of a tensor product for commutative rings
  • tensor product with the Ring isomorphic
  • tensor product with the localized ring and localization of a module
\begin{align*} S^{-}M \otimes_{S^{-1}A} S^{-1}N \cong& (M \otimes_A S^{-1}A) \otimes_{S^{-1}A}S^{-1}N \\ \cong& M \otimes_{A} S^{-1}N \\ \cong& M \otimes_{A} (N \otimes_A S^{-1}A) \\ \cong& (M \otimes_A N) \otimes_A S^{-1}A \\ \cong& S^{-1}(M \otimes_A N) \end{align*}
