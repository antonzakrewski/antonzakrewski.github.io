minimal polynomial of an algebraic element as power of the characteristic polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(L\) be a finite field extension and \(\alpha \in L\). Then for the characteristic polynomial \(\chi_{\alpha}\) of \(x \mapsto \alpha \cdot x\) and the minimal polynomial of an algebraic element \(f_{\alpha}\) there exists

\begin{align*} \chi_{\alpha} = (f_{\alpha})^k \end{align*}

for some \(k\)

2. Proof

By Gradlemma we conclude

\begin{align*} [L : K] =& [L : K(\alpha)] \cdot [K(\alpha) : K] =& k \cdot n \end{align*}

Let \(\beta_1,...,\beta_k\) be a basis of \(L\) over \(K(\alpha)\).

Then we get a basis of

\begin{align*} \alpha^0 \cdot \beta_1,...,\alpha^{n-1} \cdot \beta_k, \alpha^{0} \cdot \beta_2,...,\alpha^{n-1} \beta_2 , ... , \alpha^0 \cdot \beta_k,..., \alpha^{n-1} \cdot \beta_k \end{align*}

Thus the vectorspace-endomorphism is given by

\begin{align*} \begin{pNiceMatrix} f & 0 & ... & 0\\ 0 & f & & 0 \\ \vdots & & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & ... & f \\ \end{pNiceMatrix} \end{align*}

where \(f\) is the matrix

\begin{align*} \begin{pNiceMatrix} 0 & 0 & ... & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & ... & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & \ddots & & 0 \\ \vdots & & \ddots & & \vdots \\ 0 & ... & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ \end{pNiceMatrix} \in K^{n \times n} \end{align*}
