precomposition with a proper surjection as embedding between mapping spaces

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T}_X)\), \((Y, \mathcal{T}_Y)\) and \((Z, \mathcal{T}_Z)\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \twoheadrightarrow Y\) a proper quotient map. Then the precomposition \(f^{ \rightarrow}\) on the mapping space with the compact open topology is a continuous map

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow}: \mathrm{Map}(Y,Z) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Map}(X,Z) \\ g \mapsto g \circ f \end{align*}

is a topological embedding

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • precomposition with a surjection as injection
  • precomposition for the compact open topology
  • precomposition with a proper surjection as open map
