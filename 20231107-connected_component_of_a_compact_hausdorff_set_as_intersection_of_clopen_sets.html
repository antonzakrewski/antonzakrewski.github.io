connected component of a compact hausdorff set as intersection of clopen sets

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a compact hausdorff topological space. then for \(x\) the connected component \(C(x)\) is the intersection of clopen sets containing \(x\)

2. Proof

2.1. \(C(x) \subseteq\) intersection

connected component as subset of intersection of clopen sets

2.2. \(C(x) \supseteq\) intersection

Proof by contradiction

Let \(C(x)\) be the connected component and \(C = \bigcap_{i \in I} C_i\) the intersection of all clopen sets containing \(x\). Then \(C(x)\) and \(C\) are closed as the connected component is closed resp. intersection of closed sets is closed. Suppose \(C\) is not connected, then there exist two disjoint closed sets \(A_1,A_2\) in the subspace topology of \(C\). By closedness in the subspace topology, \(A_1,A_2\) are also closed in \(X\). Since compact hausdorff spaces are T4-Space, there exists disjoint open neighbourhoods \(V_1,V_2\). Furthermore, \(S \coloneqq X \setminus (V_1 \cup V_2)\) is closed, thus also compact (cf. closed subset of a compact space as compact set). Because \(X \setminus C_i\) is an open covering of \(S\), there exists finitely many \(C_1,...,C_n\) such that

\begin{align*} S \subseteq \bigcup_{i=1}^n X \setminus C_i \subseteq X \setminus C \end{align*}

Since the finite union of clopen sets is clopen, \(\bigcup_{i = 1}^n X \setminus C_i\) is thus clopen. Furthermore, by de morgan rules \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n C_i = X \setminus \bigcup_{i=1}^n X \setminus C_i\) is also clopen.

Also, we conclude that

\begin{align*} S \subseteq& \bigcup_{i=1}^n X \setminus C_i && \vert X \setminus \\ V_1 \sqcup V_2 \supseteq& \bigcap_{i=1}^n C_i \end{align*}

Thus, \(V_1 \cap \bigcap_{i=1}^n C_i, V_2 \cap \bigcap_{i=1}^n C_i\) are open and a disjoint open covering of the clopen set \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n C_i\) and therefore, \((V_1 \cap \bigcap_{i=1}^n C_i) \cup S\) is clopen (cf. disjoint open cover of a clopen set induces clopen sets)
