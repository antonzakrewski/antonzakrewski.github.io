locally compact space and covering like not in general free and proper

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a topological group, \(X\) a topological space and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a topological group action Suppose \(X\) is locally regular compact, \(G\) is proper and free.

Then \(\varphi\) is in general not covering-like

Note, here Hausdorffness is omitted

2. Proof

Consider the cyclic group \(\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\) as group and space \(X\), both with the indiscrete topology. Then \(\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\) has the indiscrete topology (cf. product of indiscrete spaces as indiscrete space) and by universal property of the indiscrete topology, the map

\begin{align*} +: \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\\ (m,n) \mapsto& x + y \mod{2} \end{align*}

is continuous and furthermore a well-defined group action binary operation of a group as group action

Then \(+\) is free (cf. binary operation of a group action as free action) and proper, as \(\mathbb{Z}/2 \mathbb{Z}\) is finite.

Furthermore, \(X = \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}\) is locally regular compact, as the only nonempty open set is \(X\) itself, but it is already compact as finite set (cf. finite set as compact set)

Nonetheless, the only nonempty open set is \(X\), hence we conclude

\begin{align*} 1 + X = X \end{align*}

with nonempty intersection.

Therefore, our space is not covering like, as \(1 \neq 0\)
