perfect field of prime characteristic and frobenius as automorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field with characteristic \(\mathrm{char}(K) = p\) for a prime \(p\).

TFAE:

  1. \(K\) is a perfect field
  2. the frobenius endomorphism is an field-automorphism

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose Frobenius is not surjective, then there exists an \(\alpha \in K\) with no \(p\)-th root in \(K\) Let \(L\) be the splitting field for \(X^p - \alpha\) and let \(\beta\) be a root of \(X^p - \alpha\)

Then

\begin{align*} (X - \beta)^p =& X^p - \beta^p \\ =& X^p - \alpha \in K[X] \end{align*}

and hence the minimal polynomial of \(\beta\) divides \((X - \beta)^p\). Since \(\beta \not \in K\), it follows, that

\begin{align*} f_{\beta} = (X - \beta)^k \end{align*}

for \(k \geq 2\). Therefore, \(\beta\) is a multiple root, contradiction to \(K\) being perfect and \(\beta\) separable

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition

Suppose \(f \in K[X]\) is irreducible and not separable Then by formal derivation in characteristik p as zero and algebraische Ableitung eines irreduziblen Polynoms und mehrfache Nullstelle it follows, that there exists an \(g \in K[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} f =& g(X^p) \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^{i \cdot p} \end{align*}

Since Frobenius is an automorphism and hence surjective, there exist \(\beta_i \in K\) such that

\begin{align*} \beta_i^p = \alpha_i \end{align*}

Therefore

\begin{align*} f =& \sum \beta_i^p X^{i \cdot p} \\ =& \sum (\beta_i X^i)^p \end{align*}

and by linearity of \(^p\) it follows,

\begin{align*} f =& (\sum \beta_i X^i)^p \\ =& (\sum \beta_i X^i)^{p-1} \cdot (\sum \beta_i X^i)^1 \end{align*}

showing that \(f\) is reducible, contradiction
