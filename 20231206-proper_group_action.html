proper group action

1. Definition

Let \(G\) be a topological group, \(X\) a topological space and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a topological group action

Then \(\varphi\) is said to be proper, if for all compact \(K \subseteq X\), the set

\begin{align*} \{g \in G \vert g K \cap K \neq \emptyset\} \end{align*}

is finite
