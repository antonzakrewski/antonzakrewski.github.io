Eigenvalues and commuting matrices

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain (commutative ring ?),\(A,B \in R^{n \times n}\) matrices such that \(\lambda \in R\) is an Eigenwert of \(AB\) Then \(\lambda\) is also an eigenvalue of \(BA\)

2. Proof

2.1. \(\lambda = 0\)

this is equivalent to \(BA\) with nontrivial kernel. This follows from the Determinantenproduktsatz, as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{det}(AB) =& \mathrm{det}(A) \cdot \det(B) \\ =& \mathrm{det}(BA) \end{align*}

and Äquivalenz: Determinante ungleich Null und reguläre Matrix

2.2. \(\lambda \neq 0\)

By assumption there exists an element \(v \in R^n\) such that

\begin{align*} AB v = \lambda v \end{align*}

Thus it follows that

\begin{align*} BA(Bv) =& B (ABv) \\ =& B \lambda \cdot v \\ =& \lambda Bv \end{align*}
