postcomposition with adjoints as adjoint

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{L} \vdash \mathcal{R}\) be adjoint functors for \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}, \mathcal{R}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) Then for an indexing category \(\mathcal{J}\), postcomposition is an adjoint on the functor categories

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{C}) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{D}) \\ \mathcal{R}^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{D}) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{C}) \end{align*}

see: postcomposition with a functor for functor categories

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \eta: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{C})}(\mathcal{L}^{ \leftarrow}(-), -) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{J}, \mathcal{D})}(-, \mathcal{R}^{ \leftarrow}(-)) \\ (\epsilon: (\mathcal{L} \circ \mathcal{F}) \rightarrow \mathcal{G}) \mapsto& (\mathcal{R} \circ \epsilon: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{R} \circ \mathcal{G}) \end{align*}
