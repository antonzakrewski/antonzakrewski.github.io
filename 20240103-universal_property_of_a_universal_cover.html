universal property of a universal cover

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E\) be a topological space, \(p: E \rightarrow B\) a covering map TFAE:

  1. \(p\) is a universal cover
  2. for any covering map \(p': E' \rightarrow B\) and \(e' \in \mathrm{Fib}_b^{p'}\), there exists a unique covering map
\begin{align*} f: E \rightarrow E' \end{align*}

with \(f(e) = e'\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption, there exists a representing object \(p\) with \(e\). Then by natural transformation from covariant hom functor as element, we get a natural isomorphism

\begin{align*} \tau_{i}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Cov}(B)}((p:E \rightarrow B),-) \cong \mathrm{Fib}_{b} \end{align*}

applying \(p': E' \rightarrow B\) we get an isomorphism

\begin{align*} \tau_{i}(p') \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Cov}(B)}(p,p') \cong \mathrm{Fib}_{b}^{p'} \end{align*}
