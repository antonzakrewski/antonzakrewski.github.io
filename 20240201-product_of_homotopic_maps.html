Product of homotopic maps

1. Proposition

Let \(X_i\) be a family of topological spaces and \(Z\) be another topological space. Suppose \(f_i,g_i: Z \rightarrow X_i\) are continuous maps. TFAE:

  1. for each \(i \in I\) it holds, that \(f_i \sim g_i\) are homotopic
  2. the induced maps \(\prod f_i \sim \prod g_i\) are

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

by assumption, for each \(i \in I\) there exists a homotopy \(H_i: [0,1] \times Z \rightarrow X_i\) applying the universal property results in

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Note that by composition of homotopic maps, it follows, that

\begin{align*} [\pi_i] \circ [\prod f_i] =& [\pi_i \circ \prod f_i] \\ =& [f_i] [\pi_i] \circ [\prod g_i] =& [\pi_i \circ \prod g_i] \\ =& [g_i] \end{align*}

where \(\pi_i \sim \pi_i\) and \(\prod f_i \sim \prod g_i\) by assumption
