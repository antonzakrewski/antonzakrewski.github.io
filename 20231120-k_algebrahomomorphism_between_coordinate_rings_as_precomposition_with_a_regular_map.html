K-Algebrahomomorphism between coordinate rings as precomposition with a regular map

1. Proposition

Let \(F\) be an algebraically closed field, \(V \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n, W \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^m\) algebraic sets.

Then for an algebra-homomorphism \(\varphi: \mathcal{O}(W) \rightarrow \mathcal{O}(V)\), there exists a polynomial map \(f: V \rightarrow W\), such that

\begin{align*} f^{ \rightarrow} = \varphi \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. konstruktion

Let

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}(W) = K[Y_1,...,Y_m]/ \mathcal{I}(W) \end{align*}

Then we define

\begin{align*} \overline{y}_i \coloneqq \pi_{\mathcal{I}(W)}(y_i) \end{align*}

Suppose

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathcal{O}(W) \rightarrow \mathcal{O}(V)\\ \end{align*}

is an algebra-homomorphism Let

\begin{align*} f_i \coloneqq \varphi(\overline{y}_i)) \in \mathcal{O}(V) \end{align*}

Then by coordinate ring as set of polynomial maps, we can identify

\begin{align*} f: V \rightarrow& \mathbb{A}^m p \mapsto& (f_1(p),...,f_m(p)) \end{align*}

as polynomial map

2.2. wohldefiniert bezüglich codomain

Claim:

\begin{align*} f(V) \subseteq W \Leftrightarrow \mathcal{I}(W) \subseteq \mathcal{I}(f(V)) \end{align*}

Let \(G \in \mathcal{I}(W)\), then \(G = 0 + \mathcal{I}(W)\) and hence \(G(\overline{y}_1,...,\overline{y}_m) = 0 \in \mathcal{O}(W)\).

Therefore,

\begin{align*} \varphi(G(\overline{y}_1,...,\overline{y_m})) = 0 \in \mathcal{O}(V) \end{align*}

because \(\varphi\) is a homomorphism.

Furthermore, we get

\begin{align*} \varphi(G(\overline{y}_1,...,\overline{y}_m)) =& G(\varphi(\overline{y}_1),...,\varphi(\overline{y}_m)) =& G(f_1,...,f_m) \end{align*}

d.h. für alle \(P \in V\) alle \(G \in I(W)\) folgt

\begin{align*} G(f_1(P),...,f_m(P)) = 0 \end{align*}

damit \(f(P) \in W\)

2.3. gleichheit

\(\overline{y}_1,...,\overline{y}_m\) erzeugen \(\mathcal{O}(W)\). Das heißt, es reicht, zu zeigen, dass \(f^*(\overline{y}_i) = \varphi(\overline{y}_i)\)

Einsetzen der definitionen ergibt

\begin{align*} \varphi(\overline{y}_i) = f_i \overline{y}_i \circ f = f^*(\overline{y}_i) \end{align*}
