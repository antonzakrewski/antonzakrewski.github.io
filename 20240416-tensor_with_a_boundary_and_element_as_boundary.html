tensor with a boundary and element as boundary

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}), \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{ModR})\) the categories of chain complexes on category RMod Then for

  1. chain complexes \(C \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{RMod}), D \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathrm{ModR})\)
  2. the graded module of cycles \(Z(-)\), the graded module of boundaries \(B(-)\)
  3. the general tensor functor of modules resp. the tensor product of chain complexes of modules
  4. the canonical morphism from tensor product of cycles gives cycle of tensor product
  5. the chain complex of homology and the forgetful functor from chain complexes of modules to graded modules

The induced map

\begin{align*} Z(C) \otimes_R B(D) \rightarrow& \mathrm{GMod} (x_n \otimes_R y_m) \mapsto& (x_n \otimes_R y_m \in C_n \otimes_R D_m \hookrightarrow (C \otimes_R D)_{n+m}) \end{align*}

given by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} Z(C) \otimes_R B(D) \arrow[]{r}{} & Z(C \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{GMod}(H_n(C \otimes_R D)) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is the zero morphism

2. Proof

for an element \(x \otimes y \in Z(C) \otimes_R B(D)\) we may choose a preimage \(y' \in Z(C)\) and get

\begin{align*} d^C(x) \otimes_R y + (-1)^{p +1} x \otimes_R d^D(y') =& 0 \otimes_R y \pm x \otimes_R y \\ =& \pm x \otimes_R y \end{align*}

Thus it remains to choose the sign wisely
