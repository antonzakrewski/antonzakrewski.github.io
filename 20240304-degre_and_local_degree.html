degre and local degree

1. Proposition

Let \(f: S^n \rightarrow S^n\) be a continuous map and \(n \geq 1\). Assume there exists a \(y \in S^n\) such that

\begin{align*} f^{-1}[y] = \{x_1,...,x_k\} \end{align*}

for some \(k \geq 0\) and that there exist open neighbourhoods \(U_i\) of \(x_i\) such that \(f\) is a local homeomorphism at \(U_i\). Then it holds for the singular homological degree

\begin{align*} \mathrm{deg}(f) = \sum_{i=0}^{k} \mathrm{deg}_{x_i}(f) \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. we may assume, that \(U_i\) are pairwise disjoint, after applying hausdorffness and intersecting finitely many times.

Then it follows for \(X \coloneqq S^n, A \coloneqq S^n \setminus \{x_1,...,x_k\}, U \coloneqq S^n \setminus \bigcup U_i\) by excision theorem, since \(\overline{U} = U \subseteq \mathrm{int}(A) = A\), that

\begin{align*} H_n^{\mathrm{sing}}(S^n, S^n \setminus \{x_1,...,x_k\}) \simeq& H_n(S^n \setminus (S^n \setminus \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i), S^n \setminus \{x_1,...,x_k\} \setminus (S^n \setminus \bigcup_{i \in I} U_i)) \\ =& H_n(\bigcup U_i, \bigcup (U_i \setminus x_i)) \\ =& H_n(\coprod U_i, \coprod (U_i \setminus x_i)) \\ \simeq \bigoplus_{i \in I} H_n(U_i, U_i \setminus x_i) \end{align*}

where the last isomorphism follows from relative singular homology commutes with coproducts

Moreover there exists an isomorhpism

\begin{align*} H_n(S^n) \rightarrow H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{y\}) \simeq H_n(V_i, V_i \setminus \{y\}) \end{align*}

making following diagram commute

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H_n(S^n) \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{f_*}& H_n(S^n) \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}\\ H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{x_1,...,x_k\}) \arrow[]{r}{f_*}& H_n(S^n, S^n \setminus \{y\}) \\ \bigoplus H_n(U_i, U_i \setminus \{x_i\}) \arrow[]{u}{\simeq}& \bigoplus H_n(V_i, V_i \setminus \{y\}) \arrow[]{u}{\simeq} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
