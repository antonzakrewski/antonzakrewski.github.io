relative singular chains of a mapping cylinder and mapping cone quasi isomorphic

1. Proposition

Let \(X,Y\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map. Then for the mapping cylinder \(\mathrm{Cyl}(f)\) and the mapping cone \(C(f)\) there exists a quasi-isomorphism for the relative singular chain complexes

\begin{align*} C_{*}^{\mathrm{sing}}((C(f), *) \simeq C_*^{\mathrm{sing}}(\mathrm{Cyl}(f), X) \end{align*}

where \(*\) is cone point.

2. Proof

Let \(C^+(X)\) be

\begin{align*} C^+(X) \coloneqq (X \times [0,5)/\sim) \subseteq C(f) \end{align*}

Then \(C^+(X)\) is contractible, as it is collapsed at \(0\).

Thus the chaincomplexes

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C(f), \{*\}) \simeq C^{\mathrm{sing}}(\mathrm{}C(f), C^+(X)) \end{align*}

are quasi-isomorphic

Thus we get an isomorphism

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C(f), *) \simeq C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C(f), C^+(X)) \end{align*}

By excision of homology we get a quasi-isomorphism

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C(f), C^+(X)) \simeq C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C(f) \setminus \{*\}, C^{\mathrm{sing}}(X) \setminus \{*\}) \end{align*}

Here the chain complexes

\begin{align*} C^{\mathrm{sing}}(\mathrm{Cyl}(f), X) \simeq C^{\mathrm{sing}}(C(f) \setminus \{*\}, C^+(X) \setminus \{*\}) \end{align*}

are quasi-isomorphic
