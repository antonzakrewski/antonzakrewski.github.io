covering map induces natural transformation on monodromy functor

1. Proposition

Let \(B,E,E'\) be topological spaces and \(p: E \rightarrow B, p: E' \rightarrow B\) covering maps and \(f: (p: E \rightarrow B) \rightarrow (p': E' \rightarrow B)\) a map of covering.

Then the canonical map

\begin{align*} f_{b}: \mathrm{Fib}_b^p \rightarrow& \mathrm{Fib}_b^{p'} \\ e \mapsto& f(e) \in p^{-1}(b) \end{align*}

is a natural transformation for the monodromy functors

\begin{align*} \tau_p, \tau_{p'}: \tau_{\leq 1}(B) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set} \end{align*}

2. Proof

We have to show, that for a path \(\gamma: b \leadsto b'\) the following diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} b \arrow[]{d}{[\gamma]} & & \mathrm{Fib}_b^p \arrow[]{d}{\tau_{\gamma}^p(1)} \arrow[]{r}{f_{b}} & \mathrm{Fib}_{b}^{p'} \arrow[]{d}{\tau_{\gamma}^{p'}(1)} \\ b' & & \mathrm{Fib}_{b'}^p \arrow[]{r}{f_{b'}} & \mathrm{Fib}_{b'}^{p'} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

follows from unique path lifting for a covering map, where by assumption

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & {[0,1]} \arrow[swap]{ld}{\tau_{\gamma}^p} \arrow[near start]{dd}{\gamma} \arrow[]{rd}{\tau_{\gamma}^{p'}} & \\ E \arrow[near start]{rr}{f} \arrow[swap]{rd}{p} & & E \arrow[]{ld}{p'} \\ & B & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commutes

\begin{align*} f \circ \tau_{\gamma}^{p} =& \tau_{\gamma} \circ f \end{align*}
