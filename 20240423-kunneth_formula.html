algebraic kunneth formula

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a hereditary ring, \(C,D\) chain complexes of \(R\)-module, where \(C\) is a chain complex of projectives . Then for \(n \geq 0\) ,there exists a natural split exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C) \otimes_R H(D) \arrow[]{r}{\mu} & H(C \otimes D) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_1^R(H(C), H(D))[1] \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

of graded modules (cf. forgetful functor from chain complexes of modules to graded modules) where

\begin{align*} \mu: H(C) \otimes_R H(D) \rightarrow H(C \otimes D) \end{align*}

is the map out of the tensor of homology to the homology of the tensor

2. Proof

An explicite description for each \(n\) is given by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \bigoplus_{p + q = n} H_p(C) \otimes_R H_q(D) \arrow[]{r}{} & H_n(C \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{} & \bigoplus_{p + q = n - 1} \mathrm{Tor}_1^R(H_p(C), H(D))[1] \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

For a \(C\) boundary cycle short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & Z \arrow[]{r}{} & C \arrow[]{r}{} & B[1] \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Since \(R\) is hereditary, each \(Z,B\) is a chain complex of projectives

Therefore

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 & Z \otimes_R D & C \otimes_R D & B[1] \otimes_R D & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact (cf. projective module as flat module)

Using fundamental theorem of short exact chain complexes this gives a long exact

\begin{align*} H(B \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & H(Z \otimes_R D) \end{align*} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H(B \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & H(Z \otimes_R D) & H(C \otimes D) & H \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

since $B,Zfq are flat, we get

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} B \otimes H(D) \arrow[]{r}{} & Z \otimes H(D) \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{} & B[1] \otimes_R H(D) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then we obtain a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{coker}(\partial) \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(\partial) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then

\begin{align*} \partial: B \otimes H(D) & Z \otimes H(D) \end{align*}

is given by

\begin{align*} \iota \otimes \mathrm{id} \end{align*}

where \(\iota: B \rightarrow Z\) is the inclusion map

Since

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & B \arrow[]{r}{} & Z \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is a projective resolution

The homology of the chain complex

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} B \otimes_R H(D) \arrow[]{r}{\iota \otimes \mathrm{id}} Z \otimes_R H(D) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

computse as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_0^R(H(C), H(D)) \simeq& H_0(B \otimes_R H(D) \rightarrow Z \otimes_R H(D)) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{coker}(\iota \otimes_R \mathrm{id}) \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Tor}_0^R(H(C), H(D)) \simeq& H_1(B \otimes_R H(D) \rightarrow Z \otimes_R H(D)) \\ \simeq& \mathrm{ker}(\iota \otimes_R \mathrm{id}) \end{align*}

Thus we get a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C) \otimes_R H(D) \arrow[]{r}{\mu} \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_1^R(H(C), H(D))[1] \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
