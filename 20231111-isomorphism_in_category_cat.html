isomorphism in category cat as object-bijective, fully faithful functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{Cat}\) be Category Cat, then the isomorphisms are the surjective, fully faithful object-injective functors

2. Proof

2.1. surjective, fully faithful as iso

Suppose \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) is a surjective, fully faithfull functor. Then for for \(A_1,A_2,A_3 \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{D})\), since \(\mathcal{F}\) is surjective and object-injective, there exists a unique \(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_i)\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}( \mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_i)) = A_i \end{align*}

Suppose \(f: A_1 \rightarrow A_2, g: A_2 \rightarrow A_3\), then since \(\mathcal{F}\) is full and \(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_i) \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\), there exist unique morphisms

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{-1}(f): \mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_1) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_2) \mathcal{F}^{-1}(g): \mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_2) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_3) \end{align*}

such that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(f)) =& f \\ \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(g)) =& g \end{align*}

then let

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{-1}: \mathcal{D} \rightarrow& \mathcal{C} \\ A \mapsto& \mathcal{F}^{-1}(A) \\ f \mapsto& \mathcal{F}^{-1}(f) \end{align*}

Then by uniqueness (faithfullness), it follows that \(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(\mathrm{id}_{A_1}) = \mathrm{id}_{\mathcal{F}^{-1}(A_1)}\)

Furthermore, applying functorality

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}( \mathcal{F}^{-1}(g) \circ \mathcal{F}^{-1}(f)) =& \mathcal{F}( \mathcal{F}^{-1}(g)) \circ \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(f)) \\ =& f \circ g \end{align*}

hence by uniqueness, it follows that

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{-1}(f \circ g) =& \mathcal{F}^{-1}(f) \circ \mathcal{F}^{-1}(g) \end{align*}

2.2. iso are surjective, fully faithful
