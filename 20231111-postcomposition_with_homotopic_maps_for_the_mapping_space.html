postcomposition with pointed homotopic maps for the based mapping spaces

1. Proposition

Let \((X,x_0), (Y,y_0), (Z,z_0)\) be based spaces, \(f,f': Y \rightarrow Y\) be pointed homotopic based maps and \(\mathrm{Map}_*(X,Z), \mathrm{Map}_*(Y,Z)\) the Based Mapping spaces with the compact open topology

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}, f'^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Map}_{*}(X,Y) \rightarrow \mathrm{Map}_{*}(X,Z) \end{align*}

are homotopic

2. Proof

follows from postcomposition with homotopic maps for the mapping spaces and homotopic map and restriction of the domain.

Note that it is welldefined, as for a pointed homotopy \(H\) from \(f\) to \(f'\), it follows by slight abuse of notation, that \(H(t)\) is at each point \(t\) a based map.
