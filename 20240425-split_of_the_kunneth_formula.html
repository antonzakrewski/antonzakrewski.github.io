2. Proof

By quasi isomorphism for a chain complex over a hereditary ring there are degreewise projective chain complexes \(P,Q\) and quasi-isomorphisms

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} C & \arrow[]{l}{} P \arrow[]{r}{}& H(C) \\ D & \arrow[]{l}{} Q \arrow[]{r}{}& H(D) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

By algebraic kunneth there exists a commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H(P) \otimes_R H(Q) \arrow[]{r}{} & H(P \otimes_R Q) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_1^R((H(P), H(Q))[1] \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C) \otimes_R H(D) \arrow[]{r}{} & H(C \otimes_R D) \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{Tor}_1^R((H(C), H(D))[1] \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

By the Five-Lemma the middle is also an isomorphism

Therefore it suffices to show, that the outer sequence splits

Thne the maps

\begin{align*} P \rightarrow H(C) \simeq H(P) \\ Q \rightarrow H(D) \simeq H(Q) \end{align*}

induces \(P \otimes_R Q \rightarrow H(P) \otimes_R H(Q)\)

And this induces a map

\begin{align*} H(P \otimes_R Q) \rightarrow H(P) \otimes_R H(Q) \end{align*} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} H(P) \otimes_R H(Q) \arrow[]{rd}{\mu}\arrow[]{rr}{}& & H(P) \otimes_R H(Q) \\ & H(P \otimesR Q) \arrow[]{ru}{r}& \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Here

\begin{align*} r\mu([x] \otimes [y]) =& r [x \otimes y] \\ =& H(\alpha' \otimes \beta')([x \otimes y]) \\ =& H(\alpha'(x) \otimes \beta'(y)] \\ =& H(\alpha')[x] \otimes H(\beta')[y] \end{align*}
