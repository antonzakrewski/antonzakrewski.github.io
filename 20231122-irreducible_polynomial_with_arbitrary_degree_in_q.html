irreducible polynomial with arbitrary degree in Q

1. Proposition

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}^+\).

Then there exists an irreducible polynomial \(f \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) with degree \(\mathrm{deg}(f) = n\)

2. Proof

Let \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) be a prime number.

then

\begin{align*} f = x^n + p \in \mathbb{Z}[X] \end{align*}

is irreducible by Eisenstein.

Thus by localization preserving irreducible elements in an UFD, \(f \in \mathbb{Q}[X]\) is irreducible
