tensor product as zero object and nontrivial bilinear map

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(M,N\) \(A\)-modules and \(M \otimes_A N\) the tensor product

TFAE:

  1. \(M \otimes_A N = 0\)
  2. every bilinear map \(M \times N \rightarrow P\) is the zero morphism

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from the universal property as each bilinear map \(f: M \times N \rightarrow P\) factors as

\begin{align*} f(m,n) =& \overline{f}(m \otimes n) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

note that the zero object suffices, as every bilinear map has a unique extension given by the unique zero morphism.
