integers as subring of the center

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(1: \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow A\) the initial morphism (cf. Initial Object in Ring) Then the image \(1[ \mathbb{Z}] \hookrightarrow C(A)\) is a subring of the center

2. Proof

follows from distributivity, as

\begin{align*} 1(n) \cdot m =& (\sum_{i=1}^n 1) \cdot m \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{n} 1 \cdot m \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{n} m \cdot 1 \\ =& m \cdot (\sum_{i=1}^n 1) \\ =& m \cdot n_r \end{align*}
