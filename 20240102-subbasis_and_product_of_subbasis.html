subbasis and product of subbasis

1. Proposition

Let \(X_i\) be a family of topological spaces, \(\mathcal{S}_i\) a Subbasis of \(X_i\) and \(\prod_{i \in I} X_i\) the product topology. Then set \(\mathcal{S}\) containing

\begin{align*} S_i \prod_{j \neq i} X_j \end{align*}

is a Subbasis of \(\prod_{i \in I} X_i\)

2. Proof

Suppose \(O \subseteq \prod_{i \in I} X_i\) is open, then by assumption, there exist \(O_i \subseteq X_i\) open for \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) such that

\begin{align*} O = \bigcap_{i = 1}^n O_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X_j \end{align*}

Furthermore, each \(O_i\) is by assumption generated by a finite intersection

\begin{align*} O_i = \bigcap_{l = 1}^{k_i} S_i \end{align*}

hence since finitely many finite intersection are again finite, we get

\begin{align*} O =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^n \bicgap_{l = 1}^{k_i} S_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X_j \end{align*}

for \(S_i \times \prod_{j \neq i} X_j \in \mathcal{S}\)

Hence \(O\) is a finite intersection of elements in \(\mathcal{S}\), hence \(\mathcal{S}\) is a subbasis
