saturation of an ideal with respect to a multiplicatively closed set as ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq R\) a multiplicatively closed set and \(I \subseteq R\) an ideal. Then the saturation with respect to \(S\), \(I^S\) is an ideal

2. Proof

2.1. additive group

since \(0 \cdot 1 = 0 \in I\), we conclude that \(0 \in I^S\). Let \(a \in I^S\), then there exists an \(s \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} a \cdot s \in I \end{align*}

Then for \(-a\) we get

\begin{align*} (-a) \cdot s = - (a \cdot s) \in I \end{align*}

hence \(-a \in I^S\)

2.2. multiplicative

Let \(r \in R\) and \(a \in I^s\), then by assumption, there exists an \(s \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} a \cdot s \in I \end{align*}

Furthermore, \(r \cdot (a \cdot s) \in I\), hence \(r \cdot a \in I\)
