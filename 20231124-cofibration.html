cofibration (topoloyg)

1. Definition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T}_X)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(\iota: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map.

Then \(\iota\) is said to be a cofibration, if it satisfies the Homotopy Extension Property, i.e:

1.1. HEP

Let \(X,Y,A\) be topological spaces and \(\iota: A \rightarrow X\) a continuous map. Then \(A\) satiesfies the homotopy extension property, if for a map \(f: X \rightarrow Y\), the precomposition \(f \circ \iota: X \rightarrow Y\) and a homotopy

\begin{align*} H: A \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

such that

\begin{align*} f_{\iota[A]} = H(-, 0) \end{align*}

there exist an extension

\begin{align*} \overline{H}: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow& Y \\ H(-,0) =& f \end{align*}
