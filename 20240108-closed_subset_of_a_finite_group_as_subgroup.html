closed subset of a finite group as subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a finite group and \(H \subseteq G\) a nonempty subset. TFAE:

  1. \(H\) is closed under binary operation
  2. \(H\) is a subgroup

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(h \in h\). Then it suffices to show, that \(h^{-1} \in H\), as then it follows, that \(h \cdot h^{-1} = e \in H\). Consider

\begin{align*} h^{1}, h^{2}, ... h^{\mathrm{ord}(G)} \end{align*}

By assumption, each \(h^n \in H\). Furthermore, \(h^{\mathrm{ord}(G)} = e\) (cf. Satz von Fermat). Hence we conclude, that

\begin{align*} h^{\mathrm{ord}(G) - 1} = h^{-1} \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{ord}(G) \geq 1\), since \(h \in G\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
