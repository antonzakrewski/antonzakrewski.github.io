ab-enriched valued functor category as ab-enriched category

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a small category and \(\mathcal{A}\) an Ab-enriched category Then the functor category

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{A}) \end{align*}

is also ab-enriched

2. Proof

2.1. abelian group

For \(\mathcal{F}, \mathcal{G} \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{A}))\) we define

\begin{align*} \iota: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{A})}(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}) \rightarrow& \prod_{C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{A})}(\mathcal{F}(C),\mathcal{G}(C)) \\ \eta \mapsto& \prod_{C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})} \eta(C) \end{align*}

This is a set-theoretic injection

2.2. composition

Suppose there exist functors \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G},\mathcal{H}\) and natural transformations

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F} \arrow[]{r}{\eta_1,\eta_2} & \mathcal{G} \arrow[]{r}{\nu_1,\nu_2}& \mathcal{H} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then consider

\begin{align*} (\eta_1 + \eta_2) \circ (\nu_1 + \nu_2) \end{align*}

Furthermore, for \(C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\), we get

\begin{align*} (\eta_1 + \eta_2) \circ (\nu_1 + \nu_2)(C) =& (\eta_1 + \eta_2)(C) \circ (\nu_1 + \nu_2)(C) \\ =& (\eta_1(C) + \eta_2(C)) + (\nu_1(C) + \nu_2(C)) \\ =& \eta_1(C) \nu_1(C) + \eta_1(C) \nu_2(C) + \eta_2(C) \nu_1(C) + \eta_2(C) \nu_2(C) \\ =& (\eta_1 \nu_1)(C) + (\eta_1 \nu_2)(C) + (\eta_2 \nu_1)(C) + (\eta_2 \nu_2)(C) \\ \end{align*}
