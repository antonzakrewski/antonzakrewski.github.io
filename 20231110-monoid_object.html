monoid object

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(M \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\). Then \(M\) is said to be a monoid object, if

  1. there exists a morphism \(\eta: 1 \rightarrow M\) for the tensor unit \(1\)
  2. there exists a morphism for the <> \(\mu: M \otimes M \rightarrow M\), called the multiplication functor
  3. associativity holds, i.e.
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} (M \otimes M) \otimes M \arrow[]{d}{\mu \otimes \mathrm{id}_{M} } \arrow[]{r}{\alpha} & M \otimes (M \otimes M) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id}_{M} \otimes \mu} & M \otimes M \arrow[]{ld}{\mu}\\ M \otimes M \arrow[]{r}{\mu} & M & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

for the associator \(\alpha\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 1 \otimes M \arrow[]{r}{\eta \otimes \mathrm{id}_{M} }& M \otimes M & \arrow[]{l}{\mathrm{id}_{M} \otimes }M \otimes 1 \\ & M & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
