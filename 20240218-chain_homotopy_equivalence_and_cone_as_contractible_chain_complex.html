chain homotopy equivalence and cone as contractible chain complex

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(A_*,B_*\) are chain complexes and \(f: A_* \rightarrow B_*\) a chain map

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is a chain homotopy equivalence
  2. the mapping cone \(C(f)\) is contractible chain complex

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(g: D_n \rightarrow C_n\) be a homotopy inverse, \(h_1: D_n \rightarrow D_{n+1},h_2: C_n \rightarrow C_{n+1}\) the homotopies.

Take

\begin{align*} H_n = \begin{pNiceMatrix} h_1 & \\ g & h_2 \end{pNiceMatrix}: D_{n+1} \otimes C_{n} \rightarrow D_n \otimes C_{n-1} \end{align*}

Then as stated below, this suffices

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} D_{n+1} \otimes C_{n} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & D_n \otimes C_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}} \arrow[swap]{ld}{H_n}& D_{n-1} \otimes C_{n-2} \arrow[swap]{ld}{H_{n-1}}\\ D_{n+1} \otimes C_{n} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & D_n \otimes C_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & D_{n-1} \otimes C_{n-2} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(C(f)\) is contractible, then there exists a map

\begin{align*} H_n = \begin{pNiceMatrix} h_1 & g_2 \\ g_1 & h_2 \end{pNiceMatrix}: D_{n+1} \otimes C_{n} \rightarrow D_n \otimes C_{n-1} \end{align*}

for

\begin{align*} h_1: D_n \rightarrow& D_{n+1} \\ h_2: C_n \rightarrow& C_{n+1} \\ g_1: D_n \rightarrow& C_n \\ g_2: C_{n-1} \rightarrow& D_{n+1} \end{align*} \begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} D_{n+1} \otimes C_{n} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & D_n \otimes C_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}} \arrow[swap]{ld}{H_n}& D_{n-1} \otimes C_{n-2} \arrow[swap]{ld}{H_{n-1}}\\ D_{n+1} \otimes C_{n} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & D_n \otimes C_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{\partial} & D_{n-1} \otimes C_{n-2} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

satisfying

\begin{align*} (d,c) =& (\mathrm{id} - 0)(d,c) \\ =& H(\partial(d) + f(c), - \partial(c)) + (\partial)(h_1(d) + g_2(c), h_2(c) + g_1(d)) \\ =& (h_1(\partial (d)) + h_1(f(c)) - g_2(\partial (c)), - h_2(\partial (c)) + g_1(\partial (d)) + g_1(f(c))) + (\partial h_1(d)) + \partial(g_2(c)) + fh_2(c) + fg_1(d), - \partial h_2(c) - \partial g_1(d)) \\ =& (h_1 \partial(d) + \partial h_1(d) - g_2(\partial (c)) + \partial (g_2(c)) + h_1f(c) + fh_2(c) + fg_1(d), g_1(\partial (d)) - \partial (g_1(d)) - \partial h_2(c) - h_2(\partial(c)) + g_1f(c)) \\ \end{align*}

Since the \((d,-)\) is independent from \(c\), we conclude that

\begin{align*} g_2(\partial(c)) + \partial (g_2(c)) + h_1(f(c)) + fh_2(c) = 0 \end{align*}

analogously

\begin{align*} g_1(\partial(d)) - \partial(g_1(d)) = 0 \end{align*}

Showing

\begin{align*} (d,c) =& (h_1 (\partial(d)) + \partial (h_1(d)) + fg_1(d), - \partial h_2(c) - h_2(\partial(c)) + g_1f(c)) \end{align*}

Here \(g_1: D_n \rightarrow C_n\) suffices as homotopy inverse for \(f\) with

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}(d) - fg_1(d) =& h_1 \partial (d) + \partial h_1(d) \\ \mathrm{id}(c) - g_1f(c) =& \partial (-h_2)(c) + (-h_2)(\partial(c) \end{align*}
