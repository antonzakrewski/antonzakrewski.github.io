idempotent and trivial unit

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(e\) an idempotent unit. Then \(e = 1\)

2. Proof

suppose \(e \neq 1\), then by assumption

\begin{align*} (1 - e) \cdot e =& e - e^2 \\ =& e - e \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

hence \(e\) is a zero divisor. Since a unit is not a zero divisor, it follows, that \(e\) is not a unit, thus contraposition
