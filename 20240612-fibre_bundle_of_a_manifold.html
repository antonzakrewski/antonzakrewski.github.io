fibre bundle of a manifold

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be an \(n\)-dimensional manifold, \(p: E \rightarrow M\) a Fibre bundle, where each point \(m \in M\) has an open neighbourhood \(U\) with

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U] \cong U \times V \end{align*}

where \(V\) is an open subset of an \(l\)-dimensional manifold \(F\)

Then \(E\) is an \(k+l\)-dimensional manifold

2. Proof

2.1. locally euclidean

Let \(e \in E\), then choose a neighbourhood \(U \subseteq M\) of \(p(e)\) such that

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U] = U \times V \end{align*}

Then both \(U\) and \(V\) are by assumption locally euclidean. Therefore also the product (cf. finite product of locally euclidean spaces as locally euclidean space)

2.2. hausdorff

Let \(e \neq e' \in E\). Then w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(p(e) = p(e')\) as otherwise choosing disjoint neighbourhoods in \(M\) and taking preimages suffices.

Then it follows that \(e = (e_1,f), e' = (e_1,f')\). As \(V\) is a subspace of a Hausdorff space, hence hausdorff (cf. subspace of a hausdorff space as hausdorff space), we may choose disjoint neighbourhoods

\begin{align*} U \times V_1, U \times V_2 \end{align*}

for \(V_1 \cap V_2 = \emptyset\)

2.3. secondcountable

Let \(e \in E\) and \(O\) a neighbourhood. Then w.l.o.g. by passing to a neighbourhood \(U\) of \(p(e)\) such that

\begin{align*} p^{-1}[U] \cong U \times V \end{align*}

Then as \(U \times V\) is the countable product of second countable spaces, it is second countable. Note that since \(M\) is second countable, there exists a countable cover of \(M\) by such \(U\). Therefore a basis is given by the countable union of those countable basis of \(U \times V\) (cf. countability of a countable union of countable sets)
