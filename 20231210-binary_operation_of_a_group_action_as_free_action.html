binary operation of a group action as free action

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group. Then the group action on

\begin{align*} \cdot: G \times G \rightarrow& G \\ (g,h) \mapsto& g \cdot h \end{align*}

is a free group action

2. Proof

group action follows from binary operation of a group as group action

Suppose \(g,h \in G\) such that

\begin{align*} g \cdot h = h \end{align*}

then we conclude

\begin{align*} g \cdot h =& e \cdot h \\ \end{align*}

and multiplying with \(h^{-1}\) gives

\begin{align*} g =& e \end{align*}
