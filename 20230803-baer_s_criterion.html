Baer's criterion

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) a left \(R\)-module

TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is injective
  2. for each left ideal \(\mathfrak{a} \hookrightarrow R\) every map \(\mathfrak{a} \rightarrow M\) can be extended to a map
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathfrak{a} \arrow[]{r}{\iota} \arrow[bend left]{rr}{} & R \arrow[dashed]{r}{}& M \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathfrak{a} \arrow[hookrightarrow, ]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{rd}{}& \\ R \arrow[dashed]{r}{} & M \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose given an extension problem

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{rd}{\alpha}\arrow[rightarrowtail, ]{d}{\iota} & \\ B \arrow[dashed]{r}{f} & M \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

for any module \(B\) and a submodule \(A\)

Let \(\Epsilon\) be the partially ordered set of all extensions

\begin{align*} \alpha': A' \rightarrow M \end{align*}

of \(\alpha: A \rightarrow M\)

where \(\alpha^1 \leq \alpha^2 :\Leftrightarrow \alpha^1 = \alpha^{2}_{\vert A_1}, A_1 \hookrightarrow A_2\)

Using Zorn's lemma this has a maximal element, e.g. taking the sequential colimit or the union

Let \(\overline{\alpha}: \overline{A} \rightarrow M\) be a maximal element. Suppose \(\overline{A} \subsetneq B\), then there exists an element \(b \in B \setminus \overline{A}\).

Consider

\begin{align*} J \coloneqq& \{r \in R : r \cdot b \in \overline{A}\} \\ =& \overline{A} : (b) \end{align*}

as left ideal (cf. ideal quotient)

Then by assumption there exists an extension

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} J \arrow[]{d}{\iota} \arrow[]{r}{\cdot b} & \overline{A} \arrow[]{r}{\overline{\alpha}} & E \\ R \arrow[dashed]{rru}{}& & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Let \(\widehat{A}\) be the submodule

\begin{align*} \widehat{A}: \overline{A} + \langle b\rangle \subseteq B \end{align*}

with \(\widehat{A} \supseteq \overline{A}\).

Then we define

\begin{align*} \widehat{\alpha}: \widehat{A} \rightarrow& E \\ \widehat{a}(\overline{a} + r \cdot b) \mapsto& \overline{a} + f(r) \end{align*}

This gives an extension \(\widehat{A}\), thus a contradiction TODO
