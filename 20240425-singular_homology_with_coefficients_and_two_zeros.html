singular homology with coefficients and two zeros

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(M\) an abelian group and \(H_k(X,M)\) the singular homology. Suppose

\begin{align*} H_k(X), H_{k-1}(X) = 0 \end{align*}

Then so is

\begin{align*} H_{k}(X, M) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

corollary of universal coefficient formula for singular homology and 0 as flat module, as we get a short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{}& H_k(X,M) \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \arrow[]{r}{}& 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
