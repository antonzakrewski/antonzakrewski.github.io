surjective morphism of sheaves

1. Definition

Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(\mathcal{C}\) a sufficiently complete and cocomplete category and \(\mathcal{F}_1, \mathcal{F}_2: \mathrm{Op}(X) \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be presheaves. Then a morphism of sheaves is said to be surjective, if the sheafification of the presheaf image is canonically isomorphic (?) to the domain

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Sheaf}(\mathrm{im}(\varphi)) = \mathcal{F}_2 \end{align*}
