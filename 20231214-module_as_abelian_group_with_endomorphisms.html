module as abelian group with ring homomorphism to the endomorphism ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring and \(M\) an abelian group.

TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is a right \(A\)-module
  2. there exists a ring-homomorphism \(\varphi: A \rightarrow \mathrm{End}(M)\) where \(\mathrm{End}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M)\) is the endomorphism ring for \(M\) regarded as \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module (cf.abelian group as module over the integers)

For a left module structure this is given by the opposite ring

\begin{align*} A^{\mathrm{op}} \rightarrow \mathrm{End}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M) \end{align*}

(right opposite ?)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from the ring homomorphism for the scalar multiplication

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
