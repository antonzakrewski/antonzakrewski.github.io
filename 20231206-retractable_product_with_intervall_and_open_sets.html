retractable product with interval and open-weak

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(A \subseteq X\) a subspace and Let

\begin{align*} Y \coloneqq A \times [0,1] \cup X \times \{0\} \subseteq X \times [0,1] \end{align*}

be a subspace of the product. Suppose there exists a retraction

\begin{align*} R: X \times [0,1] \rightarrow Y \end{align*}

Then a for a subset \(O \subseteq Y\), TFAE:

  1. \(O\) is open
  2. \(O \cap A \times [0,1], O \cap X \times \{0\}\) are open

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from the definition of a subspace topology

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

openness and neighbourhood for all points:

For \(x \in A \times (0,1] \cap O\), choose \(A \times (0,1] = Y \cap (X \times (0,1])\) as open set.

For \(x \in X \times \{0\}\), we may assume w.l.o.g. that \(x \in \overline{A}\), as otherwise, \(X \setminus (\overline{A} \times [0,1]) \cap Y = (X \setminus \overline{A}) \times \{0\}\) is open.

For \(n \in \mathbb{N}^+\), let \(U_n\) be the largest open set \(U_n \subseteq X\) such that

\begin{align*} (U_n \cap A) \times [0, \frac{1}{n}) \subseteq O \end{align*}

This set is welldefined, as the arbitrary union of such thats is open (cf. Openness of a union of open sets) and is of this form. (note, that \(\emptyset \times [0, \frac{1}{n})\) is open, hence it also exists)

Let

\begin{align*} U = \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} U_n \subseteq X \end{align*}

Then since \((A \times [0,1]) \cap O\) is open by assumption, we conclude, that \(\mathrm{pr}_1(A \times \{0\} \cap O) \subseteq U\)

It suffices to show, that \(x \in U\), because then by construction, there exists a \(U_n\) with \(x \in U_n\) and therefore,

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_1((U_n \times \{0\} \cap O)) \times [0,\frac{1}{n}) \end{align*}

is open and therefore also

\begin{align*} Y \cap \mathrm{pr}_1((U_n \times \{0\} \cap O)) \times [0,\frac{1}{n}) \end{align*}

in the subspace topology

Let \((x,t)\) be a point for a fixed \(t > 0\). Then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_2 \circ \mathrm{Ret}(x,t) =& t \end{align*}

since \(\mathrm{pr}_2 \circ \mathrm{Ret}(x,t)\) is continuous, hence also the restriction of a continuous map and by closure and continuous maps

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_2 \circ \mathrm{Ret}[X, t] =& \mathrm{pr}_2 \circ \mathrm{Ret}[\overline{A}, t] \\ \subseteq& \overline{ \mathrm{pr}_2 \circ \mathrm{Ret}[A, t]} \\ =& \overline{\{t\}} \\ =& \{t\} \end{align*}

Hence by codomain

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_1 \circ \mathrm{Ret}(x,t) \in A \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Ret}(x,t) \in A \times \{t\} \end{align*}

by Preimages of neighbourhoods under continuous maps, we conclude, that there exists an open neighbourhood \(V \subseteq X\) and an \(\varepsilon > 0\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_1 \circ \mathrm{Ret} [ V \times (t - \varepsilon, t + \varepsilon) ] \subseteq U_n \end{align*}

Therefore, as above,

\begin{align*} \mathrm{pr}_1 \circ \mathrm{Ret}(V \times \{t\}) \subseteq U_n \end{align*}

or \(V \cap A \subseteq U_n\).

By definition of \(U_n\), it follows, that \(V \subseteq U_n\) and hence \(x \in U_n\) since \(x \in V\) Therefore, \(\mathrm{pr}_1 \circ \mathrm{Ret}(x,t) \in U_n\) implies \(x \in U_n\)
