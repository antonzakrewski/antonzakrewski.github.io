translation of the reals as covering-like group action

1. Proposition

The group action

\begin{align*} +: \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ (z,x) \mapsto& x + z \end{align*}

is a covering-like group action

2. Proof

for \(x \in \mathbb{R}\), choose \(B_{\frac{1}{3}}(x)\) as epsilon-neighbourhood. Then

\begin{align*} n + (x- \frac{1}{3}, x + \frac{1}{3}) \cap (x - \frac{1}{3}, x + \frac{1}{3}) = \emptyset \end{align*}

for \(n \neq 0\), hence \(+\) is covering like
