filtered colimit functor in a grothendieck category as exact functor

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a grothendieck category, \(I\) a filtered category and

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Fun}(I, \mathcal{A}) \end{align*}

the functor category. Then the colim functor is exact

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

existence follows from cocompleteness

2.2. left exact functor

by assumption, filtered colimits commute with finite products. Thus

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}(\mathrm{lim}_I \mathcal{J}_i) = \mathrm{lim}( \mathrm{colim} (\mathcal{J}_i)) \end{align*}
