identity as covering map

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, then the identity morphism

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{X}: X \rightarrow X \end{align*}

is a covering map

2. Proof

taking the open cover \(X\), then \(\mathrm{pr}_{X}: X \times \{1\} \rightarrow X\) is the desired homeomorphism cf. product with the terminal object canonically iso
