injective morphisms on stalks imply injective morphism of sheaves

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}\) set valued sheaves. Suppose \(f: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}\) is a morphism of presheafs

TFAE:

  1. for each \(p \in X\) and stalks \(\mathcal{F}_p, \mathcal{G}_p\), \(f\) induces an injection \(f_p: \mathcal{F}_p \rightarrow \mathcal{G}_p\)
  2. \(f(U)\) is an injection for each \(U\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(U \subseteq X\) be open and \(s,t \in \mathcal{F}(U)\), such that

\begin{align*} f(s) = f(t) \in \mathcal{G}(U) \end{align*}

Then it follows, that for each \(x \in X\)

\begin{align*} f(s)_x = g(t)_x \in \mathcal{G}_x \end{align*}

and hence since the stalks are isomorphic, it follows, that

\begin{align*} s_x = t_x \end{align*}

by commutativity of

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[]{d}{} \arrow[]{r}{f[U]} & \mathcal{G}(U) \arrow[]{d}{}\\ \mathcal{F}_x \arrow[]{r}{\simeq}& \mathcal{G}_x \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

cf. derived colim

therefore since germs uniquely define stalks for a sheaf, it follows, that

\begin{align*} s = t \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose there exist sections \(s,t \in \mathcal{F}(U)\) such that

\begin{align*} f(U)(s)_x = f(U)(t)_x \end{align*}

for each \(x \in U\)

Then by equal germs imply equality on neighbourhoods we conclude, that there exists a neighbourhood \(V \subseteq U\) such that

\begin{align*} s_{\vert V} = t_{\vert V} \end{align*}
