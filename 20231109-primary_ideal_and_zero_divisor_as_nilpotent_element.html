primary ideal and zero divisor as nilpotent element

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{q}\) an ideal

TFAE:

  1. each zero divisor in \(A/\mathfrak{q}\) is nilpotent
  2. \(\mathfrak{q}\) is a primary ideal

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(x,y \in A\) with \(x \cdot y \in \mathfrak{q}\), w.l.o.g. with \(y \not\in \mathfrak{q}\) Then \(x \in A/\mathfrak{q}\) is a zero divisor, therefore \(x\) is also nilpotent. Hence there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(x^n \in \mathfrak{q}\) and therefore \(x \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{q}}\). Analogously for \(y\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(a_1,a_2 \in A \ \mathfrak{q}\) are nontrivial zero divisors in \(A/\mathfrak{q}\) with \(a_1 \cdot a_2 = 0\) Then \(a_1,a_2 \not\in \mathfrak{q}\), hence \(a_1,a_2 \in \sqrt{ \mathfrak{q}}\). Thus we conclude, that there exists \(n,m\) such that \(a_1^n \in \mathfrak{q}, a_2^m \in \mathfrak{q}\)
