extension of a chain map

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(\mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) be the category of chain complexes. Suppose \(P\) is a chain complex of projectives and \(M \in \mathrm{Ch_*}(\mathcal{A})\) another chain complex

Assume given \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) and a chain map \(\sigma_{\leq n}(f): \sigma_{\leq n}(P) \rightarrow \sigma_{\leq n}(M)\).

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{r}{} & P_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} \arrow[dashed]{d}{\sigma_{n+1}} & P_n \arrow[]{r}{d_n} \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_{n}} & P_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_{n-1}} & ... \\ ... \arrow[]{r}{} & M_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} & M_n \arrow[]{r}{d_n} & M_{n-1} \arrow[]{r}{} & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

→

TFAE:

  1. this \(\sigma_{\leq n}(f)\) extends to a chain map \(\sigma_{\leq n+1}(f:): \sigma_{\leq n+1}(P) \rightarrow \sigma_{\leq n+1}(M)\)
  2. the map
\begin{align*} p_{n+1} \rightarrow H_n(M) \end{align*}

induced by

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} & \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1}^P) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & \mathrm{ker}(d_n) \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_n} & \\ & & \mathrm{ker}(d_{n}) \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & H_{n} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is the zero morphism

2. Proof

somewhat sloppy written, todo with universal properties

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose there exists such a map \(\sigma_{n+1}\). Then it follows, that

\begin{align*} \sigma_n \circ d_{n+1} = d_{n+1} \circ \sigma_{n+1} \end{align*}

hence it follows, that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{im}(\sigma_{n} \circ d_{n+1}) = \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1} \circ \sigma_{n+1}) \hookrightarrow \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1}) \hookrightarrow \mathrm{ker}(d_n) \end{align*}

And thus composed with

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(d_n) \twoheadrightarrow \mathrm{ker}(d_n)\mathrm{im}(d_{n+1}) \end{align*}

results in the zero morphism

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose the morphism is the zero morphism. Then it follows, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} & \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1}) \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_{n, \vert \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1})}} \\ M_{n+1} \arrow[twoheadrightarrow, ]{r}{d_{n+1}} & \mathrm{im}(d_{n+1}) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and thus by projectivity, there exists a map

\begin{align*} \sigma_{n+1}: P_{n+1} \rightarrow M_{n+1} \end{align*}

making the diagram commute, hence also making

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & P_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_{n+1}} & P_n \arrow[]{d}{\sigma_{n}} \\ & M_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} & M_n \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

commute.
