stabilizer in general not a normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(X\) a set and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a group action. Then for \(x \in X\), the stabilizer \(G_x\) is in general not a normal subgroup

2. Proof

Let \(G\) be a group and \(H \subseteq G\) a subgroup, which is not a normal subgroup. Then for the group action on the cosets it follows, that

\begin{align*} G_{H} = H \end{align*}
