aut group acts transitively on an irreducible polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f \in K[X]\) an irreducible polynomial and \(\alpha_1,\alpha_2 \in \overline{K}\) a root in some algebraic closure \(\overline{K}\) Then for the splitting field

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Aut}_K(K(f)) \end{align*}

acts transitively on the roots see:

  • aut group of a splitting field acts on roots

2. Proof

Let \(\alpha_1,\alpha_2\) be roots of \(f\). Then since \(f\) is irreducible, it follows, that \(f\) is the minimal polynomial. Hence by Fortsetzung einer einfachen, algebraischen Körpererweiterung anhand einer Nullstelle there exists a field-homomorphism

\begin{align*} f: K \rightarrow \overline{K} \alpha_1 \mapsto& \alpha_2 \end{align*}

for some algebraic closure \(\overline{K}\).

Then by normale Körpererweiterung induziert K-Automorphismus it follows, that \(\sigma \coloneqq f_{\vert K(f)/K}: K(f) \rightarrow K(f)\) is a welldefined field-automorphism with \(\sigma(\alpha_1) = \alpha_2\)

Hence \(\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(f)\) satisfies the condition
