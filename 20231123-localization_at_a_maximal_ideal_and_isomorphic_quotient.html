localization at a maximal ideal and isomorphic quotient

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Integral domain, \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq A\) a maximal ideal. Then for the localization \(A_{\mathfrak{m}}\) at \(\mathfrak{m}\), with maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{n} \coloneqq \langle \mathfrak{m}\rangle\), it holds for \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) and the quotient rings, that

\begin{align*} \varphi: A/\mathfrak{m}^n \rightarrow& \mathfrak{A}_m/\mathfrak{n}^n \\ a + \mathfrak{m}^n \mapsto& a + \mathfrak{n}^n \end{align*}

is an ring-isomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

follows from $\mathfrak{n}n = ⟨ ι[mn]⟩

2.2. homomorphism

follows from construction

2.3. injection

minimal kernel and monomorphism Suppose

\begin{align*} a + \mathfrak{n}^n = 0 \end{align*}

then there exists an \(k \in \mathfrak{n}^n\)

\begin{align*} a = k \end{align*}

By construction, \(k = \frac{k_1}{s}\) for some \(s \in A \setminus \mathfrak{m}\) and \(k_1 \in \mathfrak{m}^{n}\)
