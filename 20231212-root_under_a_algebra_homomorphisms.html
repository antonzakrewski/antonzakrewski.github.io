root under A-Algebra homomorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(B_1,B_2\) be A-Algebras

\begin{align*} \varphi: B_1 \rightarrow B_2 \end{align*}

an A-Algebrahomomorphism.

Suppose \(f \in A[X]\) is a polynomial and \(b \in B_1\) a root of \(f\) Then \(\varphi(b)\) is a root in \(B_2\)

2. Proof

Let \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i\) for \(\alpha_i \in A\). Then we get

\begin{align*} f(\varphi(b)) = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i \varphi(b)^i \end{align*}

and since \(\varphi\) is an module-homomorphism, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i \varphi(b)^i =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \varphi(\alpha_i) \varphi(b^i) \\ =& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \varphi(\alpha_i \cdot b^i) \\ =& \varphi(\sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i b^i) \\ =& \varphi(0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
