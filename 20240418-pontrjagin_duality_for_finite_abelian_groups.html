pontrjagin duality for finite abelian groups

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{FinAb}\) the category of finite abelian groups, then the contravariant hom-functor is an equivalence

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(A, ): \mathrm{FinAb}^{\mathrm{op}} \rightarrow \mathrm{FinAb} \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{FinAb}\) is seen as subcategory of \(\mathrm{Ab}\)

2. Proof

2.1. bijective on objects

corollary of

  • finite abelian group isomorphic to contravariant hom S1

2.2. bijective on morphisms

As by the classification of finitely generated abelian groups it suffices to show that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(-,S^1): \mathrm{Hom}(\mathbb{Z}/ (p_1)^n \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/ (p_2)^m\mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{FinAb}}(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/ (p_2)^m \mathbb{Z},S^1, \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{FinAb}}(\mathbb{Z}/(p_1)^n \mathbb{Z}, S^1)) \\ f \mapsto& f^{ \rightarrow} \end{align*}

is a bijection.

using the canonical isomorphism this reduces to

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathbb{Z}/(p_2)^m \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}& \mathbb{Z}/(p_1)^n \mathbb{Z} \arrow[]{d}{\simeq}\\ \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/ (p_2)^m \mathbb{Z},S^1) & \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/ (p_1)^n \mathbb{Z},S^1) \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.2.1. injective

Suppose \(f,g: \mathbb{Z}/(p_1)^n \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}/(p_2)^m\) are different morphisms, then it follows that \(f(1) \neq g(1) \mod{p_2^m}\), as \(1\) is a generator of \(\mathbb{Z}/(p_1)^n \mathbb{Z}\). Hence for \(\iota: 1 \mapsto& 1 \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Ab}}(\mathbb{Z}/ (p_2)^m \mathbb{Z},S^1)\) this results in

\begin{align*} \iota(f)(1) =& \iota(f(1)) \\ =& f(1) \neq g(1) \mod{p_2^m} \end{align*}

Therefore \(f^{ \rightarrow}, g^{ \rightarrow}\) are different morphisms.
