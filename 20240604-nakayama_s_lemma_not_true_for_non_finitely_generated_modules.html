nakayama's lemma not true for non finitely generated modules

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. Then Nakayama's lemma is in general not true (unless \(M\) is finitely generated module)

2. Proof

consider \(\mathbb{Q}\) as abelian group resp. \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module. It is not finitely generated (cf. rationals not of finite type Z-algebra) Then consider the ideal \((2) \subseteq \mathbb{Z}\).

It follows that

\begin{align*} (2) \cdot \mathbb{Q} = \mathbb{Q} \end{align*}

as for \(\frac{a}{b}\) \(\frac{a}{2b}\) suffices.

But multiplication with a nonzero element is a bijection, hence

\begin{align*} (1 - 2 \cdot k) \cdot \mathbb{Q} \neq 0 \end{align*}
