infinite amount of prime numbers

1. Proposition

There exist infinitely many prime numbers

2. Proof

2.1. a)

Proof by contradiction Suppose there exists finitely many prime numbers \(p_i\) Then consider

\begin{align*} p \coloneqq \prod p_i + 1 \end{align*}

Then it follows that \(p_i \not\mid p\).

2.2. b) constructive

2.2.1. Proposition

For each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) there exists a prime number \(p\) with \(p > n\)

2.2.2. Proof

Consider \(n! + 1\). Then any prime factor of it suffices.
