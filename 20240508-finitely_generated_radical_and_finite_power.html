finitely generated radical and finite power

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subesteq A\) an ideal such that the ideal \(\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\) is finitely generated. Then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}^n \subseteq \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = (a_1,...,a_m)\) for generators \(a_i\). Then there exist \(n_i \in \mathbb{N}\) with \(a_i^{n_i} \in \mathfrak{a}\).

Let for example \(n = \sum n_i\). Then by product of ideals and generators we get

\begin{align*} (a_1,...,a_m)^{n} = ( \prod_{l_1 + ... l_m = n} a_i^{l_i}) \end{align*}

where for each generator \(\prod_{l_1 + ... l_m = n} a_i^{l_i}\) by Pigeonhole principle there exists an \(l_j > n_j\). Hence each generator of \(\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}^n\) is an element of \(\mathfrak{a}\)
