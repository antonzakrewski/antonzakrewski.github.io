weak homotopy equivalence in general not a homotopy equivalence

1. Proposition

Let \(X,Y\) be weakly homotopy equivalent topological spaces. Then \(X,Y\) are in general not homotopy equivalent spaces.

2. Proof

Consider the space of converging sequences

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Seq} \coloneqq \{0\} \cup \{ \frac{1}{n} \vert n \in \mathbb{N}^+\} \end{align*}

and \(\mathbb{N}\). then for

\begin{align*} f: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow& \mathrm{Seq} \\ 0 \mapsto& 0 \\ n \mapsto& \frac{1}{n} \end{align*}

2.1. weak homotopy equivalence

both \(\mathbb{N}\) and \(\mathrm{Seq}\) are totally disconnected spaces, hence by homotopy group of a totally disconnected space they are equivalent

2.2. not Homotopy equivalent

Let \(g: \mathrm{Seq} \rightarrow \mathbb{N}\) be a continuous map. By universal property of the converging sequence space, we conclude, that \(g\) is constant.

Hence any map

\begin{align*} g \circ f: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{N} \end{align*}

is constant.

Since \([0,1]\) is pathconnected, it follows, that for continous map \(H: [0,1] \times \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{N}\), that

\begin{align*} H[0,1] \times \{n\} = \{k\} \end{align*}

and by \(H(0,-) = g \circ f = \mathrm{const}\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} H(t,n) = \mathrm{const} \end{align*}

hence there does not exist a homotopy to \(\mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{N}}\)
