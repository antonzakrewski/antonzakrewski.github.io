morphism between quasi-projective varieties

1. Definition

Let \(X \subseteq \mathbb{P}^n\) be a quasi-projective variety A moprhism \(\varphi: X \rightarrow \mathbb{P}^m\) is an equivalence class of \((f_0,...,f_m)\) homogeneous polynomials with \(\mathrm{deg}(f_i) = \mathrm{deg}(f_j)\) with

\begin{align*} (f_0,...,f_m) \sim (g_0,...,g_m) \Leftrightarrow f_i \circ g_j - f_j g_i \in I(X) \forall i,j \end{align*}

and for each point \(p \in X\), there exist \(f_0,...,f_m\) such that \(\exists i : f_i(p) \neq 0\)
