irreduzibles Polynom als separables Polynom in einem endlichen Körper

1. Satz

Let \(F\) be a finite field and \(f \in F[X]\) an irreducible polynomial. Then \(f\) is separable

2. Beweis

corollary of finite field as perfect field

alternatively:

Let \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i \in K[X]\) be an irreducible polynomial. Suppose \(f\) is not separable, then it follows by algebraische Ableitung eines irreduziblen Polynoms und mehrfache Nullstelle, that

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}f = 0 \end{align*}

Hence by formal derivation in characteristik p as zero there exists an \(g \in F[X]\) such that

\begin{align*} g(X^p) = f \end{align*}
