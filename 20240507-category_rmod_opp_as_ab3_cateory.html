category RMod opp as AB4 category

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{RMod}\) the category RMod Then the opposite category \(\mathrm{Rmod}^{\mathrm{op}}\) is an Ab4 category

2. Proof

it suffices to show that arbitrary products of exact sequences are exact. We know that limits commute with limits, hence products commute with kernels. Therefore it remains to show that the image commutes with products.

Let \(\varphi_i: M_i \rightarrow \mathrm{im}(\varphi_i) \hookrightarrow N_i\) be module-homomorphisms

Then for \(n_i \in \mathrm{im}(\varphi_i)\) choose a preimage \(m_i\). It follows that

\begin{align*} (\prod \varphi_i)(m_i) = (n_i) \end{align*}

hence \(\prod \mathrm{im}(\varphi_i) \simeq \mathrm{im}(\prod \varphi_i)\)
