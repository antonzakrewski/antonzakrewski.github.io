finitely generated A-Algebra and generators

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a (commutative) ring and \(B\) an A-Algebra.

TFAE:

  1. there exists an algebrabic ring-epimorphism of a free A-Algebra \(\varphi: A[X_1,...,X_n] \twoheadrightarrow B\)
  2. there exists a finite subset \(a_1,...,a_n\) such that the generated A-Algebra of \(a_1,...,a_n\) is given by \(S\)

2. Proof

Let \(a_i \coloneqq \varphi(X_i)\) resp. \(\varphi\) the morphism generated by \(X_i \mapsto a_i\) (cf. free A-Algebra)

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(b \in B\), then there exists a linear combination

\begin{align*} \sum \prod_{j} \alpha_{i,j} a_{i,j} = b \end{align*}

for \(\alpha_{i,j} \in A\) Hence \(b \in \langle a_1,...,a_n\rangle\) as \(\langle ...\rangle\) is closed under multiplication, scalar multiplication with \(\alpha_{i,j}\) and addition.

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

analagously to 1) \(\implies\) 2), but backwards
