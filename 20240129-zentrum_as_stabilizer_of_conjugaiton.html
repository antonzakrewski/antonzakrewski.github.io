center as intersection of stabilizer of conjugation

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group. Then the center is the intersection of the stabilizers \(G_h\) of the conjugation as group action

2. Proof

2.1. \(Z(G) \subseteq \bigcap_{h \in G} G_h\)

Let \(g \in Z(G)\), then for arbitrary \(h \in G\) we get

\begin{align*} g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} =& g \cdot g^{-1} \cdot h \\ =& 1 \cdot h \\ =& h \end{align*}

hence \(g \in G_h\)

2.2. \(\bigcap_{h \in G} G_h \subseteq Z(G)\)

Let \(g \in \bigcap_{h \in G} G_h\) be an elemnet, then for arbitrary \(h \in H\) we get

\begin{align*} g \cdot h \cdot g^{-1} = h \end{align*}

resp.

\begin{align*} g \cdot h = h \cdot g \end{align*}
