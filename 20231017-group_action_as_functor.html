group action as functor

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(\varphi: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a group action. Then \((X,\varphi)\) is canonically identified with a functor

\begin{align*} \triangle: B(G) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set} \\ * \mapsto& X \\ g \mapsto& (\varphi(g,-): X \rightarrow X) # g \in G \mapsto& (x \mapsto g \cdot x) \\ # g\cdot \in \mathrm{Hom}_{B(G)}(G,G) \mapsto& (x \mapsto g \cdot x) \end{align*}

where \(B(G)\) is the categorification of a group
