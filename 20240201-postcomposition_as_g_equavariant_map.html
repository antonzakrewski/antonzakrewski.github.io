postcomposition as g-equivariant map

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(A,B\) be sets, \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(G,A), \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(G,B)\) the hom-sets as hom set as g-sets. Suppose \(f: A \rightarrow B\) is a map, then postcomposition

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(G,A) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(G,B) \\ g \mapsto& f^{ \leftarrow} \circ g \end{align*}

is a G-equivariant map

2. Proof

Let \(g_1,g_2 \in G\), then

\begin{align*} g_1 \cdot (f^{ \leftarrow} \circ h(g_2)) =& g_1 \cdot f \circ h(g_2) \\ =& g_1(f \circ h)(g_2) \\ =& (f \circ h)(g_1 \cdot g_2) \\ =& f^{ \leftarrow} \circ h(g_1 \cdot g_2) \end{align*}
