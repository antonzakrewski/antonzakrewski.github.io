height theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq (a_1,...,a_r) \subseteq A\) an ideal. Suppose \(\mathfrak{p}\) is a minimal prime ideal containing \(\mathfrak{a}\), then

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{p}) \leq r \end{align*}

in particular

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{a}) \leq r \end{align*}

2. Proof

Proof by induction

2.1. induction basis

follows from the principal ideal theorem

2.2. induction step

using height of a prime ideal and local ring we may assume that \(A_{\mathfrak{p}}\) is local

Let \(\mathfrak{q} \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}\) be a prime ideal. Then we claim that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{q}) \leq r - 1 \end{align*}

As \(\mathfrak{p}\) is minimal containing \(\mathfrak{a}\), we conclude that one generator \(a_i \not\in \mathfrak{q}\), w.l.o.g. \(a_1\) using the correspondence theorem for prime ideals and quotient ring w.l.o.g. we may assume that \(\mathfrak{q} \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}\) such that there is no prime ideal between \(\mathfrak{q}\) and \(\mathfrak{p}\) (cf. existence of a maximal subprime ideal)

Then \(\mathfrak{p}\) is the a minimal ideal containing \(\mathfrak{q} + (a_1)\) as

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{q} \subseteq \mathfrak{q} + (a_1) \subseteq \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

It follows that

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{q} + (a_1)} =& \bigcap_{ \overline{\mathfrak{p}} \supseteq \mathfrak{q} + (a_i)} \\ =& \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

For \(i = 2,...,r\) htere is a \(q_i \in \mathfrak{q}\), xi ∈ A$ such that

\begin{align*} a_i^{k_i} = q_i + x_i a_1 \end{align*}

Then consider

\begin{align*} (a_1,a_2^{k_2},...,a_r^{k_r}) = (a_1,q_2,...,q_r) \end{align*}

Then \(\mathfrak{p}\) is minimal over \((a_1,a_2^{k_2},...,a_r^{k_r})\) and hence also minimal over \((a_1,q_2,...,q_r)\).

This shows that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p}/(q_2,...,q_r) \end{align*}

is minimal over

\begin{align*} (a_1,q_2,...,q_r)/(q_2,...,q_r) \end{align*}

But

\begin{align*} (\overline{a}_1) \subseteq A/(q_2,...,q_r) \end{align*}

generates the ideal \((a_1,q_2,...,q_r)/(q_2,...,q_r)\)

By the principal ideal theorem we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}((a_1,q_2,...,q_r)/(q_2,...,q_r)) \leq 1 \end{align*}

showing

Since \((q_2,...,q_r) \subseteq \mathfrak{q} \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\) we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{q}) = 0 \end{align*}

i.e. \(\mathfrak{q}\) is minimal over \((q_2,...,q_r)\).

By induction we conclude that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ht}(\mathfrak{q}) \leq r - 1 \end{align*}
