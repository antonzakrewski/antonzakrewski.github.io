equivalence of a categories and monoidal structure

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) be an adjoint equivalence.

Suppose \(\mathcal{D}\) is a monoidal category, then there exist a unique monoidal structure on \(\mathcal{F}\) and a unique monoidal structure on \(\mathcal{C}\) such that \(\mathcal{F}\) is a monoidal equivalence
