tor of finitely generated groups as finitely generated group

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be finitely generated abelian groups. Then Tor \(\mathrm{Tor}_1^{\mathbb{Z}}(A,B)\) is also finitely generated.

2. Proof

using classification of finitely generated abelian groups and tor additive in both variables this reduces to

\begin{align*} A = \mathbb{Z}/n \mathbb{Z} \\ B = \mathbb{Z}/m \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

for \(n,m \in \mathbb{N}_0\).

For \(n = 0,1\) or \(m = 0,1\) this results in \(0\) (cf. tor functor for a flat module)

Otherwise it is

\begin{align*} \mathbb{Z}/ \mathrm{gcd}(n,m) \mathbb{Z} \end{align*}

(cf. tor1 of finite cyclic groups)

Thus it is a finite sum of finitely generated module and hence finitely generated module
