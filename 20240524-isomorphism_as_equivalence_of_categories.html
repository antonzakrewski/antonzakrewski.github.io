isomorphism as adjoint equivalence of categories

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) an isomorphism Then \(\mathcal{F}\) as a functor is an adjoint equivalence

2. Proof

corollary of:

  • adjoint equivalence of categories and fully faithful and essentially surjective functor
  • isomorphism in category cat as object-bijective, fully faithful functor
