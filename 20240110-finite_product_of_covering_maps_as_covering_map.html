finite product of covering maps as covering map

1. Proposition

Let \(B_1,B_2\) be topological spaces and \(p_1: E_1 \rightarrow B_1, p_2: E_2 \rightarrow B_2\) be covering maps. Then

\begin{align*} p_1 \times p_2: E_1 \times E_2 \rightarrow B_1 \times B_2 \end{align*}

is also a covering map

2. Proof

Let \((b_1,b_2) \in B_1 \times B_2\). Then by assumption, there exist neighbourhoods \(U_1 \subseteq B_1, U_2 \subseteq B_2\) such that

\begin{align*} p_1^{-1}[U_1] \simeq& F_1 \times U_1 \\ p_2^{-1}[U_2] \simeq& F_2 \times U_2 \end{align*}

Hence for \(U_1 \times U_2\), we get

\begin{align*} (p_1 \times p_2)^{-1}[U_1 \times U_2] =& p_1^{-1}[U_1] \times p_2^{-1}[U_2] \\ \simeq& (F_1 \times U_1) \times (F_2 \times U_2) \\ \simeq (F_1 \times F_2) \times (U_1 \times U_2) \end{align*}

where limits commute with limits and finite products of discrete spaces are discrete
