subgroup of a free group as free group

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a free group and \(U \subseteq G\) a subgroup. Then \(U\) is also free

2. Proof

Choose a generator of a group \(S\), such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Free}(S) \simeq G \end{align*}

using the fundamental group, we get a pushout

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} |[label={[label distance=-2mm]-45:\ulcorner}]| \mathrm{colim}_{S' \subseteq S} \coprod_{S'} S^0 \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{} & \mathrm{colim}_{S' \subseteq S} \coprod_{S'} D^1 \\ \mathrm{colim}_{S'}(*) & \mbox{} \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

… Thus we get an group-isomorphsim

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Free}(S) \simeq \mathrm{colim}_{S' \subesetq S}(\mathrm{Free}(S')) \simeq \bigvee_{S} S^1 \end{align*}

By fundamental theorem of covering theory, there exists a covering \(p: E \rightarrow \bigvee_S S^1\) with \(\pi_1(E,1) = H\). by covering space of a 1-dimensional CW Complex as 1-dimensional CW-Complex and by the classification of one dimensional CW Complexes up to homotopy, we get a Homotopy equivalence

\begin{align*} E \simeq \bigvee_{T} S^1 \end{align*}

Hence by the previous reasoning, we get

\begin{align*} H \simeq \pi_1(\bigvee_{T} S^1) \simeq \mathrm{Free}(T) \end{align*}
